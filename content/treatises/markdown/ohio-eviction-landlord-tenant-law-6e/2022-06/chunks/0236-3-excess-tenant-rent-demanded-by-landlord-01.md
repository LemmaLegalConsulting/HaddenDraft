# 3. Excess Tenant Rent Demanded by Landlord

## Source text

In the Housing Choice Voucher program (“HCVP”), a landlord may not demand or accept any rent payment from a tenant in excess of the tenant’s portion of the contract rent228 (“excess tenant rent”). HUD Tenancy Addendum, form HUD-52641- A (July 2019), ¶ 5(e).

Therefore, a landlord may not evict a tenant for nonpayment of excess tenant rent, 228 See note 224, above.

265 even if the tenant agreed to pay it. E.g., Jackson v. Hammond, No. 2006-CVG-31171 (Ohio Mun., Cleve land, Hous. Div., Feb. 15, 2007); Greene v. Edwards , No. CVG - 0301061 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland Heights, Sept. 10, 2003);McCuller v. Taylor, No. 00- CVG-13529 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., July 28, 2000).

If a tenant has paid excess tenant rent, and then fails to pay the tenant’s portion of the contract rent for one or more months, the excess tenant rent may be set-off against the unpaid rent. E.g., Evans v. Washington , No. 98 -CVI-4274 (Ohio Mun., Akron,
Aug. 20, 1998).

A tenant who has paid excess tenant rent also may recover a money judgment against the landlord in the amount of those payments. E.g., Powells v. Radey , No.

96898, 2012 WL 170891 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 19, 2012); Pinkney v. Strahorn,
No. 2006 -CV-08394 (Ohio C.P., Montgomery Cty., Apr. 23, 2008); Gonzalez v.

Gonzalez, No. 2010-CVG-9940 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Aug. 22, 2011);

Thompson v. Womack , No. 2007 -CVG-26006 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div.,
Mar. 27, 2008, revised Apr. 24, 2008); Camp v. Thomas , No. 06 -CVF-9711 (Ohio Mun., Dayton, June 21, 2007) (partial summary judgment); Greene v. Edwards, No.

CVG-0301061 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland Heights, Mar. 18, 2004);Dennis v. Dudley, No.

2002-CVG-12760 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Jan. 28, 2003); Fletcher v.

Shell, No. 1998-CVG-23988 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Oct. 2, 2000). See also Ray v. Thirty LLC, No. A-08-1020, 2009 WL 1819288 (Neb. App., June 23, 2009);

Crutchley v. Costa, No. SP5270/01, 2001 WL 1666007 (N.Y. Dist. Ct. Dec. 6, 2001), adhered to on reargument, 2002 WL 452200 (N.Y. Dist. Ct. Feb. 13, 2002).

In these circumstances, HCVP participantsalso have succeeded on a claim against the landlord under the federal False Claim Act (“FCA”). E.g., United States, ex rel.

Carmichael v. Gregory, 270 F. Supp.3d 67 (D.D.C. 2017); United States v. Baran, No.

CV 14-02639 RGK (AJWx), 2015 WL 5446833 (C.D. Cal. Aug. 28, 2015); Coleman
v. Hernandez, 490 F. Supp. 2d 278 (D. Conn. 2007);U.S. ex rel. Smith v. Gilbert Realty Co., 840 F. Supp. 71 (E.D. Mich. 1993). See Cummings v. Hale, No. 15 -cv-04723- JCS, 2017 WL 3669622 (N.D. Cal. May 17, 2017) (utility payments);United States ex
rel. Holmes v. Win Win Real Estate, Inc. No. 2:13-cv-02149-APG-GWF, 2015 WL 6150594 (D. Nev. Oct. 19, 2015) (homeowner’s association and management fees).

See also Kelly v. Denault , 374 F. Supp.3d 884 (N.D. Cal. 2018) (motion to dismiss denied); U.S. ex rel. Sutton v. Reynolds , 564 F. Supp.2d 1183 (D. Ore. 2007) (recognizes the tenant’s FCA claim; denies summary judgment due to factual disputes).

The FCA provides for treble damages and civil penalties between $5,500 and $11,000 per violation. 28 C.F.R. § 85.3(9).

If the tenant initiated the fraud, the court may refuse to award any amount to the tenant under the FCA. E.g., Sterns v. Lane, No. 2:08-cv-175, 2010 U.S. Dist. LEXIS 96981 (D. Vt. Sept. 15, 2010). In addition, some PHAs may find it grounds to terminate the family’s HCVP participation under 24 C.F.R. § 982.552(c)(1)(iv).

If the tenant agreed to the “side payments” under duress and/or coercion, the tenant has not committed a fraud and, therefore, should not be subject to a termination of participation under 24 C.F.R. § 982.552(c)(1)(iv). See 73 Fed. Reg. 39712 (July 10,
2008) (OIG, HUD fraud alert on landlords improperly requiring HCVP tenants to pay rent in excess of the amount authorized by the HAP contract).
