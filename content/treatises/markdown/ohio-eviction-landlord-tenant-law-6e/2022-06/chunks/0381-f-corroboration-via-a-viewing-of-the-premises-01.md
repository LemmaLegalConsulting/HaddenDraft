# f. Corroboration Via a Viewing of the Premises

## Source text

A tenant may request a view of the premises by the jury or judge. R.C. 2315.02.

“If the court is of the opinion that it is proper for the jurors to have a view of property which is the subject of litigation, or of a place where a material fact occurred, it may order them to be conducted in a body under the charge of an officer to such property or place, which shall be shown to them by a person appointed by the cour t for that purpose.” Id.

“The allowance of a view of the property or scene at issue is within the sound discretion of the trial court.” Hardy v. Miller, No. 98-CA-13, 1999 WL 1256347, at *7 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Dec. 9, 1999).
