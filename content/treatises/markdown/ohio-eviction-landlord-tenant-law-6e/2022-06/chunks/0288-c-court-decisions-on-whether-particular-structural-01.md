## Source text

Defects Violate the Duty Court decisions that illustrate the types of structural deficiency that violate (or do not violate) R.C. 5321.04(A)(2) are set forth below.
(1) Miller v. Ritchie, 45 Ohio St.3d 222, 543 N.E.2d 1265 (1989) (violation
– faulty and dangerous electrical wiring, holes in the floors, inadequate plumbing, and no heating system).
(2) McKinney v. Hartz & Restle Realtors, Inc. , 31 Ohio St.3d 244, 510 N.E.2d 386 (1987) (no violation – absence of fence along portion of the premises that abuts a railroad right-of-way).
(3) Shroades v. Rental Homes, Inc. , 68 Ohio St.2d 20, 427 N.E.2d 774
(1981) (violation – defective exterior stairs leading to dwelling unit).
(4) Goodman v. Dan Rich, LLC, No. 109581, 2021 WL 927001 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 11, 2021) (no violation – patio contained loose pavers).
(5) Lloyd v. Roosevelt Props., No. 105721, 2018 WL 3814972 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug. 9, 2018) (violation – inadequate heat, lack of drinkable water, constant exposure to cigarette smoke (in building advertised as smoke-free), and rodent infestation in common area).
(6) Tillimon v. Richardson-Long, No. L-16-1055, 2017 WL 169267 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Jan. 13, 2017) (violation – failing to install heating tape on pipes and/or to close crawlspace vents needed to prevent waterline in crawlspace from bursting in winter).
(7) Arnett v. Mong, 65 N.E.3d 72, 2016-Ohio-2893 (Ohio App., 12th Dist.,
2016) (no violation – hole in the gutter and/or gutter sagging).
(8) St. Germain v. Newell, No. 9-15-14, 2015 WL 5319833 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Sept. 14, 2015) (no violation – loose handrail).
(9) Wenzke v. Baird , No. L -13-1244, 2014 WL 3408590 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., July 11, 2014) (no violation – six inoperable or ill -functioning windows).
(10) Gress v. Wechter , No. H -12-023, 2013 WL 1092731 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Mar. 15, 2013) (no violation – lack of gutters).
(11) Mullins v. Grosz , No. 10AP -23, 2010 WL 3244139 (Ohio App., 10 th Dist., Aug. 17, 2010) (no violation – lack of a handrail or gating in the porch/step/walkway area of the premises).
(12) January Inv., LLC v. Ingram, No. CA2009-09-127, 2010 WL 1757940 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., May 3, 2010) (no violation – tenant’s expenses on exterior light fixtures, landscaping, vertical blinds, and chimney sweep).
(13) Mounts v. Ravotti, No. 07-MA-182, 2008 WL 4415819 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., Sept. 26, 2008) (violation – defective steps that provided the sole means of ingress to the rental unit).
(14) Cipollone v. Hoffmeier, No. C-060482, 2007 WL 2141578 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., July 27, 2007) (no violation – absence of window latches).
(15) Heck v. Whitehurst Co., No. L-03-1134, 2004 WL 1857131 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Aug. 20, 2004) (violation – cigarette smoke from neighbor’s apartment prevented tenant from using bedroom and caused clothes in closet to smell of smoke).
(16) McGuire v. K & G Mgmt. Co., No. 98-CA-35, 1998 WL 666756 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Aug. 27, 1998) (no violation – loose fitting screen, which served its purpose of providing ventilation and keeping out insects and birds, but did not prevent tenant’s child from exiting through 319 the window).
(17) McDaniels v. Petrosky , No. 97 -APE08-1027, 1998 WL 54363 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Feb. 5, 1998) (no viola tion – tree stump in the backyard).
(18) McKenzie v. Marlowe , No. 70714, 1996 WL 715502 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 12, 1996) (violation – faulty electrical wiring).
(19) Paul v. Pulskamp , No. CA96 -06-049, 1996 WL 707132 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Dec. 9, 1996) (no violation – hole, one foot deep and two feet in diameter, in the concrete near the edge of the driveway).
(20) Roberson v. Insignia Mgmt. Group, L.P. , No. C -940968, 1995 WL 757831 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Dec. 20, 1995) (no violation– loose fitting screen, which se rved its purpose of providing ventilation and keeping out insects and birds, but did not prevent tenant’s child from exiting through the window).
(21) Pease v. Croxton Realty, No. CA-8779, 1992 WL 362463 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Dec. 7, 1992) (no violation – “a three to four inch ‘defect’ [that] existed by the driveway and cement slab and the carport”).
(22) Glover v. Massey, Nos. 56351,56802, 1990 WL 1328 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 11, 1990) (violation – open drain in the basement floor that regularly backed up, causing the floor area near the drain to flood).
(23) Aldridge v. Englewood Vill., Ltd. , No. 10251, 1987 WL 15015 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., July 22, 1987) (no violation– threshold, leading from a back door to a patio, was three-quarters of an inch too high).
(24) Simpson v. Delta Prop. Mgmt., Inc., Nos. C-850562,850575, 1986 WL 7136 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., June 25, 1986) (violation– defective fixtures such as kitchen cabinets that may fall and cause injury).
(25) Taylor v. Alexander, No. 3550, 1986 WL 7800 (Ohio App., 11th Dis t., July 11, 1986) (no violation – lack of handrail).
(26) Hershberger v. Shelmar Realty, Inc., No. CV2014-07-3154 (Ohio C.P., Summit Cty., Jan. 13, 2016), aff’d on other grounds, No. 28110, 2017 WL 420698 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., J an. 31, 2017) (no violation – no gutters or awning over front steps, which allowed water to accumulate and then freeze).
