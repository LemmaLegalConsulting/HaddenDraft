## Source text

5321.09(D) In an R.C. 5321.09 action, if a court finds that the tenant caused the condition specified in the R.C. 5321.07 notice orthe tenant intentionally acted in bad faith in rent depositing, the tenant is liable for the landlord’s damages and costs. R.C. 5321.09(D).

See Gill v. Lewis Zipkin & Assocs. , No. 78-LLT-88 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland Heights,
Oct. 31, 1979). If the tenant i ntentionally acted in bad faith in rent depositing, the tenant is also liable for the landlord’s reasonable attorney’s fees.Id.

For R.C. 5321.09(D) purposes, bad faith includes “‘a dishonest purpose, moral obliquity, conscious wrongdoing, breach of aknown duty through some ulterior motive or ill will partaking of the nature of fraud. It also embraces actual intent to mislead or deceive another.’” Wenzke v. Baird, No. L-13-1244, 2014 WL 3408590, ¶ 25 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., July 11, 2014) (no evidence that the tenant acted with the requisite degree of culpability).

When city inspections find code violations at the premises that constitute grounds for rent depositing, it precludes a finding of bad faith under R.C. 5321.09(D).

Houtsinger v. Renzelli, No. RE 123 (Ohio Mun., Athens Cty., July 31, 1987).

When a tenant is liable for costs under R.C. 5321.09(D), the costs are computed in accordance with Civil Rule 54. See Smith v. Vilk , No. 46759, 1983 WL 2902 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 15, 1983) (construing R.C. 3733.122 (now R.C. 4781.43) – the manufactured home park pr ovision on releasing rent deposits that is essentially identical to R.C. 5321.09).
