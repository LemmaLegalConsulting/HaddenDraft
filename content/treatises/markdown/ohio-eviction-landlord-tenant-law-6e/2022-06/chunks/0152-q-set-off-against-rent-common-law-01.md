# Q. Set-Off Against Rent, Common Law

## Source text

In an eviction action for nonpayment of rent , if the tenant has a monetary claim against the landlord, a common law set-off of the tenant’s claim against the landlord’s rent claim may be a defense to the eviction action. This defense is independent of the R.C. 1923.061(B) defense (see Section II.O., above).

The set -off defense may be asserted where a tenant has repaired a defective condition at the premises that the landlord was obligated but failed to repair. “In Ohio if, upon breach of a landlord’s agreement to make repairs, the tenant himself makes the repairs, he may set off the cost of the repairs in an action by the landlord for rent.” Kuhn v. Griffin , 3 Ohio App.2d 195, 205, 209 N.E.2d 824 (6th Dist. 1964). Accord Story v. Jamison , No. 92 -CVG-22935 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Mar. 11 , 1993). See also Smith v. Padgett , 32 Ohio St.3d 344, 347 n.1, 513 N.E.2d 737 (1987).132 Consequently, in an eviction action for nonpayment of rent, a tenant may treat the cost of the repairs as a set -off against the alleged unpaid rent. See Padgett, 32 Ohio St.3d at 347 n.1;Kuhn, 3 Ohio App.2d at 204-05; Story v. Jamison, No. 92-CVG-22935 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Mar. 11, 1993). If the cost of the repairs equals or exceeds the amount of the alleged unpaid rent, the requirements of the set -off defense are met.
