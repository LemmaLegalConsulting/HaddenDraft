# c. PHA Termination Decision Upheld, Substantive

## Source text

Grounds King v. Akron Metro. Hous. Auth. , No. 25664, 2011 WL 2420232 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., June 15, 2011) Court of appeals affirmed trial court’s decision that upheld PHA termination for unauthorized occupant, based on evidence that included: admission of guest at the hearing that he stayed two nights per week; and admission of guest to the police that he “lived at her apartment, kept clothing there, and received his mail there.” Hammond v. Akron Metro. Hous. Auth. , No. 25425, 2011 WL 2175801 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., June 1, 2011) Court of appeals affirmed trial court’s decision that upheld PHA termination for unauthorized occupant, based on evidence that included: complaint of guest’s mother that he lived there; admission of participant that guest stayed overnight one or two nights per week, for several months; and guest’s receipt of mail there.

Miller v. Lake Metro. Hous. Auth., No. 11-CV-000613 (Ohio C.P., Lake Cty., Nov. 17, 2011) Court upheld PHA termination for unauthorized occupant, based on the admission of guest that he stayed up to three nights per week, which resulted in violation of PHA’s guest limitation, 90 days per 12 months.
