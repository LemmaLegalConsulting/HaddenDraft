# 1. Content of a Magistrate’s Decision

## Source text

When an eviction action is tried to a magistrate, the magistrate is required to prepare and file a magistrate’s decision. Ohio R. Civ. P. 53(D)(3)(a).

In eviction actions, a magistrate’s decision may be devoid of both findings of fact and conclusions of law. Miele v. Ribovich, 90 Ohio St.3d 439, 443-44, 739 N.E.2d 333 (2000);68 State ex rel. GMS Mgmt. Co., Inc. v. Callahan , 45 Ohio St.3d 51, 55, 543 N.E.2d 483 (1989).

However, a magistrate may sua sponte, or in response to a party’s motion under Civil Rule 53(D)(3)(a)(ii), include findings of fact and/or conclusions of law in the magistrate’s decision. State ex rel. GMS Mgmt. Co., Inc. v. Callahan , 45 Ohio St.3d 51, 55, 543 N.E.2d 483 (1989).
