An employee may lease and reside in residential premises pursuant to a contract of employment (“employee tenant”).262 R.C. Chapter 5321 has modified the common law rules regarding employee tenants.

Under common law, an employee tenant is either a servant or a tenant. services. Annotation, Nature of Occupancy of Person Occupying Premises of Employer as Part of Compensation, 39 A.L.R. 1145, 1145-46 (1925).

Under common law, an employee tenant’s (servant’s or tenant’s) right to possession of the premises terminates as soon as the employment contract is terminated, even if the employer wrongfully terminated the contract. McKibben v.

Rosselot, 4 Ohio App. 331 (12th Dist. 1915). See also Annotation, Right After Termination of Employment to Continue Occupancy of House Owned by Former Employer, 35 A.L.R. 576, 577 (1925).

R.C. Chapter 5321 has modified this common law distinction between servant and
tenant. Whenever a person resides in residential premises pursuant to an employment contract, the R.C. 5321.01 definitions of rental agreement, tenant, and landlord supersede the common law.

These employment contracts are rental agreements under R.C. 5321.01(D) because they establish rules and conditions c oncerning the use and occupancy of residential
premises. They meet the R.C. 5321.01(D) definition of rental agreement irrespective of whether the employee’s occupancy is related to the performance of the employment services.

Likewise, the employee is a te nant under R.C. 5321.01(A) and, if the employer owns or manages the residential premises, the employer is a landlord under R.C.

5321.01(B). See Grant v. Detroit Ass’n of Women’s Clubs , 443 Mich. 596, 505 N.W.2d 254 (1993) (when the essential characteristics of a landlord-tenant relationship are present, an employment contract may create a tenancy).

The status of tenant for employee tenants means that they are protected by the R.C.

Chapter 5321 tenant rights. For example, upon termination of the employment contract, the employer landlord may not seek to recover possession of the premises by terminating the utilities or changing the locks. See Chapter XII, below. Also, a month- to-month or week-to-week employment contract/rental agreement263 may be a periodic tenancy whose termination is subject to the R.C. 5321.17 notice requirements.

R.C. Chapter 5321 also expands the substantive rights of employee tenants to remain in possession of the premises.
