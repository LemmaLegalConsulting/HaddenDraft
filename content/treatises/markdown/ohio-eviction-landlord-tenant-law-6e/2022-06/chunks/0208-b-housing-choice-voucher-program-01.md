# b. Housing Choice Voucher Program

## Source text

In the Housing Choice Voucher program (“HCVP”), if a family fails to furnish (or furnishes inaccurate) information at a recertification, the housing authority is permitted, in some circumstances, to terminate the family’s participation in the HCVP and thereby terminate the family’s HCVP lease.

One of the HCVP family obligations is to “supply any information requested by the PHA or HUD for use in a regularly scheduled reexamination or interim reexamination of family in come or composition in accordance with HUD requirements.” 24 C.F.R. § 982.551(b)(2). A violation of the HCVP family obligations is grounds for terminating the family’s HCVP participation. 24 C.F.R.

§ 982.552(c)(1)(i). If the family’s HCVP participationis terminated, the family’s lease terminates automatically. HUD Tenancy Addendum, form HUD -52641-A (July 2019), ¶ 13.

However, if the family’s failure to report full and accurate information is an unintentional error, and not fraud, it is not grounds for terminating the family’s HCVP
participation. McClarty v. Greene Metro. Hous. Auth. , No. 2011 -CA-30, 2011 WL 3890295 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Sept. 2, 2011); Smith v. Hamilton County, Ohio, No.

C-060315, 2007 WL 1095680 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Apr. 13, 2007). See also Sweeney
v. Hous. Auth. of Chester County, 215 B.R. 97 (Bankr. E.D. Pa. 1997);Ellis v. Ritchie, 803 F. Supp. 1097 (E.D. Va. 1992).
