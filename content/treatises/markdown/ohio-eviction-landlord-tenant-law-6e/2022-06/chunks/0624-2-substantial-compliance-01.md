# 2. Substantial Compliance

## Source text

A contract for the sale of a residential dwelling may be a land installment contract even if it fails to include all of the provisions required by R.C. 5313.02(A). Elkins v.

Colburn, No. 18-CA-893, 2019 WL 2754096 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., June 21, 2019);

Phillips v. May, No. 2003-G-2520, 2004 WL 2526409 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Nov. 9, 2004); Gollihue v. Russo, 152 Ohio App.3d 710, 789 N.E.2d 1151 (10th Dist. 2003);

Real Flo Props. v. Kelly , No. L -99-1099, 1999 WL 1203751 (Ohio App., 6th Dist.,
Dec. 17, 199 9); Shimko v. Marks , 91 Ohio App.3d 458, 632 N.E.2d 990 (5th Dist.

1993); Young v. Hodapp, No. CA85-08-094, 1986 WL 14965 (Ohio App., 12th Dist.,
Dec. 29, 1986). But see Lovejoy v. Diel , No. 2020CA -06-067, 2021 WL 1246295 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Apr. 5, 2 021) (an agreement does not constitute a land installment contract if it omits an R.C. 5313.02(A) provision); Standard Fed. v.

Asencio, No. 98 -CA-007011, 1999 WL 61425 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Feb. 9, 1999) (omission of an R.C. 5313.02(A) element renders thecontract voidable by the parties).

“The purpose of R.C. Chapter 5313.02 is to protect the buyer by making specific information available in a land installment contract.” Real Flo Props. v. Kelly, No. L- 99-1099, 1999 WL 1203751, at *2 (Ohio App., 6th Dist ., Dec. 17, 1999). Accord Elkins v. Colburn, No. 18-CA-893, 2019 WL 2754096 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., June 21, 2019); Phillips v. May, No. 2003-G-2520, 2004 WL 2526409 (Ohio App., 11th Dist.,
Nov. 9, 2004); Young v. Hodapp , No. CA -85-08-094, 1986 WL 14965 (O hio App., 12th Dist., Dec. 29, 1986); Akron First Seventh Day Adventist Church v. Smith , No.

11577, 1984 WL 3956 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Sept. 27, 1984).

“The statute was not designed as a way for sellers to escape from contracts they wished to void.” Young v. Hodapp, No. CA85-08-094, 1986 WL 14965, at *3 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Dec. 29, 1986). Accord Elkins v. Colburn, No. 18-CA-893, 2019 WL 2754096 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., June 21, 2019); Phillips v. May , No. 2003 -G-2520, 2004 WL 2526409 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Nov. 9, 2004); Real Flo Props. v. Kelly ,
No. L-99-1099, 1999 WL 1203751 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Dec. 17, 1999).

Therefore, a contract may be a land installment contract if it substantially complies with the R.C. 5313.02(A) requirements. Elkins v. Colburn, No. 18-CA-893, 2019 WL 2754096 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., June 21, 2019); Phillips v. May , No. 2003 -G-2520, 2004 WL 2526409 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Nov. 9, 2004);Gollihue v. Russo, 152 Ohio App.3d 710, 789 N.E.2d 1151 (10th Dist. 2003); Real Flo Props. v. Kelly, No. L-99- 1099, 1999 WL 1203751 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Dec. 17, 1999); Whitacre v. Starks ,
No. 1998-CA-0307, 1999 WL 547943 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 29, 1999); Shimko
v. Marks, 91 Ohio App.3d 458, 632 N.E.2d 990 (5th Dist. 1993).

“[T]he proper inquiry for determining whether the land installment contract [is] valid is to determine if all the essential elements of a contract [are] present.” Young v.

Hodapp, No. CA85-08-094, 1986 WL 14965, at *3 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Dec. 29, 1986). See, e.g., Hudson v. Bates, No. 93-CVG-7406 (Ohio Mun., Marion Cty., Jan.

26, 1993) (“the ‘Land Contract’ does contain sufficient elements to fit within the [R.C.

5313.01] definition of a land installment contract”).

“Ohio law does not require a metes and bounds legal description of property in order to create a binding agreement to sell that property.” Phillips v. May, No. 2003- G-2520, 2004 WL 2526409, ¶ 36 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Nov. 9, 2004). But the contract must contain a description of the particular land to be conveyed or must furnish the means of identifying it with reasonable certainty and specificity. Id. See also Nofzinger v. Blood, No. H-02-014, 2003 WL 1464519 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Mar.

14, 2003).

Also, if a contract provision declares that the c ontract is a statement of intent to enter into a formal land installment contract in the future, the declaration can be a factor for finding that the contract is not a land installment contract, even though the contract contains some of the R.C. 5313.02 el ements. E.g., Grexa v. Hollenbaugh, No. 2014- T-0039, 2014 WL 5421222 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Oct. 27, 2014).
