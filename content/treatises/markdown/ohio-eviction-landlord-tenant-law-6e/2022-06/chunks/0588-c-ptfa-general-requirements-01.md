## Source text

When the PTFA applies, the new owner acquires title subject to the rights of any bona fide tenants under their respective rental agreements. The new owner becomes the landlord under the rental agreements, like a purchaser of tenant -occupied real property under Ohio common law. See Section XXII.B, above.

However, the new owner’s right to terminate the rental agreement is subject to certain PTFA requirements.

With respect to written leases, absent a lease violation, the lease continues until the end of the lease te rm, with two exceptions. First, the new owner may, with a 90 -day notice of termination, terminate the lease in order to use the property as the new owner’s personal residence. Second, if the lease has less than 90 days remaining when the new owner acquir es title, the lease continues until either: (1) the new owner provides the tenant with a 90 -day notice of termination, or (2) the tenant vacates the property at the end of the lease term or thereafter.

With respect to month -to-month tenancies, absent ca use for termination, the tenancy continues until either: (1) the new owner provides the tenant with a 90 -day 605 notice of termination and the notice period expires, or (2) the tenant provides the new owner with a 30-day notice of termination under R.C. 5321.17(B) and the notice period expires.

With respect to Housing Choice Voucher program (“HCVP”) tenancies, the HCVP lease is treated the same as other written leases. In addition, the Housing Assistance Payments (“HAP”) contract continues, with the new ownersubject to the terms of the HAP contract. The PTFA also bars the new owner from terminating the HCVP lease on the “other good cause” business ground that it will assist in the sale of the property.

The new owner (or the new owner’s agent) is the only part y who may serve the 90-day notice of termination. See HUD Notice, 74 Fed. Reg. 30106, 30107 (June 24, 2009).

The service of the 90-day notice of termination may not precede the confirmation of the foreclosure sale because, before the confirmation: (1) t he determination as to which entity (or person) will be the new owner has not been made; (2) none of the potential new owners (such as the mortgagee) is the new owner at that time; and (3) since none of the potential new owners (aside from the mortgagor) possess title to the property, the potential new owners are without standing to serve a notice of termination.

Since the 90-day notice of termination is analogous to the R.C. 5321.17(B) notice of termination, two conclusions reasonably follow. First, gener ally the tenant is obligated to pay rent during the 90 -day notice period. See Section I.D.4, above.

Second, the bar against serving the R.C. 1923.04 notice to vacate prior to the expiration of the tenancy should apply to the 90-day notice period. See Section I.C.3.e., above.
