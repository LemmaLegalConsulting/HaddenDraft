# 4. Right to Counsel in Eviction Actions

## Table-of-contents context

- XXII. Miscellaneous Landlord-Tenant
  - J. Municipal Ordinances and Court Rules:

## Source text

Generally, Ohio law does not provide parties in a civil action (including eviction
actions) with a right to counsel. Robinson v. Mays, No. L-19-1065, 2020 WL 1528012 625 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Mar. 31, 2020) (no constitutional right to counsel in eviction actions; upheld trial court’s ruling that required tenant to proceed pro se when tenant’s attorney could not appear at the eviction hearing due to illness).

Two Ohio municipalities have enacted ordinances that establish a tenant’s right to counsel in eviction actions. Those ordinances (and municipalities) are Cleveland Codified Ordinances pt. III.C, tit. IX, ch. 375, § 375.12, and Toledo Municipal Code
pt. 17, tit. 3, ch. 1768.

The Cleveland ordinance, implemented July 2020, entitles covered individuals to full legal representation in eviction actions filed in the Cleveland Municipal Court, Housing Division. Covered individuals are tenants who have at least one child in the household and whose inco me does not exceed one hundred percent of the federal poverty guidelines.

The Toledo ordinance, implemented January 2022, entitles covered individuals to full legal representation in eviction actions filed in the Toledo Municipal Court, Housing Division. Covered individuals are tenants whose annual gross income does not exceed two hundred percent of the federal poverty guidelines.
