Likewise, when a court dismisses a first eviction a ction due to the landlord’s waiver (via future rent acceptance) of the notice to vacate, the landlord must serve a new notice to vacate before filing a second eviction action against the tenant. See N.

Also, when a landlord serves the tenant with a second notice to vacate and the service of the second notice is subsequent to the landlord’s filing of the eviction complaint, the second notice to vacate waives the first notice to vacate. RLJ Mgmt.
Co. Inc. v. Lewis, No. 2020-CVG-33669 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., June 2, 2021). As a result, in these circumstances: (1) the landlord is attempting to rely on a notice to vacate that was not served prior the filing of the complaint; and (2) therefore, the court lacks subject-matter jurisdiction over the eviction action. Id.; DePalmo v. George, No.

88-CVG-0572 (Ohio Mun., Licking Cty., Aug. 23, 1988). The proper disposition of the eviction action is a dismissal either by the landlord or the court, with the landlord retaining the option to file a second eviction action. Id.
