Foreclosure R.C. 5313.07 and 5313.08 govern the action for possession that a vendor may bring if a vendee breaches the contract. While R.C. 5313.07 and 5321.08 limit the remedies available to the trial court in an action for breach of a land installment contract, they are not jurisdictional statutes. Davis v. Johnson , No. L -19-1268, 2021 WL 1415 04, ¶ 42 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Jan. 15, 2021) (“Initially, we note that R.C. 5313.07 is not a jurisdictional statute, but rather is a limit on the remedies available to the trial court”).

R.C. 5313.07 and 5313.08 divide land installment contracts into t wo groups relative to a vendor’s action for possession.

The first group is comprised of land installment contracts that have been in effect for less than five years and upon which less than twenty percent of the purchase price has been paid.

The second group is comprised of land installment contracts that have been in effect for five or more years or upon which twenty percent or more of the purchase price has been paid.

The determination on whether twenty percent of the purchase price has been paid is b ased on the face amount of the contract price, which excludes payments for insurance premiums, real estate taxes, or interest on unpaid principal, unless the contract stipulates otherwise. Stiffler v. F.I.O.P. Assocs., LLC, No. 28501, 2020 WL 1082366 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Mar. 6, 2020); Lindhorst v. Elkadi, No. 80162, 2002 WL 999315 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 16, 2002); Whitacre v. Starks, No. 1998-CA- 0307, 1999 WL 547943 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 29, 1999); Ramsey v. Farris, No.

64179, 1993 WL 497059 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 2, 1993); Smith v. Blackburn, 31 Ohio App.3d 251, 511 N.E.2d 132 (4th Dist. 1987); Burdge v. Welsh, No. 82AP-846, 1983 WL 3367 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Feb. 22, 1983).

When a vendor loans money to the vendee subsequentto the execution of the land installment contract, and the parties agree to add the loan amount to the principal of the land installment contract, the loan amount is nonetheless excluded from the purchase price for the determination on whether twenty perc ent of the purchase price has been paid. Waters v. Williams, No. 06-CA-88, 2007 WL 172032 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Jan. 22, 2007) (five years after execution of the contract, $20,000 loan).

When a land installment contract is renegotiated and the final contract declares that the earlier contract(s) are null and void, the five -year period for R.C. 5321.07,.08 purposes begins to run as of the execution date of the final contract.Smith v. Williams,
No. 2012-CA-00117, 2013 WL 1282002 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Ma r. 28, 2013). Cf.

Mohn v. Ousley, No. 2015-CA-00073, 2015 WL 9393954 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Dec.

21, 2015) (termination agreement for first contract waived application of its time and payments in computation of time and payments under second contract).

With respect to land installment contracts in the second group, the vendor may recover possession of the property only through a foreclosure action and a judicial sale.

641 R.C. 5313.07,.08. See also Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Watkins, 23 Ohio App.3d 20, 491 N.E.2d 701 (8th Dist. 1984);Hudson v. Bates, No. 93-CVG-7406 (Ohio Mun., Marion Cty., Jan. 26, 1993).

With respect to land installment contracts in the first group, the vendor may recover possession of the property either through an eviction action or through a foreclosure action and a judicial sale. R.C. 5313.07,.08. See also Casto v. Prokop ,
No. 93-T-4853, 1995 WL 407217 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., June 9, 1995).

Two appellate districts are split on whether, in the eviction action, the burden of proof, relative to the twenty -percent standard, is on the vendor or the vendee. The Second District has held that this burden of proof is on the vendor.394 White v. Daniel,
No. 12319, 1991 WL 262675 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Dec. 10, 1991). The Fifth District has held that the twenty-percent standard is an affirmative defense and, therefore, the burden of proof is on the vendee. Sanders v. Crawford , No. 2006 -CA-00194, 2007 WL 1413218 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., May 14, 2007).

If the burden of proof is on the vendor , “[w]hen [the vendor] cannot state the balance due on the contract, he cannot meet his burden of proving that less than twenty per cent has been paid on the contract.” White, 1991 WL 262675, at *4.
