# 4. Oral Agreement, Partial Performance

## Table-of-contents context

- XXIII. Land Installment Contracts,
  - B. Contract Requirements and Related
    - 4. Oral Agreement, Partial Performance

## Source text

“[P]art performance of an oral land installment contract for the sale of real estate can be sufficient to remove the contract from the operation of the statute [of frauds, R.C. 1335.05].” Shimko v. Marks , 91 Ohio App.3d 458, 461, 632 N.E.2d 990 (5th
Dist. 1993). E.g., Gay v. Parr , No. CVG -00-141 (Ohio County Ct., Harrison Cty.,
Dec. 21, 2000) (substantial repairs to the premises and installment payments on the down payment). See also Monea v. Lanci , No. 2009 -CA-0083, 2009 WL 4690214 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Dec. 7, 2009) (summary judgment reversed due to genuine issue of material facts; occupant alleges payment of two -thirds of purchase price and substantial improvements to the property).

“Ohio courts generally consider the following factors to be relevant in determining the applicability of the part performance doctrine: (1) evidence of a change in possession; (2) payment of all or part of the consideration for the land; and (3) improvements, alterations or repairs upon the land by the possessor.” Monea v. Lanci,
No. 2009-CA-0083, 2009 WL 4690214, ¶ 21 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Dec. 7, 2009).

For a discussion on the statute of conveyances, the statute of frauds, and the partial performance exception, see Section XXII.A, above.
