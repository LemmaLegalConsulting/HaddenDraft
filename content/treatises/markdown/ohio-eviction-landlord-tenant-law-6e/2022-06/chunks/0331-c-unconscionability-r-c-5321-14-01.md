# c. Unconscionability, R.C. 5321.14

## Source text

Late payment fees are subject to R.C. 5321.14, which prohibits and makes unenforceable any late payment fee that is unconscionable. This prohibition against unconscionable fees does not supplant the penalty/liquidated damages restriction, but rather supplements it. Wadsworth v. Starcher, No. 97-A-0054, 1998 WL 553160 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., June 26, 1998).

Court decisions finding a late payment fee to be unconscionable and thus unenforceable under R.C. 5321.14 include:
(1) Drake Townhouses, LLC v. Woodberry, No. C-160632, 2017 WL 3168981 (Ohio App., 1 st Dist., Ju ly 26, 2017) (monthly rent of $ 710;

359 late payment fee of $10 per day, with a maximum of $150 per month, is unconscionable; late fee of $10 per day, with a maximum of $50, would not be unconscionable).
(2) Campus Vill. Toledo Univ. Park, LLC v. Mowrer, 68 N.E.3d 219, 2016- Ohio-4754 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., 2016) (late payment fee of $5 per day is unconscionable; one -time late payment fee of $40 would not be unconscionable).
(3) Anderson v. Ballard , No. L -10-1007, 2010 WL 3292685 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Aug. 20, 2010) (monthly rent of $400; late payment fee of $55 if one day late).
(4) Miller v. Hamm , No. 15-CV-0580 (Ohio C.P., Marion Cty., June 22,
2016) (monthly lot rent of $250; late payment fee of $5 per day).
(5) N. River Place Apts. v. Hucks , No. CVG -01-00240 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, Nov. 20, 2001) (monthly rent of $500; late payment fee of $35 on the sixth day of the month and $5 per day thereafter; the $5 per day provision is unconscionable and unenforceable).
(6) Gentil v. Williams, No. 96-CVG-1095 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, Nov. 12,
1996) (monthly rent of $450; late payment fee of $15 per day ($405 for August 1996 and $105 for September 1996)).
(7) Respiratory & Clinical Supply Corp. v. Freshour , No. 88 -CV-F-128 (Ohio Mun., Hillsboro, Dec. 23, 1988) (monthly rent of $220; late payment fee of $3 per day up to ten days and $5 per day thereafter).
(8) Schwenkel v. Daley, No. 92-CVG-2825 (Ohio Mun., Lakewood, Jan. 19,
1993) (monthly rent of $300; late payment fee of $5 per day ($280 for September 1992 and $130 for October 1992)).

One court found t hat any award of late payment fees for a specified month was unconscionable, given the circumstances in the case. The circumstances were: (1) the landlord’s prior indication that it would not accept rent for that month (due to its service of the notice to vacate); and (2) the tenant’s move -out prior to that month, on the mistaken understanding that, by so moving, she would not owe any rent for that month.

Drake Townhouses, LLC v. Woodberry, No. C-160632, 2017 WL 3168981 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., July 26, 2017).

One court found a late payment fee to be enforceable because the fee was not procedurally unconscionable as a matter of law. Good Knight Props., LLC v. Adam ,
No. L-13-1231, 2014 WL 4656870 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Sept. 19, 2014).
