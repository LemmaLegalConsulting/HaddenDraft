# d. R.C. 4781.40 (Former R.C. 3733.11)

## Source text

Dearwester’s Gem City Estates v. Nelson, No. 14320, 1994 WL 681104 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Dec. 7, 1994) In an eviction action for nonpayment of rent, the R.C. 3733.11(B) requirement to disclose the rental fee is not an element of the park operator’s claim. Any failure to meet the disclosure requirement is a defense for which the resident has the burden of proof.

Callahan v. Croxford , No. 2006 -CVF-00621, 2007 WL 4359907 (Ohio Mun., Athens Cty., July 31, 2007) $200 per month rent under verbal agreement, but no written agreement or written disclosure. During a 17 -month occupancy, the resident paid the lot rent only for three months. “R.C. 3733.11(H)(4) prohibits a park operator from charging ‘any fee, charge, or assessment, including a rental 581 fee’ unless the terms are set forth in writing.” Therefore, the court granted judgment to the resident on the park operator’s counterclaim for lot rent.

Reck v. Lucas , No. 2006 -CVG-00235, 2006 WL 4399761 (Ohio Mun., Auglaize Cty., Aug. 25, 2006) Because the park operator failed to offer the residents a written lease, the park operator’s attempt to raise the rent, due to late payment of rent, was not permitted.

Bennett v. Croxford, No. 96-CVH-000488, 1997 WL 810044 (Ohio Mun., Athens Cty., Apr. 18, 1997) The park operator violated R.C. 3733.11(B) in failing “to disclose fully the rates, charges and assessments for water prior to the commencement of the Plaintiff’s [resident’s] occupancy.” The court awarded the resident a judgment in the amount of the resident’s water payments and held that the resident was not liable for the unpaid or unbilled water charges.

Apel v. Wolf , No. CVG-960027, 1996 WL 740085 (Ohio Mun., Newton Falls, Sept. 10, 1996) The park operator violated R.C. 3733.11(B) in assessing a $15 per month surcharge for taxes and insurance without the required disclosure. The court awarded the resident a judgment in the amount of the resident’s surcharge payments.

Estep v. Tisdale, No. 93 -CVG-700, 1993 WL 837913 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, Nov. 29, 1993) The park operator failed to provide the resident with a written disclosure of the rental fee or the electric service fee before the execution of the rental
agreement. Therefore, R.C. 3733.11(H)(4) barred the park operator from evicting the resident for nonpayment of either of these fees.
Props. Ltd. v. Coffee, No. 93-CVG-8597 (Ohio Mun., Marion Cty., Nov.

16, 1993) R.C. 3733.11(B) barred the park operator’s claim for a $15 per month surcharge for water service because the park operator had not disclosed the fee in writing before the execution of the rental agreement.

Miller Mobile Homes, Inc. v. Reeves , No. 92 -CVG-15700, 1993 WL 837914 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Sept. 23, 1992) R.C. 3733.11(B) and 3733.11(H)(4) barred the park operator’s claim for various fees (cost of moving resident’s home, storing the home, and cleaning the lot) because the fees had not been disclosed in writing before the execution of the rental agreeement. A $200 entrance fee violated R.C.

3733.11(H)(5) because it was a transfer of ownership fee. The court granted the resident credit for the entrance fee.

Pets Jowers v. Eastgate Vill., Ltd., No. CA98-10-095, 1999 WL 374131 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., June 7, 1999) The park rule required registration of dogs and prohibited dogs over twenty pounds. The park operator was evicting a third -party resident for violating this park rule. Pending the eviction, the third-party’s dog bit the daughter of a residen t (plaintiff) in the third -party’s home. The park operator was not liable under R.C. 955 (strict liability) or common law because they limit a landlord’s liability to cases in which the landlord permits the dog in the common area and the dog bite here didnot occur in the common area. The court found its decision to be consistent withBundy
(below) and Heitman (below) because, in those cases, the dog bite occurred in the common area and the landlord permitted the dogs in common area by not enforcing the relevant park rules.

Burgess v. Tackas, 125 Ohio App.3d 294, 708 N.E.2d 285 (8th Dist. 1998) The park rule limited residents to one dog. A resident had two dogs and, at the resident’s door, one of the two dogs bit a visitor. The park operator was not liable under R.C. 955 (strict liability) or common law because they limit a landlord’s liability to cases in which the landlord permits the dog in the common area and the dog bite here did not occur in the common area.

Thompson v. Irwin, No. CA97-05-101, 1997 WL 666079 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Oct. 27, 1997) The park rule required that pets be on a leash at all times. A resident’s dog broke a chain and escaped in violation of the park rule. The dog then bit a third-party in the common area. The park operator was not liable under R.C. 955 (strict liability) or common law because they limit a landlord’s liability to cases in which the landlord permits the dog in the common area and here the landlord neither permitted nor acquiesced in the dog being kept in the common area.
