## Source text

The federal Low Income Housing Tax Credit (“LIHTC”) program (see 26 U.S.C.

§ 42) uses income tax credits to fund construction, rehabilitation, and acquisition/ rehabilitation of rental units for low-income families. For a general description of the LIHTC program, see National Housing Law P roject, HUD Housing Programs:

Tenants’ Rights, ¶ 1.11 (5th ed. 2018). The Ohio Housing Finance Agency (“OHFA”) administers the LIHTC program for the state of Ohio.

Sections III.L.2-4, below, provide a brief summary of the program’s set -aside of low-income units, rent structure, and eviction rules.
