# d. Payment by Mail

## Source text

When a rental agreement provides that the tenant is to pay rent by mailing the payments to the landlord, the tenant’s timely mailing of a rent payment to the landlord, in an envelope properly addressed and stamped, constitutes acceptance of the rent by the landlord. Townhomes Mgmt. v. Carter, No. 84AP-851, 1985 WL 9874 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Feb. 19, 1985);Vistula Mgmt. Co. v. Riley, No. CVG-93-19379 (Ohio Mun., Toledo, Hous. Div., Jan. 20, 1994),aff’d, No. L-94-069, 1994 WL 590503 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Oct. 28, 1994); Edwards v. Smith , No. M’8711-CVG-37183 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Dec. 8, 1987);Norskog v. Atha, 61 Ohio Law Abs. 604, 102 N.E.2d 907 (Ohio Mun., Dayton, 1951).

In such circumstances, the tenant’s responsibility does not include proper delivery of the mail by the Postal Service. Id.

When a lease permits rent payment by mail, the tenant’s rent payment is timely if the envelope containing the rent payment is postmarked by the due date, unless the lease expressly requires landlord receipt by the due date. Crossings, LLC v. Coinmach Corp., No. 2008-CVG-01528 (Ohio Mun., Elyria, June 12, 2008).
