# 1. Consolidated or Separate Trials

## Source text

In an eviction action, the parties may assert non -eviction claims. The complaint often alleges an eviction claim and a monetary claim. See Section I.E.2.f, above. The tenant may file counterclaims. See Section I.I, above.

The court may consolidate the trials on these claims and counterclaims. R.C.

1923.081; Ohio R. Civ. P. 42. R.C. 1923.081 “permits joinder of causes of action for possession, past due rent, damages, and counterclaims, in one trial.” Forney v.

Climbing Higher Enters., Inc., 158 Ohio App.3d 338, 345, 815 N.E.2d 722 (9th Dist.

2004). Accord Brown v. Brown , No. 04 -CA-000018, 2005 WL 91 4490, ¶ 23 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Apr. 14, 2005).

The trial on the eviction claim and the trial on the landlord’s monetary claim may be consolidated. Smith v. Wright, 65 Ohio App. 2d 101, 103, 104 n.2, 416 N.E.2d 655 (8th Dist. 1979). The trial on the ev iction claim and the trial on the tenant’s counterclaims may be consolidated. See, e.g., Behrle v. Beam, 6 Ohio St.3d 41, 451 N.E.2d 237 (1983). The trial on the landlord’s monetary claim and the trial on the tenant’s counterclaims may be consolidated. See Ohio R. Civ. P. 42(A).

Alternatively, the court may hold separate trials on any of these claims and
counterclaims. Hamdan v. Traish , No. 14 -MA-14, 2015 WL 6697330, ¶ 19 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., Oct. 26, 2015);Oakwood Mgmt. Co. v. Richards, No. 99AP-627, 1999 WL 1124692, at *1 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Dec. 9, 1999);Sholiton Indus., Inc. v. Royal Arms, Ltd., No. 17480, 1999 WL 355898, at *9 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., June 4, 1999);

Smith v. Wright, 65 Ohio App. 2d 101, 103, 104 n.2, 416 N.E.2d 655 (8th Dist.1979);

Siegler v. Batdorff , 63 Ohio App.2d 76, 84 -85, 408 N.E.2d 1383 (8th Dist. 1979);

Gibraltar Constr. Corp. v. Fortune, No. 396, 1979 Ohio App. LEXIS 9223 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Nov. 28, 1979). See generally Ohio R. Civ. P. 42(B).

The decision on whether to hold consolidated or separate trials on these claims and counterclaims generally is committed to the discretion of the trial court. Sholiton Indus., Inc. v. Royal Arms, Ltd., No. 17480, 1999 WL 355898 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., June 4, 1999); Siegler v. Batdorff, 63 Ohio App.2d 76, 408 N.E.2d 1383 (8th Dist.

1979). See also Palombo v. Hayes, Nos. 65781,66714, 1995 WL 229120 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Apr. 13, 1995) (R.C. 1923.081 consolidation is discretionary).

“Where an action for forcible entry and detainer is joined with an action for unpaid rent or other damages, the matter is typically divided into two separate hearings. In the first hearing on the claim for forcible entry and detainer (the ‘first cause’), the court determines whether the plaintiff, typically a landlord or property owner, is entitled to possession of the premises. If the plaintiff prevails on the first cause and the occupant is evicted, a second hearing will be held on the ‘second cause’ for unpaid rent or
damages. Often, until an occupant has been evicted and has vacated the premises, a plaintiff cannot know the full extent of his or her damages in the second cause.” Mathews v. Cooper, No. 109974, 2021 WL 3560200, ¶ 77 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug.

12, 2021).

However, the decision on whether to consolidate the trial on the eviction claim and the trial on the landlord’s monetary claim is subject to the requirements of R.C.

1923.081 and Civil Rule 12(A)(1).

R.C. 1923.081 provides that the trials on these landlord claims may be consolidated unless there is good cause not to do so. R.C. 1923.081 specifies three examples of good cause not to consolidate the trials: a tenant’s request to file an answer, a tenant’s request to file a counterclaim, and a tenant’s request to conduct discovery. R.C.

1923.081 also provides that the trials on these landlord claims cannot be consolidated if the adverse party in the eviction action is a deceased resident of a manufactured 79 home park.59 Civil Rule 12(A)(1) bars consolidation of the trials on these landlord claims if the date of the consolidated trial precedes the expiration of the Civil Rule 12(A)(1) time period for filing an answer to the landlord’s monetary claim and, as of the trial date, the tenant has not filed an answer. Wodzisz v. Bayes, No. 96-APE07-891, 1997 WL 142715, at *4 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 25, 1997);Smith v. Wright, 65 Ohio App.2d 101, 103, 416 N.E.2d 655 (8th Dist. 1979). See also Ruminski v. Matulis , No. 1219, 1985 WL 4235 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Dec. 6, 1985).

When a tenant files a counterclaim under R.C. 1923.061(B),60 the court is required to consolidate the trial on the eviction claim and the trial on the tenant’s counterclaims.

Studer v. Roark , No. 94 -CA-38, 1995 WL 347963 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Mar. 21, 1995); Sandefur Mgmt. Co. v. Smith, 21 Ohio App.3d 145, 486 N.E.2d 1234 (10th Dist.

1985). In these cases, the court cannot determine the eviction claim without an adjudication of the tenant’s counterclaims. Id.
