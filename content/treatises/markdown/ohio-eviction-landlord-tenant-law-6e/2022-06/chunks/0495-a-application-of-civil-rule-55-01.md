# a. Application of Civil Rule 55

## Source text

When a landlord’s complaint includes an eviction claim and a monetary claim for unpaid rent (or property damage), the tenant’s presence at the eviction hearing constitutes an appearance in the action for Civil Rule 55(A) purposes. Koob v. Rue, 73 Ohio App.3d 418, 597 N.E.2d 550 (2nd Dist. 1992); Newlon v. Tallman, No. CA- 2520, 1988 WL 17835 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Jan. 22, 1988); Rader v. Swanson, No.

80AP-954, 1981 WL 3191 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., May 14, 1981);Harris v. Hampton,
No. 2005-CVG-43533 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Mar. 24, 2006).

Therefore, in such cases, if the landlord files an application for default judgment on the monetary claim, the tenant must be served with written notice of the application at least seven days prior to the hearing on the application. Ohio R. Civ. P. 55(A). See Koob, 73 Ohio App.3d at 420 -21; Newlon, 1988 WL 17835, at *1; Rader, 1981 WL 3191, at *1-2.

“Without the requisite notice and hearing under Civ.R. 55(A), a default judgment is void and shall be vacated upon appeal.” Hartmann v. Ohio Crime Victims Reparations Fund, 138 Ohio App.3d 235, 238, 741 N.E.2d 149 (10th Dist. 2000).

Accord AMCA Int’l Corp. v. Carlton, 10 Ohio St.3d 88, 461 N.E.2d 1282 (1984). See also Rader v. Swanson , No. 80AP-954, 1981 WL 3191 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., May 14, 1981) (reversed denial of Civil Rule 60(B) motion).
