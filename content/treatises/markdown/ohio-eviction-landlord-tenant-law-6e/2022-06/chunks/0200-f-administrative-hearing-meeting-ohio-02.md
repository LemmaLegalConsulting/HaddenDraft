Independent Mgmt. Servs. of Ohio, Inc. v. Davis , No. 2014-CVG-01244 (Ohio Mun., Elyria, July 18, 2014) (project-based subsidized housing) The meeting “is not a mere formality, but conf ers on Defendant, beneficiary of the federal subsidy, a first chance at procedural due process to meet with Plaintiff to avoid the potential termination.” Held the notice of termination invalid because it “suggests that the decision to terminate Defendant ’s tenancy already occurred” and thus “[i]t is not clear what benefit could be derived from the tenant’s ‘discussion’ of a matter already decided.” Akron Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Wilkinson, No. 2001-CVG-110 (Ohio Mun., Akron, April 25, 2001) (Public Housing) The housing authority asserted two grounds for termination of the tenancy. With respect to one (i.e., unauthorized occupant), the tenant was entitled to an opportunity for a grievance procedure hearing. With respect to the other (i.e., drug-related criminal activity), the tenant was not entitled to an opportunity for a grievance procedure
hearing. In such circumstances, the housing authority may not serve the R.C. 1923.04 notice to vacate until after the tenant has been afforded an opportunity for a grievance procedure hearing on the relevant ground.

Plains Plaza Ltd. v. Rupe , No. 90 -CVG-11-43 (Ohio Mun., Athens Cty., Mar. 11,
1991) (Section 8 New Construction) Under 24 C.F.R. § 880.607 and HUD Handbook 4350.3, ¶ 4-20 (now HUD Handbook 4350.3 REV -1,196 ¶ 8-13(B)), the landlord’s failure to meet with the tenant and meaningfully discuss the proposed termination of tenancy prior to filing the eviction action deprived the court of jurisdiction to evict the tenant. Settlement discussions between counsel for the parties and discovery proceedings are not a substitute for the required informal meeting between the landlord and tenant. At the required informal meeting, the landlord’s representative must be an impartial decision maker.

Plains Plaza, Ltd. v. McKinley , No. CIG -90-5-34 (Ohio Mun., Athens Cty., Feb. 8,
1991) (Section 8 New Construction) The landlord failed to provide the tenant with an informal meeting that satisfied the requirements of HUD Handbook 4350.3 (now HUD Handbook 4350.3 REV -1, ¶ 8 - 13(B)) because the landlord’s representative at the meeting had been involved with the gathering of the disputed information.

Lorain Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Lawson , 40 Ohio Misc.2d 25, 532 N.E.2d 1323 (Ohio Mun., Elyria, 1987) (Public Housing) The housing authority’s fa ilure to comply with several grievance procedure requirements (24 C.F.R. §§ 966.54-.58) was a valid defense to the eviction action.

Deerwood Mgmt. Co. v. Flint, No. M85-CVG-24397 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Oct.

15, 1985) (Section 8 Additional Assistance) 24 C.F.R. § 886.128 required the landlord to give the tenant a meaningful opportunity to be heard at a meeting. The meeting is not a mere formality. The meeting must be conducted by an agent for the landlord who is at least willing to listen and considerthe stated position of the tenant. This standard was not met because the landlord’s agent had his mind made up before the meeting was commenced.
