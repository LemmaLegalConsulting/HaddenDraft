## Source text

When a tenant fails to maintain or use the premises in compliance with the tenant’s R.C. 5321.05 obligations,350 the landlord may bring an action against the tenant under R.C. 5321.05(C) for actual damages, reasonable attorney’s fees, and, where applicable, injunctive relief to access the premises.

The landlord has “the burden to prove [tenant’s] negligent or intentional conduct by a preponderance of the evidence….[The tenant does] not have to prove that she did not violate the statute or the terms of the lease.”Douglas v. Campost Props., LLC, No.

2016-CV-102094, slip op. at 2 (Ohio Mun., Elyria, July 21, 2020).
