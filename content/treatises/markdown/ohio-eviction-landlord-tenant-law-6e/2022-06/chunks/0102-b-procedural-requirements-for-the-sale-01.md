The summary sales process has sixteen basic provisions.82 First, the home must be unoccupied for a period of three days following the entry of the eviction judgment. R.C. 1923.12(A).

Second, the park operator may then provide the titled owner of the home with a written notice to remove the home from the park within fourteen days from thedate of the delivery of the notice. Id. The required content of the notice is specified in R.C.

1923.12(B). The notice may be served by personal delivery or by ordinary mail sent to the last known address of the owner. R.C. 1923.12(A).

Third, if the owner does not remove the home during the fourteen-day period, the park operator may request the court to use the summary sales process. R.C.

1923.12(A), 1923.13(B).

Fourth, the park operator must conduct a search of the appropriate public records and make reasonably diligent inquiries to identify any and all persons who have an outstanding right, title, or interest in the home. R.C. 1923.12(C).

Fifth, if the park operator requests the court to use the summary sales process, the request must include: (1) th e park operator’s certification of service of the fourteen - day notice, and (2) based on the park operator’s searches and inquiries, the name and last known address of each person who has an outstanding right, title, or interest in the home (“identified interested person”). R.C. 1923.12(C).

Sixth, if personal property has been abandoned on the residential premises and the park operator has knowledge of any person who has an outstanding right, title, or interest in any of the personal property, the park oper ator must list those items of personal property, and the name and last known address of those persons, on its request for the writ of execution. R.C. 1923.12(C), 1923.13(B), 1923.14(B)(2),(7).

Seventh, the clerk of court may require the park operator to pay an advance deposit sufficient to secure payment of the appraisal of the manufactured home and the advertisement of the sale of the home. R.C. 1923.12(C).

Eighth, upon the park operator’s request, the court shall issue a writ of execution that authorizes the use of the summary sales process, provided the requirements of R.C.

1923.12 have been satisfied. R.C. 1923.13(B).

Tenth, within sixty days after receiving the writ of execution, if the manufactured home is abandoned, the bailiff (or sheriff) shall commence proceedings for the sale of the home in accordance with the procedures for the sale of goods on execution under R.C. Chapter 2329. R.C. 1923.14(B)(3). In addition to all notices required under R.C.

2923.13, the bailiff shall serve the identified interested persons with a written notice of the date, time, and place of the sale. Id. Notwithstanding any statutory provision to the contrary, the titled owner of the home may not secure a stay of execution relative to the sale. Id.

Eleventh, if the manufactured home is sold under the summary sales process, the park operator may deny the purchaser of the home the right to maintain the home in the manufactured home park. R.C. 1923.14(B)(3).

Twelfth, if the manufactured home cannot be sold on two occasions “due to a want of bidders,” the clerk of court shall issue a certificate of title of the home to the park operator, free and clear of all security interests, liens, and encumbrances.Id. The clerk shall issue the certificate of title in the manner prescribed in R.C. 4505.10. Id.

Thirteenth, if the manufactured home is abandoned and has a value of less than three thousand dollars and if there is no identified interested person, after servin g the notices required under R.C. 2923.13, the bailiff may cause the destruction of the home or the clerk of court may issue a certificate of title of the home to the park operator, free and clear of all security interests, liens, and encumbrances. Fourteenth, at any time prior to the issuance of the writ of execution, the titled owner of the manufactured home may remove the home from the park (or other place of storage) upon payment of all outstanding tax liens on the home and, unless the owner is indigent, all unpaid court costs in the eviction action. R.C. 1923.14(B)(5).

Fifteenth, after the issuance of the writ of execution, the titled own er of the manufactured home may remove the home from the park (or other place of storage) at any time up to the day before the scheduled sale, destruction, or transfer of ownership, upon payment of all moving and storage costs, all outstanding tax liens on the home, and, unless the owner is indigent, all unpaid court costs in the eviction action. Id.

Sixteenth, at any time after the issuance of the writ of execution, the holder of any outstanding lien, right, title, or interest in the manufactured home may stop the summary sales process by: (1) commencing a proceeding to repossess the home under R.C. Chapters 1309 and 1317, and (2) then paying all outstanding monthly rent due the park operator, for the period between the issuance of the writ and the sale o f the property under R.C. Chapters 1309 and 1317. R.C. 1923.14(B)(2),(6).
