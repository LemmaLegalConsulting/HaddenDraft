## Source text

An FED complaint must conform with the requir ements of Civil Rule 10(D).

Small v. Williams, No. 44418, 1982 WL 5247 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 25, 1982) (dicta); Point Rental Co. v. Posani, 52 Ohio App.2d 183, 368 N.E.2d 1267 (10th Dist.

1976); Dayton Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Westfall, No. 79-CVG-7932 (Ohio Mun., Dayton,
Oct. 15, 1979).

Therefore, if the tenancy and thus the eviction action is founded on a written lease, a copy of the lease must be attached to the complaint or the reason for the omission must be stated in the complaint. Id. Cf. Cincinnati Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Morgan, 155 Ohio App.3d 189, 194, 800 N.E.2d 64 (1st Dist. 2003), rev’d on other grounds, 104 Ohio St.3d 445, 820 N.E.2d 315 (2004) (“when a landlord such as CMHA seeks to evict a tenant based on the terms of a lease agreement, the landlord should at the very least provide the trial court with a copy of the lease”).

If only part of the lease (instead of the entire lease) is attached to the complaint, this Civil Rule 10(D) requirement is not met. Green v. Smith, No. 2014-CVG-025805 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Nov. 12, 2014).

39 Also, the landlord’s belief that the tenant has a copy of the lease is an insufficient reason for failing to attach it. Point Rental Co. v. Posani , 52 Ohio App.2d 183, 185, 368 N.E.2d 1267 (10th Dist. 1976).

“The proper procedure in attacking the failure of a plaintiff to attach a copy of a written instrument [in this case, a written lease] or to state a valid reason for his failure to attach same is to serve a motion for a definite statement, pursuant to Civ.R. 12(E).”
Id. at 186. Accord Fletcher v. Univ. Hosp. of Cleveland , 120 Ohio St.3d 167, 897 N.E.2d 147 (2008);Trabuco Homes, L.L.C. v. Brewer, No. 109595, 2021 WL 2375962 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 10, 2021); Asset Acquisitions Group, L.L.C. v. Gettis, 186 Ohio App.3d 586, 929 N.E.2d 506 (2nd Dist. 2010);Landskroner v. Landskroner, 154 Ohio App.3d 471, 797 N.E.2d 1002 (8th Dist. 2003).

Nonetheless, some trial courts have dismissed eviction actions for a landlord’s failure to attach a copy of the lease to the complaint. E.g., Davis v. Risten, No. 2010- CVG-951 (Ohio Mun., Canton, Mar. 1, 2010).
