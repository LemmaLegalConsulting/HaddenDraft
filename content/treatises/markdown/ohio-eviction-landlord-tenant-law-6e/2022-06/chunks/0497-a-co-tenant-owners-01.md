# a. Co-Tenant Owners

## Source text

If the premises are owned by two or more tenants in common, under Civil Rule 19(A), all of the owners are necessary parties in an action against a tenant for unpaid
rent. Peters v. Durroh , 28 Ohio App.2d 245, 277 N.E.2d 69 (10th Dist. 1971). Cf.

Oda v. Davis , 81 Ohio App.3 d 555, 611 N.E.2d 933 (2nd Dist. 1992) (managing partner and partnership are co -owners of unpaid rent claim; under Civil Rules 17(A) and 19(A), managing partner may not bring the claim without joinder of the partnership or all other partners).

A failure to join a co-tenant owner (“absent owner”) creates a risk that the absent owner will later assert the absent owner’s own claim against the tenant for unpaid rent.

Peters v. Durroh, 28 Ohio App.2d 245, 277 N.E.2d 69 (10th Dist. 1971). Cf. Carter
v. Davis, No. 87-CVG-1420-29 (Ohio Mun., East Cleveland, Aug. 22, 1987) (right of a co-tenant to demand the rent when another co-tenant fails to demand and collect the rent).
