# 6. Non-Criminal Conduct of Tenant’s Child

## Source text

“It is understandable that there is some level of noise coming from the normal day to day activity of a household with two small children.” New Longwood Assocs. v.

Perry, No. 09-CVG-12567, slip op. at 11 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Apr. 22, 2010). The noise associated with a tenant’s child is not good cause to terminate an assisted housing tenancy if the noise is “the normal, daily noises associated with children and the by-product of living with them and raising them.” Fairborn Apts. v.

Walker, No. 91 -CVG-356 (Ohio Mun., Fairborn, July 24, 1991). Accord New Longwood Assocs. v. Perry, No. 09-CVG-12567 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div.,
Apr. 22, 2010); Vistula Mgmt. Co. v. Scott , No. 92 -CVG-76 (Ohio Mun., Fremont, May 6, 1992).

In some circumstances, the improper conduct of a tenant’s child is not good cause to terminate an assisted housing tenancy if: (1) it is the first instance of such conduct,
(2) the tenant had no knowledge of the child’s conduct, and/or (3) the tenant could not 205 For the full citation for this handbook, see note 143, above.

237 reasonably have foreseen or prevented the child’s conduct.

This standard has been applied when, based on the child’s improper conduct, the landlord alleges a violation of a lease provision under which the tenant agrees not to permit certain conduct. 206 E.g., Mid-Northern Mgmt., Inc. v. Heinzeroth , 234 Ill.

App.3d 240, 599 N.E.2d 568 (1992) (“not to make or permit noises or a cts that will disturb the rights or comfort of neighbors,” subsidized housing lease,207 ¶ 13(e)). Cf.

Investors Diversified Prop. Mgmt., Inc. v. Brown , No. 87 -360-II, 1988 WL 102781
(Tenn. App. Oct. 7, 1988) (applies this standard where an eleven-year-old son violated a lease provision barring violent acts by any household member).

The improper or unlawful conduct of a tenant’s non -household member child is not good cause to terminate an assisted housing tenancy if the conduct occurs when the child is not a guest. E.g., Stark Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Marshall , No. 95 -CVG-0676 (Ohio Mun., Canton, May 15, 1995). See also Hous. Auth. of City of Jersey City v.

Thomas, 318 N.J. Super. 191, 723 A.2d 119 (1999);Charlotte Hous. Auth. v. Fleming, 123 N.C. App. 511, 4 73 S.E.2d 373 (1996); Edwards v. Christian, 61 A.D.2d 1045, 403 N.Y.S.2d 119 (1978). For a discussion on conduct of uninvited guests generally, see Section III.G.10., below.

The improper or unlawful conduct of a tenant’s non-household member child may be good cause to terminate an assisted housing tenancy if the conduct occurs when the child is a guest. Chavez v. Hous. Auth. of City of El Paso , 973 F.2d 1245 (5th Cir.

1992).
