# 3. Contract Claims

## Source text

An R.C. 5321.04 violation may give rise to a tenant’s contract claim against a
landlord. See Section IX.A, above. Similarly, a rental agreement violation may give rise to a ten ant’s contract claim against a landlord. “The breach of a contract for the lease of property may give rise to ordinary contract remedies for the injured party.” U.S.A. Parking Sys., Inc. v. Penn -Lillis Corp., No. 66936, 1995 WL 601186, at *4 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Oct. 12, 1995).

The tenant may assert these contract claims against a PHA because the R.C.

Chapter 2744 sovereign immunity does not apply to contract claims (see Section XXII.I.l., above). E.g., Bozeman v. Cleveland Metro. Hous. Auth., Nos. 92435, 92436, 621 2009 WL 3321402, ¶ 11 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Oct. 15, 2009); Kelly v. Chillicothe
Metro. Hous. Auth., No. 10-00430 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, Apr. 5, 2011).

In addition to its express terms, a residential rental agreement incorporates by operation of law the rights and duties that are established in R.C. Chapter 5321. See Section VII.B, above. Therefore, a tenant may assert a rental agreement contract claim against a PHA for any PHA violation of R.C. Chapter 5321. 391 E.g., Kelly v.

Chillicothe Metro. Hous. Auth., No. 10-00430 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, Apr. 5, 2011) (R.C. 5321.15 contractual liability). This contract claim includes any attorney’s fees provision in the relevant section of R.C. Chapter 5321. Id. See also Lewis v. Romans,
No. 43594, 1982 WL 4713, at *2 n.6 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 24, 1981).

Since punitive damages are not available in contract claims (see Section IX.A.8.e, above), they are not available in these contract claims against a PHA. See Kelly v.

Chillicothe Metro. Hous. Auth., No. 10-00430 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, Apr. 5, 2011).
