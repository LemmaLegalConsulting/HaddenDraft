Whatever statutory, Civil Rule, or local court rule requirement is applicable, it must establish a time period for filing a jury demand that is reasonable so as to comply with the Ohio Constitution. “[T]here may not constitutionally be any impairment or denial of the right of trial by jury [guaranteed by Article I, Section 5 of the Ohio Constitution].” Walters v. Griffit h, 38 Ohio St.2d 132, 133, 311 N.E.2d 14 (1974).

This means that “that right may be subject to moderate and reasonable regulation” only.
Id. Accord Hocking Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Martin, No. 97-CA-9, 1998 WL 100416, at *2 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Mar. 5, 1 998); Cuyahoga River Assocs. Ltd. P’ship v. MJK Corp., No. 68673, 1996 WL 17292, at *6 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 18, 1996); Kelly
v. Abu-Aun, No. 64869, 1994 WL 86215, at *1-2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 17, 1994).

While no court decision directly addresses this issue, the case law provides some guidance on what constitutes a reasonable time period for filing a jury demand, relative to the requirements of Article I, Section 5 of the Ohio Constitution. The decision most directly on point is Savage v. Wrig ht, No. L -75-123, 1975 Ohio App. LEXIS 8370 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov. 21, 1975). It struck down a local court rule that incorporated the R.C. 1923.09 time period for filing a jury demand (i.e., by the return day of the summons). It used an “interest of justice” standard, which is analogous to the “reasonable time” standard of Article I, Section 5 of the Ohio Constitution. Under this standard, it held that the time period for filing a jury demand in an eviction action is up to three days before the trial date. But cf. Hocking Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Martin,
No. 97-CA-9, 1998 WL 100416, at *2 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Mar. 5, 1998) (upheld constitutionality of a local court rule that provided that, if a tenant in an eviction action fails to post a $175 deposit within three days of the service of the summons, the tenant waives the right to a trial by jury).
