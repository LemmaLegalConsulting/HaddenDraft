See generally Sotir v. United States, 978 F.2d 29, 30 (1st Cir. 1992).But see Marks v. Young, No. 2456-M, 1996 WL 63033 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Feb. 14, 1996) (dicta).

The tenant’s determination, on the month(s) to which the rent payment(s) shall apply, may be evidenced by circumstances as well as words. See L & T East 22 Realty
Co. v. Earle, 192 Misc.2d 75, 745 N.Y.S.2d 369 (App. Term 2002). InEarle, the court held that it was clear to the landlord that the tenant intended to apply the payment to the current month, si nce the parties were awaiting a one -time payment by the Department of Social Services for the full arrearage.

If the tenant does not make a determination timely, the landlord may determine the months to which the rent payments shall be applied. Shields, 7 Ohio Law Abs. at 638;
Cent. W. Cas. Co. , 21 Ohio Law Abs. at 356. See also Westgate Vill. Mobile Home Park v. Fetro , No. CVG -950312, 1995 WL 907587 (Ohio Mun., Fostoria, Sept. 27, 1995).

If neither the tenant nor the landlord makes this determination tim ely, the court may determine the month(s) to which the rent payment shall be applied, based on the equities of the case. Cent. W. Cas. Co., 21 Ohio Law Abs. at 356. See generally In re Wines, 997 F.2d 852, 855 (11th Cir. 1993) (“if a creditor fails to des ignate within a reasonable time how payment is to be applied, the court may allocate the payment in an equitable manner”).
