# S. Rent Adjustment – Tenant on Active

## Source text

Duty, R.C. 1923.062 When an eviction action is for nonpayment of rent against a tenant or a manufactured home park resident (or against any member of the tenant’s or resident’s immediate family), and the tenant or resident is deployed on active duty, the tenant or resident (or immediate family member) may have a defense or remedy under R.C.

1923.062.

In such cases, the defense or remedy is available if: (1) “the tenant’s or resident’s ability to pay the agreed rent is materially affected by the deployment on active duty” (R.C. 1923.062(A)) and (2) the landlord or park operator operates five or more residential premises (R.C. 1923.062(C)).

When these conditions are met, upon the tenant’s or resident’s motion, the court is required to take one of two actions. First, it may “[a]djust the obligation under the rental agreement to preserve the interest of all parties to it.” R.C. 1923.062(A)(2).

Second, it may “[s]tay the proceedings for a period of ninety days, unless, in the opinion of the court, justice and equity require a shorter period of time.” R.C.

1923.062(A)(1). When a court selects the stay option, “the court may grant the landlord or park operator such relief as equity may require.” R.C. 1923.062(B).

Absent the tenant’s or resident’s motion, a court still may, on its own motion, grant one of these alternative defenses or remedies. R.C. 1923.062.
