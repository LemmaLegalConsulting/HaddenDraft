# D. Common Law Remedies

## Table-of-contents context

- XI. Tenant Claims for Retaliation,
  - D. Common Law Remedies

## Source text

When a landlord violates R.C. 5321.02(A), the tenant may assert common law claims against the landlord, in addition to the R.C. 5321.02(B) claim. See Section V.C, above.

For example, in these cases, the tenant has a common law claim against the landlord for punitive damages, if the landlord’s conduct is malicious, intentional, and
wanton. Davis v. Reed, Nos. 68699,68700, 1996 WL 347814, at *5 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 20, 1996);Burroughs v. Douglas, No. 46030, 1983 WL 2865, at *3-4 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 8, 1983).

If the tenant has suffered serious emotional distress as a resul t of the retaliation, the tenant may have a common law claim against the landlord for intentional or negligent infliction of serious emotional distress. Burroughs, 1983 WL 2865, at *6.

See generally Yeager v. Local Union 20, Etc., 6 Ohio St.3d 369, 453 N.E.2d 666 (1983) (intentional); Schultz v. Barberton Glass Co., 4 Ohio St.3d 131, 447 N.E.2d 109 (1983) (negligent).
