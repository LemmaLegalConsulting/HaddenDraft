## Source text

Deposits R.C. 5321.01(E) defines a security deposit as “any deposit of money or property to secure performance by the tenant under a rental agreem ent.” See also Small v.

Williams, No. 44418, 1982 WL 5247, at *3 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 25, 1982) (“a security deposit is held by a lessor to insure performance of all the lessee’s duties under the lease”).

“A security deposit, although it is in the possession and under the control of the landlord, is the property of the tenant.” Dovi Interests, Ltd. v. Somerset Point Ltd.

P’ship, No. 82788, 2004 WL 254211, ¶ 11 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 12, 2004). The tenant possesses title to the security depos it. Id. A tenant retains title to the security deposit, subject to the rights and duties established in R.C. 5321.16. In re Ctr. Apts., Ltd., 277 B.R. 747 (Bankr. S.D. Ohio 2001) (applying Ohio law).

Security deposits “are a personal obligation between the landlord and the tenant in the form of a pledge.” Dovi Interests, Ltd. v. Somerset Point Ltd. P’ship, No. 82788, 2004 WL 254211, ¶ 12 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 12, 2004). Accord Castlebrook,
Ltd. v. Dayton Props. Ltd. P’ship , 78 Ohio App.3d 340, 604 N.E.2d 808 (2nd Dist.

1992); Tuteur v. P. & F. Enters., Inc., 21 Ohio App.2d 122, 255 N.E.2d 284 (8th Dist.

1970).
