# 1. Month-to-Month Tenant

- XVII. Landlord Claim for Rent

## Source text

Generally, three factors determine the end date of the time period for which a month-to-month tenant is obligated to pay rent: (1) the date on which the tenancy is terminated; (2) the date on which the tenant delivers possession of the premises to the landlord; and (3) any time period the landlord does not lease the prem ises as a result of the tenant’s violation of the rental agreement.

As to the third factor, the tenant’s obligation to pay rent for that period is subject to the landlord’s duty to mitigate damages. See Section XVII.B, below.

When a month-to-month tenant vacates the premises without giving the landlord a notice of termination, and the landlord has neither terminated the tenancy nor requested the tenant to leave, the tenant generally is obligated to pay rent for the month in which the tenant vacated (“vaca tion month”) and for the month after the vacation month.

Snyder v. Waldron, No. 12-CA-9, 2013 WL 3988775, ¶¶ 25,26 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., July 26, 2013) (citing this book, 3d edition, section XVII.A.1). The tenancy is not terminated until the end of the month after the vacation month because the tenant’s vacation of the premises is, in effect, the tenant’s R.C. 5321.17(B) 30 -day notice of
termination. The tenant’s obligation to pay rent for the month after the vacation month is based on the tenant’s obligation to pay rent during the term of the tenancy.

When a month -to-month tenant gives the landlord a notice of termination that specifies a termination date that is less than 30 days before the periodic rental date, and the tenant vacates the premises pursuant to that notice, the tenant is generally obligated to pay rent for the vacation month and for the month after the vacation month. E.g., Bowman v. Cmty. Mgmt. Corp., 14 Ohio App.3d 31, 469 N.E.2d 1038 (1st Dist. 1984) (notice Sept. 21, vacated Sept. 30 , security deposit awarded for Oct. rent). See also Adams v. Relmax , 111 N.E.3d 758, 2018 -Ohio-1751 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., 2018);

Barran v. Kinas, No. 85085, 2005 WL 991690 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Apr. 28, 2005);

Fenell v. Mayflower Manor Apts., No. 17224, 1995 WL 699919 (Ohio App., 9th Dist.,
Nov. 29, 1995); Wilson v. Thomas , No. L -87-405, 1988 WL 69153 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 30, 1988).

When a month-to-month tenant properly terminates the tenancy and timely vacates the premises, but returns the keys post-vacation, the tenant is generally obligated to pay rent for a post -vacation period: either up to the date the keys are returned or for an entire month post-vacation. McGreevy v. Bassler, No. 09AP-381, 2010 WL 177255 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Jan. 19, 2010) (liability up to the date the keys are returned);

Knipp v. Sadler, No. 609-04, 2009 WL 2710053 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Aug. 31, 2009) (liability for an entire month post-vacation).

When a month-to-month tenant gives the landlord a 30-day notice of termination, and before the expiration of the 30-day period the landlord locks the tenant out of the premises, the tenant is not liable for any rent that accrues subsequent to the lockout.

Gordon v. Morris, No. 2000-CA-69, 2001 WL 85797 (Ohio App., 2nd D ist., Feb. 2, 2001). For a discussion on the tenant’s claim for the lockout, see Chapter XII, above.

When a month-to-month tenant vacates the premises in response to the landlord’s notice to vacate, and the reason for the notice is not a tenant violation of the rental agreement, the tenant is obligated to pay rent only for the period up to the date on which the tenant delivers possession of the premises to the landlord. E.g., VanHoose
v. McGee , No. CA97 -04-008, 1997 WL 598127 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Sept. 29, 1997). The landlord has terminated the tenancy with the service of the notice to vacate.

For the period between the termination of the tenancy and the tenant’s delivery of possession, the tenant is a holdover tenant and thus liable for t he rent. See Section XVII.A.4, below. For the period after the tenant’s delivery of possession, there is neither a tenancy nor any other basis on which to hold the tenant liable for rent.

When a month-to-month tenant vacates the premises in response to the landlord’s notice to vacate, and the reason for the notice is the tenant’s violation of the rental agreement, the tenant is generally obligated to pay rent for the vacation month, but not for the month after the vacation month. E.g., Simoudis v. Diehl, 116 Ohio App.3d 296, 688 N.E.2d 16 (11th Dist. 1996) (notice to vacate for nonpayment of rent; tenant vacates eleventh day of the month); Raymond v. Blanchard Inv. Co., No. 84AP-1000, 1985 WL 9931 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 28, 1985) (notice to vacate f or causing damage to the premises; tenant vacates twenty-seventh day of the month); Hashemi v.

Jim Owen & Co., No. 76AP-879, 1977 Ohio App. LEXIS 8510 (Ohio App., 10th Dist.,
Apr. 21, 1977) (notice to vacate for nonpayment of rent; tenant vacates third dayof the month); Moran v. Foster , No. 97 -CVG-300 (Ohio Mun., Lakewood, Dec. 30, 1997) (notice to vacate for nonpayment of rent; tenant vacates third day of the month).

However, in these circumstances, if the tenant returns the keys for the premises in the month after the vacation month, the tenant is generally obligated to pay rent for the 513 vacation month and for all or part of the month after the vacation month. E.g., Griffin
v. Currie, No. 4065, 1988 WL 126385 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Nov. 25, 1988) (liable for the entire month after the vacation month);Jordan v. McShan, No. 91-CVG-20057 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Apr. 9, 1992) (liable up to the date the keys are returned in the month after the vacation month). See also Peters v. Durroh , 28 Ohio App.2d 245, 277 N.E.2d 69 (10th Dist. 1971).

When a landlord’s notice (under R.C. 1923.04 or R.C. 5321.11) terminates a month-to-month tenancy for a tenant’s violation of the rental agreement, the tenant’s rental obligation post-termination is an element of the damages that the landlord has suffered as a result of the tenant’s violation.
