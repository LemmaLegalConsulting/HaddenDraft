Under the bankruptcy laws, an assisted housing tenant 233 can prevent an eviction for nonpayment of rent and reinstate the lease, provided the tenant otherwise qualifies for bankruptcy relief.

A project-based assisted housing tenant 234 may secure this relief in a chapter 13
bankruptcy. A Housing Choice Voucher program (“HCVP”) tenant also may secure this relief in a chapter 13 bankruptcy, but generally there is little benefit in reinstating a HCVP lease because it does not require good cause for nonrenewal (see Section III.A, above).235 See, e.g., In re Burch , 401 B.R. 153 (Bankr. E.D. Pa. 2008) (due to the landlord’s nonrenewalof the HCVP lease, there did not exist an unexpired lease term for the tenant to assume in the chapter 13 bankruptcy).

In some jurisdictions, a Public Housing tenant may also secure this relief in a chapter 7 bankruptcy.
