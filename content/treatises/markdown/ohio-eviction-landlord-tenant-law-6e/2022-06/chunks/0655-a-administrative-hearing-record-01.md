## Source text

The HCVP participant is obligated to file a praecipe requesting the PHA to prepare and file with the court the requisite transcript of the admin istrative hearing. Woerner
v. Mentor Exempted Vill. Sch. Dist. Bd. of Educ., 84 Ohio App.3d 844, 619 N.E.2d 34 (11th Dist. 1993).

The PHA is then obligated “to prepare and file in the court ... a complete transcript of all the original papers, testimony, and evidence offered, heard, and taken into consideration in issuing the final order.” R.C. 2506.02. The PHA is required to file the administrative record within forty days of the filing of the notice of appeal. Id.

659 “The failure to timely file a transcript pursuant to R.C. 2506.02 does not, on that basis alone, entitle a party to a judgment against the agency, unlike an appeal unde r R.C. 119.12.” Martin v. City of Cleveland Bd. of Bldg. Standards & Bldg. Appeals ,
No. 86039, 2005 WL 3120211, ¶ 10 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Nov. 23, 2005). In order to enforce compliance with R.C. 2506.02, a trial court may invoke its contempt powers.
Id. Alternatively, a trial court may reverse the PHA’s decision based on its failure to present to the court any reason for its late filing. E.g., Crim v. Cuyahoga Metro. Hous.

Auth., No. CV -17-875960 (Ohio C.P., Cuyahoga Cty., Apr. 20, 2017). “[T]he trial court may exercise discretion in determining the appropriate sanction when a party fails to comply with R.C. 2506.02.”Martin v. City of Cleveland Bd. of Bldg. Standards & Bldg. Appeals, No. 86039, 2005 WL 3120211, ¶ 10 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Nov. 23, 2005).
