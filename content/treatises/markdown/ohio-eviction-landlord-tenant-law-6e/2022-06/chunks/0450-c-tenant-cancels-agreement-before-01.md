Occupancy, Security Deposit Rights When a prospective tenant cancels the rental agreement before the tenant has commenced occupancy of the premises, and the reason for the cancellation is the landlord’s failure to make necessary or agreed repairs to the premises, the R.C.

5321.16(B) requirements apply to the tenant’s security deposit. Gladden v. Mitchell,
No. C -040359, 2005 WL 323685 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Feb. 11, 2005); Mayer v.

McGreevy, No. 99AP-707, 2000 WL 329043 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 30, 2000).

See also Riegel v. Huber Inv. Corp., No. 87AP-368, 1987 WL 18205 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Oct. 6, 1987).

When a prospective tenant cancels the rental agreement before the tenant has commenced occupancy of the premises, and the landlord has complied with all of the landlord’s relevant duties, generally the R.C. 5321.16(B) requirements are still applicable to the tenant’s security deposit. Christe v. GMS Mgmt. Co. , 124 Ohio App.3d 84, 705 N.E.2d 691 (9th Dist. 1997); Simone v. Hill’n Dale Homes, No. 89-B- 9, 1989 WL 158232 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., Dec. 29, 1989); Dearwester v. Lagos, 33 Ohio App.3d 199, 514 N.E.2d 1136 (3rd Dist. 1986).

However, in these circumstances, the case law is split on whether the R.C.

5321.16(B) requirements apply to the tenant’s securitydeposit if the prospective tenant never received possession ( e.g., keys) of the premises. Compare Christe v. GMS
Mgmt. Co., 124 Ohio App.3d 84, 705 N.E.2d 691 (9th Dist. 1997) (applicable without possession), and Simone v. Hill’n Dale Homes, No. 89-B-9, 1989 WL 158232 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., Dec. 29, 1989) with Kanistros v. Holeman , No. 20528, 2005 WL 388178 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Feb. 18, 2005) (not applicable without possession),Cox
v. Parish, 66 Ohio App.3d 138, 583 N.E.2d 1039 (8th Dist. 1990),Sand v. Prokos, No.

95-CA-1664, 1996 WL 159687 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Mar. 27, 1996), and Geis v.

Zylka, 70 Ohio Misc.2d 28, 650 N.E.2d 211 (Ohio Mun., Toledo, Hous. Div., 1995).

In Cox, Sand, and Geis, the inapplicability of R.C. 5321.16(B) to the tenant’s security deposit deprived the tenants of the right to recover statutory damages and reasonable attorney’s fees under R.C. 5321.16(C). The courts still awarded the tenants judgments for the amount of their security deposits. In Kanistos, the court construed the deposit as earnest money that the owner was entitled to retain.
