the tenant to engage in these activities.

If a landlord of project -based subsidized housing commences an eviction action against a tenant because the tenant engaged in one (or more) of these activities, the landlord is not allowing the tenant to engage in the activity. Therefore, in such cases, the landlord has violated HUD regulations and the lease.

These violations of the HUD regulations and the lease constitute a retaliation defense to the eviction action. And, unlike the retaliation defense under the First and/or Fourteenth Amendments (see Section III.I.1., above), tenants m ay invoke this retaliation defense irrespective of whether the landlord’s conduct constitutes government action.
