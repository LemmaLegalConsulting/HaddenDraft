Court decisions holding that Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6 do not apply to these monetary claims include: Vargyas v. Brasher , No. L -14-1193, 2015 WL 502854 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Feb. 6, 2015); 37 Harris v. Hampton , No. 2005 -CVG-43533 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Mar. 24, 2006).

37 The Vargyas court relies heavily on the decision in G.K.G. Builders, Inc. v. Burgess , 13 N.E.2d 745, 2014 -Ohio-2431 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., 2014). This reliance on Burgess is misplaced because the complaint in Burgess alleged only an FED claim and the decision in Burgess addressed only the application of the Civil Rules to an FED claim. The reasoning in the Vargyas decision is also flawed by the court’s failure to acknowledge (let alone address) the distinction between the FED claim and the monetary claim, which results in the court holding only implicitly that the monetary claim is an FED claim for Civil Rule 1(C) purposes.
