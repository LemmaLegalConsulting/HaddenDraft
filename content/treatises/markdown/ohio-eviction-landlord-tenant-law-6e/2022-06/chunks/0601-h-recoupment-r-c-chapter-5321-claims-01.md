# H. Recoupment, R.C. Chapter 5321 Claims

## Table-of-contents context

- XXII. Miscellaneous Landlord-Tenant
  - H. Recoupment, R.C. Chapter 5321 Claims

## Source text

The “supreme court has held that a claim that would be barred by the statute of limitations if brought in an action for affirmative relief is nonetheless available as a defense or under a common law theory of recoupment when it arises out of the same transaction as the plaintiff’s claim for relief and is offered to reduce the plaintiff’s right to relief. Riley v. Montgomery (1984), 11 Ohio St.3d 75, 78, 463 N.E.2d 1246.” Johnson v. Brown, No. 2002-CA-76, 2003 WL 1193795, ¶ 27 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist.,
Mar. 14, 2003).

In Johnson, the landlord asserted a claim for tenant damage to the rental property.

The court held that the tenant’s claim for sewer repair costs, incurred as a result of the landlord’s failure to remedy a sewage backup problem, arose from thesame transaction for recoupment purposes.
