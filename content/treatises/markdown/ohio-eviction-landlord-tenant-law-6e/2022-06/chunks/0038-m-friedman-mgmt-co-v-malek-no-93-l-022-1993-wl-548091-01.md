When the month -to-month tenancy is created by a written lease, a tenant’s substantial compliance with the thirty-day requirement may be sufficient to terminate the tenancy. E.g., Claypool v.Dews, No. 18-CA-0925, 2018 WL 6930496, (Ohio App., 7th Dist., Dec. 31, 2018) (written notice delivered two days late). The Claypool decision relied on the line of cases that hold that “‘hypertechnical construction’ of a residential lease provision should not be required where a party has substantially complied with a notice provision.” Id., ¶ 13.

In determining the periodic rental date for R.C. 5321.17(B) purposes, the court should consider any established practice between the parties that allows the ten ant to pay rent late. Smith v. Mallory , No. 52982, 1987 WL 20215 (Ohio App., 8th Dist.,
Nov. 19, 1987).

The parties to a month -to-month tenancy may not enter into or enforce any agreement, oral or written, that shortens the time period for an R.C. 5321.17(B) notice of termination. Detweiter v. Galt, No. 01-CA-34, 2001 WL 1682822, at *3 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Dec. 26, 2001) (invalid oral agreement for thirty -day notice requirement irrespective of periodic rental date); DeBenedictus v. Gialamas, No. 96 -L-006, 1997 WL 51453, at *3 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Jan. 24, 1997) (invalid written agreement for twenty-day notice requirement). Any such agreement would violate the R.C. 5321.06 prohibition against rental agreement provisions that are inconsistent with or prohibited by R.C. Chapter 5321. Id.

However, the R.C. 5321.06 prohibition does not preclude the parties, at any time, from mutually agreeing to terminate a month-to-month tenancy as of a specified date.
