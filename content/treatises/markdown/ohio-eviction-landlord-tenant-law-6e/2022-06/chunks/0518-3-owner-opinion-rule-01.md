# 3. Owner Opinion Rule

## Table-of-contents context

- XVIII. Landlord Claim for Property
  - F. Practice and Procedure Issues
    - 3. Owner Opinion Rule

## Source text

Under the owner opinion rule, a landlord may give opinion testimony on the value of the premises pre-damage and post-damage and on the cost of repairing the premises.

Rowe v. Sylvester, Nos. 65014,65022, 1994 WL 118371 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Apr. 7, 1994); Kearney v. Crown, No. 93-T-4945, 1994 WL 587382 (Ohio App., 11th Dist.,
Aug. 12, 1994). See generally Tokles & Son, Inc. v. Midwestern Indem. Co., 65 Ohio St.3d 621, 605 N.E.2d 936 (1992); Bishop v. East Ohio Gas Co., 143 Ohio St. 541, 56 N.E.2d 164 (1944).

541 Likewise, when a landlord’s personal property has been damaged, the landlord may give opinion testimony on the value of the property pre-damage and post-damage.

See generally Tokles & Son, Inc. v. Midwestern Indem. Co. , 65 Ohio St.3d 621, 605 N.E.2d 936 (1992); Bishop v. East Ohio Gas Co. , 143 Ohio St. 541, 56 N.E.2d 164 (1944).
