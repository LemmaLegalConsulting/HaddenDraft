# K. Public Housing Turnkey III

## Source text

(Homeownership) Some public housing authorities (“PHAs”) administer a homeownership program known as the Turnkey III program. See 24 C.F.R. part 904.

Under the program, a family enters into an agreement (“Homebuyers Ownership Opportunity Agreement”) that provides the family with an opportunity to reside in and eventually purchase a PHA dwelling unit. 24 C.F.R. § 904.103(c)(2). If the family violates its ob ligations under the agreement or no longer meets the homeownership potential requirements, the PHA may terminate the agreement. 24 C.F.R.

§§ 904.107(m),(o).

In an eviction action against such families, a potential issue is whether the agreement is a land installment contract (and thus subject to R.C. 5313.07) or a lease with an option to purchase. If the agreement is a land installment contract, and if the agreement has been in effect for at least five years or the family has paid at least twenty percent of the purchase price, the PHA may not enforce its right of possession (if any) through an eviction action. See Section XXIII.E.2., below. In these circumstances, the PHA may enforce its right of possession (if any), and terminate the family’s interest in the property, only through a foreclosure action. Id.

One Ohio appellate court has held that the agreement is a land installment contract (and thus subject to R.C. 5313.07). Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Watkins, 23 Ohio App.3d 20, 491 N.E.2d 701 (8th Dist. 1984).

The case law from other states is split on this issue. Compare Hous. Auth. of City of Spartanburg v. Elmore , No. 94 -CP-42-832 (S.C.C.P., Spartanburg Cty., Nov. 30,
1994) (follows Watkins) with Philadelphia Hous. Auth. v. Barbour, 592 A.2d 47 (Pa.
Super. 1991) (the agreement is a lease with an option to purchase, not a long-term sales contract). See also Ayers v. Philadelphia Hous. Auth. , 908 F.2d 1184 (3d Cir. 1990) (HUD Turnkey III regulations preempt state foreclosure law notice and opportunity to cure requirements; reserved for the state court the matter of whether Turnkey III homebuyers are ten ants subject to an eviction action or sufficiently similar to mortgagors to require ejectment in the court of common pleas). Cf. Kopanuk v. AVCP Reg’l Hous. Auth., 902 P.2d 813 (Alaska 1995) (barred use of eviction action for breach of homeownership agreement under analogous Indian housing authority program).
