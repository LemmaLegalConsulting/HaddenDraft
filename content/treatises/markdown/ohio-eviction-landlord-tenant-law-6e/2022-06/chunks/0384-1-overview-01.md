The R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se claim does not extend to violations of administrative rules. Chambers v. St. Mary’s Sch., 82 Ohio St.3d 563, 697 N.E.2d 198 (1998). Nonetheless, it still applies to R.C. 5321.04(A)(1) code violations. Sikora v.

Wenzel, 88 Ohio St.3d 493, 727 N.E.2d 1277 (2000).

The R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se claim also may not apply where the type of injury suffered does not fall within the type of injuries that the R.C. 5321.04(A)(1) code provision is intended to avoid. Smith v. Heslop, Inc. , No. 27465, 2015 WL 5043864 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Aug. 26, 2015) (R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se claim did not apply because the window building code provision was intended to protect tenants from fire hazards, not from breaking glass during horseplay).

The elements of the R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se claim and related matters are discussed in Sections IX.B.2 -7, below. The relationship between the R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se claim and a common law negligence claim is discussed in Section IX.C.3, below.
