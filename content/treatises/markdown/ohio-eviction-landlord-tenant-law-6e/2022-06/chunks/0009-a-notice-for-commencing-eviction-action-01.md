## Source text

Proper service of an R.C. 1923.04 notice to vacate the premises(“notice to vacate”) is a condition precedent to the commencement of any eviction action. Eureka Multifamily Group v. Terrell, No. L-14-1152, 2015 WL 2329168, ¶ 11 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., May 15, 2015);Ebbing v. Mathis, No. CA2012-10-201, 2013 WL 2423702, ¶ 11 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., June 3, 2013);Eberly v. Irons, No. 2006AP-01-0004, 2007 WL 2351644, ¶ 36 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Aug. 9, 2007); Classic A Props. v. Brown , No.

02-CA-2868, 2003 WL 22461816, ¶ 24 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Sept. 30, 2003);Colbert
v. McLemore, No. 81961, 2003 WL 21434169, ¶ 6 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 19, 2003); Gvozdanovic v. Woodford Corp. , 139 Ohio App.3d 11, 30, 742 N.E.2d 1145 (1st Dist. 2000); Shimko v. Marks, 91 Ohio App.3d 458, 463, 632 N.E.2d 990 (5th Dist.

1993); Associated Estates Corp. v. Bartell, 24 Ohio App.3d 6, 9, 492 N.E.2d 841 (8th
Dist. 1985); Voyager Vill. Ltd. v. Williams, 3 Ohio App.3d 288, 290, 444 N.E.2d 1337 (2nd Dist.1982); Dayton Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Russell, 16 Ohio Op.3d 94, 95-96 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., 1980);Sternberg v. Washington, 113 Ohio App. 216, 221, 177 N.E.2d 11 525 (9th Dist. 1960).8 This notice requirement is in addition to whatever notice may be necessary to effect a termination of the lease or tenancy. Gvozdanovic v. Woodford Corp. , 139 Ohio App.3d 11, 29, 742 N.E.2d 1145 (1st Dist. 2000) ; Cincinnati Metro. Hous. Auth. v.

McCollum, 45 Ohio App.2d 197, 200, 341 N.E.2d 857 (1st Dist. 1975).
