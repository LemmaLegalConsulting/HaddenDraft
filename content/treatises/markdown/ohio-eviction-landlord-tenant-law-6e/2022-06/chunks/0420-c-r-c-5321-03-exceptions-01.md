# C. R.C. 5321.03 Exceptions

## Table-of-contents context

- XI. Tenant Claims for Retaliation,
  - C. R.C. 5321.03 Exceptions

## Source text

R.C. 5321.03 provides that, in certain specified circumstances, “[n]otwithstanding section 5321.02 of the Revised Code, a landlord may bring an action under Chapter 1923 of the Revised Code for possession of the premises.” Those circumstances are discussed in Section II.T.3, above.

By the plain meaning of the above-quoted provision, R.C. 5321.03 only concerns a landlord’s right to bring an eviction action even though it constitutes retaliation under R.C. 5321.02(A). Therefore, the R.C. 5321.03 exceptions are inapplicable altogether to any rent increase or reduction in service that is retaliation under R.C. 5321.02(A).

As to a retaliatory eviction, while R.C. 5321.03 clearly bars a tenant’s R.C.

5321.02(B) retaliation defense, the case law is split on whether it also bars a tenant’s R.C. 5321.02(B) retaliation claim for actual damages and reasonable attorney’s fees.

Compare Maurer v. Gabriel, No. 17310, 1996 WL 1740 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Jan. 3,
1996) (not a bar to the claim), Harris v. Winston, No. 2004-CVG-28660 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Mar. 16, 2005), and Belvoir Cliffs Apts. v. Bembry , 56 Ohio
Misc. 37, 383 N.E.2d 1170 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, 1978) with Fay Gardens Mobile Home Park v. Newman, 14 Ohio App.3d 144, 470 N.E.2d 164 (12th Dist. 1983) (bars the claim; construing R.C. 373 3.09,.091 (now R.C. 4781.36,.37) – the manufactured home park provisions that prohibit retaliation and are essentially the same as R.C.

5321.02,.03).
