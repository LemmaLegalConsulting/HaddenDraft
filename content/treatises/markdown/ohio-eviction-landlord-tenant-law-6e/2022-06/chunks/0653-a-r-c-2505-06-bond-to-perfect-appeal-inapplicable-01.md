# a. R.C. 2505.06 Bond to Perfect Appeal, Inapplicable

## Source text

In an appeal from a PHA decision that terminates a family’s participation in the HCVP, an issue that may arise is whether the family is required to post a bond, under R.C. 2505.06, to perfect the appeal. The proper resolution of this issue is that the family is not required to post the bond.

An R.C. 2505.06 bond is not required when there is neither a monetary judgment against the appellant nor any appellee interest that needs to be secured by a bond pending the appeal. Cleavinger v. Bd. of County Comm’rs of Hamilton County , 72 406 Inconsistent with this general rule, one appellate court held that the thirty-day period begins to run on the effective date of the termination of the family’s participation in the HCVP .

Downie v. Lake Metro. Hous. Auth. , No. 2014 -L-060, 2015 WL 1000211, ¶ 19 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Mar. 9, 2015). The effective date of termination is often subsequent to the date on which the notice of the hearing decision is issued and mailed. For exa mple, in Downie, the PHA issued and mailed the hearing decision on August 22, 2013, and the effective date of the termination was September 30, 2014.

657 Ohio App.3d 187, 594 N.E.2d 135 (1st Dist. 1991). See also Houghtaling v. City of Medina Bd. of Zoning Appeals, 134 Ohio App.3d 541, 731 N.E.2d 733 (9th Dist. 1999) (bond not required absent monetary damage award); Mahoney v. City of Berea , 33 Ohio App.3d 94, 514 N.E.2d 889 (8th Dist. 1986) (bond not required absentmonetary damage award).

In an appeal from a PHA decision that terminates a family’s participation in the HCVP, the PHA does not have a monetary judgment (or claim) against the family or any interest that needs to be secured by a bond pending the appeal. If the PHA continues to make HCVP payments on behalf of the family pending the appeal, the PHA incurs no damages because: (1) those HCVP payments will be made in accordance with the program’s rent subsidy requirements, and (2) if the termination decision is upheld on appeal, federal law does not require the HCVP landlord or the HCVP tenant to refund those HCVP payments.

The circumstances are analogous to a landlord’s eviction action against a HCVP
tenant. In those actions, the PHA is required to make HCVP payments on behalf of the tenant during the pendency of the eviction action, including any appeal. 24 C.F.R.

§ 982.311(b).
