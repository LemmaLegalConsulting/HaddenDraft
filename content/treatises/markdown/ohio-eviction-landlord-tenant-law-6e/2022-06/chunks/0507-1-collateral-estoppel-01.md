## Source text

Because the doctrine o f collateral estoppel applies to an eviction action (see Section I.M.8, above), if a court finds an R.C. 5321.05 violation when ruling on an eviction claim,353 this finding is binding on the parties when the court later rules on the landlord’s monetary claim under R.C. 5321.05(C). Cf. Great Lakes Mall, Inc. v. Deli Table, Inc., No. 93-L-154, 1994 WL 587559 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Sept. 16, 1994) (nonpayment of rent); Chan v. Birch , No. 15249, 1992 WL 64190 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Mar. 25, 1992) (nonpayment of rent).

353 Under R.C. 5321.11 and 1923.02(A)(8), a tenant’s R.C. 5321.05 violation may be grounds to terminate the tenancy and evict the tenant. See Section I.D, above.
