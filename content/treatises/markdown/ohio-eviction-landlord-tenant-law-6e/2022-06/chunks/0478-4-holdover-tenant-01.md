For example, where a tenant notifi ed the landlord of the tenant’s intent to vacate at the end of the lease term and, approximately seven to ten days after the lease term ended, the landlord gave the tenant a demand notice to vacate, an implied contract did not arise. After the expiration of a fixed term ( e.g., one-year) lease, absent an agreement of the parties on a new lease term, the landlord’s continued acceptance of monthly rent converts the holdover tenancy to a month -to-month tenancy. Adams v. Relmax, 111 N.E.3d 758, 2018 -Ohio-1751, ¶¶ 10,11 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., 2018); McCain v.

Brewer, No. 2014-CA-8, 2015 WL 302829, ¶ 13 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Jan. 23, 2015);

Under this month -to-month tenancy, the tenant “subjects himself or herself to a renewal or continuance of the liability to pay rent as fixed by the terms and conditions of the prior lease.” Fennell v. DeMichiei, No. 106966, 2019 WL 350874, ¶ 19 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 24, 2019).

Also, “generally the [other] terms and conditions o f the original lease govern the holdover lease term.” Capella III, L.L.C. , 190 Ohio App.3d 133, ¶ 18. Accord Wengerd v. Martin, No. 99-CA-0004, 2000 WL 354148 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Apr. 5, 2000); Bill Swad Chevrolet, Inc. v. Ricart Jeep Eagle, Inc., 129 Ohio App.3d 501, 718 N.E.2d 470 (10th Dist. 1998); Craig Wrecking Co. v. Loewendick & Sons, Inc. , 38 Ohio App.3d 79, 526 N.E.2d 321 (10th Dist. 1987).
