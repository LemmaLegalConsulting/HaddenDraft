# 3. Receiver to Manage Premises Due to Landlord’s

## Source text

Noncompliance with Court Order, R.C. 2735.01 When a landlord is unwilling or unable to make repairs or provide services to the premises that a court has ordered, R.C. 2735.01 provides authority for the appointment of a receiver to manage, repair, and provide services to the premises. E.g., Avondale
Cmty. Council v. Lentine, No. C-110221, 2012 WL 171892 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Jan.

20, 2012).

R.C. 2735.01 specifies circumstances where courts of common pleas may appoint a receiver as part of their equitable powers. R.C. 2735.01(A)(4) provides for the appointment of a receiver to carry a judgment into effect. R.C. 2735.01(A)(7) provides for the appointment of a receiver in “all other cases in which receivers have been appointed by the use of equity.” The equity authority of courts has included the authority to appoint a receiver if necessary to secure the full effect of a judgment. 65
Am. Jur. 2d Receivers § 28 (1972).

This receivership authority is in addition to the R.C. 3767.41 receivership authority, which is discussed in Sections IX.G.1,2, above.

The relevant court order to make repairs or provide services may be issued, for example, under an R.C. 5321.15(C) claim (see Section XII.B.3, below) or an R.C.

5321.04 contract claim (see Section IX.A.7, above). When a tenant obtains a court order for such repairs or services and the landlord fails to comply with the order, the tenant may request the court to appoint a receiver to carry out the judgment under R.C.

2735.01(A)(4) and/or 2735.01(A)(7).

When the appointment of a receiver is ancillary to final relief in equity, a receiver may be appointed only if there is no less stringent means for protecting the rights of the parties. Hoiles v. Watkins, 117 Ohio St. 165, 157 N.E. 557 (1927).

Therefore, to justify the request for a receiver under R.C. 2735.01( A)(4) and/or 2735.01(A)(7), the tenant may be required to show that: (1) the tenant obtained an order that requires the landlord to make repairs or provide services to the premises, (2) the tenant attempted to enforce the order through contempt proceedings , (3) despite contempt orders requiring the landlord to make the repairs or provide the services, the landlord continues to fail to comply with the order, and (4) the appointment of a receiver will make a difference,i.e., the repairs or services will be made if a responsible person collects the rent and makes arrangements for the repairs or services.

When a tenant decides to file an R.C. 2735.01 request for a receiver, before filing, a helpful next step for the tenant is to identify a qualified housing man agement company (or individual) which (who) is willing to be appointed as the receiver.
