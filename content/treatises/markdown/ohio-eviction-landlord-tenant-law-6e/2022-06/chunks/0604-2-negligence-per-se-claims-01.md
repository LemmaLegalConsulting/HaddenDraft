# 2. Negligence Per Se Claims

## Source text

An R.C. 5321.04(A) violation may give rise to a tenant’s negligence per se claim against a landlord. See Section IX.B, above. The claim often arises from a defective condition at the rental premises.

Under Moore, tenants may assert these claims against PHAs under the R.C.

2744.02(B)(4) exception. Appellate court decisions on these tenant claims against PHAs are summarized below.

24847, 2010 WL 625822, ¶ 15 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Feb. 24, 2010).

Three courts upheld denials of the PHA’s motion for judgment on the pleadings, with remands for a determination on whether the defective condition was a “physical defect” under R.C. 2744.02(B)(4): Diaz v. Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth., No. 92907, 2010 WL 27868 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 7, 2010) (defective window); Williams v.

Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth., No. 92964, 2009 WL 4857623 (Ohio App., 8th Dist.,
Dec. 17, 2009) (defective pool table); Bozeman v. Cleveland Metro. Hous. Auth., Nos.

92435,92436, 2009 WL 3321402 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Oct. 15, 2009) (lead paint).

One court denied the tenant’s claim against the PHA on the ground that the defective physical condition did not cause the injury. Canidate v. Cuyahoga Metro.
Hous. Auth., No. 101753, 2015 WL 1121444 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 12, 2015) (2- 1 decision). The tenant slipped as a result of water on the floor of the first -floor common area. The water came from an overflowing sink in a third -floor apartment.

The defective condition was a missing ceiling tile in the first-floor common area.

One court held that an alleged lack of adequate security service did not constitute a physical defect under R.C. 2744.02(B)(4). Moncrief v. Bohn, 9 N.E.3d 508, 2014 - Ohio-837, ¶ 15 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., 2014).

One court held that the laundry chute, in which the tenant’s body was found, was not defective and thus not a physical defect under R.C. 2744.02(B)(4). Gibbs v.

Columbus Metro. Hous. Auth. , No. 11AP -711, 2012 WL 1852086 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., May 22, 2012).

One court upheld a denial of summary judgment because a material issue of fact existed on whether the PHA had notice – actual or constructive – of the defective laundry pole. Dickerson v. Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth., Nos. 100650,100943, 2014 WL 5408456 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Oct. 23, 2014).
