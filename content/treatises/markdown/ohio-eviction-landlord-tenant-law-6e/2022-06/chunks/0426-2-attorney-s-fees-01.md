# 2. Attorney’s Fees

## Table-of-contents context

- XII. Tenant Claims for Lockout or
  - B. R.C. 5321.15(C) Remedies
    - 2. Attorney’s Fees

## Source text

When a tenant prevails on an R.C. 5321.15(A) claim, under R.C. 5321.15(C), the tenant is entitled to recover reasonable attorney’s fees.

The attorney fee award is mandatory, if the tenant prevails on the R.C. 5321.15
claim. Risch v. Samuel, No. C-190159, 2020 WL 1488724 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Mar.

25, 2020); Conner v. Conner, 114 N.E.3d 281, 2018-Ohio-2698 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., 2018); Gaitawe v. Mays, No. 25083, 2012 WL 485907 3 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct.

12, 2012); Crenshaw v. Rowland , 196 Ohio App.3d 717, 965 N.E.2d 341 (6th Dist.

2011); Filyo v. Cannon, No. 95-CA-1, 1995 WL 776946 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Dec.

21, 1995); Lewis v. Romans, 70 Ohio App.2d 7, 433 N.E.2d 622 (8th Dist. 1980).

The amount of the attorney fee award is a matter within the discretion of the trial
court. Gaitawe v. Mays, No. 25083, 2012 WL 4859073 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct.

12, 2012); Crenshaw v. Rowland , 196 Ohio App.3d 717, 965 N.E.2d 341 (6th Dist.

2011); Thomas v. Papadelis, 16 Ohio App.3d 359, 476 N.E.2d 726 (9th Dist. 1984);

Lewis v. Romans, 70 Ohio App.2d 7, 433 N.E.2d 622 (8th Dist. 1980).

When an action includes an R.C. 5321.15 claim and one or more other claims, the computation of the R.C. 5321.15(C) attorney’s fees is based only on the time spent on the R.C. 5321.15 claim or on claims interrelated to the R.C. 5321.15 claim. See, e.g., Crawford v. Tivener, No. 15-CA-22, 2016 WL 5638379, ¶ 59 (Ohio App., 5th Dist.,
Sept. 22, 2016). The interrelated claims include a common law trespass claim arising from the unlawful exclusion of the tenant from the premises. Cf. Helfrich v. Mellon,
No. 06-CA-69, 2007 WL 1881537, ¶ 73 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 27, 2007) (R.C.

5321.04(B) claim). The interrel ated claims do not include the landlord’s claim for unpaid rent. Crawford v. Tivener, No. 15-CA-22, 2016 WL 5638379,¶ 59 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Sept. 22, 2016).
