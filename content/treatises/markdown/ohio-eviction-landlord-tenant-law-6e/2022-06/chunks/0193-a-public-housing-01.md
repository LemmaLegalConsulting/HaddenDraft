In Public Housing, th e time period for the notice depends on the grounds for the termination of tenancy.

For nonpayment of rent cases, under HUD rules, there are two alternative time periods.

One is a thirty-day period, which applies during national emergencies, when HUD determines that additional time is required for families to secure available funding.

HUD interim final rule, 86 Fed. Reg. 55693 (Oct. 7, 2021); HUD Notice PIH 2021-29, H 2021-06, issued Oct. 7, 2021.183 On October 7, 2021, HUD made this determination and thereby invoked this thirty-day notice requirement. Id. HUD determined that this thirty-day notice requirement will remain in effect until a subsequent notice is issued rescinding the determination. Id.

Ohio courts have held that an assisted housing184 landlord’s failure to comply with this thirty-day notice requirement is grounds to grant a tenant’s motion to dismiss.E.g., Neilan Park Apts. 2, LLC v. Brown , No. 2020 - CVG-22643 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Oct. 2, 2020).

The other time period fornonpayment of rent cases,which applies when the thirty- day period is not invoked, isa fourteen-day period.185 24 C.F.R. § 966.4(l)(3)(i). For this notice rule, drug-related criminal activity is not per se a threat to the health and safety of other residents or housing authority employees. Hous. Auth. of Jersey City v. Myers, 295 N.J. Super. 544, 549, 685 A.2d 532 (1996). “Rather, some drug - related criminal activity constitutes such a threat to PHA tenants and employees ..., while other drug-related criminal activities are not such a threat.” Id.

For example, in Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Coley , No. 2001 -CVG-19335 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hou s. Div., May 2, 2003, and Jan. 29, 2004), the housing authority provided the tenant with a three -day (instead of thirty-day) notice of termination, based on the tenant’s drug-related criminal activity. The tenant had been charged with a minor misdemeanor for possession of one marijuana cigarette in her rental unit. The court held that this criminal activity did not constitute the requisite threat to health and safety for a time period of less than thirty days.

Lastly, in all other cases, the time period is thirty days. 24 C.F.R. § 966.4(l)(3)(i).
