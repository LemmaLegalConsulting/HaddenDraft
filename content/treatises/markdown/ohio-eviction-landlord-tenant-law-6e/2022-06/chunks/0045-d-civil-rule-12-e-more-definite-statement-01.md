Civil R ule 12(E) establishes the degree of specificity that is required for the allegations in an FED complaint.23 It permits a motion for more definite statement if the allegations are so vague and ambiguous that the tenant cannot reasonably be required to frame a responsive pleading.

Landlords often use form FED complaints that fail to comport with the specificity requirement of Civil Rule 12(E). These form complaints typically allege, with respect to the landlord’s right to possession, only that the tenant is unlawfully and forcibly detaining possession of the premises.
