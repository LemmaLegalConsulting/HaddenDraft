## Source text

When an eviction action concerns a marital residence and is filed before the issuance of a final d ecree of divorce and division of property, municipal courts and county courts are without subject -matter jurisdiction to hear the eviction action.

Yergan v. Yergan , No. 2901 -M, 1999 WL 493942 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., July 14, 1999); Slansky v. Slansky , 33 O hio App.2d 127, 293 N.E.2d 302 (8th Dist. 1973);

Blevins v. Gosa, No. 2003-CVG-4465 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Sept. 22,
2003) (common law marriage); Humphrey v. Heine, No. M9201-CVG-004176 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Mar. 3, 1992) (common law marriage).

However, municipal courts and county courts have subject -matter jurisdiction to hear an eviction action concerning a marital residence when the eviction action is filed after the final decree of divorce and division of property.58 Baker v. Revels, No. 03AP- 410, 2004 WL 453273 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 11, 2004); Eisler v. Rudy , No.

1466, 1989 WL 70066 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., June 23, 1989);Talbott v. Talbott, Nos.

86AP-248,342,370, 1986 WL 13924 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Nov. 13, 1986).

When an issue in a pending divorce proceeding is whether the property is a marital residence, and a third party brings an eviction action against the wife (or husband) to secure possession of the property, the municipal court may stay the eviction action until there is a final order in the divorce proceeding. Amick v. Sickels, No. 2006-CVG- 01277 (Ohio Mun., Athens Cty., Feb. 12, 2007), aff’d on other grounds, 177 Ohio App.3d 337, 894 N.E.2d 733 (4th Dist., 2008). In Amick, the husband and wife lived for six years in a property owned by the husband’s mother, with the expectation that she would give the property to them. When they began a divorce proceeding, the husband’s mother sold the property to a third-party.
