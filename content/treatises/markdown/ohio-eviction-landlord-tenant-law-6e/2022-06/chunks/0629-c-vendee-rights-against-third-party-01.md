Purchaser When a vendor sells th e contracted real property to a third party, the third party purchases the property subject to the rights of the vendee. Oberholtz v. Oberholtz, 79 Ohio App. 540, 74 N.E.2d 574 (9th Dist. 1947); State Fid. Fed. Sav. & Loan Ass’n v.

Wehrby, 25 Ohio Misc. 221, 263 N.E.2d 801 (C.P., Montgomery Cty., 1970).

The general rule in Ohio is that “the purchaser of land in the actual possession of a third person is chargeable with constructive notice of the occupant’s title and equities even though the fact of such possession is not known to the purchaser.” Sinclair Ref.
Co. v. Chaney, 114 Ohio App. 538, 549, 184 N.E.2d 214 (3rd Dist. 1961). See also State Fid. Fed. Sav. & Loan Ass’n v. Wehrby , 25 Ohio Misc. 221, 263 N.E.2d 801 (C.P., Montgomery Cty., 1970).

But one appellate court has held that a vendee in possession under an oral (and thus unrecorded) land contract does not have any enforceable rights “as against a subsequent purchaser who buys the property from the vendor with knowledge of the vendee’s possession but without knowledge of the oral land contract.” Bishop v. Rice,
No. 21247, 2006 WL 574396, ¶ 15 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Mar. 10, 2006).
