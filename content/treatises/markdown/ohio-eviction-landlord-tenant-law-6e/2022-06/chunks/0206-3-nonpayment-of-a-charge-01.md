## Source text

Nonpayment of a late payment fee is not good cause to terminate a project -based subsidized housing tenancy. HUD Handbook 4350.3 REV -1,202 ¶ 6-23(F). E.g., Waterside Estate Apts. v. Keen , No. 94 -CVG-2296 (Ohio Mun., Massillon, Dec. 13, 1994). See also Seldin Co. v. Calabro, 702 N.W.2d 504 (Iowa App. 2005).

Nonpayment of a small charge may be a minor violation of the lease and, for that reason, may fail to constitute good cause to terminate an assisted housing tenancy (see Section III.G.12, below). E.g., Lorain Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Johnson, No. 2021-CVG- 01447 (Ohio Mun., Elyria, Aug. 13, 2021) (tenant without income unable to pay charge for locks installed for security concerns and annual fee ($57.27) for seasonal use of PHA’s air conditioner in a high-rise unit); Dayton Inv. Group-7 v. Lathern, No. 15866, 1996 WL 741822 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Dec. 13, 1996) ($10, $11, and $25 charges);

Jones v. Burns , No. R90 -CVG-2776 (Ohio Mun., Portage Cty., Nov. 29, 1990) ($3 charge); ABC Mgmt. Co. v. Williams , No. M86 -CVG-34323 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Nov. 19, 1986) ($5 and $10 charges). Cf. Sumner v. McKinley, No. M9306-CVG- 17388 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., July 30, 1993) (equity, $20 charge).

Nonpayment of a disputed charge may be good cause to commence an eviction action against an assisted housing tenant, provided certain procedural requirements have been satisfied. One appellate court has held that, before an assisted housing landlord may c ommence an eviction action against a tenant for nonpayment of a 202 For the full citation for this handbook, see note 143, above.

233 disputed charge, the tenant “must be afforded an opportunity to be heard before a neutral adjudicator,” before whom the tenant must be afforded “an opportunity to confront and to cross -examine adverse witnesses.” Gorsuch Homes, Inc. v. Wooten , 73 Ohio App.3d 426, 429, 597 N.E.2d 554 (2nd Dist. 1992). Since the reference to the right to confront and cross -examine adverse witnesses would be unnecessary if the neutral adjudication required a court action (and judgment), it suggests that the neutral adjudication may be an administrative hearing.

However, one municipal court has held that, before an assisted housing landlord may commence an eviction action against a tenant for nonpayment of a dispu ted charge, the landlord must obtain a court judgment that establishes both the existence and the amount of the tenant’s liability. Wilmington Green Apts. v. Estep , No. 88 - CVG-64 (Ohio Mun., Wilmington, Apr. 7, 1988). Cf. Hous. Auth. of City of Atlantic City v. Taylor , 171 N.J. 580, 796 A.2d 193 (2002) (nonpayment of a charge cannot serve as basis for eviction for nonpayment of rent, but may serve as basis for action for monetary judgment). The Estep court further held that, if the landlord obtains a monetary judgment and the tenant fails to satisfy it, the landlord may then commence an eviction action against the tenant for nonpayment of the monetary judgment.
