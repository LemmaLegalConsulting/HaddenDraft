# b. Applicability to Housing Cooperatives

## Source text

An FED action may be used to evict a member of a housing cooperative, notwithstanding the member’s ownership interest.2 Gvozdanovic v. Woodford Corp., 139 Ohio App.3d 11, 742 N.E.2d 1145 (1st Dist. 2000). See also Murphy v. Brooklyn Acres Mut. Homes, Inc., No. 68593, 1996 WL 284856 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 30, 1996); Cent. Park Place v. McDowell, 38 Ohio App.2d 29, 311 N.E.2d 533 (8th Dist.

1974).

“A [cooperative] member is both a landlord by virtue of being an owner of the cooperative and a tenant by virtue of the proprietary lease.” Gvozdanovic, 139 Ohio App.3d at 23. See also Kohler v. Snow Vill., Inc. , 16 Ohio App.3d 350, 352 -53, 475 N.E.2d 1298 (8th Dist. 1984).

However, the member’s right of possession depends entirely uponthe terms of the proprietary lease. Gvozdanovic, 139 Ohio App.3d at 28.

Therefore, if the member breaches the proprietary lease, the cooperative may bring an FED action against the member under R.C. 1923.02(A)(6), which permits an FED action “[i]n any other case of the unlawful and forcible detention of lands or tenements.” Gvozdanovic, 139 Ohio App.3d at 28.
