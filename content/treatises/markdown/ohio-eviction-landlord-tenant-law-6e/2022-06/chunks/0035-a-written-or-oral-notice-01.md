# a. Written or Oral Notice

## Source text

The R.C. 5321.17 notice of termination (“R.C. 5321.17 notice”) may be a written notice or an oral notice. Shewman v. Coons, No. 89-CA-46, 1990 WL 34814 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Mar. 29, 1990). See also Feldmann v. Ponziani, No. 14857, 1995 WL 232476 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Apr. 21, 1995).

If a month-to-month tenancy is created pursuant to a written leas e agreement and the lease requires a written thirty -day notice of termination, the requirement for a written notice is not per se enforceable. See Section VII.K.1, below, for the longstanding line of cases that “concern oral notification of termination of the lease which occurred before the written notification was required. In those cases, the tenant failed to provide written notification.” Claypool v. Dews, No. 18-CA-0925, 2018 WL 6930496, ¶ 19 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., Dec. 31, 2018). The courts hold th at, in these circumstances, to require written notification would be unconscionable under R.C.

5321.14.

Likewise, if a written lease for a fixed term (e.g., one year) requires a written thirty- day notice to prevent commencement of a month -to-month tenancy at the end of the fixed term, the requirement for a written thirty-day notice is not per se unenforceable.

Knipp v. Sadler , No. 6 -09-04, 2009 WL 2710053 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Aug. 31, 2009); Meadowbrook Dev. Corp. v. Roberts , No. 79747, 2001 WL 1654540 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 27, 2001); Ballard v. KMG Investors, Ltd. P’ship, No. 14-93-5, 1993 WL 293944 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Aug. 3, 1993); McGowan v. DM Group IX, 7 Ohio App.3d 349, 455 N.E.2d 1052 (10th Dist. 1982).

However, in either of these circumstances, if the tenant provides the landlord with a timely oral notice of termination but not a written notice, enforcement of the written notice requirement would be both hypertechnical and unconscionable. Id.; Seginak v.

ABC Mgmt. Co., No. 3816, 1987 WL 17258 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Sept. 18, 1987).

R.C. 5321.17 does not specify the permissible methods of service for the R.C.

5321.17 notice. When a landlord elects to serve the tenant with a written R.C. 5321.17 notice, the courts are likely to permit service by any of the methods that R.C. 1923.04 permits for the notice to vacate. See, e.g., DePalmo v. George , No. 88 -CVG-0572 (Ohio Mun., Licking Cty., Aug. 23, 1988) (written R.C. 5321.17 notices are not required to be served personally upon the tenantand may be served by leaving them at the premises).
