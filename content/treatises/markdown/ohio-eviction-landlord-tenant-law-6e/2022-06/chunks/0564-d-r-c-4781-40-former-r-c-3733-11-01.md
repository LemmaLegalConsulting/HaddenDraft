General issues LeMay v. Seckler , No. OT -04-032, 2005 WL 1414449 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 17, 2005) R.C. 3733.11(I) attorney’s fees may be awarded based on the issuance of a declaratory judgment. An award of damages is not a prerequisit e to an award of attorney’s fees under R.C. 3733.11(I).

Sale of home Kantner v. Gibson, No. 2-07-17, 2007 WL 414177 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist.,
Nov. 26, 2007) A “potential tenant’s credit history, rental history, and income are reasonable factors for a landlord to consider when deciding whether a lease should be extended” to such purchasers. Court of appeals upheld the trial court’s finding that the park operator’s application of these criteria was unreasonable for four reasons. First, the park operator failed to inform the resident of its reasons for denying the rental application and thus denied the resident an opportunity to cure. Second, the park operator failed to give the resident a copy of the park guidelines that were allegedly violated.

Third, the resident had remedied many of the violations and was making a good faith effort to comply with the park operator’s requests. Fourth, the park operator initially requested the resident to vacate even before giving the resident a rental application to complete.

Bennington v. Austin Square, Inc., No. 2005-CA-00095, 2005 WL 52245 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Jan. 9, 2006) The enforcement of an upgrade rule, at the time of sale, violated R.C.

3733.11(H)(1),(2). Because it was a selective enforcement of the upgrade rule, it also violated R.C. 3733.11(C).

Euclid Beach Ltd. v. Brockett, No. 75047, 1999 WL 1129086 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 9, 1999) The park operator violated R.C. 3733.11(H) because it “effectively prevented Brockett [resident] from selling her home by refusing to accept prospective buyers, as evidenced by the two real estate contracts that never resulted in the sale of the home.” Anderle v. Ideal Mobile Home Park, Inc. , 101 Ohio App.3d 122, 655 N.E.2d 203 (8th Dist. 1995) The park operator violated R.C. 3733.11(H)(5) in charging a $300 transfer fee to any resident who sold a home to a person wishing to remain in the park.

Santilla v. Sahara Mobile Home Park & Sales , No. 93-L-028, 1993 WL 407233 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Sept. 30, 1993) The park operator did not violate R.C. 3733.11(H)(3) when it refused to enter into a rental agreement with a purchaser of a home. The refusal “was based on concerns over lack of employment and lack of established credit.” Trailer Mart, Inc. v. Semenach, No. 42629, 1981 WL169157 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 26, 1981) When a park operator violates R.C. 3733.11(H)(3), the resident may recover actual damages under R.C. 3733.11(C) and the resident may recover damages (including nominal damages) under R.C. 3733.14.

White v. Leichtman, No. 838, 1980 Ohio App. LEXIS 13362 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Mar. 31, 1980) The court upheld a park rule that required new owners (and not current
owners) to install a cement pad under their home. A current owner challenged the park rul e under R.C. 3733.11(H)(1),(2),(3). She claimed that she had lost a prospective sale of her home because of the park rule.

The court held that R.C. 3733.11(H)(3) did not support the claim because it confers rights on the purchaser, not the seller. The c ourt held that R.C.

3733.11(H)(1),(2) are irrelevant to the claim.

Jameson v. Sommer’s Mobile Home Sales, Inc. , No. 83769 (Ohio C.P., Lorain Cty., May 13, 1980) R.C. 3733.11(F),(K) prohibit a park operator from requiring the purchaser of a home to move the home from the park, if the requirement is imposed 579 because the park operator was not the agent for the sale.
Cmty. Gardens Park & Sales, Inc. v. Roe, No. M8109-CVG-027176 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Nov. 6, 1981) “O.R.C. 3733.11(H) prohibits a park operatorfrom denying [a] sale of the trailor on the site and requires a one year lease to the new occupant if reasonable.” Mentor Trailer Park, Inc. v. Clark, No. 79-CVG-492 (Ohio Mun., Mentor, June 7, 1979) A park operator may not require all purchasers of homes within the park to move, without regard to the age and condition of the home. However, R.C. 3733.11(H) does not prohibit the eviction of an owner for noncompliance with park rules merely because, after a writ of restitution had been granted, the owner sought to sell the home.

Age of home LeMay v. Seckler , No. OT -04-032, 2005 WL 1414449 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 17, 2005) A park rule that requires pre -1977 mobile homes to be moved from the park upon sale is unreasonable, arbitrary, or capricious in violation of R.C.

3733.11(C).

Czerwonko v. Sahara Mobile Home Park & Sales, Inc., 137 Ohio App.3d 266, 738 N.E.2d 461 (11th Dist. 2000) The park rule concerned homes that were twenty -one years old or older.

It required the removal of such homes from the park if the owner moved out of the home or sold the home. The park rule violated R.C. 3733.11(C) because it was unreasonable. The park rule violated R.C. 3733.11(H)(3) because it was an unreasonable refusal to enter into a rental agreement with the purchaser of the home.

Freyermuth v. Navarre Vill. Homes Austin Square, Inc. , No. 1999 -CA- 00187 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Jan. 24, 2000) The park rule concerned homes that were over twelve years old. It did not permit such homes to remain in the park without the wri tten consent of
management. The park rule violated R.C. 3733.11(C) because it was unreasonably enforced. The park rule violated R.C. 3733.11(A)(2) because it deprived an owner of the right to renew a tenancy. In this case, the park rule also violated R.C. 3733.11(H)(1) and 3733.11(H)(2) because it denied the owner the right to sell the home and required removal of the home on the basis of the sale.

White v. Superior Mobile Homes, Inc., No. 98-T-0011, 1999 WL 1320188 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Dec. 10, 1999) The park rule concerned homes that were over twenty years old at the time of a change in ownership. Under the rule, the park operator would not enter into a rental agreement with any new owner of such homes. The park rule violated R.C. 3733.11(C) because it was unreasonable. The park rule violated R.C. 3733.11(H)(3) because it was an unreasonable refusal to enter into a rental agreement with the purchaser of the home.

Disclosure of fee/rent May v. Petrick, No. 2009-CA-00031, 2010 WL 1452604 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Apr. 8, 2010) Court of appeals upheld trial court’s finding that the park operator failed to offer the resident a written lease and, therefore, failed to provide the resident with written disclosure of a late fee and moving fee. Therefore, R.C. 3733.11(B) and 3733.11(H)(4) barred a damage award for the fees.

Simco Mgmt. Corp. v. Snyder , No. 98 -CA-210, 2000 WL 309396 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., Mar. 20, 2000) R.C. 3733.11(B) does not protect a resident from liability or eviction for nonpayment of a rental fee, if the rental fee was disclosed orally to the resident and the resident agreed to pay the disclosed amount. The R.C.

3733.11(H)(4) requirement for written disclosure of the rental fee was met by a rental slip stating the amount of the rent, ev en though the resident filled in the amount.
