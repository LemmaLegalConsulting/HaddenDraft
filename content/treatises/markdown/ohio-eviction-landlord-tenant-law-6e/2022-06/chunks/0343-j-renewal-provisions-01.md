# J. Renewal Provisions

## Source text

“[T]he law of Ohio, as a ge neral rule, does not mandate a landlord to renew or extend a lease for a definite period of time unless provided for within the lease agreement.” Mesnick v. De Ville Apts. , No. 71517, 1997 WL 599376, at *1 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Sept. 25, 1997).

A provision for renewal of a lease, upon certain acts or omissions of the landlord or tenant, is enforceable. Frank v. Flynn , 120 Ohio App. 361, 197 N.E.2d 657 (8th
Dist. 1964).289 For example, a lease may include an option to renew that requires a party to give a specified notice in order to exercise the option.In re Kindinger, 219 B.R. 214 (Bankr.

N.D. Ohio 1998) (applying Ohio law).

Conversely, a lease may provide that its term shall automatically renew to a specified renewal term unless, by a specified number of days before the end of the term, the tenant provides the landlord with written notice of nonrenewal. The automatic renewal (and notice of nonrenewal) provision may be used with any type or combination of initial term and renewal term.

It may be a lease with an initial term of one year that renews for a one -year term.

E.g., Hackman v. Szczygiel, No. 06AP-187, 2006 WL 3199278 (Ohio App., 10th Dist.,
Nov. 7, 2006); Angelo v. Parker, No. 13140, 1987 WL 25776 (Ohio App., 9th Dist.,
Nov. 25, 1987). But see Meadowbrook Dev. Corp. v. Roberts , No. 79747, 2001 WL 1654540, at *4 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 27, 2001) (the 60 -day notice requirement for nonrenewal was unreasonable under the facts of this case).

It may be a lease for a fixed-term that renews to a month-to-month tenancy. E.g., Adair v. Landis Props., Inc., No. 08AP-139, 2008 WL 4174130 (Ohio App., 10th Dist.,
Sept. 11, 2008) (initial three-month term); Cummings v. Getz, No. CA84-09-105, 1985 WL 8164 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Feb. 11, 1985) (initial one -year term); McGowan v.

DM Group IX , 7 Ohio App.3d 349, 455 N.E.2d 1052 (10th Dist. 1982) (initial six - month term).

It may be a lease that is a written month-to-month tenancy. E.g., Knipp v. Sadler,
No. 6-09-04, 2009 WL 2710053 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Aug.31, 2009); Seginak v. ABC
Mgmt. Co., No. 3816, 1987 WL 17258 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Sept. 18, 1987).

However, in these cases, if the tenant provides the landlord with timely oral notice, but not written notice, the courts frequently apply principles of substantial compliance, unconscionability, and/or equity to relieve the tenant from the renewal of the tenancy.

“Where the provision for an extention or renewal [of a lease] does not expressly require notice, Ohio law holds the mere holdin g over under the lease constitutes an election to exercise the option which binds both parties for the term designated in the option.” In re Kindinger, 219 B.R. 214, 217 (Bankr. N.D. Ohio 1998) (applying Ohio law).

When two or more tenants are parties to a lease agreement that gives the tenants an option to renew, the option may not be exercised by only one of the tenants. L & H Realty Co. v. Oberholtz, 65 Ohio App.2d 279, 418 N.E.2d 401 (9th Dist. 1979).

When the renewal provision concerns a month -to-month tenancy, it must be consistent with the requirements of R.C. 5321.17(B). R.C. 5321.06 and 5321.13(A).

However, one court has held that a sixty -day notice of nonrenewal requirement does not conflict with R.C. 5321.17(B). Kenko Corp. v. Singer, No. 81-03-0023, 1982 WL 3141 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., May 26, 1982).

The renewal option may establish a higher rent under the renewal. Chorpenning
v. Carmack , No. 23933, 2008 WL 726553 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Mar. 19, 2008);

Bohlmann v. Cox, No. 17166, 1995 WL 641128 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Nov. 1, 1995).

In Carmack, a one-year lease had a monthly rent of $1,275 and, upon the expiration of the one-year term, a renewal provision established a month -to-month tenancy with a monthly rent of $2000.

However, where the lease does not contain t he renewal option with the increased rent and, instead, it is contained in a separate notice, the notice is only an offer to rent at the increased rent amount. Bohlmann v. Cox, No. 17166, 1995 WL 641128 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Nov. 1, 1995). In these circ umstances, the tenant’s tender of a lesser amount is a counteroffer. Id. The landlord’s acceptance of the lesser amount is an acceptance of the counteroffer. Id.

Also, an automatic renewal provision may expressly provide that the renewal will not occur if the landlord timely notifies the tenant of a change in the monthly rent (or other terms of the lease). E.g., Ganios v. Ferrick , No. 2005 -CA-00239, 2006 WL 751508 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Mar. 20, 2006). In Garios, the lease contained this nonrenewal fea ture, in a year -to-year automatic renewal provision. Prior to the expiration of the second one -year term, the landlord notified the tenant of a rent
increase. The Court held that this prevented the automatic renewal of the lease and, therefore, the tenancy became a month-to-month tenancy.
