# c. Payment by Bank Deposit

## Source text

“It has long been settled in this state that when a rental agreement provides that the tenant is to pay rent by depositing the payments into the landlord’s bank account, the tenant’s depositing of the rent into the landlord’s bank account constitutes acceptance of the rent by the landlord unless the landlord has given the bank contrary instructions.” Ebbing v. Mathis, No. CA2013-01-014, 4806940, ¶ 22 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Sept. 9, 2013). Accord Kachelmacher v. Laird, 92 Ohio St. 324, 110 N.E. 933 (1915); Urban P’ship Bank v. Mosezit Acad., Inc., No. 100712, 2014 WL 4244346 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug. 28, 2014); N. Face Props., Inc. v. Lin , No. CA2010 -09-083, 2013 WL 2423802 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., June 3, 2013);Associated Estates Corp. v. Bartell, 24 Ohio App.3d 6, 492 N.E.2d 841 (8th Dist. 1985); Rogers v. Jones , No. 2014 -CVG- 353 028113 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Oct. 30, 2014); Woodson v. Howard , No. 2008 - CVG-9709 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., June 30, 2008) (electronic deposit);

McConnelsville Assocs. v. Gladden, No. 92-CVG-56 (Ohio County Ct., Morgan Cty.,
Sept. 28, 1992).
