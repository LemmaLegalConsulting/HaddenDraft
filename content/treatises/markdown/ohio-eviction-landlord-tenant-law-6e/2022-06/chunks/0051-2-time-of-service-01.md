# 2. Time of Service

## Source text

In an eviction action, the summons and complaint must be served at least seven days before the trial date for the eviction claim. R.C. 1923.06(A).29 Under R.C. 1.14, this seven-day time period is computed by excluding the first day and including the last day, and excluding any Sunday or holiday that would otherwise be the last day.

To ensure that there is adequate time to effect timely service of process, some courts have a policy of scheduling the trial date for the eviction claim on a specified number of days (often between 14 and 21 days) from the date on which the complaint is filed.

27 Civil Rule 1(C) establishes this standard for determining whether Civil Rule 4(B) applies to eviction actions. See Section I.B, above.

28 These R.C. 1923.06 service of process rules became effective March 30, 1999, and September 29, 1999. They supplanted the R.C. 1923.06 provision that had required service to be made “as in the Rules of Civil Procedure.” See note 6, above.
