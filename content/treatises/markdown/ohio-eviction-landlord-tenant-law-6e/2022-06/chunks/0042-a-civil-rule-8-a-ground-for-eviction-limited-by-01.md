Complaint The ground for the eviction action that the landlord alleges in the complaint is the only ground on which the landlord may rely. M.L.R. Props., Inc. v. Baer , No. L-85- 330, 1986 WL 6703, at *3 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 13, 1986);Boardwalk Glenville
Apts. v. Cage , No. 2007 -CVG-3065 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., July 13, 2007); Jackson v. Hammond , No. 2006 -CVG-31171 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous.

Div., Feb. 15, 2007); Cmty. Gardens Park & Sales, Inc. v. Roe , No. M8109 -CVG- 027176 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Nov. 6, 1981); Inner City Hous. v. Sebastian, No.

81-CV-15029 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., June 29, 1981).

Consequently, if a notice to vacate or a notice of termination states more than one reason for the eviction and the FED complaint alleges some but not all of those reasons, the landlord may rely on only those reasons alleged in the complaint. M.L.R. Props.,
Inc. v. Baer , No. L -85-330, 1986 WL 6703, at *1,3 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 13,
1986) (notice to vacate);Inner City Hous. v. Sebastian, No. 81-CV-15029 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., June 29, 1981) (notice of termination).
