# H. Attorney’s Fees for Time Spent

## Source text

Litigating an Appeal In an R.C. 5321 claim for attorney’s fees, if a party is awarded fees and then successfully defends an appeal of the judgment, the party is entitled to recover reasonable attorney’s fees for the time spent on the appeal (“appellate attorney’s fees”).

Klein v. Moutz, 118 Ohio St.3d 256, 888 N.E.2d 404 (2008);Cristal v. DRC Int’l, Inc., 74 Ohio App.3d 493, 599 N.E.2d 706 (8th Dist. 1991); Breault v. Williamsburg Estates, No. L-86-116, 1986 WL 13169 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov. 21, 1986). See also Christe v. GMS Mgmt. Co. , 88 Ohio St.3d 376, 379, 726 N.E.2d 497 (2000) (Lundberg Stratton, J., concurring in judgment only). Cf. Parker v. I & F Insulation Co., No. C -980502, 1999 WL 420066 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., June 25, 1999) (R.C.

1345.09(F) claim, relies in part on Breault). But see Zeallear v. F & W Props. , No.

99AP-1215, 2000 WL 1015345 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., July 25, 2000) (declines to award appellate attorney’s fees because the appeal is not frivolous under Appellate Rule 23); Nolan v. Sutton , 97 Ohio App.3d 616, 647 N.E.2d 218 (1st Dist. 1994) (same).

Either the appellate court or the trial court may determine and award the appellate attorney’s fees. Klein v. Moutz, 118 Ohio St.3d 256, 888 N.E.2d 404 (2008).

The appellate court may determine and award the appellate attorney’s fees under Appellate Rule 24. Kreps v. Pesina, No. L-95-377, 1996 WL 430846 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Aug. 2, 1996); Breault v. Williamsburg Estates, No. L-86-116, 1986 WL 13169 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov. 21, 1986). See also Christe v. GMS Mgmt. Co. , 88 Ohio St.3d 376, 379, 726 N.E.2d 497 (2000) (Lundberg Stratton, J., concurring in judgment only).

The trial court may determine and award the appellate attorney’s fees on a remand to determine and award costs. Alcorso v. Correll , No. 110218, 2021 WL 4317396, (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Sept. 23, 2021);Cristal v. DRC Int’l, Inc., 74 Ohio App.3d 493, 557 599 N.E.2d 706 (8th Dist. 1991);Breault v. Williamsburg Estates, No. L-86-116, 1986 WL 13169 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov. 21, 1986). A party may request the appellate attorney’s fees in the trial court via a motion for supplemental attorney’s fees. E.g., Breault, 1986 WL 13169, at *3-4.

Whether a remand of the fee issue is cost effective and promotes judicial efficiency may depend on whether, aside from the fee issue, the appellate court is remanding the case for further proceedings. Klein v. Moutz , 118 Ohio St.3d 256, 888 N.E.2d 404, ¶ 17 (2008).

Another reason for appellate courts to remand the fee issue is: “‘The trial court is in a better position to determine a fee award, for it may hold a hearing, take testimony, create a record, and otherwise evaluate the numerous factors associated with calculating an attorney fee award.’” Klein v. Moutz, No. 23473, 2008 WL 2745960, ¶ 6 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., July 16, 2008) (quotingKlein v. Moutz, 118 Ohio St.3d 256, 888 N.E.2d 404, ¶ 13 (2008)).
