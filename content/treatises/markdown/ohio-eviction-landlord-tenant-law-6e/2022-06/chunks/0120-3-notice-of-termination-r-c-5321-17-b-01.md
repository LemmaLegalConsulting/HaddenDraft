# 3. Notice of Termination, R.C. 5321.17(B)

## Source text

When service of an R.C. 5321.17(B) notice of termination isrequired to terminate a tenancy, proper service of a proper notice is an element of the landlord’s case in chief.

For a discussion on when this notice is required to terminate a tenancy, and on the content and service requirements for this notice, see Section I.D, above.

In such cases, if a landlord does not properly serve a proper R.C. 5321.17(B) notice, it is a complete defense to the eviction action.E.g., Mockensturm v. Razeq, No.

L-85-149, 1986 WL 1692 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Feb. 7, 1986).

Also, the trial court lacks subject -matter jurisdiction over the eviction action because, absent compliance with the R.C. 5321.17(B) notice requirements, the tenancy has not been terminated (see Section I.D, above) and, consequently, the R.C. 1923.04 notice to vaca te has been served before the termination of the tenancy and thus prematurely (see Section I.C.3.e, above).
