# 3. Court of Common Pleas

## Source text

Courts of common pleas are courts of general jurisdiction. State ex rel. Shoop v.

Mitrovich, 4 Ohio St.3d 220, 448 N.E.2d 800 (1983). This means that they have jurisdiction in all actions except those excluded sp ecifically by statute. State ex rel.

Mastracci v. Rose , 79 Ohio App. 556, 72 N.E.2d 582 (10th Dist. 1947); Fellers v.

Belau, 87 Ohio Law Abs. 54, 178 N.E.2d 530 (Ohio C.P., Paulding Cty., 1961).

With one exception, and subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of housing divisions 563 (see Section XX.E, below),the landlord-tenant claims that are discussed in Chapters I- XIX, above, fall within the subject-matter jurisdiction of courts of common pleas. The one exception is an R.C. 5321.07(B) claim, which must be f iled in the applicable municipal or county court. See Chapter VIII, above.

Generally, courts of common pleas have subject -matter jurisdiction in eviction actions, and it is concurrent with the jurisdiction of the applicable municipal or county
court. Seventh Urban, Inc. v. Univ. Circle Prop. Dev., Inc., 67 Ohio St.2d 19, 24, 423 N.E.2d 1070 (1981). See R.C. 1923.01(A), 2305.01.

However, a court of common pleas does not have subject-matter jurisdiction in any eviction action that falls within the jurisd iction of a housing division. 364 Maduka v.

Parries, 14 Ohio App.3d 191, 192 n.3, 470 N.E.2d 464 (8th Dist. 1984). A housing division has exclusive jurisdiction, within the territory of the municipal court, in eviction actions. R.C. 1901.181. See also Maduka, 14 Ohio App.3d at 192 n.3.
