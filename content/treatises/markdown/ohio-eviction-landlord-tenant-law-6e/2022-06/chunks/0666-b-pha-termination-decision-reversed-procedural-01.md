McAlister v. Akron Metro. Hous. Auth. , No. CV -2007-08-5813 (Ohio C.P., Summit Cty., Apr. 14, 2008) PHA termination for illegal drug activity, based on participant’s guilty plea to charges of cocaine, marijuana and obstructing justice. Participant contends she pled guilty in order to enter a felony drug court program. Court held the guilty plea is not dispositive of the issue, and participant is “entitled to present evidence of the circumstances surrounding the guilty plea and then the Hearing Officer can determine as to whether there is a preponderance of the evidence that the household member engaged in the activity.” Court remanded the case for these purposes.

Skipper v. Akron Metro. Hous. Auth., No. CV-2007-07-5114 (Ohio C.P., Summit Cty.,
Dec. 27, 2007) The ground for termination in the PHA notice was participant’s failure to appear (and furnish requested information) at 2007 annual re-exam. The ground for termination in the PHA decision was the fai lure to appear at the 2007 annual re -examination “is a repeat pattern from the 2006 reexamination appointment.” Court held that, in relying on the failure to attend 2006 re -exam and “the repeat pattern,” the PHA decision was based on grounds not stated in the PHA notice and, therefore, violated due process.
