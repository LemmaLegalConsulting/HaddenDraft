## Source text

When the water is supplied through a mastermeter that is subject to PUCO regulation, Ohio Admin. Code § 4901:1-18-08(H) (“the PUCO rule”) may require the landlord to pay for the water service, irrespective of whether R.C. 5321.04(A)(6) requires it. The PUCO rule requires the landlord to be designated as the customer on all applications for utility service to residential mastermetered premises.

273 In 2001, the city of Cleveland enacted an ordinance that supersedes the Bess decision.

Cleveland, OH, Code pt. 3C, tit. IX, ch. 375, § 375.05. The ordinan ce requires a residential landlord to pay for the water, electric, and gas services unless certain conditions are met.

It reasonably follows that, in requiring the landlord to be the customer, the PUCO rule requires the landlord to pay for the utility service. Therefore, where the PUCO Rule applies, if the landlord requires the tenant to pay for the water service, the requirement would violate the PUCO Rule and thus R.C. 5321.06. R.C. 5321.06 prohibits any lease provision that is inconsistent with or prohibited by R.C. Chapter 5321 or any other rule of law (e.g., the PUCO Rule).
