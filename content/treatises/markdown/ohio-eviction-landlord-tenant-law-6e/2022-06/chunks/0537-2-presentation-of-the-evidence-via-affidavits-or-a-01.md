# 2. Presentation of the Evidence Via Affidavits or a

## Source text

Hearing In an R.C. Chapter 5321 claim for attorney’s fees, evidence in support of (or in opposition to) the fee request may initially be presented to the court through affidavits.

Manninen v. Alvarez, No. CA2016-01-017, 2016 WL 4426451, ¶ 9 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Aug. 22, 2016); Manninen v. Alvarez, No. CA2013-CA-106, 2014 WL 107852, ¶ 19 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Jan. 13, 2014);Oakwood Mgmt. Co. v. Young, No. 92AP- 207, 1992 WL 324338, at *4 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Oct. 27, 1992); Breault v.

Williamsburg Estates, No. L -86-116, 1986 WL 13169, at *2 (Ohio App., 6th Dist.,
Nov. 21, 1986); Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Watkins, 23 Ohio App.3d 20, 25, 491 N.E.2d 701 (8th Dist. 1984);Drake v. Menczer, 67 Ohio App.2d 122, 126, 425 N.E.2d 961 (8th Dist. 1980) (Krenzler, J., concurring). Cf. Westbourne Dev. Co. v. Sigma Sys.

Health Mgmt. Specialists, Inc., No. 91AP-856, 1992 WL 11197, at *3 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Jan. 16, 1992) (attorney’s fees under commercial lease; “if the parties to an action agree that attorney’s fees should be determined via a non -oral hearing based upon affidavits, the trial court could proceed in that fashion”).

However, if there is conflicting evidence or the non -moving par ty otherwise challenges the fee request, the court must hold an evidentiary hearing to resolve the
issue. Meacham v. Miller , 79 Ohio App.3d 35, 606 N.E.2d 996 (4th Dist. 1992);

Oakwood Mgmt. Co. v. Young, No. 92AP207, 1992 WL 324338 (Ohio App., 10th Dist.,
Oct. 27, 1992); Mazer v. Fish, No. C-890345, 1990 WL 88752 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., June 27, 1990). See also Davis v. Reed , Nos. 68699,68700, 1996 WL 347814 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 20, 1996). Cf. Westbourne Dev. Co. v. Sigma Sys. Health Mgmt.

Specialists, Inc., No. 91AP-856, 1992 WL 11197 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Jan. 16, 1992) (commercial lease).

The moving party’s lawyer has a right to testify to the court with respect to the amount of attorney services provided. Nobis v. E.A. Zicka Co., 31 Ohio App.3d 104, 508 N.E.2d 980 (1st Dist. 1986). This right becomes a requirement if a timely objection is made to the attorney’s affidavit of attorney hours, since the affidavit evidence is hearsay. See Fay Gardens Mobile Home Park v. Newman, 14 Ohio App.3d 144, 470 N.E.2d 164 (12th Dist. 1983).

The non -moving party has a right to confront and cross -examine the moving party’s attorney with respect to the amount of attorney services provided and to the other factors relevant to the reasonableness of the fee request. Meacham v. Miller, 79 Ohio App.3d 35, 606 N.E.2d 996 (4th Dist. 1992);Mazer v. Fish, No. C-890345, 1990 WL 88752 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., June 27, 1990). See, e.g., Hoerner v. GMS Mgmt.

Co., No. 46736, 1983 WL 2892 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 15, 1983).

Evidence of the reasonableness of the attorney’s hours and hourly rate is usually presented through the testimony of an attorney who is familiar with the type of case involved and the hourly rate customarily charged in the locality for similar legal
services. See, e.g., Hall v. Lacheta, No. 92AP-020013, 1992 WL 362546 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Nov. 18, 1992); Budac v. Nahmi, No. 14901, 1991 WL 115908 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., June 19, 1991); Schuab v. Roper , No. 90 -CVG-26859 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Nov. 6, 1991).
