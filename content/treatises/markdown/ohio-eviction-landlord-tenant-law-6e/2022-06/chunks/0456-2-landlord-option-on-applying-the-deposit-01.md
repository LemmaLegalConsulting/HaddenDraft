# 2. Landlord Option on Applying the Deposit

## Table-of-contents context

- XVI. Security Deposit
  - G. Use/Return of the Security Deposit,
    - 2. Landlord Option on Applying the Deposit

## Source text

R.C. 5321.16(B) provides a landlord with the right to apply the security deposit (in whole or in part) to the payment of past due rent and to the payment of damages caused by the tenant’s violation of R.C. 5321.05 or the rental agreement. See Vardeman v.

Llewellyn, 17 Ohio St.3d 24, 476 N.E.2d 1038 (1985).

The election to exercise this right is a discretionary decision of the landlord. R.C.

5321.16(B) permits a landlord, but does not require a landlord, to apply the tenant’s security deposit to the tenant’s rent or damage liability. Papadakes v. Woodall, No.

47848, 1984 WL 195552 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Nov. 29, 1984);Small v. Williams, No.

44418, 1982 WL 5247 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 25, 1982).

For example, even though the amount of a tenant’s security deposit is larger than the amount of the tenant’s unpaid rent, the landlord may bring an action for a money judgment for the unpaid rent.329 Papadakes, 1984 WL 195552, at *1-2. In such cases, the burden is on the tenant to assert the security deposit as a set -off against the rent
claim. See Salem v. Roberts, No. 94-CA-28, 1996 WL 131160 (Ohio App., 4th Dist.,
Mar. 18, 1996).

However, in these circumstances, t he landlord is still required under R.C.

5321.16(B) to deliver to the tenant the security deposit (less any permissible
deductions) and the requisite written itemization (if applicable) within thirty days after the termination of the rental agreement and delivery of possession. See Campus Vill.

Toledo Univ. Park, LLC v. Mowrer, 68 N.E.3d 219, 2016-Ohio-4754 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., 2016); Papadakes, 1984 WL 195552, at *2.
