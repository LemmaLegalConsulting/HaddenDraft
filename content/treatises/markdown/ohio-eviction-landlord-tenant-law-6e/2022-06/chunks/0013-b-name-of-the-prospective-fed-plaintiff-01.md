# b. Name of the Prospective FED Plaintiff

## Source text

Under R.C. 1923.04(A), t he notice to vacate must be signed by or otherwise contain the name of the party who will be the plaintiff in the complaint, if an eviction action is thereafter commenced. Riley v. Parker, No. 109600, 2021 WL 2012263 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 20, 2021); UMH OH Buckeye II, L.L.C. v. DeCarlo, No. 108912, 2019 WL 6606376 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 5, 2019); Gabriel v. Lakeside Tavern of Dayton, Inc. , No. 6578, 1980 Ohio App. LEXIS 12894, at *5 -7 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., May 22, 1980); Furr v. Pat terson, No. 2021 -CVG-004661 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., June 22, 2021); Flipping Lake County, Ltd. v. Egan , No. 21- CVG-0305 (Ohio Mun., Euclid, May 12, 2021); 128 Fleek Newark LLC v. Hassett ,
No. 20 -CVG-02022 (Ohio Mun., Licking Cty., Oct. 21, 2020); KMW III Ltd. v.

Falacienski, No. 2020-CVG-01333R (Ohio Mun., Portage Cty., Sept. 14, 2020);Tr. of Kokosing Valley Trust v. Myers , No. 13-CVG-00428 (Ohio Mun., Mt. Vernon, Aug.

19, 2013). See also Shurtz Mgmt., Inc. v. Perry , No. 93 -CA-04, 1993 WL 290001 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., July 26, 1993) (valid notice where the notice to vacate is signed in the name of the plaintiff corporation, without the name of an officer or agent of the plaintiff corporation); Morrow v. Gates, No. CA91-11-021, 1992 WL 156116 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., June 29, 1992) (valid notice where the notice to vacate is signed only by plaintiff husband but it includes the names of plaintiff husband and plaintiff wife).

If the notice to v acate does not meet this requirement, it is an invalid notice and, therefore, the court is without subject-matter jurisdiction over the eviction action. Id.

For a discussion on the persons who may be the plaintiff in an eviction action, see Section II.CC, below.
