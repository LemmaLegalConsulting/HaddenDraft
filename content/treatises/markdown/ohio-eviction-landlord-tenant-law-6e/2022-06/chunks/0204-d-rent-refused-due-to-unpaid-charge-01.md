An assisted housing landlord may not refuse a tenant’s tender of rent, on the basis of the tenant’s nonpayment of a charge, and then evict the tenant for nonpayment of
rent. Lorain Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Noel, No. 06-CA-009006, 2007 WL 1661854 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., June 11, 2007) (maintenance charge). See also Seldin Co. v. Calabro, 702 N.W.2d 504 (Iowa App. 2005) (late payment fee); Watertown Hous. Auth. v.

Kirkland, 2 Misc.3d 280, 766 N.Y.S.2d 790 (City Ct. 2003) (utility and maintenance charges). But see W ilmington Green III v. Rickman , No. CA90 -07-013, 1991 WL 81850 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., May 13, 1991) (late payment fee).199 In Noel, the landlord assessed the tenant a $50 maintenance charge, in addition to her $32 per month rent, and refused to accept the tenant’s tender of rent without payment of the maintenance charge as well. “By including the maintenance charge in the rent statement and demanding the payment of either all or none, then by suing to terminate appellee’s [tenant’s] tenancy solely on the basis of the non-payment of rent, appellant [landlord] has effectively expanded the definition of rent to encompass other charges.” Noel, 2007 WL 1661854, at *3. The court held that this is impermissible and, thereupon, upheld the eviction judgment in favor of the tenant.

In these circumstances, a landlord may seek to terminate the tenancy for nonpayment of the maintenance charge.Noel, 2007 WL 1661854, at *3. A termination for nonpayment of a charge differs from a termination for nonpayment of rent in a t least four respects. First, the nonpayment of the charge may be a minor violation of the lease and thus not good cause to terminate the tenancy. See Section III.G.3, below.

Second, before an assisted housing landlord may terminate a tenancy for nonpayment of a disputed charge, there may be a requirement for an opportunity for a prior hearing before a neutral adjudicator. See Section III.G.3, below. Third, for Public Housing, the notice requirement for nonpayment of a charge differs from the notice requirement for nonpayment of rent. See 24 C.F.R. § 966.4(l)(3). Fourth, the charge may arise from a violation of R.C. 5321.05, which triggers the tenant’s right to a notice and an opportunity to cure under R.C. 5321.11. See Section I.D.2, above.
