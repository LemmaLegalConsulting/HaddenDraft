The rental units in the assisted housing programs have a marke t-based contract
rent. In the assisted housing programs other than Public Housing, the market -based contract rent is set forth in the lease. It is the sum of the tenant’s portion of the rent and the rent subsidy paid on behalf of the tenant. In Public Housing, it is the flat rent (see 24 C.F.R. § 960.253(b)), which is a maximum rent for the rental unit. See Walker
v. Boston Hous. Auth., No. 00-H84-CV-000149, slip op. at 19 (Mass. Hous. Ct., Boston Div., June 9, 2009) (“the flat rents best approximate the fair market value of a public housing unit”).

The market-based contract rent for an assisted housing rental unit, like the contract rent in private housing, is presumptive evidence of the rental value of the premises without the defective condition.
