## Source text

Condition Violates the Duty Factors for determining whether a defective condition violates R.C. 5321.04(A)(2) include: (1) whether the alleged defect violates housing laws, regulations, or ordinances, (2) the seriousness of the defect, (3) the extent of inconvenie nce suffered by the tenant as a result of the defect, and (4) the length of time the defect has persisted.

Pagoda Co. v. Smith, No. 76-CVG-1548, slip op. at 5 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland Heights,
Feb. 25, 1977), aff’d, No. 37936, 1979 Ohio App. LEXIS 11691, at *11 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 8, 1979); Weingarden v. Eagle Ridge Condos., 71 Ohio Misc.2d 7, 13, 653 N.E.2d 759 (Ohio Mun., Toledo, Hous. Div., 1995).269 Whether the defect violates an applicable housing, building, health, or safety code is not a dispositive factor. R.C. 5321.04(A)(2) imposes an obligation to maintain the premises in a fit and habitable condition irrespective of whether there are any such codes, and in addition to any such codes. Lloyd v. Roosevelt Props., No. 105721, 2018 WL 3814972, ¶ 21 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug. 9, 2018).

The basis for this conclusion is two-fold. First, it is evident from the legislature’s decision to establish a “fit and habitable” standard in R.C. 5321.04(A)(2) that is separate from the R.C. 5321.04(A)(1) obligation to comply with such codes.

Second, it is consistent with the implied warranty of habitability case law in other jurisdictions, which holds that a breach of the warranty may occur regardless of whether a municipal code violation exists. E.g., Glasoe v. Trinkle, 107 Ill.2d 1, 10-12, 479 N.E.2d 915 (1985); Hilder v. St. Peter , 144 Vt. 150, 160, 478 A.2d 202 (1984);

Pugh v. Holmes , 486 Pa. 272, 290, 405 A.2d 897 (1979); Park W. Mgmt. Corp. v.

Mitchell, 47 N.Y.2d 316, 328, 418 N.Y.S.2d 310 (1979); Boston Hous. Auth. v.

Hemingway, 363 Mass. 184, 200 n.16, 293 N.E.2d 831 (1973). It recognizes that “not all towns and municipalities have housing codes; where there are codes, the particular problem complained of may not be addressed.” Hilder, 144 Vt. at 160.

“The concept of fitness and habitability is concerned with defects that force a tenant to endure serious deprivations of basic needs or threat to the tenant’s health or safety ….” Wilmoth v. Akron Metro. Hous. Auth., No. 27746, 2016 WL 3322551,¶ 21 269 See also Glasoe v. Trinkle , 107 Ill.2d 1, 14, 479 N.E.2d 915 (1985) (implied warranty of habitability); Detling v. Edelbrock, 671 S.W.2d 265, 270 (Mo. 1984) (same); Teller v. McCoy, 162 W. Va. 367, 391-92, 253 S.E.2d 114 (1978) (same); Mease v. Fox, 200 N.W.2d 791, 796- 97 (Iowa 1972) (same).

317 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., June 15, 2016). It is not a warranty against all discomfort and
inconvenience. Id.

The R.C. 5321.04(A)(2) “[f]itness and habitability entails such defects as lack of water or heat, faulty wiring, or vermin infestation.” Aldridge v. Englewood Vill., Ltd.,
No. 10251, 1987 WL 15015, at *3 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., July 22, 1987). Accord Goodman v. Dan Rich, LLC, No. 109581,2021 WL 927001,¶ 31 (Ohio App., 8th Dist.,
Mar. 11, 2021); Gress v. Wechter, No. H-12-023, 2013 WL 1092731, ¶¶ 20,21 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Mar. 15, 2013); St. Germain v. Newell , No. 9 -15-14, 2015 WL 5319833, ¶ 28 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Sept. 14, 2015).

It also entails other significant interference with a tenant’s use and enjoyment of the premises. E.g., Holmes v. Cobblestone Grove, No. CA20-04-075, 2017 WL 74986 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Jan. 9, 2017) (after repair of massive plumbing leak, mold and mildew in the unit and repeated and ongoing intrusion from remediation efforts ( e.g., drywall debris everywhere, large fans and dehumidifiers running for weeks, large holes in drywall, and inaccessibility to tenant’s furnishings) that deprived tenant of reasonable use and enjoyment of the kitchen and living areas); McNelly v. Conde, 166 N.E.3d 697, 2021-Ohio-146, ¶ 8 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., 2021) (upon arriving to move in, “apartment was filthy, with water behind the walls, leakage, mold all over, and exposed wires in the kitchen and attic, which were a safety hazard”).
