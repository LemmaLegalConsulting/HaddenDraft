Partial Payment of Rent In a month-to-month tenancy, the landlord’s acceptance of a partial payment of the monthly rent renews the tenancy. Fairborn Apts. v. Herman, No. 90-CA-28, 1991 WL 10962, at *4 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Jan. 31, 1991); FMJ Props. v. Hinton, No. 50314, 1986 WL 4396, at *3 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Apr. 10, 1986).114 This renewal occurs even if the partial payment of rent is tendered and accepted after the rent due date. See, e.g., Phipps v. Cable , No. CVG -91-0099 (Ohio Mun., Portsmouth, Mar. 14, 1991).

This renewal also occurs even if the month-to-month tenancy is established in a written lease that requires good cause for termination or nonrenewal. See, e.g. , Fairborn Apts. v. Herman, No. 90-CA-28, 1991 WL 10962 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Jan.

31, 1991).

When a landlord’s acceptance of a part ial payment of rent renews a month -to- month tenancy, the landlord may not evict the tenant for nonpayment of rent for that month.115 Fairborn Apts. v. Herman , No. 90 -CA-28, 1991 WL 10962, at *4 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Jan. 31, 1991); FMJ Props. v. Hinton, No. 50314, 1986 WL 4396, at *3 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Apr. 10, 1986).
