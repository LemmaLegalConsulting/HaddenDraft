# 8. Due Date of Payment

## Source text

The date by which a tenant is required to pay the rent (“rent due date”) is generally established by a written rental agreement or by a periodic tenancy.

When a written rental agreement specifies the rent due date, at least four circumstances may modify it.

First, when a written lease contains a grace period for the payment of rent, the 355 grace period becomes the rent due date. Showe Mgmt. Corp. v. Moore, No. 08-CA-10, 2009 WL 1372986 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., May 14, 2009) (ten -day grace period);

Columbus Metro. Hous. Authority v. Jackson , No. 81AP-802, 1982 WL 4010 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 4, 1982) (ten-day grace period).

Second, when the rent due date is a Sunday, R.C. 1.14 extends the time for payment to the following Monday. E.g., Myszka v. Bledsoe, No. 00-CVG-08398 (Ohio Mun., Akron, Nov. 9, 2000).

Third, when an eviction action is dismissed or an eviction judgment is entered in favor of the tenant, with respect to the rent that accrued during the eviction action, the rent due date is a reasonable time after the conclusion of the eviction action. See Section II.M, above.

Fourth, a landlord’s course of conduct of accepting late rent payments from the tenant may change the rent due date to the latest date on which the landlord customarily accepts the rent payments. See Section II.H, above.

When a written rental agreement requires monthly rental p ayments, but fails to specify a rent due date, the rent due date is the last day of the month.Costanzo v. Gross,
No. 2008-CVG-4234 (Ohio Mun., Cuyahoga Falls, Nov. 21, 2008);Crossings, LLC v.

Coinmach Corp., No. 2008-CVG-01528 (Ohio Mun., Elyria, June 12, 2008).
