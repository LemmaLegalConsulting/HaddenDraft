# a. Summary of the Statutory Requirements

## Source text

“R.C. 5321.04(A) sets forth the obligations of a landlord who is a party to a rental agreement.” Mann v. Northgate Investors, L.L.C., 138 Ohio St.3d 175, 5 N.E.3d 594, ¶ 9 (2014).

An obligation of all residential landlords, including those who lease single family homes, is to maintain and provide services for the premises in compliance with the requirements of R.C. 5321.04(A). Murray v. Bargo , No. CA86 -11-080, 1987 WL 15440, at *2 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Aug. 10, 1987) (single family homes are not exempt).

The R.C. 5321.04(A) requirements are to:
(1) comply with the requirements of applicable building, housing, health and safety codes that materially affect health and safety, R.C.

5321.04(A)(1) (see Section VII.C.2, below);
(2) make all repairs and do whatever is reasonably necessary to put and keep the premises in a fit and habitable condition, R. C. 5321.04(A)(2) (see Section VII.C.3, below);
(3) keep all common areas of the premises in a safe and sanitary condition, R.C. 5321.04(A)(3) (see Section VII.C.4, below);
(4) maintain in good and safe working order and condition all electrical, plumbing, sanitary , heating, ventilating, and air conditioning fixtures and appliances, and elevators, supplied or required to be supplied by the landlord, R.C. 5321.04(A)(4) (see Section VII.C.5, below);
(5) provide garbage cans and provide for garbage removal if there are fou r or more rental units in the building, R.C. 5321.04(A)(5); and
(6) supply running water, reasonable amounts of hot water, and reasonable heat at all times, subject to certain exceptions, R.C. 5321.04(A)(6) (see Section VII.C.6, below).
