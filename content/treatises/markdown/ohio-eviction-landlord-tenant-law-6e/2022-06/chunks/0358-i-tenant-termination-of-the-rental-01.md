Agreement, R.C. 5321.07(B)(3) When a tenant has satisfied the requirements for rent depositing (see Section VIII.A, above), instead of rent depositing and/or filing an R.C. 5321.07(B)(2) action (see Section VIII.H, above), the tenant may terminate the rental agreement. R.C.

5321.07(B)(3). E.g., Lloyd v. Roosevelt Props., No. 105721, 2018 WL 3814972 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug. 9, 2018); Holmes v. Cobblestone Grove , No. CA2016 -04-075, 2017 WL 74986 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Jan. 9, 2017);Cont’l Enters., Ltd. v. Franklin,
No. 102899, 2016 WL 2941209 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 19, 2016); Cont’l Enters.,
Ltd. v.Hunt, No. 102200, 2015 WL 9460300 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 24, 2015) ;

Turski v. Loesch, No. L-05-1273, 2006 WL 3378415 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov. 22, 2006); Stiffler v. Canterbury Runn Apts., No. 19308, 2002 WL 31243541 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 4, 2002); CMB P’ship v. Baker, No. 18159, 2000 WL 706824 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., June 2, 2000); Trepka v. Schultz, No. 75338, 2000 WL 263734 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 9 , 2000); Leszczynski v. Brewer, No. 12523, 1991 WL 285435 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Dec. 27, 1991);Hald v. Beatley, No. 87AP-43, 1988 WL 37456 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 29, 1988); Pearson v. Huber Inv. Corp., No. 84AP-526, 1985 WL 9913 (Ohio App., 10th Di st., Mar. 21, 1985). See also Shannon Way Apts.
v. Jackson, No. 92AP-254, 1992 WL 225567 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Sept. 15, 1992) (landlord’s reasonable and prompt remedy of the defect precluded an R.C.

5321.07(B)(3) termination of the rental agreement).

The effective date of a termination of a rental agreement under R.C. 5321.07(B)(3) may be as early as the first day of the lease term, if the R.C. 5321.07 notice is waived or precedes the commencement of the rental agreement. E.g., Shoemaker v. Whitt, 129 Ohio App.3d 591, 718 N.E.2d 932 (2nd Dist. 1998) (R.C. 5321.18 waiver of the R.C.

5321.07 notice requirement). See also Harpel v. Pierce, Nos. 17163,17168, 1999 WL 55700, at *5 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Feb. 5, 1999).

With respect to the R.C. 5321.07(B)(3) remedy, appellate courts are split on whether the R.C. 5321.07 notice requirement is met where the tenant has not provided the landlord with written notice of the defective condition, but the landlord has actual notice of the condition. The Eighth Appell ate District has held that, in these circumstances, the R.C. 5321.07 notice requirement is met.Sabik v. Drake, No. 53588, 1988 WL 32111 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 17, 1988); Katzin v. Murad, No. 46553, 397 1984 WL 7150 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 5, 1984). The Sixth Appellate District has upheld a tenant’s termination of a rental agreement under R.C. 5321.07(B), in these circumstances, although the court did not explicitly address this issue. Keener v.

Ewert, 67 Ohio App.2d 17, 425 N.E.2d 914 (6th Dist. 19 79). The Seventh Appellate District has held that, in these circumstances, the R.C. 5321.07 notice requirement is not met. Rental Mgmt. v. Au, No. 92-C-66, 1993 WL 307614 (Ohio App., 7th Dist.,
Aug. 10, 1993).

When a tenant elects to terminate a rental agreement under R.C. 5321.07(B)(3), the tenant may be required to provide the landlord with oral or written notice of the
election. See Shoemaker v. Whitt , 129 Ohio App.3d 591, 594, 718 N.E.2d 932 (2nd
Dist. 1998).

A tenant is not required to rely on or comply with R.C. 5321.07 to assert a common law claim of constructive eviction. Schutte v. Nicholson , No. C -910261, 1992 WL 19396 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Feb. 5, 1992) (constructive eviction based on lack of heat for five days). For a discussion on constructive eviction, see Section XIV.C, below.
