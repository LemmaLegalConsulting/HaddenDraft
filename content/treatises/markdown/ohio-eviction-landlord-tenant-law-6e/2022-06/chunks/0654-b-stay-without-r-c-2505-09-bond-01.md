# b. Stay Without R.C. 2505.09 Bond

## Source text

In an appeal from a PHA decision that terminates a family’s participation in the HCVP, an issue that may arise is whether the family is required to post a bond, under R.C. 2505.09, to secure a stay pending the appeal.

When an appellant seeks a stay pending an R.C. Chapter 2506 appeal, R.C.

2505.09 requires the appellant to post a bond “in such sum, notless than, if applicable, the amount of the final order, judgment, or decree and interest involved.” Under R.C.

2505.09, “the trial court or appellate court may direct that sufficient sureties in certain cases means no sureties.” Lomas & Nettleton Co. v. Warren, No. 89 -G-1519, 1990 WL 93138 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., June 29, 1990).

As discussed in Section XXIV.C.3.a, above, when the appeal is from a PHA decision that terminates a family’s participation in the HCVP, the PHA does not have a monetary judgment (or claim) against the family, and it does not incur any damages in continuing to make HCVP payments on behalf of the family during the appeal.

Therefore, in these appeals, courts have granted stays of the termination decision without a requirement to post bond under R.C. 2505.09. E.g., Comston v. Licking
Metro. Hous. Auth. , No. 11 -CV-0246 (Ohio C.P., Licking Cty., Feb. 28, 2011);

Huffman v. Coshocton Metro. Hous. Auth. , No. 10 -CI-0380 (Ohio C.P., Coshocton Cty., June 16, 2010); Osman v. Columbus Metro. Hous. Auth., No. 08-CVF-07-10542 (Ohio C.P., Franklin Cty., Aug. 26, 2008); Owens v. Parma Pub. Hous. Agency , No.

CV-08-656626 (Ohio C.P., Cuyahoga Cty., Apr. 29, 2008); Hammond v. Cleveland
Metro. Hous. Auth., No. CV-07-616036 (Ohio C.P., Cuyahoga Cty., Feb . 28, 2007);

Boone v. Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth., No. CV -06-587744 (Ohio C.P., Cuyahoga Cty., May 25, 2006);Mays v. Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth., No. CV-02-466090 (Ohio C.P., Cuyahoga Cty., May 2, 2002); Taylor v. Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. , No.

353992 (Ohio C.P., Cuyahoga Cty., July 2, 1998); Smith v. Akron Metro. Hous. Auth.,
No. CV93-11-3893 (Ohio C.P., Summit Cty., Dec. 1, 1993); Gray v. Akron Metro.
Hous. Auth. , No. CV93 -11-3889 (Ohio C.P., Summit Cty., Nov. 30, 1993);

Yarborough v. Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth., No. 259548 (Ohio C.P., Cuyahoga Cty.,
Nov. 26, 1993). See also Smith v. Akron Metro. Hous. Auth., No. CV94-09-3277 (Ohio C.P., Summit Cty., Sept. 30, 1994) (nominal bond of $50).

If the family does not secure a stay pending the appeal, the family may consider filing a separate complaint for monetary relief against the PHA. In an R.C. Chapter 2506 appeal, the court may order the family’s reinstatement to the HCVP, but it cannot award monetary relief.

Typically, the family’s claim for monetary relief, brought under 42 U.S.C. §1983, would assert that the PHA violated the family’s rights under federal statutes and
regulations. The family’s monetary damages would include, but not be limited to, the amount of the HCVP payments that the PHA failed to make on behalf of the family during the appeal.
