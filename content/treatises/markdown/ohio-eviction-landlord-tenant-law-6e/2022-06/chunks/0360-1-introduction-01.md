When a landlord’s failure to maintain or repair the premises violates an R.C.

5321.04(A) obligation, the tenant may have a contract claim against the landlord (“R.C.

5321.04 contract claim”). Miller v. Ritchie , 45 Ohio St.3d 222, 543 N.E.2d 1265 (1989); Laster v. Bowman, 52 Ohio App.2d 379, 370 N.E.2d 767 (8th Dist. 1977).See also R.C. 5321.12.

“A landlord who fails to maintaina rental premises as required by R.C. 5321.04 is liable ... for damages for breach of contract, as the rental contract necessarily incorporates the duties imposed by the revised code ....” Foss v. Re ddy, No. 68836, 1995 WL 643811, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Nov. 2, 1995). Accord Bozeman v.

Cleveland Metro. Hous. Auth., Nos. 92435,92436, 2009 WL 3321402, at *3 n.4 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Oct. 15, 2009).

The R.C. 5321.04 contract claim has three eleme nts: (1) the landlord’s violation of an R.C. 5321.04(A) obligation (see Section VII.C, above), (2) the tenant’s damages as a direct result of the violation,297 and (3) prior notice to the landlord of the violation.

The elements of the claim and related ma tters are discussed in Sections IX.A.2 -12, below.

When a landlord’s failure to maintain or repair the premises violates an express provision of the rental agreement, the tenant’s contract claim is essentially the same as the R.C. 5321.04 contract claim. See Smith v. Padgett, 32 Ohio St.3d 344, 513 N.E.2d 737 (1987) (oral provision); Kuhn v. Griffin, 3 Ohio App.2d 195, 209 N.E.2d 824 (6th
Dist. 1964) (written provision). For Ohio case law that establishes that leases are contracts, see Section VII.A, above.

“The breach of a contract for the lease of property may give rise to ordinary contract remedies for the injured party.”U.S.A. Parking Sys., Inc. v. Penn-Lillis Corp., 297 In an R.C. 5321.04 contract claim, a tenant may seek injunctive relief, in addition to monetary relief. See Section IX.A.7., below. When a tenant seeks injunctive relief, the prerequisites for issuing an injunction are an additional element of the claim.
