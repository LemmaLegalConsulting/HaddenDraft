# L. Absence of Timely Demand – Rent

## Source text

Collection at the Premises “In the absence of an agreement to the contrary, the general rule in Ohio is that payment of rent is to be made at the leased premises and that it is the landlord’s duty to go to the premises to collect rent from the tenant.” Davis v. Scullark, No. 57456, 1990 WL 145642, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Oct. 4, 1990). See also Slothower v.

Lally, 74 N.E.2d 654 (Ohio Mun., Dayton, 1947).

Also, a rental agreem ent (expressly or via custom) may require the landlord to collect the rent at the premises. E.g., Romine v. Winters, No. 98 -CVG-00907 (Ohio Mun., Licking Cty., Sept. 3, 1998);Foster v. Jones, No. 93-CV-G&F-185 (Ohio Mun., Alliance, Mar. 9, 1993); Douglas v. Talley , No. 91 -CVG-19274 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Sept. 19, 1991);Kaylor v. Cunningham, No. M85-CVG-26535 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Oct. 25, 1985).

When a landlord is required to collect the rent at the premises, the landlord’s failure to appear at the premises to collect the rent bars the landlord from evicting the tenant for nonpayment of that rent. Id.

Furthermore, if a landlord is required to collect the rent at the premises on a certain day (or days) of the month, the landlord must app ear at the premises and demand payment of rent from the tenant on the required day(s), in order to evict the tenant for nonpayment of that rent. Douglas v. Talley , No. 91 -CVG-19274 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Sept. 19, 1991);Kaylor v. Cunningham, No. M85-CVG-26535 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Oct. 25, 1985).

For example, if the landlord is required to collect the rent at the premises on the second or third day of the month, “he cannot evict for non-payment of rent by merely making an unplanned, unann ounced visit to the property after the third of the month and finding the defendants [tenants] absent.” Kaylor v. Cunningham, No. M85-CVG- 26535, slip op. at 2 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Oct. 25, 1985).
