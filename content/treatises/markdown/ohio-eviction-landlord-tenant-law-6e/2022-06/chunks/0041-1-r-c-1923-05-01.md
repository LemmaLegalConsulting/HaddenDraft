# 1. R.C. 1923.05

## Table-of-contents context

- I. Eviction Action, Procedures
  - E. Content of the Complaint
    - 1. R.C. 1923.05

## Source text

Content requirements for an FED complaint are established in R.C. 1923.05, which states in relevant part:

The complaint shall particularly describe the premises so entered upon and detained, and set forth either an unlawful and forcible entry and detention, or an unlawful and forcible detention after a peaceable or lawful entry of the described premises.

R.C. 1923.05 does not require a landlord to allege in the FED complaint the existence or terms of the lease between the landlord and tenant. Steiner v. Minkowski, 72 Ohio App.3d 754, 596 N.E.2d 492 (6th Dist. 1991).

R.C. 1923.05 also does not require a landlord to request in the FED complaint the issuance of a writ of restitution in order f or the landlord to secure the writ upon the entry of the FED judgment. Marysville Estates v. Bruce , No. 14 -13-12, 2013 WL 5347438, ¶ 8 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Sept. 23, 2013). For further discussion on the writ of restitution, see Section I.O.2, below.
