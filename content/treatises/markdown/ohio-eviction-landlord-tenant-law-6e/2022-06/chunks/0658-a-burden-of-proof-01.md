# a. Burden of Proof

## Source text

The PHA has the burden of proof on its alleged grounds for terminating the family’s participation in the HCVP. Gooden v. Cincinnati Metro. Hous. Auth., No. A- 0710779, slip op. at 7 (Ohio C.P., Hamilton Cty., July 25, 2008, and Aug. 29, 2008).

See also Williams v. Hous. Auth. of City of Raleigh, 595 F. Supp.2d 627, 633 (E.D.N.C.

2008); Hassan v. Dakota County Cmty. Dev. Agency , No. A08 -0373, 2009 WL 437775, at *1 (Minn. App. Feb. 24, 2009); Meyer v. Dakota County Cmty. Dev.

Agency, No. A06-1290, 2007 WL 2703005, at *3 (Minn. App. Sept. 18, 2007).

Generally, the PHA must prove the allegations against the HCVP participant by a preponderance of the evidence. 24 C.F.R. § 982.555(e)(6); R.C. 2506.04. See also Edgecomb v. Hous. Auth. of Town of Vernon, 824 F. Supp. 312, 315 (D. Conn. 1993).
