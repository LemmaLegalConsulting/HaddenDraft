## Source text

“At early common law, a landlord generally was immune from tort liability for any injuries sustained by any person due to dangerous conditions on a leased-premises in the exclusive possession of a tenant, even if the dangerous condition existed at the commencement of the tenancy.” Shump v. First Cont’l-Robinwood Assocs., 71 Ohio St.3d 414, 417, 644 N.E.2d 291 (1994). See also Sabolik v. HGG Chestnut Lake Ltd.

P’ship, 180 Ohio App.3d 576, 906 N.E.2d 488, ¶ 11 (8th Dist. 2009).

But now, “[i]n point of fact, the exceptions have nearly swallowed up the general rule of landlord immunity.” Shump, 71 Ohio St.3d at 418.

“In order for a tenant to establis h a landlord’s negligence under common law premises liability, the plaintiff must show: (1) the existence of a duty, (2) a breach of that duty, and (3)an injury proximately resulting from the breach.” DeFreeze v. Lynch,
No. 107090, 2019 WL 993334 , ¶ 13 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 28, 2019). Accord Thatcher v. Lauffer Ravines, LLC, No. 11AP-851, 2013 WL 816428, ¶ 7 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 5, 2013) Lyle v. PK Mgmt., LLC , No. 5 -09-38, 2010 WL 1948360, ¶ 11 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., May 17, 2010).

The legal duties that give rise to this common law negligence claim include the four that are summarized below.

First, “at common law, a ‘lessor [can] be held liable for the condition of premises if there were an agreement to repair ....’”Davis-Blunt v. Myatt, 134 Ohio App.3d 213, 215, 730 N.E.2d 1003 (1st Dist. 1999). Accord Shump v. First Cont’l -Robinwood Assocs., 71 Ohio St.3d 414, 418, 644 N.E.2d 291 (1994).

Second, “at common law, a ‘lessor [can] be held liable for the condition of premises if there were ... a violation of a duty imposed by statute.’” Myatt, 134 Ohio App.3d at 215. Accord Shump, 71 Ohio St.3d at 418.

Third, “at common law it has always been the rule that a landlord who undertakes and negligently makes repairs to the premises incurs liability for injuries to tenants or their invitees that are proximately caused by the negligent repair.” Myatt, 134 Ohio App.3d at 215. Accord Shump, 71 Ohio St.3d at 418. Cf. Winston v. Wolery, No. 96- CA-2442, 1997 WL 28535, at *2,3 (Ohio App., 4th D ist., Jan. 14, 1997) (landlord’s negligent design and construction of a deck).

Fourth, a “landlord owes a duty to maintain the premises under its control [ e.g., common areas] in a reasonably safe condition and to warn of unreasonably dangerous latent conditions of which the landlord has or should have knowledge.” Sauter v. One Lytle Place , No. C -040266, 2005 WL 627794, ¶ 7 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Mar. 18, 2005). Accord Shump, 71 Ohio St.3d at 418. This includes “a duty to periodically inspect and repair the common ways of the leased premises.” Davis v. Burns, No. 9- 97-39, 1997 WL 638276, at *2 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Oct. 10, 1997).

This common law negligence claim also includes a notice requirement. “Where a dangerous condition exists which has not beencreated by the premises owner or his or her agents, one injured on the premises must show that the owner had, or in the exercise of ordinary care should have had, notice of the hazard within sufficient time to remove the condition or to warn invitees about it.” Smith v. Finn, No. L-04-1244, 2005 WL 736669, ¶ 15 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Mar. 31, 2005). Accord Waugh v. Lynch , No.

100432, 2014 WL 1340263, ¶ 10 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 20, 2014); Maynard v.

Winters, No. 2012AP-05-0035, 2012 WL 6765613, ¶ 36 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Dec.

26, 2012); Odom v. Davis, No. 02-CA-43, 2003 WL 21458704, ¶ 17 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., June 20, 2003); Davis v. Burns , No. 9 -97-39, 1997 WL 638276, at *2 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Oct. 10, 1997).

This notice requirement – the landlord knew or should have known – is the same as the notice requirement for an R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se claim (see Section IX.B.2, above). Maynard v. Winters, No. 2012AP-05-0035, 2012 WL 6765613, ¶ 25 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Dec. 26, 2012);Smith v. Finn, No. L-04-1244, 2005 WL 736669, ¶ 16 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Mar. 31, 2005).
