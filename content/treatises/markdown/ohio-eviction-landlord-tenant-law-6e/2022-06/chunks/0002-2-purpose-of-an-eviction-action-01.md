# 2. Purpose of an Eviction Action

## Source text

“‘The purpose of the forcible entry and detainer statute is to provide a summary, extraordinary, and speedy method for the recovery of the possession of real estate ....’” Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Jackson , 67 Ohio St.2d 129, 131, 423 N.E.2d 177
(1981) (quoting 24 Ohio Jur.2d Forcible Entry and Detainer § 2, at 455 (1957)).

Accord State ex rel. Weiss v. Hoover, 84 Ohio St.3d 530, 533, 705 N.E.2d 1227 (1999);

State ex rel. GMS Mgmt. Co., Inc. v. Callahan, 45 Ohio St.3d 51, 55, 543 N.E.2d 483 (1989).

An FED action “determines the right to immediate possession of the property and nothing else.” Seventh Urban, Inc. v. Univ. Circle Prop. Dev., Inc., 67 Ohio St.2d 19, 25 n.11, 423 N.E.2d 1070 (1981). Accord Hussain v. Sheppard, No. 14AP-686, 2015 WL 915467, ¶ 6 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Feb. 24, 2015); Miami Valley Hous. v.

Jackson, No. 25020, 2012 WL 5382834, ¶ 5 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Nov. 2, 2012);

Long v. MacDonald, No. 3-02-10, 2002 WL 31007937, ¶ 8 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Sept.

9, 2002).

The ultimate issue in any FED action is solely whether the party who commenced the action has a present right to possession of the real property. Fodor v. First Nat’l Supermarkets, Inc., 63 Ohio St.3d 489, 494, 589 N.E.2d 17 (1982); Sholiton Indus.,
Inc. v. Royal Arms, Ltd. , No. 17480, 1999 WL 355898, at *9 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., June 4, 1999); Fenner v. Parkinson, 69 Ohio App.3d 210, 213, 590 N.E.2d 339 (10th
Dist. 1990); Cent. Park Place v. McDowell , 38 Ohio App.2d 29, 31, 311 N.E.2d 533 (8th Dist. 1974).

A party’s right to present possession is not necessarily based on ownership of the title to the property. For example, a lessor, as defined in R.C. 1923.01(C)(2), may bring an FED action even though the lessor is not the owner of the title to the property.

KDI Mgmt. Servs., Inc. v. Enerchem, Inc. , No. C -960587, 1997 WL 119898 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Mar. 19, 1997). For a discussion on the real party in interest standard in an FED action, see Section II.CC, below.

An FED action “is not an action to determine ownership of the titl e to the property.” Behrle v. Beam , 6 Ohio St.3d 41, 44, 451 N.E.2d 237 (1983). Accord Tucker v. Pfirsch, No. 2014-CA-0036, 2014 WL 3540883, ¶ 24 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., July 16, 2014). “Forcible entry and detainer actions determine neither legal nor equitable title.” Brainard v. Cardwell , Nos. 75870,75896, 2000 WL 897793, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., July 6, 2000). When the right to present possession is based on ownership of the title and a dispute exists as to which party holds present record title, it may create jurisdictional issues in the FED action. See Section I.L.3, below.
