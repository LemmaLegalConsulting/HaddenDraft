## Source text

The notice requirement for the R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se claim is three-fold:

the landlord received notice of the defective conditions of the rental premises, the landlord knew of the defective conditions of the rental premises, or the landlord should have known of the defective conditions of the rental premises. Shroades v. Rental Homes, Inc., 68 Ohio St.2d 20, 25-26, 427 N.E.2d 774 (1981), as clarified or modified by Sikora v. Wenzel , 88 Ohio St.3d 493, 498, 727 N.E.2d 1277 (2000). See also DeFreeze v. Ly nch, No. 107090, 2019 WL 993334 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 28, 2019); Wiles v. Miller, 3 N.E.3d 226, 2013-Ohio-3625 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., 2013);

Holloway v. McDaniel, No. 91599, 2009 WL 2263608 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., July 30, 2009); Avila v. Gerdenich Realty Co., No. L-07-1098, 2007 WL 4216972 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov. 30, 2007); Lilly v. Bradford Inv. Co. , No. 06AP -1227, 2007 WL 1640850 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., June 7, 2007);Boyd v. Hariani, No. 22500, 2005 WL 2087824 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Aug. 31, 2 005); Smith v. Finn, No. L-04-1244, 2005 429 WL 736669 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Mar. 31, 2005);Walker v. Barnett Mgmt., Inc., Nos.

84188,84210, 2004 WL 2847810 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 9, 2004).

Two significant aspects of this notice requirement are clear fr om the face of the
requirement. First, if the landlord has personal knowledge of the defective condition or the landlord received notice of the defective condition from a source other than the tenant (e.g., a code inspector), the tenant is not required to provide the landlord with additional notice. Second, the notice requirement does not require notice in writing.
