## Source text

When a court enters an eviction judgment against a manufactured home park resident, R.C. 1923.09(B) and R.C. 1923.11(B) require that the judgment entry include the authority of the park operator to remove the manufactured (or mobile) home and to exercise the right of sale, destruction, or transfer of ownership of the home under R.C.

1923.12, 1923.13(B), and 1923.14(B) (“summary sales process”). Oak Park Mgmt.
Corp. v. Via , No. 07 -CA-0022, 2008 WL 2168505 (Ohio App. , 9th Dist., May 27, 2008).

However, the failure of a trial court to include this right of sale, destruction, or transfer of ownership in the judgment entry does not deprive the park operator of this right and, therefore, constitutes harmless error. Id.

A manufactured home park operator may not invoke this right of summary sale after: (1) it has failed to inform the court that it may elect to proceed under R.C.

1923.13(B); (2) it has executed a writ of restitution and thereby has assumed dominion and control over both the land and any personal property; and (3) thereupon, the court has closed the eviction action. Colonial Oaks Prop., LLC v. Kilby , No. 2018-CVG- 03281 (Ohio Mun., Elyria, Dec. 9, 2020).

A manufactured home park operator also may not invoke this right of summary sale if it has not complied with the procedural requirements for the sale (see Section I.O.4.b, below). Id.
