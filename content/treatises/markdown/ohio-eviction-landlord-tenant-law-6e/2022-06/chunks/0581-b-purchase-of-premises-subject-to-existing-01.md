## Source text

Tenancy A purchaser of real estate, which is in the actual possession of a tenant, is charged with knowledge of, and acquires title subject to, the tenant’s rights under the existing
tenancy. Four Howards, Ltd. v. J & F Wenz Rd. Inv., L.L.C. , 179 Ohio App.3d 399, 902 N.E.2d 63 (6th Dist. 2008); Endersby v. Schneppe , 73 Ohio App.3d 212, 596 N.E.2d 1081 (3rd Dist. 1991); Kemp v. Feldman, 84 Ohio App. 154, 81 N.E.2d 319 (2nd Dist. 1948); Dunkel v. Hedges , 15 Ohio App. 259 (4th Dist. 1921); Smith v.

Gallagher, No. M9109 -CVG-034190 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Dec. 4, 1991);

Cassidy v. Vaccariello, No. M8907-CVG-026434 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Aug. 28, 1989).

For a discussion on the application of this law to tenancies under the Housing Choice Voucher program, see Section III.H.4, above.

Under this law, the purchaser acquires title subject to, among other things, the tenant’s right topay rent in accordance with the established course of dealings between the tenant and the previous owner (see Section II.H, above). Smith v. Gallagher, No.

M9109-CVG-034190 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Dec. 4, 1991); Cassidy v.

Vaccariello, No. M8907 -CVG-026434 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Aug. 28, 1989);

Classic Real Estate v. Bowen, No. M79-CVG-01404 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Mar.

12, 1979).

When a third party purchases a tenant -occupied property, three other issues that may arise, among the seller, buyer , and tenant, concern: the tenant’s rental payment obligations (see Section VII.F.10, above); negligence per se liability for defective conditions that existed at the property at the time of the sale (see Section IX.B.6, above); and security deposit liabi lity for deposits paid to the seller (see Section XVI.H.6, above).
