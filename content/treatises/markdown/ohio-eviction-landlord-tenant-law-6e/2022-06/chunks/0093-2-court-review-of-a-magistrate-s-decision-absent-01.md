# 2. Court Review of a Magistrate’s Decision, Absent

## Source text

Objections In an eviction action, absent objections to the magistrate’s decision (see Section I.N.3, below), the court may adopt the magistrate’s decision without making an independent analysis of the issues. Miele v. Ribovich, 90 Ohio St.3d 439, 443, 739 N.E.2d 333 (2000). Under Civil Rule 53(D)(4)(c), the court is only required to determine whether there is “an error of law or other defect on the face of the magistrate’s decision.” Miele, 90 Ohio St.3d at 443.70
