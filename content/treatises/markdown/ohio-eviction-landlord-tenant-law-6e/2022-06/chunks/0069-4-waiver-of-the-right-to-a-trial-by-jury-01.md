# 4. Waiver of the Right to a Trial by Jury

- I. Eviction Action, Procedures

## Source text

Ohio law prohibits any residential rental agreement provision that purpo rts to waive the right of the parties to a trial by jury in an eviction action.Herman and Kettle Props., Inc. v. Klenk, No. 18-CVG-02895 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton, Mar. 15, 2019). This prohibition arises from R.C. 5321.06, which prohibits any provision in a rental agreement that is inconsistent with or prohibited by R.C. Chapter 5321 or any other rule of law. Id. A jury waiver provision is inconsistent with R.C. 1923.10, which is a statutory rule of law that established the right to a trial by jury in eviction actions. Id.

A jury waiver provision is also inconsistent with the Ohio Constitution, which includes 63 a constitutional rule of law that, for the reasons discussed in Section I.J.1, above, establishes the right to a trial by jury in eviction actions.

The Eleventh Appellate District, in two decisions, has held that these jury waiver provisions are not unconscionable under R.C. 5321.14 and thereupon has enforced
them. Mentor Lagoons, Inc. v. Mayor, No. 10-180, 1985 WL 7807 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Mar. 1, 1985); Mentor Lagoons, Inc. v. Geldart , No. 6 -265, 1979 WL 208017 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Jan. 29, 1979). But, in these decisions, the courts did not rule on (or even consider) whether these jury waiver provisions are prohibited under R.C.

5321.06. Since these jury waiver provisions are prohibited under R.C. 5321.06, whether they are unconscionable under R.C. 5321.14 is immaterial.

However, a party may waive the right to a trial by jury on other g rounds. The waiver may result from, among other things: (1) a failure to file timely a jury demand,
(2) a failure to pay a required advance deposit or rent deposit, (3) a failure to file jury instructions in accordance with a local court rule,or (4) a failure to object at trial to the lack of a jury, when a jury has been demanded timely.

In an eviction action, like any other action, the failure of a party to demand timely a trial by jury constitutes a waiver of the party’s right to a trial by jury. Preston v.

Thornton, No. 29771, 2021 WL 1293724 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Apr. 7, 2021 );

Oakwood Mgmt. Co. v. Richards, No. 99AP-627, 1999 WL 1124692 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Dec. 9, 1999); Long v. Abele, No. CT98 -0018, 1998 WL 752040 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Oct. 9, 1998); Cuyahoga River Assocs. Ltd. P’ship v. MJK Corp., No. 68673, 1996 WL 17292 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 18, 1996); Knoll Group Mgmt. Co. v.

Wolfe, Nos. 93-CA-553,554, 1994 WL 326347 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., June 28, 1994);

Adm’r of Veterans Affairsv. Jackson, 41 Ohio App.3d 274, 535 N.E.2d 369 (9th Dist.

1987).

The local court rules determine whether a non -indigent party’s failure to pay an R.C. 1901.26(A)(3) advance deposit constitutes a waiver of the right to a trial by jury.

If the local court rule states that nonpayment of the advance deposit constitutes a waiver of the right to a trial by jury, nonpayment of the deposit will constitute a waiver of that right.46 Walters v. Griffith, 38 Ohio St.2d 132, 311 N.E.2d 14 (1974);Arlington Natural Gas Co. v. Martens , 173 Ohio App.3d 450, 456, 878 N.E.2d 1088 (3rd D ist.

2007); Hocking Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Martin, No. 97-CA-9, 1998 WL 100416, at *3 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Mar. 5, 1998). If the local court rule fails to state that nonpayment of the advance deposit constitutes a waiver of the right to a trial by jury, nonpayment of the deposit cannot constitute a waiver of that right. Warrick v.

Gaudynski, No. L-86-358, 1987 WL 18442, at *2 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Oct. 16, 1987).

When a court orders a tenant to make an advance deposit with the court in connection with t he tenant’s jury demand, the tenant’s failure to make the deposit constitutes a waiver of the right to a trial by jury.Burton Carol Mgmt., LLC v. Tessmer,
No. 2015-L-035, 2015 WL 6126186, ¶ 11 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Oct. 19, 2015);Gem
Mgmt. Co. v. Walk er, No. 60139, 1991 WL 26635, at *1 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb.

28, 1991); Martin v. Rogers , 42 Ohio App.3d 110, 111, 536 N.E.2d 665 (8th Dist.

1987).

When a court rule requires any party who demands a trial by jury in an eviction action to file timely j ury instructions with the court, a party’s failure to comply with that requirement constitutes a waiver of the party’s right to a trial by jury. Cotofan v.

Cully, No. 58285, 1991 WL 76028 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 9, 1991).

When a party has demanded time ly a trial by jury but the court nonetheless conducts a bench trial to which no objection is made, the case law is split on whether the failure to object to the lack of a trial by jury constitutes a waiver of the right to a trial by jury. Compare Benningt on v. Robinson , No. 1999 -CA-00212, 2000 WL 222156 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Feb. 7, 2000) (waiver), Nenadal v. Landerwood Co. ,
No. 65428, 1994 WL 189375 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 12, 1994), and Henning v.

Steiner, No. 2725, 1992 WL 281326 (Ohio App., 9th Dis t., Oct. 7, 1992) with Carl Sectional Home, Inc. v. Key Corp. , 1 Ohio App.3d 101, 439 N.E.2d 915 (10th Dist.
1981) (no waiver).
