# d. Statement of a Termination Date, and the Thirty -Day

## Source text

Requirement The R.C. 5321.11 notice must advise the tenant that the tenancy will terminate upon a date specified in the notice. Lovett v. Brown, No. 97-CVG-5586 (Ohio Mun., Akron, Aug. 20, 1997); Hood v. Kusior, No. 97-CVG-3823 (Ohio Mun., Akron, July 23, 1997). Cf. M.L.R. Props., Inc. v. Baer, No. L-85-330, 1986 WL 6703 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 13, 1986) (construing R.C. 3733.13 (now R.C. 4781.45) – the manufactured home park provision for a notice of termination that is essentially the same as R.C. 5321.11).

This requirement is not met if the notice uses conditional language such as “may lead to,” “may result in,” or “could result in” your eviction if you fail to comply in the
future. Green v. Sephus , No. 90 -CVG-1768-38, slip op. at 3 (Ohio M un., East Cleveland, Jan. 8, 1991).

The termination date specified in the R.C. 5321.11 notice may not be less than thirty days after the date on which the notice has been served. R.C. 5321.11.

For example, if an R.C. 5321.11 notice specifies that the tenancy will be terminated on November 30th, the notice must be served on or before October 31st. If the notice is served on November 2nd, it provides the tenant with only twenty-eight days advance notice and thus fails to comply with R.C. 5321.11. Willowood Apts. v. Page, No. 96- L-003, 1996 WL 535235, at *3 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Sept. 6, 1996).
