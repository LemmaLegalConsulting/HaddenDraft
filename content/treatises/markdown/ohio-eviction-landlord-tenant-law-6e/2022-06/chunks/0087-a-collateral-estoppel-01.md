“[C]ollateral estoppel ... precludes the relitigation, in a second action of an issue that has been actually and necessarily litigated and determined in a prior action which was based on a different cause of action.”Whitehead v. Gen. Tel. Co. of Ohio, 20 Ohio St.2d 108, 112, 254 N.E.2d 10 (1969). Accord Namenyi v. Tomasello, No. 2013-CA- 75, 2014 WL 5089113 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 10, 2014);McAlpine v. Patrick, No.

86453, 2006 WL 562191 (Ohio App., 8th Dist. Cty., Mar. 9, 2006).

Therefore, the court’s determination of an issue, when it rules on the eviction claim, is binding on the parties when the court later rules on other claims or counterclaims in that action or in a second action. The case law below is illustrative.

If the court finds nonpayment of rent when ruling on the evictionclaim, this finding is binding on the parties when the court later rules on the landlord’s monetary claim for nonpayment of rent. Great Lakes Mall, Inc. v. Deli Table, Inc., No. 93-L-154, 1994 WL 587559, at *1-3 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Sept. 16, 1994);Chan v. Birch, No. 15249, 1992 WL 64190, at *2 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Mar. 25, 1992).

If the court finds in a first eviction action that a tenant overpaid her rent and, as a result, does not owe any rent for certain future months, this finding is binding on the parties when the court rules in a second eviction action for nonpayment of rent for one (or more) of those specified months. Boyd v. Williams , Nos. 07 -CVG-11772,14908 (Ohio Mun., Akron, Jan. 18, 2008).

If the court finds no racial discrimination in th e nonrenewal of the lease when it rules on the eviction claim, this finding is binding on the parties when the court later rules on the tenant’s counterclaim for racial discrimination. Palombo v. Hayes, Nos.

65781,66714, 1995 WL 229120, at *7-8 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Apr. 13, 1995).

If an eviction judgment is based on the existence of an oral lease, this finding is binding on the parties when the court later rules on the tenant’s counterclaim for breach of an alleged land installment contract. Tillimon v. Rideout, No. L-00-1388, 2001 WL 1518111, at *2 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov. 30, 2001).
