## Source text

When a landlord violates R.C. 5321.04(A)(8) or 5321.04(B), the tenant may assert common law claims against the landlord, in addition to the R.C. 5321.04(B) claim. See Section V.C, above. For example, the tenant may have a claim for:
(1) trespass, Helfrich v. Mellon, No. 06 -CA-69, 2007 WL 1881537 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 27, 2007);Meacham v. Miller, 79 Ohio App.3d 35, 606 N.E.2d 996 (4th Dist. 1992); Baker v. Loudermilk, No. 76AP-770, 1977 Ohio App. LEXIS 7427 (Ohio App., 10 th Dist., Mar. 29, 1977);

Barnes v. Welborn , No. 04 -6855, 2005 WL 5533987 (Ohio C.P., Montgomery Cty., July 5, 2005);
(2) invasion of privacy , Helfrich v. Mellon , No. 06 -CA-69, 2007 WL 1881537 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 27, 2007); Meacham v. Miller, 79 Ohio App.3d 35, 606 N.E.2d 996 (4th Dist. 1992); and/or
(3) implied covenant of quiet enjoyment violation, Helfrich v. Mellon, No.

06-CA-69, 2007 WL 1881537 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 27, 2007).

Also, in connection with these common law claims, the tenant has a cla im for punitive damages, if the landlord’s conduct is malicious. See Meacham v. Miller, 79 Ohio App.3d 35, 606 N.E.2d 996 (4th Dist. 1992); Baker v. Loudermilk, No. 76AP- 770, 1977 Ohio App. LEXIS 7427 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 29, 1977).

If a tenant is awarded punitive damages in any of these common law claims, the tenant may also be entitled to an award of attorney’s fees under Ohio common law.

See Section XIX.B, below.
