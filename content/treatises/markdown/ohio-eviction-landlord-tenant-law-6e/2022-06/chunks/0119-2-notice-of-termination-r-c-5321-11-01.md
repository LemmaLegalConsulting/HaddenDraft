## Source text

When service of an R.C. 5321.11 notice of termination is required to terminate a tenancy, proper service of a proper notice is an essential element of the landlord’s case in chief. For a discussion on when this notice is required to terminate a tenancy, and on the content and service requirements for this notice, see Section I.D, above.

In such cases, if a landlord does not properly serve a proper R.C. 5321.11 notice, it is a complete defense to the eviction action. E.g., Chillicothe Metro. Hous. Auth. v.

Anderson, No. 1406, 1988 WL 69118 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., June 28, 1988);Sandefur
