# D. Vendor Breach of the Land Installment

## Source text

Contract If a vendor defaults upon the land installment contract ( e.g., fails to convey title upon vendee’s complete performance), the vendee may: (1) affirm the contract and bring an action for specific performance, (2) affirm the contract and bring an action for damages, or (3) disaffirm the contract and recover the paymen ts that the vendee has
made. Tyler v. Martin, 11 Ohio Law Abs. 660 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., 1932).

If the vendee affirms the contract and brings an action for damages, “the measure of damages is the difference between the value of the real estate at the t ime when conveyance should have been made and the balance due upon the contract of sale ....”
Id. at 661.

When a vendor’s breach of the land installment contract results in a foreclosure sale, the vendee may have an additional claim for unjust enrichment. In these circumstances, the vendee’s improvements to the real property “that added value to the property are compensable under the doctrine of unjust enrichment.” Zumbrink v.

Hercules, No. 1392, 1996 WL 339947, at *8 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., June 21, 1996).
