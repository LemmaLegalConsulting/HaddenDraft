## Source text

for Eviction At trial, a landlord may be permitted to amend the FED complaint under Civil Rule 15(B) so as to add a new ground. See, e.g., N.W. Ohio Food Marts, Inc. v. Mugg, No.

L-86-115, 1987 WL 7791 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Mar. 13, 1987).

But, if the tenant would be prejudiced by the amendment, Civil Rule 15(B) requires the court to deny the motion to amend or to grant a continuance to enable the tenant to prepare a defense for the new ground. Id., 1987 WL 7791, at *2 (“Furthermore, once appellee was given leave to amend his complaint pursuant to Civ. R. 15(B), the case was continued, giving appellant ample time to prepare a rebuttal argument”).
