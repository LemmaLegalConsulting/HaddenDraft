# 2. Final Appealable Order

## Source text

An eviction action often includes an eviction claim and one or more non-eviction
claims. The complaint may allege an eviction claim and a monetary claim. See Section I.E.2.f, above. The tenant may file counterclaims. See Section I.I, above.

Furthermore, the court may decide the eviction claim separate from the non - eviction claims, and the court may enter an eviction judgment before it enters a judgment on the non-eviction claims. See Section I.M.1, above.

In such cases: (1) the eviction judgment is a final appealable order, Witkowski v.

Arditi, 123 Ohio App.3d 26, 702 N.E.2d 1231 (7th Dist. 1997); Skillman v. Browne, 68 Ohio App.3d 615, 589 N.E.2d 407 (6th Dist. 1990),87 and (2) Civil Rule 54(B) does not apply, Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Jackson , 67 Ohio St.2d 129, 423 N.E.2 d 177 (1981).88 Consequently, in any eviction action, the parties are permitted to appeal immediately from the eviction judgment, even if one or more non -eviction claims are still pending in the case.

At least one appellate court has held that, in these ca ses, the appeal from the eviction judgment may be filed either within thirty days from the eviction judgment or within thirty days from the order that disposes of the remaining claims.Knop v. Davet,
No. 2016-G-0074, 2017 WL 1404477, ¶ 10 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Apr. 17, 2017).
