# A. Authority Under R.C. Chapter 5321

## Table-of-contents context

- XIX. Attorney’s Fees
  - A. Authority Under R.C. Chapter 5321

## Source text

The R.C. Chapter 5321 provisions that authorize a tenant to recover attorney’s fees are:
(1) R.C. 5321.02(B) (retaliation, see Chapter XI, above);
(2) R.C. 5321.04(B) (entry upon the premises, see Chapter X, above);
(3) R.C. 5321.15(C) (utility termination, lockout, seizure of personal property, see Chapters XII and XIII, above); and
(4) R.C. 5321.16(C) (security deposit, see Chapter XVI, above).

These provisions “serve[ ] as encouragement to the private bar to provide representation to tenants who normally could not afford to hire an attorney.” Drake v.

Menczer, 67 Ohio App.2d 122, 123, 425 N.E.2d 961 (8th Dist. 1980). They permit tenants to enforce their statutory right s without incurring attorney fee expense s that such enforcement requires and that exists solely because of their landlord’s action.See Alcorso v. Correll, No. 110218, 2021 WL 4317396, ¶ 49 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Sept.

23, 2021).

The R.C. Chapter 5321 provisions that authorize a landlord to recover attorney’s fees are:
(1) R.C. 5321.05(C) (maintenance/use of the premises, see Chapter XVIII, above); and
(2) R.C. 5321.09(D) (bad faith rent depositing, see Section VIII.E, above).
