Third, Mecca Mgmt., Inc. v. Gouse, No. L-97-1185, 1997 WL 728635 (Ohio, App., 6th Dist., Nov. 21, 1997), reversed a trial court decision granting the equity defense where the emergency assistance needs of the tenant’s mother were only relevant to one of the three months of unpaid rent.

Fourth, Hilltop Vill. v. West, No. L-88-120, 1989 WL 10277 (Ohio App., 6th Dist.,

In some eviction actions, especially those against project -based assisted housing tenants, the facts may give rise to an argument that there was no material breach of the lease or, in the alternative, that the court should exercise its equity power to relieve the tenant from forfeiture of the lease, even if a material breach occurred. If a jury is hearing the eviction action, the issue of whether a material breach occurred is a fact question for the jury to decide, whereas the issue of whether equity should relieve a forfeiture is a matter fo r the judge to decide. See Ashmore v. Eversole , No. 15672, 1996 WL 685568, at *4 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Nov. 29, 1996). See generally Takis, L.L.C. v. C.D. Moreland Props., Inc. , 180 Ohio App.3d 243, 905 N.E.2d 204 (10th
Dist. 2008) (equity claim seeking rescission of lease).

A judge may be less inclined to allow the tenant to maintain possession on equity grounds following a jury finding of good cause for eviction. See, e.g. , Cincinnati
Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Foster, No. 88-CV-05432 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty. Aug. 31, 1988). Therefore, when a tenant intends to assert a legal defense and an equity defense, depending on the facts and judicial assignment of the case, it may be more advantageous for the tenant not to demand a jury or to waive the tenant’s jury demand.
