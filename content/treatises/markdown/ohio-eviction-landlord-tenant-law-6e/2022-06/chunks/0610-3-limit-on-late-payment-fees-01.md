# 3. Limit on Late Payment Fees

## Table-of-contents context

- XXII. Miscellaneous Landlord-Tenant
  - J. Municipal Ordinances and Court Rules:
    - 3. Limit on Late Payment Fees

## Source text

At least three municipalities have enacted ordinances that establish a limit on the amount of the fee that a landlord may assess a tenant for late payment of the monthly rent (“late payment fee”). Those ordinances (and municipalities) are: Akron OH Municipal Code tit. 15, ch. 150,§ 150.52; Cleveland Codified Ordinances pt. III.C, tit.

IX, ch. 375, § 375.02(c); Toledo Municipal Code pt. 17, tit. 3, ch. 1770, § 1770.06.

The Akron ordinance limits late payment fees to the larger of twenty -five dollars or eight percent of the monthly rent amount.

The Cleveland o rdinance limits late payment fees to the larger of twenty -five dollars or five percent of the monthly rent amount. It also contains a second limit on late payment fees that applies when the tenant is not obligated to pay the entire contract rent (e.g., the tenant receives a rent subsidy). In these circumstances, late payment fees also may not exceed twenty -five percent of the portion of the monthly contract rent that the tenant is obligated to pay under the rental agreement.

The Toledo ordinance limits late payment fees to the larger of twenty-five dollars or five percent of the monthly rent amount.

These ordinances supplement the Ohio common law and R.C. 5321.14 limitations on the amount of late payment fees. Those limitations are discussed in Section VII.F.9, above.
