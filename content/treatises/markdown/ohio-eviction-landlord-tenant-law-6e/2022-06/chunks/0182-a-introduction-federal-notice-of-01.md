Termination and Good Cause To terminate the tenancy of a tenant who resides in Public Housing or subsidized housing (collectively referred to as assisted housing), 163 the landlord must serve the tenant with a notice of termination in accordance with federal requirements. Th e federal notice of termination is a notice that proposes to terminate the tenancy and informs the tenant of the reason for the proposed termination of tenancy.164 If the tenant resides in project -based assisted housing,165 the notice also informs the tenant of the tenant’s right to attend an administrative hearing or meeting before an eviction action is commenced.

A landlord’s termination of an assisted housing tenancy is also subject to a good cause requirement. A project -based assisted housing landlord may not terminate a lease during its term or refuse to renew a lease at the end of its term, except for good
cause. A Housing Choice Voucher program (“HCVP”) landlord may not terminate a lease during its term except for good cause.
