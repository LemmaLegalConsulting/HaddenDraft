R.C. 5321.04(A)(3) requires a landlord to keep all common areas of the premises in a safe and sanitary condition.

This requirement is similar to the landlord’s common law duty concerning 270 According to many experts, the best practice is for the landlord to remediate, at the same time, the infestation of the dwelling unit and th e infestation of the tenant’s personal property.

The experts state that this approach is both effective and prevents the spread of the infestation (which disposition of the personal property may cause).

Generally, the court decisions seem to view the R.C. 5321.04(A)(3) obligation as a codification, not an expansion, of this common law.

However, one court decision upheld an R.C. 5321.04(A)(3) violation that was based on repeated vandalism of a tenant’s car, a burglary of a tenant’s apartment, garbage spread on the lawn, and drug -dealing in the complex. Turski v. Loesch, No.

L-05-1273, 2006 WL 3378415 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov. 22, 2006).
