# b. Exterior Maintenance for Single-Family Dwellings

## Source text

“The yard of a single-family home is considered to be part of the tenant’s property to possess and control.” Good v. Murd, No. L-13-1235, 2014 WL 2170302,¶ 10 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., May 23, 2014). As a result, these premises typically do not contain a common area.

With the absence of a common area, some exterior maintenance work for these premises fall outside the R.C. 5321.04(A) obligations. A rental agreement provision that requires the tenant to perform that work would generally not violate R.C. 5321.06.

A typical example of those work items is a lease provision that requires the tenant to perform lawn care. E.g., Douglas v. Campost Props., LLC , No. 2016-CV-102094 (Ohio Mun., Elyria, July 21, 2020). While that lease provision may require the tenant to mow the lawn on a regular basis, it “does not remotely imply a duty by [the tenant] to care for the beds in any respect, including pulling weeds.” Id., slip op. at 4.

In large part, R.C. 5321.04(A)(1) and R.C. 5321.04(A)(2) define the limits of these exterior maintenance work items. R.C. 5321.04(A)(1) excludes any exterior maintenance work item that falls within the landlord’s obligation under an applicable code that materially affects health and safety. R.C. 5321.04(A)(2) excludes any exterior maintenance work item that fall s within the landlord’s obligation to put and keep the premises in a fit and habitable condition. For these excluded items, the rental agreement may require the tenant to perform the work, but only if the landlord provides the tenant with reasonable compensation for it. See Section II.R., above.

Conversely, with the absence of a common area, some exterior maintenance work for these premises fall within the tenant’s obligation under R.C. 5321.05(A)(1) – “to keep that part of the premises that he occupies and uses safe and sanitary.”See Section VI.D.2, above. For those exterior maintenance work items, the landlord may assume responsibility for them under a rental agreement provision. See R.C. 5321.13(F). But absence that assumption of responsibility,those items are the tenant’s obligation, even if the rental agreement is silent on this matter.
