## Source text

Non-Party Child If a tenant’s child is a member of the tenant’s household but not named as a party in the eviction action, the child’s claims against the landlord relative to the tenancy are not compulsory counterclaims. McCord v. Costy, No. CA87-05-036, 1987 WL 27190, at *3-4 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Dec. 7, 1987).

However, in these circumstances, if the tenant asserts a counterclaim against the landlord based on the personal injury of the tenant’s minor child (e.g., the elevated levels of lead in the blood of the tenant’s minor child), the claim of the tenant’s minor child agains t the landlord for these personal injuries falls under the Civil Rule 19.1(A)(3) compulsory joinder rule. Mason v. Douglas, No. 12-CA-000011, 2012 WL 5984906 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Nov. 26, 2012).
