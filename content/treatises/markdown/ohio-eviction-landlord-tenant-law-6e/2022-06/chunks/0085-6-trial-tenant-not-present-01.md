# 6. Trial – Tenant Not Present

## Source text

In an eviction action, the tenant is not required to attend the trial, and the tenant may be represented at the trial even if the tenant is not present.Forman v. Macias, No.

79AP-122, 1979 Ohi o App. LEXIS 11693 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., June 26, 1979);

CMHA v. Miller, No. 2003-CVG-09190 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Dec. 3, 2003). See generally Schaedler v. Shinkle, No. CA99-09-025, 2000 WL 1283776, at *1 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Sept. 11, 2000) (“nothing requires a defendant to appear at trial where he is represented by counsel”).

If a landlord wants the tenant to be present at the trial, the landlord may subpoena the tenant. Forman v. Macias, No. 79AP-122, 1979 Ohio App. LEXIS 11693 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., June 26, 1979).

If a tenant does not make an appearance in the eviction action, does not attend the trial, and is not represented by counsel at the trial, R.C. 1923.07 requires the court to try the case as though the tenant were present. “Thus, under R.C. 1923.07, even when a tenant fails to appear after being properly served, the municipal court must conduct a formal judicial examination of the evidence and reach a determination on a complaint for forcible entry and detainer.” T&R Props., Inc. v. Wimberly, 158 N.E.3d 137, 2020- Ohio-4279, ¶ 20 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., 2020).

Therefore, the landlord is still obligated to introduce competent evidence that proves the elements of the landlord’s eviction claim. T&R Props., Inc. v. Wimberly , 158 N.E.3d 137, 2020 -Ohio-4279 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., 2020); Aultman Home for Aged Women v. Ott , No. CA -7688, 1989 WL 64055 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 5, 1989); Fields v. Travis, No. 87AP-578, 1987 WL 873892 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Oct.

22, 1987); Hampshire Heights, Inc. v. Van Wormer , 17 Ohio Op.3d 287 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., 1980).

Civil Rule 55 is not applicable to FED actions. Hampshire Heights, Inc. v. Van Wormer, 17 Ohio Op.3d 287 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., 1980);Siegler v. Batdorff, 63 Ohio App.2d 76, 408 N.E.2d 1383 (8th Dist. 1979) (dicta).

If the landlord (or landlord’s attorney) has not been sworn in, the landlord’s (or landlord’s attorney’s) statements at trial are not co mpetent evidence to prove the elements of the eviction claim. Aultman Home for Aged Women v. Ott, No. CA-7688, 1989 WL 64055 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 5, 1989) (attorney’s statements);Fields v.

Travis, No. 87AP -578, 1987 WL 873892 (Ohio App., 10th Dist ., Oct. 22, 1987) 83 (landlord’s statements);Hampshire Heights, Inc. v. Van Wormer, 17 Ohio Op.3d 287 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., 1980) (attorney’s statements).
