5321.05(A) Violation “A landlord bears the burden of submitting sufficient evidence linking any alleged damage to the tenant’s failure to fulfill obligations under R.C. 5321.05 or a lease agreement.” Whitestone Co. v. Stittsworth , No. 06AP-371, 2007 WL 2334795, ¶ 26 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Jan. 23, 2007). Accord Levine v. Kellogg , 153 N.E.3d 663, 2020-Ohio-1246, ¶ 40 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., 2020); Hensel v. Childress, 145 N.E.3d 1159, 2019-Ohio-3934, ¶ 28 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., 2019);Warner v. Evans, No. 27536, 2015 WL 3398400, ¶ 8 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., May 27, 2015); Snyder v. Waldron, No.

12-CA-9, 2013 WL 3988775, ¶ 28 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., July 26, 2013); Cincinnati
Ins. Co. v. Evans , No. WD -09-012, 2010 WL 2334795, ¶ 46 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 11, 2010); Dupal v. Reindle, No. 79413, 2002 WL 450128, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 14, 2002);Kelley v. Johnston, No. 01-CA-5, 2001 WL 1479243, at *3 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Nov. 14, 2001).

“To establish the necessary link, the landlord must generally present evidenc e regarding the condition of the premises both before the tenant moved in and after he moves out.” Estie Inv. Co. v. Braff, No. 2017-L-172, 2018 WL 5433223. ¶ 26 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Oct. 29, 2018). Accord Dupal v. Reindle , No. 79413, 2002 WL 450128, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 14, 2002).

Also, if a landlord fails to prove that the damage occurred during the tenancy, the landlord has not met this burden of proof. Vlcek v. Brogee , No. 25499, 2013 WL 5436930, ¶¶ 47,48 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Sept. 27, 2013); Kelley v. Johnston, No. 01- CA-5, 2001 WL 1479243, at *4 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Nov. 14, 2001).See also Dupal
v. Reindle, No. 79413, 2002 WL 450128, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 14, 2002).

And, if the tenant did not violate R.C. 5321.05 (A), it necessarily follows that the need to clean the premises is not the result of an R.C. 5321.05(A) violation. Kovac v.

Whay Corp., No. 65469, 1994 WL 581528, at *6 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Oct. 20, 1994).

In these circumstances, the cost of cleaning i s simply part of the landlord’s “normal cost of doing business.” Id.

In addition, if a landlord’s general practice is to make certain improvements to the dwelling unit for each new tenant, whether or not the underlying condition constitutes damage to the premises beyond normal wear and tear, the improvement is not a cost incurred as a result of an R.C. 5321.05(A) violation. E.g., Cuzzort v. Rose, No. CA- 9791, 1986 WL 12632 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Nov. 6, 1986) (replaces toilet seats);

Snapp v. Rossiter , No. 14 -CVF-00579 (Ohio Mun., Cambridge, Aug. 27, 2015) (replaces throw rugs); Johnson v. Morelli Props. LLC , No. 2008-CVF-023275 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Oct. 7, 2009) (thoroughly cleans; re-keys locks); Berkeley Park, LLC v. Pile , No. 2005 -CVG-046214 (O hio Mun., Franklin Cty., Apr. 11, 2007) (“tune[s]” units, with painters and cleaners); Cliffview Manor Apts. v. Jefferson , No.

91-CVG-11203 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Mar. 27, 1992) (cleans carpets).

Two other examples of a landlord not meeting t his burden of proof are: Jones v.

Simondis, No. 97 -T-0073, 1998 WL 156871 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Mar. 27, 1998) (door blown off hinges by wind, an act of God);Gonzalez v. Gonzalez, No. 2010-CVG- 9940 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Aug. 22, 2011) (bro ken windows – no evidence on causation).
