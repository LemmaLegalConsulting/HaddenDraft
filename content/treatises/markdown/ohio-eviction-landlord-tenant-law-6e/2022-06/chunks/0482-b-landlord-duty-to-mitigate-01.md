# B. Landlord Duty to Mitigate

## Source text

“[L]andlords have a duty, as all parties to contracts do, to mitigate their damages caused by a breach.” Dennis v. Morgan , 89 Ohio St.3d 417, 419, 732 N.E.2d 391 (2000). Accord Frenchtown Square P’ship v. Lemstone , 99 Ohio St.3d 254, 791 N.E.2d 417 (2003).

The duty to mitigate exists where, in response to a notice to vacate, a tenant vacates the premises before the lease or tenancy has expired. E.g., Dennis v. Morgan, 89 Ohio St.3d 417, 732 N.E.2d 391 (2000) (notice to vacate for disturbances);Gerber v. Lewis,
No. 70955, 1996 WL 715504 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 12, 1996) (notice to vacate for nonpayment of rent);Hashemi v. Jim Owen & Co., No. 76AP-879, 1977 Ohio App.

LEXIS 8510 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Apr. 21, 1977) (notice to vacate for nonpayment of rent).

The duty to mitigate also exists where, absent any notice to vacate, a tenant vacates the premises before the lease or tenancy has expired. E.g., Oldendick v. Crocker, 70 N.E.3d 1033, 2016-Ohio-5621 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., 2016); Snyder v. Waldron, No.

12-CA-9, 2013 WL 3988775 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., July 26, 2013); Zunshine v.

Wallace F. Ackley Co., No. 99AP-531, 2000 WL 329008 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar.

30, 2000); Hines v. Riley , 129 Ohio App.3d 379, 717 N.E.2d 1133 (4th Dist. 1998);

Angelo v. Parker, No. 13140, 1987 WL 25776 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Nov. 25, 1987);

Lyon v. Howard, No. 5-86-22, 1987 WL 20290 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Nov. 10, 1987);

519 Shenk v. Green, No. C-860415, 1987 WL 11204 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., May 13, 1987).

When a tenant vacates the premises before the lease or tenancy has expired, the “[l]andlord[] mitigate[s] by attempting to rerent the property.” Dennis v. Morgan, 89 Ohio St.3d 417, 419, 732 N.E.2d 391 (2000).

The duty to mitigate requires a landlord to make reasonable efforts to obtain a new tenant for the property. Frenchtown Square P’ship v. Lemstone, 99 Ohio St.3d 254, 791 N.E.2d 417, ¶ 19 (2003); Dennis v. Morgan, 89 Ohio St. 3d 417, 419, 732 N.E.2d 391 (2000).348 “What constitutes ‘reasonable efforts’ is a fact-specific determination….There is no bright-line test for determining reasonableness.” Tillimon v. Myles, No. L-17-1032, 2018 WL 679455, ¶ 10 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Feb. 2, 2018).

“Whether a landlord made reasonable efforts to mitigate damages is a question of fact to be resolved by the trier of fact.” Beatley v. Schwartz, No. 03AP-911, 2004 WL 1244360, ¶ 12 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., June 8, 2004). Accord Oldendick v. Crocker, 70 N.E.3d 1033, 2016-Ohio-5621, ¶ 62 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., 2016).
