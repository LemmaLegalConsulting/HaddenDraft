# B. Normal Wear and Tear

## Source text

Wilhelm v. Ison, No. 92-CA-97, 1993 WL 76961 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Mar. 15, 1993) Condition/repair: cleaned carpet. Court decision (reverses): condition of the carpet did not constitute damage to the premises beyond normal wear and tear.

Scott v. Rice, No. 9-92-48, 1993 WL 46480 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Feb. 11, 1993) Condition/repair: painted two walls due to crayon marks on the walls. Court decision (affirms): crayon marks on the walls constituted damage to the premises beyond normal wear and tear.

Swartz v. Luker, No. CA91-07-051, 1991 WL 278243 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Dec. 30, 1991) Condition/repair: cleaned and painted walls and baseboards. Court decision (affirms):

none of these items constituted damage to the premises beyond normal wear and tear.

Saam v. Mosser, No. S-86-25, 1986 WL 15255 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Dec. 24, 1986) Condition/repair: defrosted refrigerator, painted the apartment, and replaced three light
bulbs. Court decision (affirms): none of these items constituted damage to the premises beyond normal wear and tear.

Brown v. Williams, No. 2012-CVG-17491, slip op. at 4 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous.

Div., Mar. 11, 2014, and May 2, 2014) Condition/repair: painted interior walls and hardwood floors in high traffic areas where paint had come off the floor, secured ceiling tiles that were sagging due to age, resealed 533 two toilets that had come loose over time, and re -attached ceramic soap dish that had come loose over time. Court decision: none of these items constituted damage to the premises beyond normal wear and tear.

Gonzalez v. Gonzalez, No. 2010-CVG-9940 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Aug.

22, 2011) Condition/repair: carpet dirty, stove dirty and burners replaced. Court decision: none of these items constituted damage to the premises beyond normal wear and tear.

Johnson v. Morelli Props. LLC , No. 2008 -CVF-023275 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty.,
Oct. 7, 2009) Condition/repair: burned out light bulbs and broken steps. Court decision: both items constituted damage to the premises beyond normal wear and tear.

Williams v. Wright, No. 2000-CVG-01040 (Ohio Mun., Marietta, Aug. 15, 2001) Condition/repair: removed trash, painted and wallpapered due to marks on the walls, and replaced carpet due to “burning holes” in the carpet. Court decision: all of these items constituted damage to the premises beyond normal wear and tear.
