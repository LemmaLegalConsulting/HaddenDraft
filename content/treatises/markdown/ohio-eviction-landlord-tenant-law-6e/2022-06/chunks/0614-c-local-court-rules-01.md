# c. Local Court Rules

## Source text

Some courts have adopted a local court rule on the sealing of records of an eviction
action. Two examples are Cleveland Municipal Court, Housing Division, Local Rule 6.13 and Marion Municipal Court, Local Rule 209.1.

Both of the se local rules provide that the records of an eviction action may be sealed, pursuant to the tenant’s motion, when the interests of justice in sealing the records outweighs the interests of the government and the public in maintaining a public record of the action. They also contain examples of circumstances that weigh in favor of sealing the records, which include: the dismissal of the eviction action either by the court or by the landlord; the landlord’s stipulation in support of sealing the 627 records; and, notwithstanding the eviction judgment for the landlord, extenuating circumstances led to the eviction action, five years have elapsed since the entry of the eviction judgment, and during t hat five-year period no other eviction judgment has been entered against the tenant.

Marion Municipal Court, Local Rule 209 also permits the court, on its own motion, to seal the records of an eviction action if the eviction judgment was for the tenant o r the landlord dismissed the eviction action.

The Franklin County Municipal Court has addressed this issue with a procedure not for sealing the records but, instead, for removing the records from the Court’s electronic records. The Court’s website doesnot allow electronic access to the records of an eviction action when the action is more than three years old. These records are still available to the public at the Clerk’s office. The Court also has adopted and placed on its website a standard form for a tenant’s motion to seal the records of an eviction action, which may be filed at any appropriate time.
