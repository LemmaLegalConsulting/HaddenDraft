The measure of general damages in an R.C. 5321.04 contract claim is the difference between the rental value of the premises in their defective condition and the rental value of the premises without the defective condition. 298 Miller v. Ritchie , 45 Ohio St.3d 222, 543 N.E.2d 1265 (1989); Smith v. Padgett , 32 Ohio St.3d 344, 513 N.E.2d 737 (1987).

The amount of rent that the tenant is obligated to pay under the rental agreement is presumptive, but not conclusive, evidence of the rental value of the premises without the defective condition. Miller, 45 Ohio St.3d at 227; Padgett, 32 Ohio St.3d at 347;

298 This is the same standard that some jurisdictions use in connection with their implied warranty of habitability. E.g., Glasoe v. Trinkle, 107 Ill.2d 1, 479 N.E.2d 915 (1985); Hilder
v. St. Peter, 144 Vt. 150, 478 A.2d 202 (1984); Boston Hous. Auth. v. Hemingway , 363 Mass.

184, 293 N.E.2d 831 (1973); Mease v. Fox, 200 N.W.2d 791 (Iowa 1972).

405 Pleasant v. Greenfield Station Apts. , No. 15282, 1995 WL 614359 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 20, 1995). E.g., Heck v. Whitehurst Co., No. L-03-1134, 2004 WL 1857131 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Aug. 20, 2004) (rental value without defects is contract rent of $639 per month).

The rental value of the premises without the defective condition may be an amount either higher or lower than the contract rent. Cochran v. Widra , 35 Ohio Law Abs.

608, 41 N.E.2d 875 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., 1931). Consequently, at least in theory, the amount of the general damages may exceed the amount of the contract rent. See Whitehouse Estates, Inc. v. Thompson , 87 Misc.2d 813, 386 N.Y.S.2d 733 (Civ. Ct.

1976).

For a discussion on proof issues, relative to the rental value of the premises in their defective condition, see Sections IX.A.11,12, below.

When determining the rental value of the premises in their defective condition, the tenant should be mindful of the Supreme Court’s observation that “[r]arely, if ever, will a defective rental property have a value of zero.” Miller v. Ritchie, 45 Ohio St.3d 222, 226, 543 N.E.2d 1265 (1989).

But, in egregious cases, post -Miller court decisions have found a rental value of
zero. E.g., Liggett v. Shitaker Props., No. 23425, 2010 WL 1410758 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Apr. 9, 2010) (serious infestation – each day tenant killed 30 to 40 roaches and 2 or 3 mice); Pleasant v. Greenfield Station Apts., No. 15282, 1995 WL 614359 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 20, 1995) (unbearable temperatures due to air conditioner defect);

Hansgen v. Seigel, No. 00-CVR-40822 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., May 29, 2001) (no water and no heat except for portable heater in one room);Roundtree v. Valentine, No.

98-CVR-23790 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Jan. 28, 1999) (no hot water, raw sewage/standing water in basement, and other code violations); Proctor v. Frame, 90 Ohio Misc.2d 11, 695 N.E.2d 357 (Ohio Mun., Akron, 1998) (no electrici ty and no heat); Moody v. Brady , No. 92 -CVF-549 (Ohio Mun., Clinton Cty., Mar. 8, 1993) (defective furnace made premises unsafe).

In Miller, the Supreme Court expressly rejected the reduction -in-use measure of general damages, 299 which some jurisdictions us e in connection with their implied warranty of habitability.300 45 Ohio St.3d at 226. However, insofar as the defective condition causes a reduction -in-use, it may be relevant to the rental value of the premises in their defective condition. Id.

In an R. C. 5321.04 contract claim, a tenant may recover general damages retroactive to the date on which the landlord’s R.C. 5321.04(A) violation began, subject only to statute of limitations, laches, and equitable estoppel defenses. Miller, 45 Ohio St.3d at 225-26. See also Pagoda Co. v. Smith, No. 37936, 1979 Ohio App.

LEXIS 11691, at *11 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 8, 1979). For a discussion on the statute of limitations for an R.C. 5321.04 contract claim, see Section XXII.G, below.
