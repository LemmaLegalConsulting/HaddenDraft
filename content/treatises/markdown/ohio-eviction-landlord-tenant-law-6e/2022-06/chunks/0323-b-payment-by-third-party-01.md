## Source text

When a tenant tenders a Salvation Army rent voucher to a landlord for a payment of rent, the landlord’s acceptance of the voucher constitutes an acceptance of rent. W.

Alex Vill. v. Goodwin, No. 88-CVG-179 (Ohio Mun., Eaton, Oct. 19, 1988).

When a landlord accepts a partial rent payment from the Red Cross on behalf of a tenant, the landlord is bound by the non-eviction provision in the Red Cross application that the landlord signs. Brumbaugh v. Epperl ey, No. 95 -CVGT-728 (Ohio Mun., Miami Cty., May 19, 1995) (the application stated that the $100 payment from Red Cross would result in the tenant not being evicted at that time). If a landlord commences an eviction action in violation of the non -eviction provision, the tenant may enforce the provision as a defense to the eviction action. Id.

When a landlord indicates a willingness to accept a rent payment from a county department of human services (“CDHS”) on behalf of a tenant and, as a result, the tenant does not seek the rent money from other sources, the landlord is estopped from refusing the rent payment from CDHS. Kamvouris v. Banks, No. 00-CVG-1586 (Ohio Mun., Cuyahoga Falls, July 24, 2000).
