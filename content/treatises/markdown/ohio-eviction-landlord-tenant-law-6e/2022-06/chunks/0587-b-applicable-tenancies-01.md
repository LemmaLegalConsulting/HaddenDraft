# b. Applicable Tenancies

## Source text

The PTFA applies to bona fide residential rental agreements – both written leases and oral periodic tenancies – that exist at a property when “complete title” of the property is transferred by foreclosure.

In Ohio, “complete title” is transferred upon the confir mation of the foreclosure
sale. See Hausman v. City of Dayton , 73 Ohio St.3d 671, 653 N.E.2d 1190 (1995);

Bates v. Postulate Invs. , 176 Ohio App.3d 523, 892 N.E.2d 937 (8th Dist. 2008).

Therefore, the PTFA applies to any bona fide residential rental agreement that has been entered into before the confirmation of the foreclosure sale, even if it was entered into after the commencement of the foreclosure action.

A bona fide residential rental agreement means: (1) the tenant is not the child, spouse, or parent of the mortgagor (former landlord); (2) the lease or tenancy was the result of an arms-length transaction; and (3) the rent is not substantially less than the fair market rent (or the rent is reduced or subsidized due to a federal, state, or local subsidy).

The owner to which the PTFA applies is the immediate successor in interest to the property pursuant to the foreclosure (“new owner”). In Ohio, this means the purchaser of the property at the foreclosure sale.
