4.1 and 4.6 – Eviction Claim The R.C. 1923.06 provisions that permit service of process by a combination of ordinary mail and posting conflic t with Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6. Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6 permit service of process by ordinary mail, but only after service of process by certified or express mail has been attempted. See Akron Metro. Hous. Auth. v.

Thompson, No. 14428, 1990 WL 106217 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., July 25, 1990). Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6 do not permit service of process by posting in any circumstances.

See Ohio R. Civ. P. 4.1, Staff Notes (Civil Rule 4.1 “eliminate[d] the possibility of doorknob service”).

Also, the R.C. 1923.06 provisions that permit service of process by serving “a person of suitable age and discretion found at the premises” conflict with Civil Rule 4.1(C). Civil Rule 4.1(C) permits such residence service only if the person is “then residing therein.” One issue that arises from these conflicts is whether Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6 apply to an eviction action, notwithstanding R.C. 1923.06. If they apply to an eviction action, the R.C. 1923.06 service of process provisions that are in conflict with these Civil Rules are invalid and of no force and effect by virtue of Article IV, Section 5(B) of the Ohio Constitution. See Hiatt v. S. Health Facilities, Inc. , 68 Ohio St.3d 236, 626 N.E.2d 71 (1994). “The Ohio Rules of Civil Procedure ... must control over subsequently enacted statutes purporting to govern procedural matters.” Id. at 237.

Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6 apply to an eviction action unless their appli cation would interfere with the summary nature of eviction proceedings. See Section I.B, above.
