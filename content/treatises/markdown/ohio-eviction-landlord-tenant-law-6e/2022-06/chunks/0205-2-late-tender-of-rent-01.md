# 2. Late Tender of Rent

## Source text

A late tender of rent, if tendered a few days late, is a minor violation of the lease and, for that reason, may fail to constitute good cause to terminate an assisted housing tenancy (see Section III.G.12, below). E.g., Sumac Real Estate Holding, LLC v.

Mitchell, No. 2014-CVG-00670 (Ohio Mun., Euclid, Apr. 18, 2014); Jenkins v. Rung,
No. 93-CVGT-601 (Ohio Mun., Miami Cty., July 5, 1993) (five days late); Columbus
Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Baker, No. 91-CVG-010490 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Apr. 19,
1991) (one day late). See also Gersten Cos. v. Deloney , 212 Cal. App.3d 1119, 261
Cal. Rptr. 431 (1989) (five days and seven days late). Cf. Skyline Terrace Apts. v.

Pease, No. 95-CVG-2392 (Ohio Mun., Canton, July 17, 1995) (“in the interest of fair play, one should not be evicted from her residence for being a day or two late”);

Amherst Vill. Mgmt. Co. v. Martinez, No. 97-CVG-00620 (Ohio Mun., Bowling Green, July 15, 1997) (two days late under repayment agreement for rent arrearage).

A late tender of rent, with a tender of the late payment fee, if tendered within the month that the rent is due, is not good cause to terminate an assisted housing tenancy “where the tardiness was not flagra nt or for long periods of time.” Park Forest of Blackman v. Smith, 112 Mich. App. 421, 429, 316 N.W.2d 442 (1982). For subsidized housing tenancies, it is not good cause to terminate the tenancy even if the tenant repeatedly makes such payments, unless i t has an adverse impact on the building, as defined in 24 C.F.R. § 247.3(c)(2). Am. Nat’l Bank & Trust Co. v. Dominick, 154 Ill.

App.3d 275, 280 n.3, 507 N.E.2d 512 (1987).201 Also, a lease provision imposing a late payment fee arguably “implies that the only penalty for a late rental payment is the late fee charge, not termination of the lease.” Park Forest of Blackman v. Smith , 112 Mich. App. 421, 428 -29, 316 N.W.2d 442 (1982). Accord Vistula Mgmt. Co. v. Armstrong, No. L-93-168, 1994 WL 262571, at 200 For the full citation for this handbook, see note 143, above.

201 In these circumstances, the tenant also may have a defense based on the landlord’s course of conduct of accepting late rent payments. See Section II.H, above.
