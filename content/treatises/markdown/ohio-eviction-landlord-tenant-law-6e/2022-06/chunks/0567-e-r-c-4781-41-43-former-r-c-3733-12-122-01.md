# e. R.C. 4781.41-.43 (Former R.C. 3733.12-.122)

## Source text

Superior Mobile Homes, Inc. v. White, Nos. 98-T-0072,0081, 1999 WL 974136 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., July 23, 1999) The resident attempted to deposit rent with the court of common pleas. R.C. 3733.12 required the resident, if opting to rent deposit, to deposit the rent with the county court.

As a result, the court upheld an eviction for nonpayment of rent.

Wickham v. Woodlyn Acres , No. H-96-015, 1996 WL 612314 (Ohio App., 6th Dist.,
Oct. 25, 1996) The court permitted the resident to contin ue rent depositing until the park operator furnished the home with drinkable water and thereby remedied the R.C. 3733.10(A)(4)
violation. The park operator was furnishing water to the home that was undrinkable due to its iron content.

Gettysburg Homeowners Ass’n v. Ellenburg Capital Corp., 80 Ohio App.3d 555, 609 N.E.2d 1315 (12th Dist. 1992) The R.C. 3733.12 notice must be “sufficiently specific to enable the park operator to discern the nature of the alleged problem and to remedy the problem within a reasonable period of time.” A notice fails to meet this standard if it merely cites to sections of the Ohio Administrative Code as asserted violations and contains no specific instances of acts or omissions that allegedly constitute violations.

Rex Hill, Inc . v. Cherry , No. 2588, 1991 WL 95243 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., May 29, 1991) R.C. 3733.12 does not permit a resident to rent deposit when the resident remedies the park operator’s violation of R.C. 3733.10 and the park operator refuses to reimburse the resident for the resident’s costs in remedying the violation.

Woodlake Assocs. v. Heringhausen , No. WD-89-27, 1991 WL 3810 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Jan. 18, 1991) The R.C. 3733.12 notice must contain specific instances of violations of the park operator’s obligations under the statute or the rental agreement. Under R.C. 3733.122, the court shall order the release of the rent to the park operator if there is no violation of the park operator’s obligations under R.C. 3733.10, the rental agreement, or the applicable codes. The park operator’s refusal to pay certain expenses related to utilities, and its refusal to permit the residents to choose their own supplier for propane gas, did not constitute a violation of the park operator’s obligations under R.C.

3733.10, the rental agreement, or the applicable codes.

Woodside Terrace Mobile Home Owners Ass’n v. Woodside Terrace Co. Ltd., 53 Ohio App.3d 20, 557 N.E.2d 804 (6th Dist. 1988) 587 When a park operator files an action for release of rent deposits under R.C. 3733.122, the park operator must file a complaint, naming the resident as a defendant, and the complaint must be served in accordance with Civil Rule 4.1. In such actions, the tenant must be given 28 days to file an answer in accordance with Civil Rule 12(A)(1). The court must conduct a trial on the rent release claim to determine whether grounds exist for releasing the rent deposit under R.C. 3733.122. In R.C. 3733.122 actions, the court may not assess costs against the resident unless the R.C. 3733.122(D) requirements are met.

Smith v. Vilk, No. 46759, 1983 WL 2902 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 15, 1983) In an action for release of rent deposits under R.C. 3733.12, the court may award the park operator costs under Civil Rule 54. Such costs are not limited to theamount (one percent of rent deposit) that the clerk of court retains under R.C. 3733.121 for processing and maintaining the rent deposit account. With respect to whether costs may be assessed against the resident only if the requirements of R.C. 3733.12(D ) are met, the court found that “from the record before us, we are unable to determine how costs were computed” and thus it presumed regularity.

Stoots v. Huber Mobile Home Park, No. 1180, 1981 WL 2863 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., July 27, 1981) A resident does not lose the right to rent deposit under R.C. 3733.12 simply because the resident, in completing a printed form at the time of the rent deposit, states the code section as R.C. 5321.07 instead of R.C. 3733.12. To commence an action under R.C.

3733.122, the park operator must file a complaint and the complaint must be served upon the resident in accordance with Civil Rule 4.1.

Shouhayib v. Yarger, No. E-9700004, 1998 WL 911706 (Ohio Mun., Perrysburg, Jan.

28, 1998) The court denied the park operator’s request for release of the rent deposit. The park operator was still furnishing the home with defective water and, therefore, was still violating R.C. 3733.10(A)(4). The water was not drinkable, had iron levels as high as 41 times the EPA’s Secondary Maximum Contaminant Level for iron, had a heavy chlorine odor, stained clothing during the laundry process, stained fixtures, destroyed heating elements, and damaged small appliances.

Quinlan v. Mobile Manor Mobile Home Park, No. 40, 1983 WL 191087 (Ohio Mun., Cuyahoga Falls, Jan. 11, 1983) The park operator’s violations included contaminated well water, inadequate water pressure, drainage problems on certain lots, and an unsafe playground area. As of the hearing date, except for the unsafe playground area, the pa rk operator had corrected the violations. The court denied the park operator’s claim of bad faith rent depositing, released part of the rent deposit, and ordered the release of the remainder of the rent deposit upon the park operator’s installation of a playground fence.

Jakacki v. Raineri, No. 179-193, 1981 WL 167768 (Ohio Mun., Portage Cty., Sept. 9, 1981) The park operator’s violations included unsafe road surface conditions in the park, non- existent or non -functional street lighting on several streets in the park, and lack of maintenance and mowing of common areas in the park. The court held that the rent deposit shall not be released until the park operator remedies these violations.

Swogger v. Merkel, No. 4139, 1989 WL 572092 (Ohio County Ct., Carroll Cty., Nov.

14, 1989) The resident’s R.C. 3733.12(A) notice to the park operator is not required to state that the rent may be deposited.
