## Source text

Tenant “As a general rule, a lease for a term of years does not terminate by operation of law upon the death of the lessee but passes to his personal representative, who becomes bound as assignee for the remaining term.” Sherman v. Carlin, 46 Ohio App.3d 149, 150, 546 N.E.2d 433 (8th Dist. 1988).

“However, a lease may be so personal in its terms as to manifest the parties’ intention that the lease terminates upon the tenant’s death.”Id.

The assisted housing lease 344 is sufficiently personal in its terms that the courts should find that the lease terminates upon the tenant’s death, absent a remaining household member. The tenant’s initial eligibility to lease the dwelling unit is based, in part, on the tenant’s low income. See Section XXIV.B.2, below. The amount of the tenant’s rental obligation under the lease is primarily based on the tenant’s income.

See Section XXIV.B.4, below. The project -based subsidized housing lease, 345 ¶ 13, prohibits altogether any assignment of the lease, and the lease does not include a provision stating that the lease is binding on the tenant’s heirs, successors, representatives, or assigns.
