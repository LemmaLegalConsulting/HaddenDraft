Kenko Corp. v. Singer, No. 81-03-0023, 1982 WL 3141 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., May 26, 1982) The lease provision required the tenant to provide the landlord with 60 days (or more) advance notice to terminate their month-to-month tenancy.

The lease provision was not unconscionable.

Landlord Option to Terminate Lease Without Cause:

Hessenhauer Corp. v. Drinks , No. 2002 -CVG-39464 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Nov. 7, 2002) The lease provision permitted the landlord, but not the tenant, to terminate the lease, during its one -year term, with a 30 -day notice of termination.

The lease provision was unconscionable.

Charges:

Tillimon v. Dukes, No. L-91-343, 1992 WL 206878 (Ohio App., 6th Dist.,
Aug. 28, 1992) The lease provision required the tenant to pay a non -refundable “application and move-in fee” in the amount of $500. Accordin g to the lease provision, the fee was not a security deposit, but rather a charge for processing the credit application and coordinating the transfer of utilities and occupancy of the dwelling. The fee was unconscionable.

McIntyre v. Silver, No. 52116, 1987 WL 11397 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 21, 1987) The lease provision assessed the tenants a surcharge for heat loss if the windows were opened when the heating system was on. The lease provision was unconscionable.

Berlinger v. Suburban Apt. Mgmt. Co. , 7 Ohio App.3d 122, 454 N.E.2d 1367 (8th Dist. 1982) The lease provision prohibited the tenant from bringing motorcycles onto the premises and imposed a charge of fifty dollars per day for every day that the tenant violated this prohibition. The prohibition was not
unconscionable. The fifty dollars per day charge was unconscionable.

377 FDA Peachtree, LLC v. Crowder, No. 18-CVG-03298, (Ohio Mun., Licking Cty., Aug. 5, 2019) Based on two lease provisions, the court held that “the manner in which late fees were calculated is unconscionable.” The first provision allocated the tenant’s payments “first to late fees and all other outstanding debts and bills, then to any past due rent, and lastly to the current month’s rent.” The second provision imposed a 10% late fee if rent is not paid on the first day of the month.

Overnight Guest Rule:

Hi-Land Terrace, Inc. v. Dinwiddie, No. 546, 1985 WL 6560 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Mar. 1, 1985) The lease provision or house rule required tenants to register and obtain management’s permission for overnight guests. The lease provision or house rule was unconscionable.

Heritage Hills, Ltd. v. Smith , No. 78-CVG-268 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, May 8, 1978) The lease provision required the tenant to report overnight guests to
management. The lease provision was unconscionable.

Jury Waiver:

Mentor Lagoons, Inc. v. Mayor, No. 10-180, 1985 WL 7807 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Mar. 1, 1985) The lease provision waived the tenant’s right to a jury trial. The lease provision was not unconscionable.

Mentor Lagoons, Inc. v. Geldart, No. 6-265, 1979 WL 208017(Ohio App., 11th Dist., Jan. 29, 1979) The lease provision waived the tenant’s right to a jury trial. The lease provision was not unconscionable.

Rosace v. Hancock, No. 95-CVG-516 (Ohio Mun., Girard, Dec. 19, 1995) The lease provision granted the landlord an express contr act lien and security interest in the tenant’s personal property so as to secure compliance with the lease. The lease provision was unconscionable.
