Prior Eviction Action When an eviction action is dismissed or an eviction judgment is entered in favor of the tenant, the landlord must provide the tenant with a reasonable opportunity to pay the rent that accrued during the eviction action. Mazzarella v. McGinnes , No. 91- 147 CVG-30291 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Dec. 30, 1991). Accord Belch v.

Witts, No. 96-CVG-5839 (Ohio Mun., Canton, Dec. 31, 1996);Spreading Oaks Vill. v.

Scruggs, No. 93 -CVG-4-22 (Ohio Mun., Athens Cty., May 21, 1993). See also Conway v. Nissley, No. 68536, 1995 WL 723298, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 7, 1995); Waterside Estate Apts., Etc. v. Keen, No. 94-CVG-2296 (Ohio Mun., Massillon,
Dec. 13, 1994). But see Julian Invs., Inc. v. Dudley, No. 98-CA-85, 1999 WL 64227, at *2,5 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Feb. 12, 1999) (dicta).

Absent this reasonable opportunity, “any dismissal of a complaint in forcible entry and detainer and any judgment entered for a tenant in such a case is rendered meaningless, as immediately following the dismissal or entry of judgment in the favor of the tenant, virtually every landlord could serve the tenant a new Revised Code 1923.04 notice to vacate based upon nonpayment of rent for the month in which the action was commenced.”116 Mazzarella v. McGinnes, No. 91-CVG-30291, slip op. at 4 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Dec. 30, 1991).

A reasonable opportunity to pay the accrued rent may be five or six days from the conclusion of the eviction action. Julian Invs., Inc. v. Dudley , No. 98 -CA-85, 1999 WL 64227, at *2,5 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Feb. 12, 1999) (five days was reasonable);

Belch v. Witts, No. 96-CVG-5839 (Ohio Mun., Canton, Dec. 31, 1996) (six days was reasonable).

This reasonable opportunity to pay rent, relative to rent that accrued during the eviction action, does not apply to any rent that accrued prior to the eviction action.

Conway v. Nissley, No. 68536, 1995 WL 723298, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 7, 1995).

Likewise, it does not apply to any rent that became due after the eviction action.

Bishop Park Towers v. Sefcik, No. 2001-L-137, 2002 WL 1173498, at *2-3 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., May 31, 2002).
