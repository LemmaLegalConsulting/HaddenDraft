## Source text

Termination For the thirty-day period of an R.C. 5321.11 notice or an R.C. 5321.17(B) notice, the tenant is obligated to pay the rent. A failure to do so would expose the tenant to an eviction action for nonpaymen t of rent, which would eliminate the notice of termination requirement (see Section I.D.1, above) and thereby accelerate the eviction process.

The landlord has a corresponding obligation to accept any tender of such rent. A landlord may not circumvent R. C. 5321.11 or R.C. 5321.17(B) by refusing to accept rent that has been tendered timely and in full and then evicting for nonpayment of rent.

See Section II.J, below.

The landlord’s acceptance of the rent for the thirty-day period (and thus before the termination of the tenancy) does not waive the R.C. 5321.11 or 5321.17(B) notice.Cf.

Edgbarton Inv., Inc. v. Target Expediting, Inc., No. L-99-1410, 2000 WL 1713816, at *3-4 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov. 17, 2000) (commercial month-to-month tenancy).

However, the landlord’s acceptance of future rent for any period after the thirty - day period may waive the R.C. 5321.11 or 5321.17(B) notice. See Section II.D, below.
