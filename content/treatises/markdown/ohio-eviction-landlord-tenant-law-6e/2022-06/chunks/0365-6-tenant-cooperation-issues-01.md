# 6. Tenant Cooperation Issues

## Source text

If a tenant denies the landlord entry to make the repairs and thereby violates R.C.

5321.05(B), the tenant waive s the right to recover damages for the landlord’s failure to make the repairs. E.g., A.C.S. Realty & Inv. Corp. v. Smith , No. 48664, 1985 WL 9025 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 23, 1985). Cf. Stoyer v. Fogelman, No. 11AP-737, 2012 WL 1059677 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 27, 2012) (R.C. 5321.07 rent deposit released to landlord because tenant denied landlord access to make repairs).

403 Also, there are circumstances where a landlord’s ability to remedy the defective condition requires the tenant’s cooperation beyond simply allowing the landlord access to the dwelling unit. For example, where the defective condition is a bed bug infestation, the landlord may require the tenant to prepare the dwelling unit for the extermination service. See Kenwood Gardens Ass’n, LLC v. Shorter, No. L-10-1315, 2011 WL 3654493 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Aug. 19, 2011). In such cases, the tenant’s lack of cooperation may be a defense to the tenant’s R.C. 5321.04 contract claim.
