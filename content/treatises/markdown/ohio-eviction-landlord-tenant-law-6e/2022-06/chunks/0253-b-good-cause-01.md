Every Ohio LIHTC project lease, in an OHFA mandated Lease Addendum, establishes a good cause requirement for any owner termination or nonrenewal of the 248 Given the good cause requirement for termination or nonrenewal of the tenancy (see Section III.L.4.b, below), the tenant has a cognizable property interest in continued occupancy (see Section III.A, above).
lease. The OHFA Lease Addendum became effective July 1, 2019.

The good cause requirement under the OHFA Lease Addendum i s as follows:

“Owner may not terminate the tenancy or refuse to renew theLease or rental agreement of Tenant except for good cause, which includes, but is not limited to, serious or repeated violations of the material terms and conditions of the Lease or house rules or a violation of applicable federal, state, or local law.” OHFA Lease Addendum,¶ 2.

Also, under federal law, a LIHTC landlord may not terminate a LIHTC tenancy, or refuse to renew a LIHTC lease, except for good cause. 26 U.S.C. § 42(h)(6)(B)(i), based on its cross -reference to subclauses (I) and (II) of 26 U.S.C. § 42(h)(6)(E)(ii).

For a comprehensive analysis of this statutory good cause requirement, see Marc Jolin, Good Cause Eviction and the Low Income Housing Tax Credit, 67 U. Chi. L.Rev. 521 (2000).

In 2004 and 2005, the IRS confirmed the existence of this good cause requirement in two issuances: IRS Revenue Ruling 2004 -82, § C (available at www.irs.gov/irb/2004-35_IRB/ar09.html); and IRS Revenue Procedure 2005 -37 (available at www.irs.gov/irb/2005-28_IRB/ar14.html).249 Court decisions relying on IRS Revenue Ruling 2004 -82, § C, in holding that a good cause requirement exists for any nonrenewal of a LIHTC lease, include:

In Passmore, a HCVP tenant relied solely on due process as the basis for a good cause requirement for nonrenewal of the lease.
