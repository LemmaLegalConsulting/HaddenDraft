Schwartz v. McAtee, 22 Ohio St.3d 14, 488 N.E.2d 479 (1986) A park operator may terminate a tenancy under R.C. 3733.13 only if: (1) the resident fails to fulfill an obligation imposed by R.C. 3733.101 that materially affects health and safety, (2) the park operator gives the resident a notice of noncompliance in accordance with R.C. 3733.13, and (3) the resident fails to remedy the noncompliance by the date specified in the notice, which shall not be less than 30 days from the date of service of the notice.

Thompson v. KMV II, Ltd., No. 2001-P-0125, 2003 WL 943871 (Ohio App., 11th Dist.,
Mar. 7, 2003) A park operator may evict a resident for one material violation of a park rule, if the park operator serves the resident with an R.C. 3733.11 notice and the resident fails to remedy the violation within the required 30-day time period.

Moonlight Mobile Home Parks, Inc. v. Eichner, No. L-93-028, 1993 WL 434584 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Oct. 29, 1993) The resident’s use of aluminum skirting instead of vinyl skirting violated a park rule but was not a material violation. Therefore, it was not grounds to evict under R.C.

M.L.R. Props., Inc. v. Baer, No. L-85-330, 1986 WL 6703 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 13, 1986) R.C. 3733.13 applies when a park operator seeks to evict a resident for violation of the park rules. R.C. 3733.13 provides that, when a resident violates R.C. 3733.101 and the violation materially affects health and safety, the pa rk operator may serve the resident with an R.C. 3733.13 notice of termination. If the resident fails to remedy the condition specified in the notice within the time specified in the notice, the rental agreement will be terminated. The R.C. 3733.13 notice must state the date on which the rental agreement will be terminated.

Leichtman v. Fike, No. 1106, 1984 WL 7338 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Apr. 27, 1984) If a resident cures the breach of the park rule within the relevant 30 -day period, the resident cannot be evicted for the rule violation.

Cont’l Inv. P’ship v. Hukill, No. 06-CV-131 (Ohio Mun., Delaware, Mar. 8, 2006) For a termination of a rental agreement for an alleged material violation of park rules, R.C. 3733.11 requires a notice of termination that in cludes: a description of the violation(s); a statement of the specific termination date, if the resident does not timely cure; and a statement that, if a second material violation of park rules occurs within six months, the rental agreement will terminate immediately. This means that R.C.

3733.11 requires two notices, each with a description of the violation(s). The park operator served three notices and only one contained a description of the violation(s).

None of the notices specified a termination dat e. Their statements that the rental agreement “shall terminate within 30 days after receipt by you of this notice” does not meet the specified date requirement. Based on these violations, court held it lacked jurisdiction over the eviction action and granted judgment for the resident.

Stites Enters., Inc. v. Dixon, No. 04-CVG-1659 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, Jan. 4, 2005) Before any termination of a tenancy for park rule violations, R.C. 1923.02(A)(1) and 3733.13 require a 30 -day notice and opportunity to cu re. A park operator may not circumvent this “jurisdictional requirement ... by claiming a lease violation and proceeding under O.R.C. 1923.02(A)(10), by merely incorporating the rules and regulations by reference into the lease.” Buckeye Lake Estates Mobi le Home Park v. Abel , No. 01 -CVG-01777, 2002 WL 32001271 (Ohio Mun., Licking Cty., 2002) The park rule prohibited additional residents for more than 14 consecutive days, without management approval. The resident violated this rule when she permitted her daughter and granddaughter to reside in her home for more than 14 consecutive days.

The violation constituted a de minimis violation (not a material violation) of the park
rules. Therefore, it was not a basis for terminating the tenancy.

Summit Mobile Home Park v. Brode, No. R97-CVG-1046 (Ohio Mun., Portage Cty., June 27, 1997), aff’d on other grounds, No. 97 -P-0080, 1998 WL 34002598 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Oct. 16, 1998) The court entered an eviction judgment in favor of the resident because the resident’s violation of the park rule, regarding the style of the required utility shed, was neither willful nor committed in bad faith.

United Mobile Home v. Scruggs, No. 93-CVG-3-8 (Ohio Mun., Athens Cty., Apr. 16, 1993) The park operator served the resident with an R.C. 3733.13 notice of termination but failed to provide the resident with the requisite 30 days to remedy the alleged
violations. The park operator served the resident with an R.C. 1923.04 notice to vacate before the 30 -day period had expired. As a result, the court entered an eviction judgment in favor of the resident.

Highland Estates Mobile Home Park v. Miller, No. 92-CVG-1054, 1993 WL 837917 (Ohio Mun., Findlay, Jan. 22, 1993) The resident’s failure to install new skirting violated a park rule,but the violation was neither willful nor committed in bad faith. Therefore, it was not grounds to evict under R.C. 3733.13. The resident’s failure to remove or conceal a hitch violated a park rule, but the violation was not grounds to evict under R.C. 3733.13 because the park operator had not been enforcing the rule uniformly against other residents.

Martin v. Woods, No. 92-CVG-714 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, Sept. 10, 1992) The purported notice of termination did not terminate the tenancy because it failed to describe the resident’s violations and it failed to include other provisions that R.C.

3733.13 requires.

Props. Ltd. v. Paquette, No. M8908-CVG-29204 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Sept. 8, 1989) A park operator must comply strictly with the R.C. 3733.13 notice requirement. This standard was not met where: (1) the R.C. 3733.13 notice contained the phrase “the two material violations were unreasonable” instead of the phrase “that the park rule is unreasonable” and (2) the notice did not contain a refe rence to any provision of a statute, code, or rule that the resident allegedly violated.

Peterson v. Woods, No. 08028 (Ohio County Ct., Adams Cty., June 1, 1988) The court dismissed the eviction complaint because the park operator sought to evict the resident for violating a park rule without serving the resident with an R.C. 3733.13 notice.
