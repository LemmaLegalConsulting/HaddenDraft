# b. Time Period for Perfecting the Appeal

## Source text

The time period for perfecting the appeal is thirty days from the date of the PHA’s final decision. R.C. 2505.07 (incorporated by R.C. 2506.01). See Jacobs v. Cuyahoga
Metro. Hous. Auth., No. 102248, 2015 WL 3647561, ¶ 16 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 11, 2015).

The thirty-day period begins to run on the datethat the PHA mails the final, written decision to the HCVP participant (or the HCVP participant’s lawyer).406 Cornacchione
v. Akron Bd. of Zoning Appeals , 118 Ohio App.3d 388, 692 N.E.2d 1083 (9th Dist.

1997); New Haven Missionary Baptist Church v. City of Norwood, No. C -961046, 1997 WL 602867 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Aug. 22, 1997); Am. Aggregates Corp. v.

Clayton Twp., Ohio Appeals Bd., No. 16311, 1997 WL 282334 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., May 30, 1997).

If the PHA uses certified mail for this service, the thirty-day period does not begin to run until the date of the participant’s receipt of the decision. Sturdivant v. Toledo
Bd. of Educ., 157 Ohio App.3d 401, 811 N.E.2d 581 (6th Dist. 2004).

The PHA must actually receive the notice of appeal within this thirty -day period.

Welsh Dev. Co. v. Warren County Reg’l Planning Comm’n, 128 Ohio St.3d 471, 946 N.E.2d 215 (2011); Dudukovich v. Lorain Metro. Hous. Auth., 58 Ohio St.2d 202, 389 N.E.2d 1112 (1979).

Therefore, an appeal is not perfected timely when: (1) a HCVP participant, before the expiration of the thirty-day period, files the notice of appeal only with the clerk of court; and (2) the PHA receivesthe clerk of court’s service of the notice of appeal after the expiration of the thirty-day period. Henson v. Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth., No.

104274, 2016 WL 7296572 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 15, 2016).
