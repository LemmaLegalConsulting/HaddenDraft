# 9. Violence Against Women Act

## Source text

The federal Violence Against Women Act of 20 13, Pub. L. 113 -4, 127 Stat. 54, together with the federal Violence Against Women Reauthorization Act of 2005, Pub.
L. 109-162, 119 Stat. 2960 (collectively, “VAWA”), provide certain rights to assisted housing tenants who are victims of domestic violence, dating violence, or stalking. 24 C.F.R. §§ 5.2001-5.2011. See also 24 C.F.R. §§ 880.504(f), 880.607(c)(5), 882.407, 882.511(g), 882.514(c), 883.605, 884.216(c), 884.223(f), 886.128, 886.132, 886.328, 886.329(f) (Section 8 project -based subsidized housing); 24 C.F.R. §§ 891.575(f), 891.610(c), 891.630 (Sections 202 and 811 PRAC); 24 C. F.R. §§ 960.103(d), 960.200(b)(8), 960.203(c)(4), 966.4(a)(1)(vi), 966.4(e)(9) (Public Housing); 24 C.F.R.

§§ 982.53(e), 982.201(a), 982.202(d), 982.307(b)(4), 982.310(h)(4), 982.315(a)(2), 982.315(b)(4), 982.353(b), 982.452(b)(1), 982.551(e), 982.551(l), 982.552(c)(2)(v) (Housing Choice Voucher program).

With respect to assisted housing eviction actions, VAWA provides tenants with two general protections. The first is that an incident (or incidents) of actual or threatened domestic violence, dating violence, or stalking is not a serious or repeated lease violation by the victim and is not good cause to terminate the tenancy of the
victim. 24 C.F.R. § 5.2005(c). The second is that criminal activity directly related to domestic violence, dating violence, or stalking, engaged in by a member of a tenant’s household or any guest or other persons under the tenant’s control, shall not be cause for termination of the tenancy of the victim, if the victim is the tenant or an immediate family member of the tenant. 24 C.F.R. § 5.2005(b)(2).

In short, “[if] a tenant violates the terms of the lease and that violation directly resulted from domestic violence, the tenant cannot be evicted from their housing.” Norwich Apts. II v. Ingram , No. L-19-1239, 2020 WL 3032860, ¶ 9 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 5, 2020).

Two Ohio court decisions that rely on VAWA in entering the eviction judgment for the tenant are: Norwich Apts. II v. Ingram , No. L -19-1239, 2020 WL 3032860 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 5, 2020); New Longwood Assocs. v. Wells, No. 09-CVG- 12569 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Sept. 16, 2009).
