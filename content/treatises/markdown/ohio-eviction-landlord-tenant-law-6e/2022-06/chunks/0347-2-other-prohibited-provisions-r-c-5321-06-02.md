## Source text

Chesser v. Chesser, No. 01-CA-00009, 2001 WL 403162 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Apr. 12, 2001) The lease provision established a five-year term, but permitted either party to terminate the lease at any time by providing the other party with an advance one -month notice. The one -month notice provision did not conflict with R.C. Chapter 5321 and, therefore, was enforceable.

DeBenedictus v. Gialamas , No. 96 -L-006, 1997 WL 51453 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Jan. 24, 1997) The lease provision permitted the tenant (or landlord) to provide the other party with 20 days (or more) advance written notice to terminate their month-to-month tenancy. The 20 -day notice provision was inconsistent with R.C. 5321.17(B), and, therefore, was unenforceable.

Cary v. Galbraith, No. L-94-002, 1994 WL 476146 (Ohio App., 6th Dist.,
Sept. 2, 1994) The lease provision required the tenant (or landlord) to provide the other party with 30 days advance written notice to terminate their month -to- month tenancy, regardless of the periodic rental date. The lease provision’s disregard of the periodic rental date was inconsistent with R.C.

5321.17(B) and, therefore, was unenforceable under R.C. 5321.06.

Kenko Corp. v. Singer, No. 81-03-0023, 1982 WL 3141 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., May 26, 1982) The lease provision required the tenant to provide the landlord with 60 days advance notice to terminate their month-to-month tenancy. The lease provision did not violate R.C. 5321.17(B) and, therefore, did not violate R.C. 5321.13.

Attorney’s Fees:

KGM Capital, LLC v. Jackson , No. C-130438, 2014 WL 2566286 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., June 6, 2014) The lease provision provided that the tenant would be liable for the landlord’s attorney’s fees that it incurred due to the tenant’s breach of the
lease. The lease provision violated R.C. 5321.13(C) and, therefore, was unenforceable.

Quinlan v. Lienesch, No. C-120716, 2013 WL 2456347 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., June 5, 2013) The lease provision provided for the award of attorney’s fees to the prevailing party, in the event of a legal dispute. The lease provision violated R.C. 5321.13(C) and, therefore, was unenforceable.

GMS Mgmt. Co. v. Ostrow , No. 88 -L-13-138, 1989 WL 140157 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Nov. 17, 1989) (dicta) The lease provision provided that, if the landlord relet the premises as a result of the tenant’s lease default, the tenant was liable for the reasonable attorney’s fees that the landlord incurred by reason of the default. The 383 lease provision violated R.C. 5321.13(C) and, therefore, was unenforceable.

Storage of Tenant’s Property:

Haddox v. Moreland , No. 95 -CA-20, 1996 WL 451361 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Aug. 5, 1996) The lease provision permitted the landlord to remove and store the tenant’s property, if the tenant abandoned the premises. The lease provision did not violate R.C. Chapter 5321 and, therefore, was enforceable.
