# 1. First and Fourteenth Amendments

## Source text

The constraints of the First and/or Fourteenth Amendments of the U.S.

Constitution apply to Public Housing and to certain project -based subsidized housing
developments. See National Housing Law Project, HUD Housing Programs: Tenants’ Rights ¶ 14.9.2.2.2 (5th ed. 2018). The determination as to whether the First and/or Fourteenth Amendments apply to any particular project -based subsidized housing development will depend on whether there is sufficient government action. Id.

The First Amendment and the Fourteenth Amendment, when applicable, prohibit a landlord from evicting a tenant in retaliation for tenant association or tenant organizing activity. Davis v. Vill. Park II Realty, 578 F.2d 461 (2d Cir.1978); Herring
v. Chicago Hous. Auth., No. 90-C-3797, 1993 WL 489767 (N.D. Ill. Apr. 22, 1993);

Walton v. Darby Town Houses, Inc. , 395 F. Supp. 553 (E.D. Pa. 1975); McQueen v.

Druker, 317 F. Supp. 1122 (D. Mass. 1970),aff’d on other grounds, 438 F.2d 781 (1st
Cir. 1971); Belvoir Cliffs Apts. v. Bembry, 56 Ohio Misc. 37, 383 N.E.2d 1170 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, 1978). See generally Thorpe v. Hous. Auth. of City of Durham, 393 U.S. 268 (1969). Cf. Carriage Hill Tenants’ Union v. Wallick Props., Inc. , No. CI- 827-44 (Ohio Mun., Athens Cty., Sept. 29, 1983) (landlord enjoined from interfering with the publication, distribution, or delivery of the tenant organization’s newsletter).

One benefit of this retaliation defense is that it applies to circumstances to whi ch the R.C. 5321.02 retaliation defense (see Section II.T, above) does not apply. For example, it applies to an eviction action commenced in retaliation for tenant association activity even if the tenant is in default in rent payments.E.g., Belvoir Cliffs
Apts. v. Bembry, 56 Ohio Misc. 37, 383 N.E.2d 1170 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, 1978).
