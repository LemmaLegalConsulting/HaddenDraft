# 3. Proximate Cause

- IX. Tenant Claims for Defective

## Source text

“[I]n addition to negligence per se, proximate cause for the injuries sustained must be established.” Shroades v. Rental Homes, Inc. , 68 Ohio St.2d 20, 25, 427 N.E.2d 774 (1981).

“‘Proximate causation’ is described as ‘some reasonable connection between the act or omission of the defendant and the damage the plaintiff has suffered.’” Stevens
v. Aaron Rental Props., Inc., No. 2009-CA-131, 2010 WL 3639339, ¶ 24 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Sept. 15, 2010). The act or omission need not be the initial or sole cause of the injury. Id.

“‘The proximate cause of an event is that which in a natural and continuous sequence, unbroken by any new, independent cause, produces that event and without which that event would not have occurred.’” Sabolik v. HGG Lake Ltd. P’ship, 180 Ohio App.3d 576, 583, 906 N.E.2d 488 (8th Dist. 2009) (quoting Atkins v. Indus.

Comm’n, 143 Ohio St. 113, 117, 53 N.E.2d 1018 (1944)).

“There may be more than one proximate c ause of an injury. When the negligent act or failure to act continues and combines to produce the injury, both causes may be proximate to the injury.” Stevens, 2010 WL 3639339, ¶ 24 (citations omitted).

“The issue of proximate cause is one of fact.”Mounts v. Ravotti, No. 07-MA-182, 2008 WL 4415819, ¶ 38 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., Sept. 26, 2008). Accord Scott v. Kirby,
No. L-05-1287, 2006 WL 1047480, ¶ 33 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Apr. 21, 2006).

Appellate court decisions finding a genuine issue of fact on pro ximate cause and, 439 therefore, reversing a summary judgment for the landlord include:
(1) Stevens v. Aaron Rental Props., Inc. , No. 2009 -CA-131, 2010 WL 3639339 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Sept. 15, 2010) (tenant fell on stairway without handrail);
(2) Lyle v. PK Mgmt., LLC, No. 5 -09-38, 2010 WL 1948360 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., May 17, 2010) (tenant fell in hallway);
(3) Morgan v. Mamone , No. 87612, 2006 WL 3804527 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 21, 2006) (tenant fell on ice patch in common area);
(4) Scott v. Kirby, No. L-05-1287, 2006 WL 1047480 (Ohio App., 6th Dist.,
Apr. 21, 2006) (guest fell on porch steps, without handrail).

Appellate court decisions finding that the tenant failed to establish the required proximate cause include:
(1) Bailey v. Ward, No. 8-16-03, 2016 WL 5873893 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist.,
Oct. 3, 2016) (insufficient evidence – mostly speculation and conjecture
– to support gutter run-off theory as cause for snow and ice on stepsand thereupon tenant’s fall);
(2) Arnett v. Mong, 65 N.E.3d 72, 2016-Ohio-2893 (Ohio App., 12th Dist.,
2016) (evidence failed to establishwhether the guest slipped on a natural or unnatural accumulation of snow or ice);
(3) Dardy v. Thompson, No. 2013-G-3157, 2014 WL 2883278 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., June 23, 2014) (failed to establish that any violation of R.C.

5321.04(A) was the proximate cause of the fire because the fire inspectors were unable to determine the cause of the fire);
(4) Sabolik v. HGG Lake Ltd. P’ship , 180 Ohio App.3d 576, 906 N.E.2d 488 (8th Dist. 2009) (tenant scalded by hot water in bathtu b due to tenant’s actions– tenant accidentally turned the hot water to its highest setting, when he tried to turn the hot water off with his foot, while having a seizure);
(5) Bogner v. Titleist Club, LLC, No. WD-06-039, 2006 WL 3825228 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Dec. 29, 2006) (evidence did not establish a causal connection between the medical symptoms and the alleged exposure to mold);
(6) Mahmoud v. Dennis , No. L -04-1183, 2005 WL 1661966 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 30, 2005) (insufficient evidence – only speculation and conjecture – as to the cause of the guest’s fall on the steps);
(7) Smalley v. Pauly , No. L -04-1106, 2004 WL 3200933 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Dec. 17, 2004) (no evidence that the lack of a handrail caused tenant’s fall on the steps);
(8) Turner v. Tiemeyer, No. CA95 -08-053, 1996 WL 56040 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Feb. 12, 1996) (no evidence that the tenant’s fall was due to the defective condition of the steps);
(9) Lilly v. Yee , No. 94 -APE06-830, 1995 WL 127886 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 23, 1995) (no evidence that the bursting of the pipe was due to any failure to maintain the steam heating system);
(10) Brady v. Koehnke , No. C -930240, 1994 WL 481758 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Sept. 7, 1994) (no evidence that the tenant’s fall from the front porch was due to code violations regarding the steps and handrail);
(11) Juresa v. Radan , No. 64951, 1994 WL 173654 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 5, 1994) (the tenant’s fall down the steps was due to the tenant’s negligence, not the lack of a handrail);
(12) Riley v. FDMA Props. , No. 92 -CA-22, 1992 WL 316356 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Oct. 7, 1992) (no evidence that the tenant’s fall down the steps was due to code violations regarding the bottom step);
(13) Harmon v. Schroeder , No. 4 -85-17, 1986 WL 14411 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Dec. 17, 1986) (no e vidence that the tenant’s physical injuries were due to a gas leak that the landlord repaired on the date that it was discovered).
