# 2. Municipal Court

## Table-of-contents context

## Source text

Municipal courts (exclusive of housing and environmental divisions) have original jurisdiction in civil actions in which the amount claimedby any party, or the appraised value of the personal property sought to be recovered, does not exceed fifteen thousand 559 dollars.361 R.C. 1901.17.

The monetary jurisdictional limit is exclusive of costs. R.C. 1901.17. Therefore, it does not apply to attorney’s fees under R.C. Chapter 5321, since they are taxed as costs (see Section XIX.C, above).

When a municipal court is enforcing the equitable rights of a party to a contract, the value of the contract may exceed the fifteen-thousand-dollar limit. Behrle v. Beam, 6 Ohio St.3d 41, 451 N.E.2d 237 (1983). For example, a municipal court has jurisdiction to order specific performance of a lease or land contract whose value exceeds fifteen thousand dollars, so long as none of the parties asserts a monetary claim for more than fifteen thousand dollars. Id.

Likewise, a municipal court has jurisdiction to hear an eviction action that concerns a land installment contract, even though the value of the property exceeds fifteen thousand dollars. Shaw v. Kadey, No. CA-7048, 1981 WL 2839 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., June 29, 1981).
