# 2. Method of Service

## Source text

If the method by which the federal notice of termination is served fails to meet any of the requirements of the applicable federal regulations, it is a defense to the eviction
action. The method of service must strictly comply with all of the requirements of the applicable federal regulations. See Section III.B, above.

Ohio state court decisions finding a method of service invalid are set forth below, along with the relevant regulation and the method of service.
(1) 24 C.F.R. § 966.4(k)(1) (Public Housing): the notice of termination was served upon a child of eight to ten years of age.Cuyahoga Metro. Hous.
Auth. v. Brown, No. 43566, 1981 WL 4963 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 21, 1981).
(2) 24 C.F.R. § 247.4(b) (project-based subsidized housing): the notice of termination was served by personal service at the dwelling unit, but not also by mail service. Fairborn Apts. v. Herman , No. 90-CA-28, 1991 WL 10962 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Jan. 31, 1991); Biltmore Apts. v.

Birdsong, No. 11-CVG-5315 (Ohio Mun., Dayton, Oct . 5, 2011). But see Marshall Plaza v. Watkins, No. 4161, 1987 WL 12113 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., June 3, 1987) (personal service but no mail service; tenant does not deny receiving the notice; because absence of mail service did not prejudice the tenant, app ellate court refused to reverse eviction judgment despite the violation of the regulation); M.L. Gabriel Commons v. King, No. 13159, 1992 WL 288781 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist.,
Oct. 14, 1992) (follows Watkins).

191 The Ohio case law is split on whether the date of service of the federal notice of termination must precede the date of service of the R.C. 1923.04 notice to vacate. For a discussion on that case law, see Section I.C.3.e, above.
