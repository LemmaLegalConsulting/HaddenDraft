Absent the filing of the required transcript or affidavit, the court will accept as true the magistrate’s factual findings.Stewart v. Hickory Hills Apts., 52 N.E.3d 259, 2015- Ohio-5046, ¶¶ 11,12 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., 2015); Pappenhagen v. Payne , 48 Ohio App.3d 176, 178, 549 N.E.2d 208 (8th Dist. 1988).76 When ruling on objections under Civil Rule 53(D), “the [trial] court shall undertake an independent review as to the objected matters ....” Ohio R. Civ. P.

53(D)(4)(d). “[T]he trial court must conduct an independent review of the facts and conclusions contained in the magistrate’s report and enter its own judgment.” Liggett
v. Shitaker Props., No. 23425, 2010 WL 1410758, ¶ 6 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Apr. 9, 2010). Accord Aban v. Schaeffer, 185 Ohio App.3d 250, 923 N.E.2d 1168, ¶ 24 (5th
Dist. 2009). “Civ.R. 53 obligates the trial court to make its own determination, without deference to the the magistrate ....” Aban, 185 Ohio App.3d at 255. “Thus, the trial court’s standard of review of a magistrate’s decision is de novo.” Liggett v. Shitaker Props., No. 23425, 2010 WL 1410758, ¶ 6 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Apr. 9, 2010).
