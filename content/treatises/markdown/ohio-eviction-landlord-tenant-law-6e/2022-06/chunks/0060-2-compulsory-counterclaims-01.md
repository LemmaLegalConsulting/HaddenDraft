## Source text

If the eviction complaint alleges only a claim for possession, Civil Rule 13(A) (compulsory counterclaim rule) does not apply to the eviction action. Soliel Tans, L.L.C. v. Timber Bentley Coe, L.L.C., No. 108125, 2019 WL 6358476 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Nov. 27, 2019); Forney v. Climbing Higher Enters., Inc., 158 Ohio App.3d 338, 815 N.E.2d 722 (9th Dist. 2004); Carter v. Russo Realtors, No. 99AP-585, 2000 WL 249137 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 7, 2000); Haney v. Roberts , 130 Ohio App.3d 293, 720 N.E.2d 101 (4th Dist. 1998);Chef Italiano Corp. v. Crucible Dev. Corp., No.

CV-2003-12-7277, 2004 WL 5491217 (Ohio C.P., Summit Cty., Oct. 28, 2004).

If the eviction complaint alleges both a claim for possession and one or more non- eviction claims, Civil Rule 13(A) applies to the non -eviction claims. Soliel Tans, L.L.C. v. Timber Bentley Coe, L.L.C., No. 108125, 2019 WL 6358476 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Nov. 27, 2019);Adams v. Romine, 130 N.E.3d 1050, 2019-Ohio-482 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., 2019); Forney v. Climbing Higher Enters., Inc., 158 Ohio App.3d 338, 815 N.E.2d 722 (9th Dist. 2004); Sherman v. Pearson, 110 Ohio App.3d 70, 673 N.E.2d 643 (1st Dist. 1996); Lable & Co. v. Flowers, 104 Ohio App.3d 227, 661 N.E.2d 782 (9th Dist. 1995); McCord v. Costy, No. CA87-05-036, 1987 WL 27190 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Dec. 7, 1987); Salyer v. Chapman, No. 1771, 1986 WL 11594 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Oct. 21, 198 6); Maduka v. Parries , 14 Ohio App.3d 191, 470 N.E.2d 464 (8th Dist. 1984); Chef Italiano Corp. v. Crucible Dev. Corp. , No. CV-2003-12-7277, 2004 WL 5491217 (Ohio C.P., Summit Cty., Oct. 28, 2004).

In these cases, Civil Rule 13(A) may apply to the non -eviction claim even if the non-eviction claim is dismissed under Civil Rule 41(A)(1)(a), provided the dismissal occurs after the time period for filing an answer and counterclaims.Soliel Tans, L.L.C.
v. Timber Bentley Coe, L.L.C., No. 108125, 2019 WL 6358476 (Ohio App., 8th Dist.,
Nov. 27, 2019).

Absent evidence that the landlord’s monetary claim reached a judgment, Civil Rule 13(A) does not bar a tenant’s subsequent action against the landlord for money
damages. Douglas v. Lewis, No. C-180201, 2019 WL 1474214, ¶ 11 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Apr. 3, 2019).

In these eviction actions, the determination on whether a counterclaim is a compulsory counterclaim is based on the “logical relations test” set forth in Rettig
Enters. v. Koehler, 68 Ohio St.3d 274, 626 N.E.2d 99 (1994). Soliel Tans, L.L.C. v.

Timber Bentley Coe, L.L.C. , No. 108125, 2019 WL 6358476 (Ohio App., 8th Dist.,
Nov. 27, 2019); McAlpine v. Patrick , No. 86453, 2006 WL 562191 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 9, 2006); Forney v. Climbing Higher Enters., Inc., 158 Ohio App.3d 338, 815 N.E.2d 722 (9th Dist. 2004); Sherman v. Pearson , 110 Ohio App.3d 70, 673 N.E.2d 643 (1st Dist. 1996).

When the claim (potential counterclaim) arises from the landlord -tenant relationship, it meets the Rettig logical relations test. Id.

Whether the counterclaim is a contract claim or a tort claim is irrelevant to this
determination. Sherman v. Pearson, 110 Ohio App.3d 70, 673 N.E.2d 643 (1st Dist.

1996). Likewise, whether the counterclaim is a claim for personal injury is irrelevant to this determination. Id.

The case law illustrates the range of claims that may be compulsory counterclaims in these eviction actions.

A compulsory counterclaim may be an R.C. 5321.02, 5321.04, 5321.15, invasion of privacy, trespass, or emotional distress claim. McCord v. Costy, No. CA87-05-036, 1987 WL 27190 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Dec. 7, 1987); Maduka v. Parries, 14 Ohio App.3d 191, 470 N.E.2d 464 (8th Dist. 1984). Cf. Salyer v. Chapman, No. 1771, 1986 55 WL 11594 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Oct. 21, 1986) (commercial tenant’s monetary claim for landlord’s failure to repair roof and heating/cooling system was compulsory counterclaim to landlord’s eviction and monetary claims for nonpayment of rent).

A compulsory counterclaim may be an R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se claim. 39 Adams v. Romine , 130 N.E.3d 1050, 2019 -Ohio-482 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., 2019);

Sherman v. Pearson, 110 Ohio App.3d 70, 673 N.E.2d 643 (1st Dist. 1996); Derrera
v. Hamlin, No. 02-CV-4851, 2003 WL 25500203 (Ohio C.P., Montgomery Cty., July 11, 2003).

A compulsory counterclaim may be a race discrimination claim. Lable & Co. v.

Flowers, 104 Ohio App.3d 227, 661 N.E.2d 782 (9th Dist. 1995). Cf. Turner v.

Crawford Square Apts. III, L.P., 449 F.3d 542 (3d Cir. 2006) (Pennsylvania res judicata law barred the FHA claim in federal court).

But a tenant’s personal injury claim sustained in a storeroom that is not a common area for tenants is not a compulsory counterclaim. Kerr v. Lakewood Shore Towers, Inc., No. 93462, 2010 WL 323424 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 28, 2010). It is a common law negligence claim based on a breach of a common law duty that does not arise under the landlord-tenant relationship. Id.
