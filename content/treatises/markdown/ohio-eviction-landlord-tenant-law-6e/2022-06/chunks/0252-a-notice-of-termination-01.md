# a. Notice of Termination

## Source text

Every Ohio LIHTC project lease, in an OHFA mandated Lease Addendum, establishes a notice of termination requirement. The OHFA Lease Addendum became effective July 1, 2019.

The notice of termination requirement under the OHFA Lease Addendum is as follows: “Prior to terminating or refusing to renew the Lease for a Minor Violation, …the Owner must first deliver written notice to the Tenant supplying sufficient details of the violation to adequately inform Tenant of its scope and provide a reasonable period of time, but in no case less than thirty (30) days from the date notice is delivered, for the Tenant to cure the violation.” OHFA Lease Addendum,¶ 2.a.

For purposes of the OHFA Lease Addendum, a Minor Violation includes: “those violations not related to health, safety, criminal activity, actions by the Tenant creating a hostile environment for other tenants of the Owner, inflicting damage to the Premises or property of Owner, or Program eligibility issues caused by Tenant’s misrepresentation or failure to provide required information.” Id.

The federal LIHTC statute and regulations do not establish a notice of termination requirement for an owner’s nonrenewal (or termination) of a low-income unit tenancy.

When the development funds for a LIHTC project include HOME funds ( see 42 U.S.C. §§ 12741-56; 24 C.F.R. part 92), in order to t erminate or refuse to renew the tenancy, the owner must serve the tenant with a written notice of termination. 42 U.S.C. § 12755(b); 24 C.F.R. § 92.253(c). The owner must serve the notice at least thirty days before the termination or nonrenewal. Id. The notice must specify the 281 grounds for the termination or nonrenewal. Id.

Whether due process requires a notice of termination for the owner’s nonrenewal (or termination) of the tenancy will depend primarily on whether the requisite government action exists.248 See Bowling Green Manor Ltd. P’ship v. Kirk, No. WD- 94-125, 1995 WL 386476 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 30, 1995);Bowling Green Manor
Ltd. P’ship v. LaChance, No. WD -94117, 1995 WL 386496 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 30, 1995). In most cases, the requisite government action will not exist.

Kirk and LaChance concluded that the requisite government action existed at a LIHTC property, but only with respect to a HCVP tenancy, and the continuing validity of this conclusion is unclear. At the time of these decisions, HUD regulations required good cause for an owner not to renew a HCVP tenancy. See former 24 C.F.R.

§§ 882.215(c) and 887.213 (1995). Both decisions relied on this good cause requirement in concluding that the requisite government action exis ted. This HCVP good cause requirement no longer exists. See Section III.A, above.

If a due process notice of termination requirement exists, it will likely be the same as the notice required in the other assisted housing programs. See Bowling Green Manor Ltd. P’ship v. Kirk, No. WD-94-125, 1995 WL 386476 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 30, 1995);Bowling Green Manor Ltd. P’ship v. LaChance, No. WD-94-117, 1995 WL 386496 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 30, 1995). This means it will likely require an advance notice that proposes to terminate the tenancy and informs the tenant of the reason for the proposed termination of tenancy. Id. See Section III.A, above.

For nonpayment of rent cases, another potential LIHTC notice of termination requirement is the thirty-day notice of termination that was established in the CARES Act, Pub. L. 116 -136, 134 Stat. 281, enacted Mar. 27, 2020, at § 4024(c) (“CARES Act provision”). The CARES Act provision applies to the LIHTC program in the same manner as it applies to the assisted housing programs. For a discussion on the terms and status of the CARES Act provision, see note 183, above.
