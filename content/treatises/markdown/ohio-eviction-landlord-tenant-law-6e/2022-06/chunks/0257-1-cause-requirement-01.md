The grounds on which a park operator may evict a mh park owner are limited to those set forth in R.C. 1923.02(A)(1),(2),(10),(11),(12). See Schwartz v. McAtee, 22 Ohio St.3d 14, 488 N.E.2d 479 (1986). 251 This establishes a cause requirement for such evictions.

R.C. 1923.02(A)(1) permits a park operator to evict a mh park owner for holding over the term of the rental agreement. R.C. Chapter 4781 (former R.C. Chapter 3733) establishes the standard for determining whether a mh park owner is a holdover tenant.

R.C. 1923.02(A)(2) permits a park operator to evict a mh park owner for nonpayment of rent, if the owner is leasing the lot under an oral tenancy.

R.C. 1923.02(A)(10) permits a park operator to evict a mh park owner for nonpayment of rent or for breach of the terms of the rental agreement. Schwartz v.

McAtee, 22 Ohio St.3d 14, 20, 488 N.E.2d 479 (1986).

R.C. 1923.02(A)(11) permits a park operator to evi ct a mh park owner who has committed two material violations of the mh park rules (or certain health and safety

1997); W. Ridge Green Co. v. Ortiz, No. 94CA-005819, 1994 WL 432162 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Aug. 10, 1994).

R.C. 1923.02(A)(12) permits a park operator to evict a mh park owner for a combination of nonpayment of rent and an unoccupied home (without notice to the park operator) for thirty consecutive days. See Section I.O.4, above.
