4.1 and 4.6 – Non-Eviction Claim The conflicts between R.C. 1923.06 and Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6 (see Section I.G.3.b, above) create an additional issue for complaints that containan FED claim and a monetary claim. The issue is whether Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6 apply to the monetary claim, irrespective of whether they apply to the FED claim. If Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6 apply to these monetary claims, the R.C. 1923.06 service of proc ess rules are invalid insofar as they are in conflict with these Civil Rules and apply to these monetary
claims. See Hiatt v. S. Health Facilities, Inc., 68 Ohio St.3d 236, 626 N.E.2d 71 (1994).

Civil Rule 1(C) is the sole authority for exempting a claim from the service of process requirements of Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6. See Ohio R. Civ. P. 1(A) and 1(C).

The Civil Rule 1(C) exemption applies to “forcible entry and detainer” claims.

For a four-fold reason, the Civil Rule 1(C) term “forcible entry anddetainer” does not encompass the monetary claim, when a landlord elects to join in a complaint an 49 FED claim and a monetary claim for unpaid rent and/or damage to the premises. First, the term “forcible entry and detainer” is an unambiguous and well -established term under Ohio law. See Section I.A.2, above. Second, its plain meaning under Ohio law is an FED claim under R.C. Chapter 1923, which is a claim for, and only for, obtaining restitution of possession of real property, based on a party’s present right to possession of the property. See Section I.A.2, above. Third, this plain meaning does not include the landlord’s monetary claim, which is a breach of contract claim for money damages (see Dennis v. Morgan , 89 Ohio St.3d 417, 419, 732 N.E.2d 391 (20 00)) and/or a statutory claim under R.C. 5321.05(C) for money damages ( see Sherwin v. Cabana Club Apts., 70 Ohio App.2d 11, 18, 433 N.E.2d 932 (8th Dist. 1980)). Fourth, when a term in a civil rule (or a statute) is unambiguous, courts are required to apply its plain meaning, without any further assistance of the rules of construction. State ex rel.

Dunbar v. Ham, 45 Ohio St.2d 112, 114, 341 N.E.2d 594 (1976) (civil rule); Cheap Escape Co., Inc. v. Haddox, L.L.C. , 120 Ohio St.3d 493, 900 N.E.2d 601, ¶ 9 ( 2008) (statute).

When a landlord elects to join in a complaint an FED claim and a monetary claim for unpaid rent and/or damage to the premises, the claims are joined under Civil Rule 18(A). See Section I.E.2.f, above.

When a plaintiff elects under Civil Rule 18(A) to join in a complaint two separate claims, each claim must be treated independently and ruled upon in accordance with the laws governing that claim. See Carter v. Bernard , No. 06 -MA-54, 2006 WL 3849855, ¶¶ 55,56 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., Dec. 28, 2006) (fraud and breach of contract actions, each seeking money damages and rescission); Raskow v. Fortner, No. 18399, 1998 WL 178570, at *2-3 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Apr. 15, 1998) (action for damages for flooding and action for injunction to remove dam to prevent future flooding); Peed v.

Moore, No. 857, 1981 WL 5165, at *3 -4 (Ohio App., 12 th Dist., July 22, 1981) (multiple causes of action, seeking various forms of relief).See also 4 Moore’s Federal Practice, § 18.23 (Matthew Bender 3d ed. 2012) (thejudgment on each claim “must be given according to the respective rights and liabilities of the parties”).36 Accordingly, the court’s jurisdiction with respect to each claim joined under Civil Rule 18(A) must be determined independently. Sommer v. Mount Carmel Health, No.

94-APE07-1087, 1995 WL 141354, at *4 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 28, 1995) (court 36 Ohio “Rule 18 is the same as Federal Rule 18.” Ohio R. Civ. P. 18, Staff Notes (1970).

had subject-matter jurisdiction in the action for an injunction, but not in the action for money damages). If a court lacks jurisdiction to hear one of t wo claims joined under Civil Rule 18(A), the court must dismiss the claim for which it lacks jurisdiction, notwithstanding the court’s jurisdiction to hear the other claim. Id.

Therefore, when a landlord elects under Civil Rule 18(A) to join in a complaint the FED claim and the monetary claim, the FED claim and the monetary claim remain independent claims. Ohio R. Civ. P. 18(A) (“may join, as independent or alternative claims”). See, e.g., Tolle v. Morris, No. 90AP-040034, 1990 WL 163807, at *1 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Oct. 18, 1990) (“A claim for back rent or damages may be joined with a complaint in forcible entry and detainer....Appellee’s second cause of action for back rent and damages is separate from the forcible entry and detainer action ....”). The FED claim is still a claim for “the right to possession and nothing else.” Seventh Urban,
Inc. v. Univ. Circle Prop. Dev., Inc. , 67 Ohio St.2d 19, 25 n.11, 423 N.E.2d 1070 (1981). The monetary claim is still a breach of contract claim for money damages and/or a statutory claim under R.C. 5321.05(C) for money damages.

In short, the monetary claim does not become an FED claim simply because it has been alleged in a complaint that also alleges an FED claim. The monetary claim, alone, is not a claim or proceeding within the purview of Civil Rule 1(C), and its joinder with the FED claim does not alter this conclusion. In these circumstances, the “forcible entry and detainer is a summary proceeding and [the] suit for damages is a regular civil proceeding...” Smith v. Wright, 65 Ohio App.2d 101, 103, 416 N.E.2d 655 (8th Dist.

1979).

Because the monetary claim is “a regular civil proceeding” to which Civil Rule 1(C) does not apply, perforce, Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6 (and all other provisions of the Ohio Rules of Civil Procedure) apply to the monetary claim.

Nonetheless, the R.C. 1923.06 case law is split on whether Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6 apply to the monetary claim.

Court decisions (and court rules) holding that Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6 apply to these monetary claims include: Pisani v. Wright , No. 2005 -CVG-1233 (Ohio Mun., Marietta, Apr. 7, 2008); Amherst Vill. Mgmt. v. Vestal , No. 99 -CVG-00936 (Ohio Mun., Bowling Green, Sept. 29, 1999) (magistrate’s decision) (“Service in the action by posting and regular mail ... would be imperfect for the second cause of action [monetary claim] as clearly violative of the Civil Rules”), objection overruled, (Ohio Mun., Bowling Green, Oct. 19, 1999), aff’d on other grounds, No. WD-99-075, 2000 WL 1595719 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Oct. 27, 2000); Mount Vernon Municipal Court, 51 Local Rules of Practice, Rule 304 (eff. May 6, 2013); Licking County Municipal Court, Notice (2012) (if eviction a nd monetary claims, “[p]osting of the summons does not meet the legal requirement for seeking monetary damages”); Memorandum from David C. Stockdale, Administrative/Presiding Judge Elect, Hamilton County Municipal Court, to Magistrates and Chief Deputy Clerk Deters (Sept. 27, 1999) (Joint Session consensus: R.C. 1923.06 provisions that allow “service of process of the second and third causes of action for back rent and other damages in forcible entry and detainer actions in a manner other than as prescribe d in Civil Rules 4 through 4.6 ...

are unconstitutional”).
