# b. Burden of Proof

## Source text

The tenant bears the burd en of proving the existence and then termination of the rental agreement, the amount of the security deposit, and the amount of the security deposit not returned. Schultz v. Wurdlow, No. 2008-CVI-031737 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Dec. 30, 2010), aff’d onother grounds, No. 11AP-62, 2012 WL 2859914 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., July 12, 2012).

“The burden falls upon the landlord to establish the lawfulness of any deductions from the security deposit.” Zeallear v. F & W Props. , No. 99AP -1215, 2000 WL 1015345, at *3 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., July 25, 2000). Accord Oldendick v. Crocker, 70 N.E.3d 1033, 2016 -Ohio-5621 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., 2016); Paxton v.

McGranahan, No. 49645, 1985 WL 6444 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Oct. 31, 1985);Schultz
v. Wurdlow, No. 2008-CVI-031737 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Dec. 30, 2010).

Two court decisions conflict on whether the landlord (or tenant) bears the burden of proving noncompliance (or compliance) with the forwarding address requirement.

Compare Dowell v. Krebs, No. 54517, 1988 WL 112857, at *3 (Ohio App., 8th Dist.,
Oct. 20, 1988) (landlord’s burden) with Schultz v. Wurdlow , No. 2008 -CVI-031737 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Dec. 30, 2010) (tenant’s burden),aff’d on other grounds,
No. 11AP-62, 2012 WL 2859914 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., July 12, 2012).
