Mihaly v. Akron Metro. Hous. Auth., No. CV-2005-11-6946 (Ohio C.P., Summit Cty.,
Dec. 14, 2006) PHA termination for drug -related criminal activity. Participant arrested for drug - related activity, when on the HCVP waiting list . She pled guilty to the drug -related criminal activity after she had been issued a voucher, but before commencement of her initial HCVP tenancy. Applying HUD regulations, court held that, at the time of her arrest and conviction, she was not a HCVP participant and, therefore, the PHA cannot terminate her participation in the HCVP for those activities.

Rodriguez v. Lorain Metro. Hous. Auth., No. 05-CV-140942 (Ohio C.P., Lorain Cty.,
Oct. 23, 2006) PHA termination for violent criminal activity that occur red in front of participant’s rental unit. Issue whether perpetrator of the crime was a member of participant’s
household. PHA’s evidence: two newspaper articles; a police report; and a court docket in an old criminal proceeding. Participant’s evidence: family’s direct testimony and perpetrator’s parole release address. Court found the newspaper articles unreliable hearsay, the police report established only the site of the crime, and the court docket not probative. Court held the PHA decision was unr easonable and not supported by reliable, substantial and probative evidence.

Blackmond v. Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. , No. CV-541895 (Ohio C.P., Cuyahoga Cty., Oct. 21, 2004) PHA termination for serious lease violation. PHA terminated the HAP contract for landlord’s HQS violations and thereafter landlord secured eviction judgment against
participant. PHA relied solely on the eviction judgment, which did not contain any factual findings. Court held the eviction judgment alone did not establish a serious lease violation and, therefore, the PHA decision was capricious, arbitrary, and unreasonable.

Chambers v. Stark Metro. Hous. Auth. , No. 1997 -CV-02490 (Ohio C.P., Stark Cty.,
Aug. 3, 1998) PHA termination for violent criminal activity. Court held PHA’s evidence of violent criminal activity – arrest for which grand jury issued “no-bill” – was insufficient.

Robertson v. Mansfield Metro. Hous. Auth., No. 87-426-C (Ohio C.P., Richland Cty.,
Mar. 28, 1989) PHA termination for failing to report a change in household composition. Court found family reported the change in household composition at its next annual recertification, and PHA failed to present any evidence that the PHA required or requested an earlier reporting of it.

Tapley v. Akron Metro. Hous. Auth., No. CV88-4-1215 (Ohio C.P., Summit Cty., Sept.
