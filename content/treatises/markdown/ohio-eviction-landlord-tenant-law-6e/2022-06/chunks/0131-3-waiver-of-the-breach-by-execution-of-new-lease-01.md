A waiver of the right to terminate a tenancy for nonpayment of rent may occur as a result of the landlord’s execution of a new lease with the tenant. E.g., Dayton Inv.

Group-7 v. Lathern , No. 15866, 1996 WL 741822 (Ohi o App., 2nd Dist., Dec. 13, 1996); Green v. Sephus , No. 90 -CVG-1768-38 (Ohio Mun., East Cleveland, Jan. 8, 1991). See also Nyack Plaza v. Parker, 18 Misc.3d 126(A), 2007 WL 4355300 (N.Y.
Sup. App. Term 2007); Joseph v. Beaumont Hous. Auth. , 99 S.W.3d 765 (Tex. App.

2003); Montgomery Gateway East I v. Herrera , 261 N.J. Super. 235, 618 A.2d 865 (1992).

Likewise, a waiver of the right to terminate a tenancy for a breach other than nonpayment of rent may occur as a result of the landlord’s execution of a new lease 106 In Carmony, the court held that the significant delay between the breach of the lease and the service of the notice of termination did not constitute a waiver of the breach because the lease stated that a delay in exercising a right under the lease did not waive the right. Lucas Metro.
Hous. Auth. v. Carmony , No. L-00-1296, 2001 WL 672150, at *2 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 15, 2001). It then held that the landlord did not waive the breach even though, during this delay, the landlord accepted future rent with knowledge of the breach. Id.

When the waiver of the breach is premised on the landlord’s execution of a new lease, the right to possession is based on the new lease. See Stepping Stones Assoc. v.

Seymour, 48 A.D.3d 581, 583-84, 853 N.Y.S.2d 562 (2008).

In these circumstances, the landlord may prevent a waiver of the breach by inserting an express provision in the new lease that reserves a right to terminate for any breach of the superseded lease. Millennia Hous. Mgmt., Ltd. v. Williams, No. 101627, 2015 WL 1276464 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 19, 2015); Lorain Metro. Hous. Auth.
v. Manago, No. 96-CA-006628, 1998 WL 46743 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Jan. 21, 1998).

This waiver issue often arises in the context of a new or amended assistedhousing lease.110 When a new assisted housing lease is executed to comply with new federal requirements, and the new lease does not expressly reserve the right to terminate for a breach of the superseded lease, the new lease may waive any breach of the superseded
lease. Millennia Hous. Mgmt., Ltd. v. Williams, No. 101627, 2015 WL 1276464, ¶ 14 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 19, 2015). See also Gallatin Hous. Auth. v. Montesillo ,
No. M2001 -02260-COA-R3-CV, 2002 WL 1800968 (Tenn. App. Aug. 7, 2002);

Superior Hous. Auth. v. Foote , No. 90 -1121, 1990 WL 198173 (Wis. App. Oct. 9, 1990). Cf. Chillicothe Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Anderson, No. 1406, 1988 WL 69118, at *2,10 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., June 28, 1988) (waiver where landlord failed to prove that it executed the new lease solely to comply with a federal requirement).

When a new lease is executed to transfer an assisted housing tenant to another unit to permit renovation of the tenant’s prior unit, the landlord may expressly reserve in the new lease (and thus pres erve) the right to terminate the new lease for a breach of the superseded lease. Lorain Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Retiree Hous. Mgmt. Inc. v. Lynch , No. 12 -CVG-02402 (Ohio Mun., Miamisburg, July 30, 2013).

Assisted housing leases are often revised, by amendment or by execution of a new lease, solely to change the rent amount and/or the household composition. The revision, when effected by a lease amendment, does not waive the right to terminate the am ended lease for a pre -amendment breach of the lease. See Millennia Hous.
