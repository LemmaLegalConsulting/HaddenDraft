“[A]ny security deposit deduction must be itemized.” Nolan v. Sutton , 97 Ohio App.3d 616, 619, 647 N.E.2d 218 (1st Dist. 1994).

329 However, if the amount of the landlord’s R.C. 5321.05(C) damage claim is less than the amount of the tenant’s security deposit, and the tenant has forfeited the security deposit, the landlord has not suffered any “actual damages” under R.C. 5321.05(C) (see Section XVIII.E.4,
below) and, therefore, is not entitled to recover R.C. 5321.05(C) attorney’s fees (see Section XVIII.D, below).

495 The purpose of the itemization requirement “is to make landlords give reasons to tenants why their security deposit is being withheld so that tenants may decide whether to pursue an action for recovery of that security deposit.” McNeill v. Wasserbauer ,
No. 72846, 1998 WL 323592, at *3 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 18, 1998). Accord Senz v. Onstott , 71 Ohio Misc.2d 67, 69, 654 N.E.2d 460 (Ohio Mun., Wadsw orth, 1994). See also Vardeman v. Llewellyn, 17 Ohio St.3d 24, 28, 476 N.E.2d 1038 (1985).

However, “there is nothing in the R.C. Chapter 5321 that states that the landlord upon the request of the former tenant must let the tenant re-enter the premises to verify the damages claimed by the landlord.” Scott v. Rice, No. 9-92-48, 1993 WL 46480, at *3 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Feb. 11, 1993).

Three court decisions, which are discussed below, illustrate the accuracy and specificity required for the itemization.

One court held that, in preparing an R.C. 5321.16(B) itemization of the cost to repair the premises, a landlord may rely on estimates from local advertisements, provided such advertisements are used in good faith. Senz v. Onstott, 71 Ohio Misc.2d 67, 69, 654 N.E.2d 460 (Ohio Mun., Wadsworth, 1994).

A second court held that the statement “$40 -cleaning” does not meet the itemization requirement under R.C. 5321.16(B) because it fails to specify the reason for the cleaning charge. Nolan v. Sutton, 97 Ohio App.3d 616, 619, 647 N.E.2d 218 (1st Dist. 1994).

A third court held that the required specificity was not met by a notice that stated:

2-Times Mowing Yard $ 40.00 Wal Mart 112.59 Rug Shampooer 29.66 Cleaning Lady 75.00 257.25 Schaedler v. Shinkle, No. CA99-09-025, 2000 WL 1283776, at *1 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Sept. 11, 2000). This notice was deficient because it “did not explain what he [landlord] had bought or why the items were necessary.” Id.

A landlord’s failure to comply with th e R.C. 5321.16(B) written itemization requirement does not preclude the landlord from bringing an action against the tenant under R.C. 5321.05. See Section XVII.C.8, below.

Likewise, it is irrelevant to the wrongful withholding determination under R.C.

5321.16(C). See Section XVI.H.2, below.
