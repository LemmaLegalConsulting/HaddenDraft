Subsidized Housing For subsidized rental housing as defined in R.C. 3767.41(A)(7), the standards and procedures for using the R.C. 3767.41 remedy vary from those used for other residential rental properties.

The R.C. 3767.41(A)(7) definition includes: Section 8 project -based assisted housing,312 RHS project -based assisted housing, Section 202 PRAC, S ection 811 PRAC, and Rent Supplement programs. It excludes: Public Housing, Section 236 (only), Section 221(d)(3) BMIR (only), Low Income Housing Tax Credit, and Housing Choice Voucher programs.

Assisted housing tenants who reside in these excluded rental units still may use the R.C. 3767.41 provisions that govern non -subsidized rental housing (see Section IX.G.1, above). Cent. Residents Council v. Lucas Metro Hous. Auth., No. L-09-1137, 2010 WL 497340 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Feb. 12, 2010) (Public Housing).

For subsidized rental housing, R.C. 3767.41(A)(2)(b) defines public nuisance. The definition is based on HUD’s physical condition standards for the project -based subsidized housing programs, 24 C.F.R. § 5.703. R.C. 3767.41(B)(2)(c) expressly requires that the definition be construed consistent with HUD and judicial interpretations of 24 C.F.R. § 5.703. R.C. 3767.41(B)(2)(c) also creates a 12 -month safe harbor for properties that receive a HUD REAC score of 75 or higher and do not 312 For the definition of “project-based assisted housing,” see note 165, above.

455 thereafter deteriorate so as to create a health or safety threat to the tenants.

For subsidized rental housing, R.C. 3767.41(B)(1)(b) establishes the notice
requirements. The notice must specify the nuisance condition and state that, if the condition is not remedied within 60 days, an R.C. 3767.41 claim may be brought. The tenant must make reasonable efforts to serve the landlord or landlord’s agent with the notice, in accordance with the Civil Rules, at the management office or at the place that tenants normally pay or send rent. If the landlord is not the owner of record, the tenant must also make reasonable efforts to serve the owner with the notice. If the owner does not receive service of the notice, the tenant must file a certification of the tenant’s attempts to serve the owner.

For subsidized rental housing, R.C. 3767.41(I)(2)(b) creates restrictions on any R.C. 3767.41 sale of the property. The sale must comply with applicable federal law and federal contracts. And the sale must be conditioned on the purchaser e xecuting a contract with HUD (or RHS) to continue the rental subsidy program, unless HUD (or
RHS) has terminated the subsidy contract.

An example of a court order appointing a receiver under R.C. 3767.41 to manage and repair subsidized housing is City of C incinnati v. PE Alms Realty LLC , No.

1500883 (Ohio C.P., Hamilton Cty., Feb. 1, 2018). The receivership order covers seven section 8 project-based properties. These properties contain a total of 660 units.
