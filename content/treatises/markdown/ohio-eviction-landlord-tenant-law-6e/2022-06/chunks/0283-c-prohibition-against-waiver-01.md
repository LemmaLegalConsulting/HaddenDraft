# c. Prohibition Against Waiver

R.C. 5321.13 and R.C. 5321.06 prohibit any agreement between a landlord and tenant that purports to waive any of the landlord’s R.C. 5321.04 obligations to maintain and provide services for the premises. Miller v. Ritchie, 45 Ohio St.3d 222, 225, 543 N.E.2d 1265 (1989). E.g., Anderson v. Ballard, No. L-10-1007, 2010 WL 3292685, ¶ 39 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Aug. 20, 2010) (“Pursuant to R.C. 5321.13(A), this obligation [to maintain the premises in a habitable condition under R.C.

5321.04(A)(2)] may not be modified or waived by agreement”). But see Marcum v.

Young, No. CA9004 -037, 1990 WL 160371 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Oct. 22, 1990) (holding that, by the terms of the lease, the tenant assumed the landlord’s R.C. 5321.04 duty to repair the water heater).

Also, a “tenant’s occupancy of defective rental premisesover a lengthy period of time does not constitute a waiver of the landlord’s duty to maintain the premises as required by R.C. 5321.04 ....” Miller, 45 Ohio St.3d at 222, syllabus no. 2.

However, a tenant may contract with a landlord to make certain repai rs to the premises in lieu of the tenant’s obligation to pay a certain portion of the rent. See Section II.R, above. Likewise, a tenant may contract with a landlord to make certain repairs to the premises in lieu of the tenant’s obligation to make certain cash payments for an option to purchase. In either case, the repair agreement would not relieve the landlord of any R.C. 5321.04 duty or liability. See Strayer v. Lindeman, 68 Ohio St.2d 32, 427 N.E.2d 781 (1981) (R.C. 5321.04 obligations are non -delegable). But, if the tenant fails to make the repairs properly and it results in a violation of R.C. 5321.04, the tenant would be liable for a breach of the repair agreement.

With respect to the option to purchase agreement, at least two limits exist for th e repair agreement. First, if the repair agreement does not reduce the cost of the option, the repair agreement would be without consideration and, therefore, invalid. Second, the total cost of the option – the sum of the reasonable value of the repair w ork plus the tenant’s remaining cash payment obligation – is subject to an unconscionability
prohibition. See Section XVI.B.6, below.
