# 7. Lead Safe Rental Housing

## Source text

Ohio’s state lead abatement law does not require inspections of rental units until after a child has been poisoned by lead. R.C. Chapter 3742; Ohio Admin . Code Chapter 3701-32.

Two Ohio municipalities have enacted ordinances that requirethe owners of rental units that were built before 1978 (“pre-1978 rental units”) to certify that their rental units are lead safe. Those ordinances (and municipalities) are Cleveland Codified Ordinances pt. III.C, tit. IX, ch. 365, enacted in 2019, and Toledo Municipal Code pt.

17, tit. 3, ch. 1760, enacted in 2020.

Both of these ordinances set forth a presumption thatpre-1978 rental units contain lead paint.

Under the Cleveland ordinance, implementation of its inspection and certification process is phased in by zip code, with full implementation by March 1, 2023. It requires all pre-1978 rental units to pass an inspection by a certified lead clearance inspector and the owner of those rental unit to then apply for a Lead Safe Certificate.

Under the Toledo ordinance, implementation of its inspection and certification process is phased in over five years. It requires the owners of pre-1978 rental units in properties that have four or fewer units, and the owners of family child-care homes that were built before 1978, to submit a Lead Safe Report and then a pply for a Lead Safe
Certificate. The Lead Saf e Certificate expires in five years. See Mack v. City of Toledo, 151 N.E.3d 151, 2019 -Ohio-5427 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., 2019) (upholds the four or fewer unit scope of the ordinance).
