# K. Continuance and Bond

## Source text

Courts have authority to grant continuances under their general supervisory power over their docket. “Independent of statute, as an incident to their authority to hear and determine cases, courts have power to grant continuances.” State ex rel. Buck v.

McCabe, 140 Ohio St. 535, 537, 45 N.E.2d 763 (1942). Accord Kidd v. Cincinnati Transit Co., 24 Ohio App.2d 101, 102, 265 N.E.2d 297 (1st Dist. 1970).

This authority to grant conti nuances of the trial date is generally applicable to an eviction action. See See v. Sabau , No. 62047, 1992 WL 30951, at *2 -4 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 20, 1992); Swartz v. Schutte, No. 14717, 1991 WL 2022, at *2 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Jan. 9, 1991); Bar Hotel Co. v. Lloyd MacKeown Buick Co., 104 Ohio
App. 69, 74, 146 N.E.2d 879 (3rd Dist. 1957). The only eviction actions in which the court may not continue the trial date are drug -related eviction actions that have been filed under R.C. 1923.02(A)(6)(a). See R.C. 1923.051(C).

In an eviction action, the decision whether to grant a continuance of the trial date is committed to the discretion of the trial court. Krantz v. Pahnke, No. 2021-CA-0043, 2022 WL 39486, ¶ 37 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Jan. 5, 2022); Oakwood Mgmt. Co. v.

Richards, No. 99AP -627, 1999 WL 1124692, at *2 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Dec. 9, 1999); Youngstown Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Barry , No. 94-CA-147, 1996 WL 734017, at *2 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., Dec. 16 , 1996); Cuyahoga River Assocs. Ltd. P’ship v.

MJK Corp., No. 68673, 1996 WL 17292, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 18, 1996);

Swartz v. Schutte, No. 14717, 1991 WL 2022, at *2 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Jan. 9, 1991).

Likewise, the decision on the length of any such continuance is generally committed to the discretion of the trial court. Bar Hotel Co. v. Lloyd MacKeown Buick Co., 104 Ohio App. 69, 146 N.E.2d 879 (3rd Dist. 1957).

However, R.C. 1923.08 establishes certain constraints on the court’s authority to grant a continuance in excess of eight days on an eviction claim, when the landlord or the tenant has requested the continuance.

When a landlord requests a continuance in excess of eight days on an eviction claim, R.C. 1923.08 prohibits the court fromgranting the continuance unless the tenant consents to it.

When a tenant requests and is granted a continuance in excess of eight days on an eviction claim, R.C. 1923.08 requires the tenant to post a bond.

The amount of the R.C. 1923.08 bond is the amountof rent that may accrue during the period of the continuance. State ex rel. Dooley v. Riley , No. 14447, 1994 WL 53838 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Feb. 24, 1994); Gem Mgmt. Co. v. Walker , No. 60139, 1991 WL 26635 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 28, 1991); Brown v. Fay Apts., 62 Ohio Misc.2d 188, 594 N.E.2d 190 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., 1991). E.g., Fair Acres MHC LLC v. Arrington, No. 2020 -CVG-0003R (Ohio Mun., Portage Cty., Jan. 28,
2020) (for two-week continuance, bond set at the rent amount prorated for two weeks).

See also Seventh Urban, Inc. v. Univ. Circle Prop. Dev., Inc. , 67 Ohio St.2d 19, 30, 423 N.E.2d 1070 (1981).

R.C. 1923.08 does not require the tenant to deposit with the court alleged past due
rent. Riley, 1994 WL 53838, at *1; Walker, 1991 WL 26635, at *1; Brown, 62 Ohio Misc.2d at 189.

This R.C. 1923.08 bond requirement is mandatory, not discretionary. Cuyahoga River Assocs. Ltd. P’ship v. MJK Corp., No. 68673, 1996 WL 17292, at *3 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 18, 1996); State ex rel. Dooley v. Riley, No. 14447, 1994 WL 53838, at *1 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Feb. 24, 1994); State ex rel. GMS Mgmt. Co., Inc. v.

Callahan, 65 Ohio App.3d 335, 338-39, 583 N.E.2d 1339 (11th Dist. 1989).

Because this bond requirement is mandatory, the court may not grant a tenant a continuance of indefinite duration on an eviction claim. Callahan, 65 Ohio App.3d at
339. “The exact duration is needed not only to determine whether the bond must be posted before the continuance is granted, but it is also needed to determinewhether the posted bond is sufficient to cover the possible loss the landlord could sustain.” Id.

The purpose of the bond is to insure that, “if the defendant [is] ... granted a continuance, the delay will not ... [be] monetarily prejudicial to the plaintiff.” Hilltop
Vill. v. West , No. L -88-120, 1989 WL 10277, at *5 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Feb. 10, 1989). Accord State ex rel. Dooley v. Riley, No. 14447, 1994 WL 53838, at *1 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Feb. 24, 1994); Brown v. Fay Apts., 62 Ohio Misc.2d 188, 1 89, 594 N.E.2d 190 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., 1991).

Accordingly, if the landlord prevails on the eviction claim, the rent deposited with the court under R.C. 1923.08 may be released to the landlord. West, 1989 WL 10277, at *5; Talbot v. Resler, 64 Ohio L aw Abs. 461, 462, 113 N.E.2d 21, 22 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., 1952).

When a tenant is required to post a bond under R.C. 1923.08 and fails to do so, the 67 tenant is not entitled to a continuance, irrespective of the tenant’s reason for requesting the continuance. Alex-Bell Oxford Ltd. P’ship v. Woods, No. 16038, 1998 WL 289028, at *4 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., June 5, 1998). However, such tenants are still entitled to a hearing on the eviction claim. Bester v. Owens, No. 17388, 1999 WL 179261, at *3 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Mar. 31, 1999).

When a continuance in excess of eight days is granted on an eviction claim, but it is not granted pursuant to a request by the tenant, R.C. 1923.08 does not require the tenant to post a bond.47 Ambrosia Coal & Constr. Co. v. C.B.G., Inc., No. 94-CA-199, 1996 WL 242915, at *3 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., May 9, 1996);Talbot v. Resler, 64 Ohio Law Abs. 461, 462, 113 N.E.2d 21 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., 1952).

For example, R.C. 1923.08 does not require the tenant to post a bond when the landlord has requested and the tenant has consented to the continuance. Talbot, 64 Ohio Law Abs. at 462. See also Bester v. Owens, No. 17388, 1999 WL 179261, at *3 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Mar. 31, 1999).

Likewise, R.C. 1923.08 does not require the tenant to post a bond when the court, on its own, continues the eviction hearing. Galilee Missionary Baptist Church v.

Bibby, No. 25746, 2011 WL 4059951, ¶ 16 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Sept. 14, 2011);

Ambrosia Coal & Constr. Co., 1996 WL 242915, at *3.
