## Source text

Webb v. C & J Props., LLC, No. CA2010-01-016, 2010 WL 3213020 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Aug. 16, 2010) “While there is a temporal proximity between the complaint and the termination, ‘such a circumstance does not create a presumption of retaliation under Ohio law.’ Without any other evidence regarding C & J’s termination of the rental agreement, Webb failed to show by a preponderance of the evidence that C & J retaliated against him.” 571 Euclid Beach Ltd. v. Brockett , No. 75047, 1999 WL 1129086 (Ohio App., 8th Dist.,
Dec. 9, 1999) The park operator violated R.C. 3733.09 when it terminated the water service to the home and thereby forced the resident to vacate the home.

Pierce v. Derickson, No. CA-3105, 1985 Ohio App LEXIS 8010 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 10, 1985) A court may awardattorney’s fees under R.C. 3733.09 only if the resident has suffered actual damages as a result of an R.C. 3733.09 violation.

Braff Mobile Home Park Co. v. McFarland , No. 8-265, 1982 WL 5815 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., July 23, 1982) The court upheld the denial of a retaliation defense. No direct evidence showed that:
(1) the resident complained to the park operator regarding the water problem, (2) the park operator knew that the resident had taken water samples to a governmental agency, or (3) the governmental agency found anything wrong with the water.

Voyager Vill., Ltd. v. Sudduth , No. 81 -CA-63, 1982 Ohio App. LEXIS 15638 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Apr. 15, 1982) The court upheld the denial of a retaliation defense. The alleged retaliation was based on the r esident’s complaint to the park operator regarding standing water. The complaint could not be the basis for a retaliation defense because the standing water was not a violation of R.C. 3733.10.

Troy v. Casale, No. 2976, 1982 WL 5894 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Mar. 31, 1982) The court upheld the denial of a retaliation defense. The resident’s complaints to the park operator involved a personal conflict between the resident and the park operator, and not any of the grounds set forth in R.C. 3733.09.

Country Club Vill. Mobile Home Park v. Gall, No. 958, 1980 WL 154220 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Oct. 20, 1980) The court upheld the award of retaliation damages to the resident. The park operator filed an eviction action in response to the resident’s complaints to the park operator, which concerned low water pressure and an inoperative outdoor light.
