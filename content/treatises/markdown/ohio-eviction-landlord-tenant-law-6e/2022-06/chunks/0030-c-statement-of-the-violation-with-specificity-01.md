# c. Statement of the Violation with Specificity

## Source text

“Inherent in the opportunity to cure a default is knowledge o f what is in default.

Absent knowledge of what must be corrected in order to avoid the specified penalty, the right to cure the default becomes a meaningless guessing game.” Gallagher v.

Borden, Inc. , 84 Ohio App.3d 185, 188, 616 N.E.2d 577 (10th Dist. 1992) (lease provision notice requirement similar to the R.C. 5321.11 notice).

The R.C. 5321.11 notice must describe with specificity the acts and omissions of the tenant that constitute the alleged R.C. 5321.05 violation. R.C. 5321.11.

Examples of statements that failed to state the tenant’s acts and omissions with the requisite specificity are set forth below.
(1) “[D]ue to your interference with the right of your neighbors to the peaceful enjoyment of their premises.” Hood v. Kusior, No. 97-CVG- 29 3823 (Ohio Mun., Akron, July 23, 1997).
(2) “[N]umerous violations of conditions of lease, specifically #30.” Avondale Hous. Action Program v. Butler , No. 88 -CV-42805 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., Mar. 23, 1989).
(3) The statement that the tenant performed acts that disturbed her neighbors and that the tenant created physical hazards. Burney View Apts. v.

Zeisler, No. 87-CV-17778 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., Feb. 3, 1988).
