The landlord’s right to terminate the tenancy or the rent subsidy in these circumstances is subject to at least three requirements.

First, the landlord may terminate the tenancy or the rent subsidy in these circumstances only if the tenant’s omission or misrepresentation of information is
fraudulent. HUD Handbook 4350.3 REV-1, ¶¶ 8-13(A), 8-17, and 8-18. To establish the requisite fraud in these cases, the landlord must prove each of the common law elements of fraud, including intent. See Powell v. D.C. Hous. Auth. , 818 A.2d 188 (D.C. App. 2003).

Unintentional omissions or errors do not constitute the requisite fraud. HUD Handbook 4350.3 REV-1, ¶¶ 8-13(A), 8-17, and 8-18. See also Boardwalk Glenville
Apts. v. Cage , No. 2007 -CVG-3065 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., July 13, 2007); Chancellor Manor v. Thibodeaux, 628 N.W.2d 193 (Minn. App. 2001).

Second, the landlord may terminate the tenancy or the rent subsidy in these circumstances only if the landlord has complied with the recertification notice requirements (relative to content and service). If the landlord fai ls to comply with those requirements, the termination of the rent subsidy and the rent increase to market rent is invalid and unenforceable. Hidden Meadows Townhomes v. Ross , No. C - 120045, 2012 WL 6674412 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Dec. 21, 2012);Independent Mgmt.
Servs. of Ohio, Inc. v. Davis, No. 2014-CVG-01244 (Ohio Mun., Elyria, July 18, 2014).

See also Somerset Homes v. Woodard , 2014 WL 2574037 (N.J. Super. , App. Div.,
2014) (eviction action); Lower E. Side I Assocs. LLC v. Estevez , 9 Misc.3d 632, 787 N.Y.S.2d 636 (Civ. Ct. 2004) (money claim for market rent).

When the market rent is invalid and unenforceable for these or other reasons, the landlord is then barred from evicting the tenant for nonpayment of the market rent.

Third, when a tenant does not report income because the tenant is unaware of its 235 existence, the tenant has not breached his recertification reporting requirements and is not liable for any rent increase based on the unknown income. Ezzard Charles Apts.
v. Collins , No. 12 -CV-22452 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., Dec. 5, 2013). In these circumstances, since any increase in the tenant’s rent based on the unknown income is invalid, the landlord may not evict the tenant for nonpayment of the increased rent.Id.
