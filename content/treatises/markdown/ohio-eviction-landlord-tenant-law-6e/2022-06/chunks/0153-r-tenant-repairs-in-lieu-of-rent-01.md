## Source text

Agreement In an eviction action for nonpayment of rent, if the rental agreement provides that the tenant is to make certain repairs to the premises in lieu of the obligation to pay certain rent (“repair agreement”), 133 the tenant’s repairs to the premises may be a defense to the eviction action. To prevail on this defense, a tenant must prove that the repair agreement e xists, that the tenant made the relevant repairs, and that the only unpaid rent is rent that has been forgiven by the repair agreement.

If the court finds that the amount of rent owed the landlord exceeds the amount of rent forgiven by the repair agreeme nt, this defense will be unsuccessful. E.g., Oakes
v. Buffalohead, No. 87 -CVG-01011 (Ohio Mun., Medina, Feb. 9, 1988); Hawkins v.

Collier, No. 87-CVG-884 (Ohio Mun., Portsmouth, Sept. 10, 1987).

When the repair agreement requires the tenant to repair R.C. 5321.04 violations, the repair agreement does not relieve the landlord of any R.C. 5321.04 duty or liability.134 A landlord’s R.C. 5321.04 duties may not be waived or modified. R.C.

5321.13. They are non-delegable. Strayer v. Lindeman, 68 Ohio St.2d 32, 427 N.E.2d 781 (1981). And any landlord liability under R.C. 5321.04 may not be waived or
modified. R.C. 5321.13.

133 As to whether this repair agreement may be an oral agreement, see Section XXII.A, below.

134 For a discussion on th e landlord’s R.C. 5321.04 duties, see Section VII.C, below. For a discussion on the landlord’s liability for a breach of the R.C. 5321.04 duties, see Chapter IX, below.

Nonetheless, the repair agreement is valid. While R.C. 5321.13 and Strayer bar such waiver, modification, or delegation, they do not bar a landlord from retaining a tenant (or another third party) to repair R.C. 5321.04 violations. “Nothing in Ohio’s Landlord Tenant Act prevents parties from agreeing to allow a tenant to perform services as a rent obligation in lieu of making financial payments.”Reid v. Plainsboro Partners, III, Nos. 09AP -442,456, 2010 WL 3610931, ¶ 36 (Ohio App., 10th Dist.,
Sept. 16, 2010). See generally Wade v. Smith , No. 3 -87-24, 1989 WL 36578 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Apr. 18, 1989).
