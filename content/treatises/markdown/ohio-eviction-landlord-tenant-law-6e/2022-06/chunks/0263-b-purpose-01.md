## Source text

R.C. Chapter 5321 is the legislature’s response to “the greater power traditionally held by the landlord.” Pool v. Insignia Residential Group, 136 Ohio App.3d 266, 270, 736 N.E.2d 507 (1st Dist. 1999). Accord Ricciardo v. Weber, No. CA-3452, 1989 WL 155719, at *5 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Dec. 22, 1989).

“[T]he Act was an attempt to balance the competing interests of landlords and tenants.” Shroades v. Rental Homes, Inc. , 68 Ohio St.2d 20, 25, 427 N.E.2d 774 (1981). Accord Mann v. Northgate Investors, L.L.C. , 138 Ohio St.3d 175, 5 N.E.3d 594, ¶ 10 (2014); Showe Mgmt. Corp. v. Mountjoy, 154 N.E.3d 141, 2020-Ohio-2772, ¶ 24 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., 2020); Cont’l Enters., Ltd. v. Franklin, No. 102899, 2016 WL 2941209, ¶ 30 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 19, 2016);Ricciardo v. Weber, No. CA- 3452, 1989 WL 155719, at *5 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Dec. 22, 1989); King v. Neufeld,
No. WD-85-14, 1986 WL 1296, at *4 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Jan. 31, 1986).

“The intent behind the Landlord -Tenant Act was to ensure equitable dealings between landlords and tenants.” Pool v. Insignia Residential Group, 136 Ohio App.3d 266, 270, 736 N.E.2d 507 (1st Dist. 1999).

To achieve this balance and equity, the legislature “intended ... to afford greater protection and remedies to tenants.” King v. Neufeld, No. WD-85-14, 1986 WL 1296, at *4 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Jan. 31, 1986).Accord Conner v. Conner, 114 N.E.3d 281, 2018-Ohio-2698, ¶ 10 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., June 20, 2018) ( “enacted R.C. Chapter 5321 … to clarify and broaden tenants’ common -law rights”); Anderson v. Ballard ,
No. L -10-1007, 2010 WL 3292685, ¶ 39 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Aug. 20, 2010) (“intended to provide tenants with greater rights than existed for their protection at common law”); Mullins v. Grosz, No. 10AP-23, 2010 WL 3244139, ¶ 23 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Aug. 17, 2010) (“attempt to clarify and broaden tenants’ rights”);Brady v.

Koehnke, No. C-930240, 1994 WL 481758, at *2 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Sept. 7, 1994) (“attempt to clarify and broaden tenants’ rights”);Ricciardo v. Weber, No. CA-3452, 1989 WL 155719, at *5 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Dec. 22, 1989) (“overall thrust of the act is to define and establish remedies for a tenant”);Howard v. Simon, 18 Ohio App.3d 14, 15-16, 480 N.E.2d 99 (8th Dist. 1984) (“intended to provide tenants with greater rights”).
