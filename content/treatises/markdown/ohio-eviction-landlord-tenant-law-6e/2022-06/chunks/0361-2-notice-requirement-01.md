# 2. Notice Requirement

## Source text

The R.C. 5321.04 contract claim includes a notice requirement. No court decision has clearly and fully addressed this issue. However, Miller v. Ritchie, 45 Ohio St.3d 222, 543 N.E.2d 1265 (1989), and lower court decisions seem to indicate that there is a notice requirement and it is the notice requirement that the Supreme Court initially established in Shroades v. Rental Homes, Inc. , 68 Ohio St.2d 20, 427 N.E.2d 774 (1981), and then clarified in Sikora v. Wenzel , 88 Ohio St.3d 493, 727 N.E.2d 1277 (2000), for the R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se claim. For a discussion on that notice requirement (“Shroades/Sikora notice”), see Section IX.B.2, below.

Miller refers to a requirement that the landlord be placed on notice of the defective
condition. Miller, 45 Ohio St.3d at 226. The notice requirement was met inMiller by the tenant’s oral requests to the landlord for repairs and by a written notice of code violations from a county building inspection department to the landlord.

One appellate court and one municipal court have held that, absent any notice to the landlord of the defective condition, the tenant may not bring an R.C. 5321.04 contract claim. Murray v. Bargo, No. CA86 -11-080, 1987 WL 15440 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Aug. 10, 1987); Crawley v. Brown , No. 2005 -CVG-16627 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Oct. 6, 2005).

Two appellate courts and one municipal court seem to hold that the notice requirement o f the R.C. 5321.04 contract claim does not require notice in writing.

Hofer v. Cooper, No. 1909, 1989 Ohio App. LEXIS 870 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Mar.

17, 1989); Doganiero v. Cox, No. 9-292, 1984 WL 6297 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Apr.

2, 1984); Lobo v. Hoelscher, 44 Ohio Misc. 46, 336 N.E.2d 475 (Ohio Mun., Dayton, 1975). But see Higgins v. Roe, No. 73-CA-3 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Apr. 2, 1979).

Application of the Shroades/Sikora notice to the R.C. 5321.04 contract claim is
reasonable. The Shroades/Sikora notice is essentially the same as the notice requirements of two analogous claims.

One is the claim for a breach of a lease covenant to make repairs. The notice requirement for this lease claim is notice from the tenant to the landlord of the need of repairs, unless the landlord has knowledge of the need. 65 Ohio Jur.3d Landlord and Tenant § 365 (2006). Accord Rittenberg v. Donohoe Constr. Co. Inc., 426 A.2d 338, 344 (D.C. App. 1981); J.C. Penney Co. v. Teddam Realty Corp. , 33 A.D.2d 1006, 1007, 307 N.Y.S.2d 750, 751 (1970); Stool v. J.C. Penney Co., 404 F.2d 562, 566 (5th
Cir. 1968).

The other is the claim for a breach of the implied warranty of habitability, which exists in other jurisdictions. The notice requirement for this implied warranty claim is notice from the tenant to the landlord of the defective condition, unless the landlord has knowledge of the defective condition. E.g., Detling v. Edelbrock, 671 S.W.2d 265
(Mo. 1984); Berman & Sons, Inc. v. Jefferson, 379 Mass. 196, 396 N.E.2d 981 (1979).
