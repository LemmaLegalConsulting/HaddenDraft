# 4. Landlord Fault Issues

## Source text

If a landlord is at fault for the defective condition, the landlord’s attempts to remedy the defective condition does not relieve the landlord of liability if the attempts are unsuccessful. Lawrence v. Triangle Capital Corp., 90 Ohio App.3d 105, 107, 628 N.E.2d 74 (2nd Dist. 1993) (landlord liable for roach infestation; “the fact that Triangle made an effort to eliminate the vermin from the Lawrence apartment did not make living conditions, such as they were, any more predictable”); Williams v. Wright, No.

2000-CVG-01040 (Ohio Mun., Marietta, Aug. 15, 2001) (landlord liable for roach infestation; “Plaintiff [landlord] attempted to correct the problem but his efforts were unsuccessful”).

However, there are circumstances where the premises are not in compliance with the R.C. 5321.04(A) standards, and it is not the result of any action or inaction by the
landlord. For example, there may be defective conditions at the rental unit solely because of a deficiency in the municipal water or sewer system.

The issue then is whether a landlord may be liable in an R.C. 5321.04 contract claim if the landlord is not at fault for the defective condition.

Two appellate districts have held that a landlord is not liable in these
circumstances. Conway v. Liddell, No. 79799, 2002 WL 398687 (Ohio App., 8th Dist.,
Mar. 14, 2002) (landlord not liable under R.C. 5321.04(A) for sewer back-up resulting solely from a defect in the public sewer system); Paul v. Semirale, No. 70438, 1996 WL 502143 (Ohio App., 8thDist., Sept. 5, 1996) (same);Stewart v. Kalman, No. 1109, 1982 WL 3509 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Aug. 13, 1982) (landlord not liable under R.C.

5321.04(A) for the inadequate water supply resulting solely from a defect in the municipal water system).

The implied warranty of habitability case law in other jurisdictions is split on this
issue. Compare Park W. Mgmt. Corp. v. Mitchell , 62 A.D. 291, 404 N.Y.S.2d 115
(1978) (fault irrelevant to liability), aff’d, 47 N.Y.2d 316, 418 N.Y.S.2d 310 (1979), and Berman & Sons, Inc. v. Jefferson , 379 Mass. 196, 396 N.E.2d 981 (1979) with George Washington Univ. v. Weintraub, 458 A.2d 43 (D.C. App. 1983) (fault relevant to liability).

Court decisions holding that fault is irrelevant to liability recognize that: (1) the claim is a contract claim, (2) consideration of fault is not incorporated into any analogous body of warranty law, and (3) the essential objective of the warranty is not to penalize the landlord for wrongdoing, but rather to make sure that the tenant receives the property and services for which the tenant is paying. Jefferson, 379 Mass. at 200- 02.
