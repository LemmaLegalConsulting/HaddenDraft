# c. Accord and Satisfaction

## Source text

When a landlord withholds a portion of the security deposit and provides the tenant with a check for the remainder of the deposit, and the tenant cashes the check, it does not necessarily create an accord and satisfaction. Tourville v. Terzuoli , No. 22802, 2009 WL 1636605 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., June 5, 2009).

In Tourville, the landlord “returned a check for the amount of the security deposit that he believed was due.” Id., ¶ 13. “[T]here was no evidence that the check was the product of a negotiation between the [landlord and tenant] regarding the amount of the security deposit that should be refunded.” Id. The court thereupon held that “there is no evidence that an accord was reached.” Id.
