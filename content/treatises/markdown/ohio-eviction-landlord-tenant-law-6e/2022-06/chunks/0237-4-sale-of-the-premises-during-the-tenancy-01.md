# 4. Sale of the Premises During the Tenancy

## Source text

Under Ohio law, a purchaser of real estate, which is in the actual possession of a tenant, acquires title subject to the tenant’s rights under the existing tenancy. See Section XXII.B, below.

This state law applies to a Housing Choice Voucher program (“HCVP”) lease.

Letter from Howard M. Schmeltzer, Special Assistant to the HUD Associate General Counsel for Litigation, to Edward G. Weil, Esq. (1985). See also EMC Mortgage
Corp. v. Smith, No. 95-04794, slip op. at 7 (Mass. Hous. Ct., Boston Div., Jan. 5, 1996) (“A new owner can seek to terminate the existing HAP contract and lease, but only in accordance with the procedures set forth in the federal statute and regulations”).

Therefore, the purchaser of a rental unit, which is occupied by a HCVP tenant, acquires the rental unit subject to the notice and good cause requirements for termination of the HCVP lease. Id. E.g., Twin Lawn Props., Ltd. v. Adams, No. CVG- 970506 (Ohio Mun., Fostoria, Mar. 16, 1998). The purchase of the rental unit, alone, is not good cause to terminate the HCVP lease. Id.

However, a termination of a HCVP lease in order to facilitate a subsequent sale of the rental unit is good cause to terminate the lease, provided the termination occurs after the first year of the tenancy. 24 C.F.R. § 982.310(d).
