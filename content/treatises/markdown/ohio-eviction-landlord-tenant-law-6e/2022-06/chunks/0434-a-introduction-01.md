## Source text

A tenant’s right tocontinued occupancy of the premises can be defined by: (1) the terms of a tenancy ( e.g., month-to-month tenancy), (2) the terms of a lease, or (3) a statutory requirement ( e.g., good cause requirement for nonrenewal of an assisted housing lease under federal statute and regulations, see Section III.A, above).

If a landlord actually or constructively evicts a tenant in violation of the tenant’s right to continued occupancy, the tenant may have a wrongful eviction claim under common law. Four of those commo n law claims are discussed in Sections XIV.B -E, below.
