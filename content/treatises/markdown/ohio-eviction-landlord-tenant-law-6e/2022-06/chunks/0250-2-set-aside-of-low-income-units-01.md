# 2. Set-Aside of Low-Income Units

## Source text

The owner of a LIHTC project is required to set aside a certain percentage of units for low-income tenants (“low-income units”). 26 U.S.C. § 42(g).

Before 2018, generally, two options existed for the set-aside requirement: (1) 20% (or more) of the units for tenants whose income is at or below 50% of area medium income or (2) 40% (or more) of the units for tenants whose income is at or below 60% of area medium income. Id. The LIHTC program uses the HUD definition of area median income.

For LIHTC properties developed since 2018, a third set -aside option has been a standard known as income averaging. 26 U.S.C. § 42(g). It permits applicants with incomes up to 80% of area medium in come, provided the average rent and income limit of the designated units does not exceed 60% of the area medium income. This income averaging standard permits a project to use rent/income levels for particular units at 20%, 30%, 40%, 50%, 60%, 70%, or 80% of area medium income.

These income limits apply only at the time of a tenant’s initial occupancy. Id. If the development is 100% low -income units, after initial occupancy, a tenant is not subject to any income limit. Id. If the development has both low -income units and market-rent units, after initial occupancy, if the income of a low -income unit tenant exceeds the initial income limit by more than 40%, the tenant is deemed to be a market- rent tenant and the next available unit at the development must be leased to a qualifying low income tenant. Id.
