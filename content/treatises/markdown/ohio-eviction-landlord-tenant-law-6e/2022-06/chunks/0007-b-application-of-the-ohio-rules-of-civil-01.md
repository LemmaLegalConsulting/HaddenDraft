The determination as to which provisions of the Civil Rules apply under the clearly inapplicable standard is made on a rule-by-rule basis. Robinson, 81 Ohio St.3d at 370;

Tower City Props., 49 Ohio St.3d at 69; Price, 70 Ohio St.2d at 132.

The focus of the courts in these cases has been whether the application of a procedural rule “would delay the ultimateresolution of an action.” T&R Props., Inc. v. Wimberly, 158 N.E.3d 137, 2020-Ohio-4279, ¶ 30 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., 2020).

Generally, the civil rules that govern the conduct of the trial apply to FED actions.

Id., ¶¶ 29-32. The reason is that, “while a forcible entry and detainer action is designed to provide an expedited outcome, nothing in the law provides for or suggests an abbreviated or summary trial.” Id., ¶ 29.

The second standard is that a Civil Rule, or a provision of a Civil Rule, applies to FED actions (and other special statutory proceedings) whenever the statute “provides for procedure by a general or specific reference to the statutes governing procedure in civil actions.” Ohio R. Civ. P. 1(C). For example, under this general or specific reference standard, Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6 apply to FED actions filed under R.C.

1923.02(A)(6)(a),(b), because R.C. 1923.051(A)(1) requires “the service and return of the summons in the action in accordance with the Rules of Civil Procedure.” Court decisions on the applicability of specific Civil Rules to FED actions are set forth below and in the sections referenced in the Civil Rule headings below.
