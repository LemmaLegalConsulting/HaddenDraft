## Source text

5321.14 Under R.C. 5321.14, any unconscionable provision in a residential lease is
unenforceable. See Section VII.K.1, below.

Therefore, a defense to an eviction action is that the lease provision on which the landlord relies to bring the action is unconscion able. E.g., Hi-Land Terrace, Inc. v.

Dinwiddie, No. 546, 1985 WL 6560 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Mar. 1, 1985) (lease provision requiring registration of and authorization for overnight guests); Fitzpatrick Realty v. Wilkey , No. 78 -CVG-8102 (Ohio Mun., Dayton , Nov. 17, 1978) (lease provision granting landlord right to declare forfeiture of lease for any tenant breach of the lease, however slight);Heritage Hills, Ltd. v. Smith, No. 78-CVG-268 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, May 8, 1978) (lease provision requiring registration of overnight guests).
