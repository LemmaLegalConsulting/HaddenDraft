Visit of family member MHC5, LLC v. Martin, No. 2011-CVG-0318 (Ohio Mun., Clermont Cty.,

Buckeye Lake Estates Mobile Home Park v. Abel , No. 01 -CVG-0177, 2002 WL 32001271 (Ohio Mun., Licking Cty., 2002) Resident permitted his daughter and granddaughter to remain in his manufactured home for more than 14 consecutive days, in violation of a park rule. The park operator had knowledge of this stay. Court held that the violation was not a material violation and, therefore, not grounds to evict.

Term of rental agreement Gitler v. Gerity, No. L-94-260, 1995 WL 458690 (Ohio App., 6th Dist.,

Reck v. Lucas , No. 2006 -CVG-00235, 2006 WL 4399761 (Ohio Mun., Auglaize Cty., Aug. 25, 2006) Because the park operator failed to offer the residents a written lease with a one-year term, “the Court finds that the defendants have been under a series of one year leases.” Ramm v. Shreves, No. 2005-CVG-0028, 2005 WL 5314591 (Ohio Mun., Athens Cty., May 4, 2005) Until the park operator offers a tenant a one-year lease in accordance with R.C. 3733.11, the tenancy remains a month -to-month tenancy, the park operator remains obligated to offer a one-year lease, and the required one- year lease period does not commence until the park operator offers a one- year lease in accordance with R.C. 3733.11.

Superior Mobile Homes, Inc. v. Russell, No. 7-89-CVG-336 (Ohio Mun., New Philadelphia, Jan. 12, 1990) If a park operator fails to offer an owner a written rental agreement with a term of at least one year, the owner’s rental agreement is deemed to be for 585 a term of one year.

Exterior alteration or addition to the home Troy Oaks Homes & Residential Club, Inc. v. Sokolowski, 78 N.E.3d 365, 2016-Ohio-8427 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., 2016) The park rule required prior approval of any alterations or changes to the exterior of the home. The purpose of the rule is to insure compliance with the park’s requirements. The court held that the rule is reasonable and that the residents violated the rule when they installed a metal roof.

Friendly Vill. v. Duty , 75 Ohio App.3d 555, 599 N.E.2d 878 (6th Dist.

1992) The park rule required prior approval of the park operator for exterior home additions, so as to insure that the additions met the park’s
requirements. R.C. 3733.11(C) does not require that park rules be related to matters of health and safety. Therefore, the park rule did not violate R.C. 3733.11(C).

Water and sewer service Rex Hill, Inc. v. Cherry, No. 2588, 1991 WL 95243 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., May 29, 1991) The park rule required residents to maintain lateral sewer lines. The park rule violated R.C. 3733.10(A)(4) and, therefore, was unreasonable, arbitrary, and capricious under R.C. 3733.11(C).

Utility fee Huntsberry v. Co untryside Assocs. , No. CA85 -08-088, 1986 WL 7105 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., June 23, 1986) A rental agreement provided for a utility fee of a specific amount. The park operator’s attempt to assess a surcharge on a 30-day notice to cover unexpectedly high uti lity costs was unlawful for two reasons. First, the surcharge was not an increase in an existing fee because it was not intended to be permanent in nature but rather was a one -time event.

Therefore, the surcharge was not an increase in an existing fee th at could be effected under R.C. 3733.11(B). Second, the surcharge violated R.C.

3733.11(C). It was unreasonable and arbitrary because it would result in major increases in utility charges upon just 45 days advance notice.
