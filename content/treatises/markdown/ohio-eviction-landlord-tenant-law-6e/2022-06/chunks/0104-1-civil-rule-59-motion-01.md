## Source text

Civil Rule 59 applies to eviction actions. Witkowski v. Arditi, 123 Ohio App.3d 26, 702 N.E.2d 1231(7th Dist. 1997) (ruling on time for filing the motion in an eviction action); Agler Green Coop. v. Rivers , No. 87AP -915, 1988 WL 37063 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 29, 1988);Mt. Olive-Metro Hous., Inc. v. Craft, No. 93-G-2858 (Ohio Mun., Cuyahoga Falls, Feb. 28, 1994); Greenfield Meadows v. Malone, No. M9111- CVG-040019 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Apr. 17, 1992).But see Summit Mgmt. Servs.
v. Gough , No. 19714, 2000 WL 1226605 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Aug. 30, 2000); 83 Zellner v. Turner , No. 92 -CA-41, 1993 W L 15640 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Jan. 26, 1993).84 In eviction actions, a Civil Rule 59 motion is often used to contest the court’s conclusions of law, similar to a Civil Rule 53(D)(3)(b) objection to a magistrate’s
decision. However, to preserve the right to appeal the trial court’s decision in an eviction action tried to a magistrate, a party must file a Civil Rule 53(D)(3)(b) objection regarding the relevant conclusion of law (or finding of fact). See Section I.N.3, above.
