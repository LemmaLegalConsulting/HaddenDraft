# H. Spouse, Not Named on Lease

## Table-of-contents context

- VII. Rental Agreement Provisions
  - H. Spouse, Not Named on Lease

## Source text

When a married couple resides in a rental unit, whether a spouse is named on the lease effects the spouse’s rights under the lease.

“When only one spouse is named on a lease, the other spouse has no rights under the lease.” Mlynek v. Mlynek, No. WD-07-056, 2008 WL 307449, ¶ 12 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Feb. 1, 2008). Accord Smith v. McCain, 66 Ohio Law Abs. 561, 118 N.E.2d 238 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., 1952).

A non-tenant-spouse resides in the rental unit solely as a member of the spouse’s
household. Mlynek v. Mlynek, No. WD-07-056, 2008 WL 307449, ¶ 13 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Feb. 1, 2008). Absent the consent of the tenant -spouse, the non -tenant- spouse does not have a right of possession. State v. O’Neal, No. C-980247, 1999 WL 162158, at *2 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Mar. 26, 1999).

If the right of possession of the tenant -spouse ends either voluntarily or involuntarily, the right of possession of the non -tenant-spouse also ends, since that right accrues only through the tenant -spouse. Smith v. McCain , 66 Ohio Law Abs.

561, 118 N.E.2d 238 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., 1952).

As to actions between spouses, R.C. 3103.04 states, in relevant part: “Neither can be excluded from the other’s dwelling, except upon decree or order of injunction made by a court of competent jurisdiction.” However, “R.C. 3103.04 is inapplicable to criminal cases.” State v. Lilly , 87 Ohio St.3d 97, 100, 717 N.E.2d 322 (1999).
