# 1. Tenant’s Right to Invite Guests

## Table-of-contents context

- VII. Rental Agreement Provisions
  - G. Guests

## Source text

Tenants have a right to invite guests to their rental unit, based on two principles of Ohio common law and, in some cases, an express provision in a lease.287 One common law basis for this right is the tenant’s common law possessory rights under the lease (or tenancy). Cooper v. Roose, 151 Ohio St. 316, 323, 85 N.E.2d 545 (1949). A lease (or tenancy) gives the tenant a right to possession and control of the
premises. Id. This possessory right includes “the right to admit people to the premises and exclude them from the premises.” Id.

A second common law basis for this right is the common law implied covenant of quiet enjoyment. See Section VII.C.9, above. “In Ohio, a covenant of quiet enjoyment is implied in every lease contract for realty.” Hamilton Brownfields Redevelopment, LLC v. Duro Tire & Wheel, 156 Ohio App.3d 525, 806 N.E.2d 1039, ¶ 23 (12th Dist.

2004). “The covenant is breached when the landlord obstructs, interferes with, or takes away from the tenant in a substantial degree the beneficial use of the leasehold.” Id.

The right of a tenant to invite guests is a beneficial use of the leasehold that this covenant protects. See State v. DeCoster, 653 A.2d 891, 894 (Me. 1995); Branish v.

NHP Prop. Mgmt., 694 A.2d 1106, 1107 (Pa. Super. 1997); L.D.L. v. State of Florida, 569 So.2d 1310, 1312-13 (Fla. App. 1990).

The common law right to invite guests includes the right to use the common areas for travel to and from the rental unit. State v. Hites, No. 1-2000-22, 2000 WL 1114809 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Aug. 8, 2000); City of Kent v. Hermann, No. 95-P-0042, 1996 WL 210780 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Mar. 8, 1996). The portions and facilities of the common area not required for this travel purpose may be excluded from the right to invite guests. Id.

287 For example, public housing leases expressly grant the tenant the right to provide reasonable accommodations to their guests. 24 C.F.R. § 966.4(d)(1).
