## Source text

In an eviction action for nonp ayment of rent, a landlord’s violation of the rental agreement, R.C. Chapter 5321, or R.C. Chapter 4781 (former R.C. Chapter 3733) may be the basis for a defense under R.C. 1923.061(B).

The R.C. 1923.061(B) defense is essentially a set -off procedure. The tenant’s counterclaim under the rental agreement, R.C. Chapter 5321, or R.C. Chapter 4781 (former R.C. Chapter 3733),119 and the tenant’s rent deposit under R.C. 1923.061(B),120 are set-off against the landlord’s rent claim.

When a tenant asserts the R.C. 1923.061(B) defense, the court issues a judgment on the landlord’s rent claim and on the tenant’s counterclaim and, based on those judgments, determines to whom a net judgment is owed. R.C. 1923.061(B). See also Sandefur Mgmt. Co. v. Smith, 21 Ohio App.3d 145, 486 N.E.2d 1234 (10th Dist. 1985).

The rent deposited with the court, if any, is used to satisfy any net judgment owed the landlord and the remainder, if any, is returned to the tenant. 121 Id. Finally, if no rent remains due after the netting of the judgments and the disbursement of the rent deposited with the court, the eviction judgment is entered in favor of the tenant. Id.

Therefore, when a tenant asserts an R.C. 1923.061(B) defense, the court must hear and issue a judgment on the landlord’s rent claim and the tenant’s counterclaim before it issues a judgment on the eviction claim. Studor v. Roark, No. 94-CA-38, 1995 WL 347963, at *2 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Mar. 21, 1995); Sandefur Mgmt. Co. v. Smith, 21 Ohio App. 3d 145, 149, 486 N.E.2d 1234 (10th Dist. 1985). Accord Schwab v.

Lattimore, 166 Ohio App.3d 12, 848 N.E.2d 912 (1st Dist. 2006) (dissent). See, e.g., Heritage Hills, Ltd. v. Bragg, No. 1379, 1987 WL 16079 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Aug.

27, 1987).
