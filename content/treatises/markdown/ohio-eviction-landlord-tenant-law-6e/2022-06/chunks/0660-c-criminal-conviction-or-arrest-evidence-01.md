# c. Criminal Conviction or Arrest Evidence

## Source text

“A record of a criminal conviction is only some evidence, not conclusive evidence, of the facts attempted to be proved in a subsequent civil proceeding.” Vancamp v.

Austintown Twp., No. 01-CA-17, 2002 WL 925197, ¶ 60 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., Mar.

19, 2002) (R.C. Chapter 2506 appeal). Accord McAlister v. Akron Metro. Hous. Auth.,
No. CV-2007-08-5813 (Ohio C.P., Summit Cty., Apr. 14, 2008) (R.C. Chapter 2506 appeal, HCVP).

“[A] guilty plea is not an admission in the technical sense of Civ.R. 36, but rather, is an admission in the sense of a confession or a statement against interest.”Vancamp, 2002 WL 925197, ¶ 64. Accord Swigart v. Swigart, 65 Ohio Law Abs. 582, 591, 115 N.E.2d 871 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., 1953); McAlister v. Akron Metro. Hous. Auth., No.

CV-2007-08-5813 (Ohio C.P., Summit Cty., Apr. 14, 2008) (R.C. Chapter 2506 appeal, HCVP).

“One of the primary reasons for allowing a party in a civil case to rebut the facts underlying a prior guilty plea is that ‘in many cases a plea of guilty may, in fact, amount to a compromise by the defendant to avoid an expensive or undesirable trial.’” Vancamp, 2002 WL 925197, ¶ 65. See also Costa v. Fall River Hous. Auth., 71 Mass.
App. Ct. 269, 283, 881 N.E.2d 800 (2008) (“a plea may result from extrinsic considerations ... by the accused (elimination of the risk of a more serious conviction and greater punishment)”). E.g., McAlister v. Akron Metro. Hous. Auth. , No. CV- 2007-08-5813 (Ohio C.P., Summit Cty., Apr. 14, 2008) (“Appellant asserts that she pled guilty to the charges in order to enter into the felony drug court program and that she understood that by entering into the Drug Court Program that the charges wo uld be dismissed if she successfully completed the program”).

A party in a civil case “‘may explain the prior conviction and may offer any evidence rebutting the fact sought to be proved by the opponent.’”Phillips v. Rayburn, 113 Ohio App.3d 374, 381, 68 0 N.E.2d 1279 (4th Dist. 1996) (quoting Hickman v.

Kraft, No. 84-CA-2, 1985 WL 11160 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Sept. 26, 1985)).

“Thus, the conviction may be admitted into evidence and accorded whatever weight the factfinder deems appropriate. It does not, however, preclude additional litigation involving the facts and legal issues underlying the conviction.” Id. at 381.

Also, an arrest, by itself, does not meet the PHA’s burden of proof in the termination of a family’s participation in the HCVP program. The PHA must prove the underlying allegations against the HCVP participant by a preponderance of the
evidence. See Section XXIV.C.6.a., above. “The standard for effecting a valid arrest is probable cause to believe that the person arrested has committeda criminal offense.” State v. Swonger , No. 09AP -1166, 2010 WL 4011201, ¶ 12 (Ohio App., 10th Dist.,
Oct. 14, 2010). “Probable cause is a lesser standard of proof than that required ... by a preponderance of the evidence.” State v. Smith, No. 09-CA-29, 2010 WL 3722257, ¶ 84 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Sept. 20, 2010). See also Nashua Hous. Auth. v. Wilson, 162 N.H. 358, 33 A.3d 1163 (2011) (Public Housing eviction).
