## Source text

Depositing and an Eviction Action When a tenant’s R.C. 5321.07 rent depositing is (or allegedly is) improper, two issues may arise in any related eviction action.

One issue arises when a tenant is rent depositing, the landlord files an eviction action for nonpayment of rent, and the landlord attempts to challenge the validity of the rent depositing in the eviction action. Cf. Maryland Apts. v. Crump, No. 96-CVG- 38691 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Jan. 14, 1997) (eviction judgment for tenant because “rent in escrow”).

The issue is whether R.C. 5321.09 is the exclusive remedy for the landlord to challenge the validity of the rent depositing. Nocourt decision has addressed this issue.

If R.C. 5321.09 is the exclusive remedy, a landlord cannot challenge the validity of the rent depositing in the eviction action unless three prerequisites are met. First, the landlord must allege an R.C. 5321.09 cl aim in the eviction complaint. Second, under R.C. 5321.09(B), the tenant must be given an opportunity to file an answer and 393 counterclaims to the R.C. 5321.09 claim as in other civil actions ( e.g., twenty-eight days to file the answer and counterclaims). Third, the trials on the eviction claim and the R.C. 5321.09 claim must be consolidated.

If these three prerequisites are not met and the landlord is thereby barred from challenging the validity of the rent depositing in the eviction action, the tenant’s r ent depositing should be a defense to the eviction action, regardless of whether the tenant complied with the R.C. 5321.07 requirements.

A second issue arises when a court releases the rent deposits to the landlord under R.C. 5321.09 and the landlord then files an eviction action against the tenant for nonpayment or late payment of the released rent.

The issue is whether the landlord may evict the tenant for nonpayment or late payment of the released rent. Court decisions are split on this issue. Compare Real
Props. Servs. Mgmt. v. Harigle, No. 3-96-21, 1997 WL 430773 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., July 30, 1997) (eviction not permitted; rent deposits released due to noncompliance with written notice requirement), and Real Props. Servs. Mgmt. v. Anatra , No. 3-96- 22, 1997 WL 430778 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., July 30, 1997)with Timbercreek Vill. Apts.
v. Myles, No. 17422, 1999 WL 335307 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., May 28, 1999) (eviction permitted; rent deposits released due to noncompliance with reasonable time requirement).
