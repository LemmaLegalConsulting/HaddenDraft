# D. Landlord

## Table-of-contents context

- VI. Ohio Landlord-Tenant Act,
  - D. Landlord

## Source text

A landlord is the owner, lessor or sublessor of residential premises, or any person that the owner, lessor or sublessor authorizes to manage the premises or to receive rent from a tenant under a rental agreement. R.C. 5321.01(B).

When an owner (or lessor or sublessor), for a fee , authorizes a person to manage the residential premises, the person is a real estate broker under R.C. 4735.01. As real estate brokers, such persons are permitted to manage the residential premises only if they are licensed real estate brokers. R.C. 4735.02.

When a vendee of a land installment contract (see Chapter XXIII, below) leases the property to a third-party, the vendor is not a landlord of the vendee’s tenant.Baraby
v. Swords, 166 Ohio App.3d 527, 851 N.E.2d 559 (3rd Dist. 2006). The vendorretains only bare legal title and reserves only those rights of entry and control as necessary to preserve the integrity of the building. Id.
