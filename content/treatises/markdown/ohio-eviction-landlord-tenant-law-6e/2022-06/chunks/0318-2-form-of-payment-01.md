# 2. Form of Payment

## Source text

The form of a rent paymen t is usually a monetary payment. Reid v. Plainsboro Partners, III, Nos. 09AP-442,456, 2010 WL 3610931 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Sept. 16, 2010).

But the form of a rent payment also may be an agreement to perform or pay for specified services. Reid v. Plain sboro Partners, III , Nos. 09AP -442,456, 2010 WL 3610931, ¶ 36 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Sept. 16, 2010). “Given its contractual basis, we see no reason to limit the term rent to financial payments. Nothing in Ohio’s Landlord Tenant Act prevents parties fro m agreeing to allow a tenant to perform services as a rent obligation in lieu of making financial payments.” Id. See also Lee
v. Wallace, 186 Ohio App.3d 18, 926 N.E.2d 328, ¶ 27 (8th Dist. 2010).

Examples of court decisions that have upheld such agre ements include: Scott v.

Schuster, No. 29253, 2019 WL 5607918, ¶ 6 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Oct. 30, 2019) (tenant required to pay the electric bills); Ramsdell v. Ramsdell, No. L-12-1113, 2013 WL 494019, ¶ 14 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Feb. 8, 2013) (tenant re quired to pay the utilities and property tax, and to make certain repairs to the property); Reid v.

Plainsboro Partners, III , Nos. 09AP442,456, 2010 WL 3610931, ¶ 37 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Sept. 16, 2010) (tenant “performed ministerial duties and permitte d her apartment to be used as a sample unit for prospective tenants to tour”); Tucker v.

Kanzios, No. 08-CA-009429, 2009 WL 1655029 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., June 15, 2009) (tenant required to perform cooking, cleaning, and driving services);Quigg v. Mullins,
No. L-89-314, 1991 WL 59886 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Apr. 19, 1991) (tenant required to make certain improvements to the garage, make repairs to the landlord’s cars, and pay half of the landlord’s electric bill);Elder v. Elder, No. 06-CVG-00752 (Ohio Mun., Bowling Green, June 15, 2006) (tenant required to pay the property tax, the insurance, and the utilities). But see Amick v. Sickles, 177 Ohio App.3d 337, 894 N.E.2d 733 (4th
Dist. 2008) (tenant’s payment of the utilities, alone, did not constitute rent).
