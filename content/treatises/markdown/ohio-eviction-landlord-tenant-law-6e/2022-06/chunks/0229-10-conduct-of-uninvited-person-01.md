# 10. Conduct of Uninvited Person

## Table-of-contents context

## Source text

The improper conduct of an uninvited person, who is neither a household member nor a guest, is not good cause to terminate an assisted housing te nancy, regardless of the person’s relationship with the tenant. Associated Estates Corp. v. Bartell, 24 Ohio App.3d 6, 492 N.E.2d 841 (8th Dist. 1985); ABC Mgmt. Co. v. Gamble , No. 83AP - 788, 1983 Ohio App. LEXIS 16070 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Dec. 15, 1983 ); Shiloh Grove Ltd. P’ship v. Odom, No. M9404-CVG-010436 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., June 2, 1994); Urban Hollow Apts. v. Lewis , No. M9403 -CVG-007533 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., May 25, 1994); Vistula Mgmt. Co. v. Singleton , No. CVG -9412643 (Ohio Mun., Toledo, Hous. Div., Nov. 29, 1994); Lakeview Terrace, Etc. v. Overton,
No. 91-CVG-25457 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Nov. 18, 1991). See also Stark Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Curtis , No. CA -9462, 1994 WL 45875 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Feb. 14, 1994); Countrybrook Apts. v. Forsythe , No. M86 -CVG-20896 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Aug. 28, 1986).

The improper conduct of such persons is not good cause to terminate an assisted housing tenancy even if the conduct is criminal activity, provided the tenant did not have advance knowledge of or participate in the criminal activity. E.g., Stark Metro.
Hous. Auth. v. Dorsey , No. 2001 -CA-00057, 2001 WL 1699 (Ohio App., 5th Dist.,
Aug. 20, 2001) (tenant instructed babysitter not to allow a specified person to enter the unit and sought housing authority’s assistance, but babysitter let the person enter the unit, at which time he smoked marijuana; the barred person was not tenant’s guest or invitee and his conduct did not constitute a violation of the lease); Stark Metro. Hous.
Auth. v. Marshall, No. 95-CVG-0676 (Ohio Mun., Canton, May 15, 1995) (tenant did not know of her non-household member son’s criminal plans, and the criminal activity did not occur when the son was the tenant’s guest);Insignia Mgmt. Group v. McCrae,
No. 94-CVG-4696 (Ohio Mun., Stark Cty., Oct. 20, 1994) (tenant not responsible for her non-household member daughter’s drug activity in the unit, where daughter entered the unit without tenant’s knowledge or permission, when tenant was out of town).See also Hous. Auth. of City of Jersey City v. Thomas, 318 N.J. Super. 191, 723 A.2d 119
(1999) (tenant unaware that her non -household member son “had surreptitiously entered the apartment” before his drug arrest; federal statute does not require “eviction 255 of tenant w hose apartment is invaded by a transgressor against his or her will”);

Charlotte Hous. Auth. v. Fleming, 123 N.C. App. 511, 473 S.E.2d 373 (1996) (tenant not responsible for the acts of her non -household member son, who engaged in drug- related criminal activity on project property, but not in tenant’s apartment and not when he was tenant’s guest); Edwards v. Christian , 61 A.D.2d 1045, 403 N.Y.S.2d 119
(1978) (“no showing that the petitioner [tenant] knew of, or in any way condoned, her son’s conduct” or that “the robbery occurred on an occasion when Ronald [son] was visting his mother”). Cf. Tyson v. New York City Hous. Auth. , 369 F. Supp. 513 (S.D.N.Y. 1974) (based on constitutional claims, held tenant not responsible for acts of non-household member son, who engaged in criminal activity on project property, but not in tenant’s apartment and not when he was tenant’s guest).

One Ohio appellate court has held that, when a non-household member is arrested in the rental unit, the burden is on the tenant “to present evidence that the person who was arrested in her apartment was not there with the consent of a household member.” Youngstown Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Scott , No. 99 -CA-238, 2001 WL 744496, at *3 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., June 26, 2001).
