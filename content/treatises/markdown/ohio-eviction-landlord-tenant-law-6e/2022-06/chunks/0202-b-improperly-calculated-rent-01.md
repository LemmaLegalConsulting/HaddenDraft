# b. Improperly Calculated Rent

## Source text

Nonpayment of an improperly calculated rent is not good cause to terminate an assisted housing tenancy. “A landlord of federally subsidized housing has good cause to terminate a tenant for nonpayment of rent only if the landlord has properly determined the amount of rent that the tenant owes.”New Longwood Assocs. v. Smith,
No. 2006-CVG-7635, slip op. at 3 (Ohio Mun. Cleveland, Hous. Div., May 1, 2006).

The improper rent computations for which courts have denied a landlord’s eviction claim for nonpayment of rent frequently have been a landlord’s failure to compute (or to compute properly) an interim rent revision. E.g., Amherst Vill. Mgmt. v. Vestal, No.

WD-99-075, 2000 WL 1595719 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Oct. 27, 2000) (failure to grant interim rent reduction for change in household composition); Gold Key Realty v.

Harris, No. 03-CVG-00475 (Ohio Mun., Fairborn, Apr. 22, 2003) (improper effective date of interim rent decrease); Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Binion , No. 2002 - CVG-5589 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Aug. 29, 2002) (failure to grant interim rent reduction for loss of employmen t); Amherst Vill. Mgmt. v. Bock , No. 99 -CVG- 00259 (Ohio Mun., Bowling Green, Mar. 30, 1999) (improper effective date of interim rent increase); Skyline Terrace Apts. v. Brunner , No. 97 -CVG-6830 (Ohio Mun., Canton, Mar. 3, 1998) (failure to grant timely int erim rent reduction for loss of employment); Pendergrass v. Kelly, No. M9110-CVG-036723 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Feb. 3, 1992) (failure to grant interim rent reduction for loss of employment);Hi- Land Terrace v. Bartley , No. 90 -CVG-222 (Ohio Mun., Hillsb oro, Nov. 14, 1990) (failure to grant interim rent reduction).
