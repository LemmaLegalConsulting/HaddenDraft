## Source text

An appeal from an eviction judgment generally is subject to the same requirements and restrictions as an appeal in other civil actions. Matters of particular significance in a tenant’s appeal from an eviction judgment include the final appealable order requirement (see Section I.Q.2, below), the stay and bond requirements (see Section I.Q.3, below), the mootness restriction (see Section I.Q.4, below), and the appeal time period, if a post-judgment motion or objection is filed (see Section I.Q.5, below).

The tenant’s decision on whether to appeal from an eviction judgment must be made on the merits of the appeal and not solely to provide the tenant with more time to reside in the premises. If a court finds that a notice of appeal is filed solely to provide the tenant with more time to reside in the premises, the filing of the notice of appeal may constitute bad faith conduct in violation of R.C. 2323.51, even if there is a legal foundation to the appeal. E.g., Hildreth v. Mims , 70 Ohio App.3d 282, 590 N.E.2d 1353 (8th Dist. 1990). Cf. 217 Williams, LLC v. Worthen , No. C-180101, 2019 WL 2613331 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., June 26, 2019) (filed objections in FED action in order to delay the eviction of the tenant).
