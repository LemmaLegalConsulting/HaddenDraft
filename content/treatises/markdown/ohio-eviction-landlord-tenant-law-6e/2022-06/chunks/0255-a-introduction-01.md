## Source text

The Ohio landlord-tenant law for manufactured ho me parks (“mh parks”) – R.C.

Chapter 4781 (former R.C. Chapter 3733) – is discussed in Chapter XXI, below. For eviction actions concerning a manufactured home, there are four types of tenancies relative to whether R.C. Chapter 5321 and/or R.C. Chapter 4781 applies.

The first is tenants (“mh park tenants”) who reside in and lease a manufactured home that is located in a mh park and who lease the home from the park operator. The mh park tenant is a “tenant” of the park operator under R.C. 5321.01(A) and R.C .

4781.01(T) (former R.C. 3733.01(D)). Therefore, the rights and responsibilities of these tenants include the tenant rights and responsibilities under R.C. Chapter 5321 and R.C. Chapter 4781. Schwartz v. McAtee , 22 Ohio St.3d 14, 19 n.2, 488 N.E.2d 479 (1986); Cooper v. Curtis, No. CA-8378, 1991 WL 147797, at *2 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., July 22, 1991).

The second is tenants (“non -mh park tenants”) who reside in and lease a manufactured home, but who do not lease the home from a park operator. The non - mh park tenant is a “tenant” of the owner of the home under R.C. 5321.01(A) but not R.C. 4781.01(T). Therefore, the rights and responsibilities of these tenants include the tenant rights and responsibilities under R.C. Chapter 5321 but not R.C. Chapter 4781.250 Schwartz, 22 Ohio St.3d at 19 n.2; Cooper, 1991 WL 147797, at *2.

The third is tenants (“mh park owners”) who reside in and own a manufactured home that is located in a mh park and who lease the lot on which the home is located from the park operator. With respect to the park operator, the mh park owner is an “owner” under R.C. 4781.01(U) (former R.C. 3733.01(E)), but not a “tenant” under R.C. 5321.01(A) or R.C.4781.01(T). Therefore, the rights and responsibilities of these owners include the owner rights and responsibilities under R.C. Chapter 4781, but not the tenant rights and responsibilities under R.C. Chapter 5321 or R.C. Chapter 4781.

The fourth is tenants (“non -mh park owners”) who reside in and own a manufactured home that is not located in a mh park and who lease the lot on which the home is located. With respect to the owner of the lot, the non-mh park owner is not an “owner” under R.C. 4781.01(U) and is not a “tenant” under R.C. 5321.01(A) or R.C.

4781.01(T). Therefore, the rights and responsibilities of these owners include none of the tenant rights and responsibilities under R.C. Chapter 5321 or R.C. Chapter 4781 and none of the owner rights and responsibilities under R.C. Chapter 4781.

Eviction actions filed against tenants who fall within any of the above four categories are brought pursuant to R.C. Chapter 1923. R.C. 1923.01(C)(10),(11), as revised in 2007, clarifies the application of R.C. Chapter 1923 to manufactured homes that are not located in mh parks.

Under some agreements for the purchase of a manufactured home, the title to the home transfers at the end of a series of payments. When the buyer occupies the home pursuant to the agreement and before title transfers, the occupancy is not a landlord - tenant relationship and, upon the buyer’s breach of the agreement, the seller is not permitted to file an eviction action against the buyer. Costantino v. Lyons, No. 3774, 1987 WL 31096 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Dec. 18, 1987); Lunsford v. Hurt, No. CVG- 0500914 (Ohio Mun., Jackson Cty., May 26, 2005). These agreements are installment contracts “governed by Title XIII of the Revised Code, Commercial Transactions.” Costantino, 1987 WL 31096, at *3. Upon the buyer’s breach of the agreement, the seller may commence a replevin action to repossess the home. Id.
