# 2. Statute of Frauds – Oral Rental Agreements

## Source text

R.C. 1335.04 and 1335.05 are the statute of frauds for real estate transactions, including rental agreements. Schloss v. Sachs, 63 Ohio Misc.2d 457, 459, 631 N.E.2d 212 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., 1993).

R.C. 1335.04,.05 provide that, in order to create a valid rental agreement or tobring an action upon a rental agreement, the rental agreement must be in writing and signed by the “party ... granting it” (R.C. 1335.04) and by “the party to be charged therewith” (R.C. 1335.05). Therefore, the rental agreement must be signed by the land lord (i.e., the grantor) and, when the tenant is “to be charged therewith,” by the tenant.

R.C. 1335.04,.05 require a rental agreement to be in writing even if it is for a term of less than three years and thus not subject to the statute of conveyances (see Section XXII.A.1, above). Findlay v. Baker Rental Co. , No. 77AP -874, 1978 Ohio App.

LEXIS 10554, at *5 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., May 9, 1978); Schloss v. Sachs, 63 Ohio Misc.2d 457, 460, 631 N.E.2d 212 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., 1993).

When a party takes possession of premises under an oral rental agreement that is invalid under the statute of frauds, a tenancy at will is created. Reid v. Plainsboro Partners, III, Nos. 09AP -442,446, 2010 WL 3610931, ¶ 33 (Ohio App., 10th Dist.,
Sept. 16, 2010); Peoples v. Holley, 181 Ohio App.3d 203, 908 N.E.2d 517, ¶ 22 (2nd
Dist. 2009); Mark v. Long, 180 Ohio App.3d 832, 907 N.E.2d 759, ¶¶ 9,10 (4th Dist.

2009); Weishaar v. Strimbu , 76 Ohio App.3d 276, 284, 601 N.E.2d 587 (8th Dist.

1991); Manifold v. Schuster , 67 Ohio A pp.3d 251, 255, 586 N.E.2d 1142 (4th Dist.

1990).

The tenancy at will converts to a periodic tenancy upon the payment and acceptance of rent. Id. The period of the periodic tenancy depends on the rental agreement’s provision for payment of rent. Id. If it provides for monthly rent 597 payments, the periodic tenancy is a month-to-month tenancy. Id.

In addition, a landlord and tenant may create a month -to-month tenancy under an express oral agreement (or by implication from monthly rent payments). May v.

Petrick, No. 2009-CA-00031, 2010 WL 1452604, ¶ 22 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Apr. 8, 2010); Amick v. Sickles, 177 Ohio App.3d 337, 894 N.E.2d 733, ¶ 19 (4th Dist. 2008);

Swartz v. Schutte, No. 14717, 1991 WL 2022, at *2 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Jan. 9, 1991).

See also Reid v. Plainsboro Partners, III, Nos. 09AP-442,456, 2010 WL 3610931, ¶33 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Sept. 16, 2010).

However, a landlord and tenant may not establish the terms or conditions of their tenancy in an oral agreement. “[A]n orallease can create nothing more than a periodic tenancy.”374 Neubauer v. Patzkowsky, No. 91AP-1236, 1992 WL 125269, at *2 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., June 2, 1992).

For a discussion on the equitable doctrine of partial performance taking an oral rental agreement out of the statute of frauds, see Section XXII.A.3, below.
