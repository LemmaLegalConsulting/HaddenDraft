# 2. Statement of Reason with Specificity

The regulations for the assisted housing programs require that the notice of termination state, with specificity, the reason for the proposed termination of tenancy.177 The reason for this specificity requirement is “‘to insure that the tenant is adequately informed of the nature of the evidence against him so that he can effectively rebut that evidence.’” Associated Estates Corp. v. Bartell, 24 Ohio App.3d 6, 10, 492 N.E.2d 841 (8th Dist. 1985) (quotingEscalera v. New York City Hous. Auth., 425 F.2d 853, 862 (2d Cir. 1970)). Accord Lucas Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Kincade, No. L-94-211, 1995 WL 112963, at *5 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Mar. 17, 1995);Cuyahoga Metro. Hous.
Auth. v. Younge r, 93 Ohio App.3d 819, 825, 639 N.E.2d 1253 (8th Dist. 1994). Cf.

Billington v. Underwood, 613 F.2d 91, 94 (5th Cir. 1980) (denial of Public Housing application); Edgecomb v. Hous. Auth. of Town of Vernon, 824 F. Supp. 312, 314 (D.
Conn. 1993) (termination of participation in Section 8 Certificate program).

To meet this specificity requirement, the notice of termination, in its statement of the reason for the proposed termination, must (1) “refer to specific instances of conduct,” Associated Estates Corp. v. Bartell, 24 Ohio App.3d 6, 10, 492 N.E.2d 841 (8th Dist. 1985), and (2) specify the dates on which the alleged incidents occurred and the names of the individuals involved in the alleged incidents, Owner’s Mgmt. Co. v.

Stern, No. 67445, 1995 WL 23152, a t *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 19, 1995);

Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Younger, 93 Ohio App.3d 819, 826, 639 N.E.2d 1253 177 The assisted housing regulations on termination of tenancy and eviction, which include this notice requirement, are listed in Section XXIV.B.6, below.

207 (8th Dist. 1994). But see Lable and Co. v. Francis, No. 89-J-39, 1991 WL 24982 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., Feb. 22, 1991) (reference to lease provision met specificity requirement).

In short, “the notice should describe the specific instances of tenant misconduct on which the landlord is relying, and should, generally speaking, answer the questions, who, what, where, when, and how as to each of the specific instances.” United Prop.
Mgmt. Co. v. Mundell , No. R86 -CVG-0850, slip op. at 2 (Ohio Mun., Portage Cty.,
Oct. 22, 1986). Accord Emerald Dev. & Economic Network, Inc. v. Foster, No. 2014- CVG-003506 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., June 30, 2014); Chillicothe Metro.
Hous. Auth. v. Lynch, No. CVG-13-00642 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, Sept. 4, 2013).See also Rainbow Group Apts. v. Eatmon, No. 2006-CVG-19273 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland,
Hous. Div., Nov. 7, 2006); Parma Pub. Hous. Agency v. Smiley , No. 05-CVG-00759 (Ohio Mun., Parma, May 5, 2005); Mt. Olive-Metro Hous., Inc. v. Craft , No. 93 -G- 2858 (Ohio Mun., Cuyahoga Falls, Feb. 28, 1994).

A notice of termination fails to state the reason for the proposed termination of tenancy with the requisite specificity if “the notice is blanketed in broad language and does not refer to specific instances of conduct” or is “written in ‘vague and conclusory’ language, and fail[s] to set forth a factual statement of the reason for termination.” Bartell, 24 Ohio App.3d at 10. Accord Kincade, 1995 WL 112963, at *5;Younger, 93 Ohio App.3d at 825.

A notice of termination also fails to state the reason for the proposed termination of tenancy with the requisite specificity if it fails to notify the tenant of the dates of the alleged incidents or the individuals involved in the alleged incidents. Owner’s Mgmt.
Co. v. Stern, No. 67445, 1995 WL 23152, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 19, 1995);

Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Younger, 93 Ohio App.3d 819, 826, 639 N.E.2d 1253 (8th Dist. 1994). Cf. Edgecomb v. Hous. Auth. of Town of Vernon, 824 F. Supp. 312, 315 (D. Conn. 1993).

In nonpayment of rent cases, a notice of termination fails to state the reason for the proposed termination of tenancy with the requisite specificity if it fails to specify “the amount due as of a certain date, and the months for which the rent was not paid.” Mt.

Olive-Metro Hous., Inc. v. Craft, No. 93-G-2858, slip op. at 8 (Ohio Mun., Cuyahoga Falls, Feb. 28, 1994). Accord Kimberly W. Co. v. Smith, No. M8710 -CVG-33507 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Dec. 1, 1987); Inner City Hous. v. Sebastian, No. 81-CV- 15029 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., June 29, 1981).

In nonpayment of market rent cases against project -based subsidized housing tenants, “the two-step procedure of raising a tenant’s rent to market rent, then evicting for nonpayment of that rent, must allow a tenant to challenge in an eviction proceeding the basis for the landlord’s decision to raise the rent to market rent. It follows that the notice of termination for nonpayment of that rent must specifically state the reason the landlord raised the tenant’s rent to market rent. Otherwise, a tenant can never be prepared to defend against the landlord’s evidence of good cause for its decision to raise the rent.” Boardwalk Glenville Apts. v. Cage, No. 2007-CVG-3065, slip op. at 8 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., July 13, 2007). Accord Independent Mgmt. Servs.

of Ohio, Inc. v. Davis, No. 2014-CVG-01244, slip op. at 3 (Ohio Mun., Elyria, July18,
2014) (“The ten-day notice here did not specify why Defendant’s rent increased [to market rent] and, therefore, this case should be dismissed”).
