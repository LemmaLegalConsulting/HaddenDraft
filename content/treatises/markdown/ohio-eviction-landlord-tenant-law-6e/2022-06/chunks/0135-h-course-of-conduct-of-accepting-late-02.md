The advance notice must reasonably advise the tenant that the course of conduct will no longer be permitted. Myszka v. Bledsoe , No. 00 -CVG-08398 (Ohio Mun., Akron, Nov. 9, 2000). A notice merely stating that the landlord does not want to carry any balance forward fails to meet this standard. Id.

The advance notice will not rescind an established course of conduct if, after initially serving the notice, the landlord continues to accept late rent payments and, on each such occasion, re -serves a similar advance notice. Crossroads Somerset Ltd. v.

Newland, 40 Ohio App.3d 20, 531 N.E.2d 327 (10th Dist. 1987).
