# B. Rental Agreement

## Table-of-contents context

- VI. Ohio Landlord-Tenant Act,
  - B. Rental Agreement

## Source text

A rental agreement is any agreement, written or oral, that establishes or modifies the terms concerning the use and occupancy of residential premises by one of the
parties. R.C. 5321.01(D).

For discussions on construi ng written rental agreements and on the rent prerequisite, see Sections VII.A and VII.F.3, below, respectively.

Four common residential occupancies that are not occupancies under a rental agreement are:
(1) An occupancy agreement in a cooperative. Kohler v. Snow Vill., Inc., 16 Ohio App.3d 350, 475 N.E.2d 1298 (8th Dist. 1984).
(2) An occupancy under a divorce decree that provides that one party shall retain, for a specified time, exclusive right to possession of a house that both parties owned prior to the marriage dissolution.256 Steiger v. Mack,
No. 1-81-25, 1981 WL 6743 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Dec. 17, 1981) (ex- husband not liable under R.C. 5321.15).
(3) An occupancy of a former mortgagor who remains in possession after a foreclosure action, without establishing a landlord-tenant relationship with the new title holder. 257 Adm’r of Veterans Affairs v. Jackson, 41 Ohio App.3d 274, 535 N.E.2d 369 (9th Dist. 1987).
(4) A life estate, even if it is designated a life lease.Evans v. Willis, No. 13- CA-120, 2014 WL 1759190 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Apr. 22, 2014).

Also, an option to purchase agreement is not a rental agreement, even if the option to purchase is an addendum to a rental agreement. Delker v. Kerr, No. L-96-110, 1996 WL 532319 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Sept. 20, 1996). See also Stacy v. Zupp, No. CA- 9566, 1994 WL 369899 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 20, 1994).

Likewise, in some circumstances, an addendum to a purchase agreement that permits the purchasers to take possession of the premises prior to closing is not a rental
agreement. E.g., Kott Enters., Ltd. v. McClain , No. L -15-1086, 2016 WL 530886 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Jan. 29, 2016).
