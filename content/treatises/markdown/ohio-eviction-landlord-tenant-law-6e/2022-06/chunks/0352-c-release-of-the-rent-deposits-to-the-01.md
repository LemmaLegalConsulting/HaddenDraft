# C. Release of the Rent Deposits to the

## Source text

Tenant A tenant may not file an application under R.C. 5321.09 for the release of the R.C.

5321.07 rent deposits to thetenant. Robinson v. Meyer, No. CA-7735, 1989 WL 94378 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Aug. 14, 1989); Weibling v. Rine , No. 77AP -355, 1977 Ohio
App. LEXIS 7537 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Aug. 30, 1977).

However, a tenant may secure the release of the rent deposits to the tenant in at least five ways.

First, if the tenant is awarded a money judgment against the landlord on an R.C.

5321.09(B) counterclaim, the court may order the clerk of court to release to the tenant the portion of the rent deposits that is necessary to satisfy the judgment. E.g., Slovak
v. Univ. Off-Campus Hous., No. 99-CA-50, 2000 WL 680479 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., May 19, 2000); Hansgen v. Seigel , No. 00 -CVR-40822 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., May 29, 2001); Roundtree v. Valentine , No. 98 -CVR-23790 (Oh io Mun., Franklin Cty., Jan. 28, 1999); Cummings v. Cortese, No. R88-CVE-2278 (Ohio Mun., Portage Cty., Dec. 4, 1989); Dicks v. Schmelzer , Rent Escrow No. 14 (Ohio County Ct., Muskingum Cty., Aug. 15, 1986); Gill v. Lewis Zipkin & Assocs. , No. 78 -LLT-88 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland Heights, Oct. 31, 1979).Cf. Chasteen v. Dix Road Prop. Mgmt.

LLC, Nos. CA2020 -04-055,056, 2021 WL 669061 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Feb. 22,
2021) (R.C. 5321.09 action in which the entire rent deposit is released to a tenant who did file counterclaims and who, pending the action, terminated the tenancy).

In these cases, if the court does not issue a release order, the tenant should nonetheless be able to attach the rent deposits to satisfy the judgment.

Second, if the tenant is awardeda money judgment against the landlord on a claim asserted in a complaint, the court may order the clerk of court to release to the tenant the portion of the rent deposits that is necessary to satisfy the judgment. E.g., O’Connor v. Maynard, No. 00 -CVG-01 (Ohio Mun., Jackson Cty., Apr. 17, 2001);

Walton v. Washington, No. 93-CVG-07401 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Dec.

7, 1993). See also Tolliver v. Warren , No. 99 -CVG-13123 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland,
Hous. Div., Dec. 28, 1999) (tenant counterclaim to landlord complaint for eviction and money judgment for unpaid rent).

In these cases, if the court does not issue a release order, the tenant should nonetheless be able to attach the rent deposits to satisfy the judgment.

Third, when the rent has been depositedwith the court in conjunction with an R.C.

5321.07(B) civil action, if the tenant vacates the premises and then files a motion to dismiss the action under Civil Rule 41(A)(2), the court may dismiss the action on “such terms and conditions as the court dee ms proper,” which may include a release of the rent deposits to the tenant. E.g., Brown v. Balliett Group , No. 98-RE-6 (Ohio Mun., 391 Mansfield, July 13, 1998) (tenant vacated as a result of the city’s condemnation of the building). Cf. Miller v. Miller, No. CVH-971763R (Ohio Mun., Portsmouth, May 23,
1998) (tenant dismissed the action under Civil Rule 41(A)(1)).

Fourth, the tenant may enter into an agreement with the landlord under which: (1) the tenant agrees to give a written notice to the clerk of courtstating that the condition specified in the R.C. 5321.07 notice has been remedied; and (2) the landlord agrees to give the tenant a specified portion of the rent deposits as soon as they are released.

Upon receiving the tenant’s written notice, the clerkof court is required to release the rent deposits to the landlord. See Section VIII.B, above.

Fifth, under R.C. 5321.07(B)(2), the tenant may file an application with the court for an order releasing all or some of the rent deposits to the tenant to enable the tenant to remedy the condition specified in the R.C. 5321.07 notice. See Section VIII.H, below.
