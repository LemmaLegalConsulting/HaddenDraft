# 4. Motion to Amend Agreed Judgment

## Source text

Judgments by consent are permissible. Wallick Props. Midwest, LLC v. Jama, No.

20AP-299, 2021 WL 3629962,¶ 10 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Aug. 17, 2021). An agreed judgment entry is a contract. Id. Therefore, an agreed judgment entry is subject to the same rules of construction as a contract. Id.

“The court’s inherent power to modify a consent decree or other injunction is now encompassed in Rule 60(b)(5) of the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure,” Thompson v.

U.S. Dep’t of Hous. & Urban Dev., 404 F.3d 821, 826 (4th Cir. 2005), and thus in Rule 60(B)(4) of the Ohio Rules of Civil Procedure. Ohio Civil Rule 60(B)(4) is virtually the same as Federal Civil Rule 60(b)(5).

The standard for determining whether to modify a consent decree because of a change in circumstances is set forth inRufo v. Inmates of Suffolk County Jail, 502 U.S.

367 (1992). Cmty. Dev. Props. Cleveland, Inc. v. Griffin , No. 87954, 2007 WL 613956, ¶¶ 19 -24 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 1, 2007). The party seeking the modification bears the initial burden of establishing that a significant change in circumstances, in the facts or the law, warrants a modification of the decree. Id. A significant change in facts warrants a modification of the decree if the change was unforeseen and, as a result of the change, the decree proves to be unworkable. Id. If the moving party meets this initial burden, the court must then determine whether the proposed modification is suitably tailored to the changed circumstances. Id.

An example of a court applying theRufo standard in granting a motion to amend a consent decree in an eviction action isCMHA v. Matthew, No. 2007-CVG-16146 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Apr. 30, 2008). The court modified a consent decree so as to extend the time period for the tenant to tender unpaid rent. The court relied on a significant change in facts, namely: as a result of a recent strain on social service 107 agencies due to the foreclosure crisis, the tenant was unable to foresee that she would be unable to get an appointment and receive financial assistance from a social service agency within the agreed upon time period for tendering the unpaid rent.
