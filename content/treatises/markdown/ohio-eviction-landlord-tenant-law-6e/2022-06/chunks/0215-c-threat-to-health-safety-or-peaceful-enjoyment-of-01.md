Three Ohio court decisions that found that the requisite threat to other residents was not met are summarized below.
(1) Akron Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Wheeler , No. 16973, 1995 WL 338538 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., June 7, 1995) (tenant’s son threatened and assaulted a housing authority officer on housing authority property and resisted arrest, but no evidence that this conduct occurred in proximity to other residents or disturbed other residents’ peaceful en joyment of their homes or impaired the physical or social environment of the development).
(2) Heritage Place Apts. v. Ely, No. 08-CVG-791 (Ohio Mun., Steubenville,
Aug. 29, 2008) (baby-sitter, with whom tenant had dating relationship, placed tenant’s child in to scalding water, causing severe burns; the crime – child endangerment – was directed solely toward the minor child; the tenant was not present and had no control over the babysitter’s criminal activity).
(3) Akron Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Jones , No. 94 -CVG-10415 (Ohio Mun., Akron, Jan. 19, 1995) (altercation and arrest in ball field across the street from the housing development, but no evidence that it was viewed by anyone at the development or in any way affected the development).
