## Source text

Claim Neither the R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se claim (see Section IX.B, above) nor R.C. Chapter 5321 supplants the tenant’s common law negligence claims against a
landlord. Sauter v. One Lytle Place, No. C-040266, 2005 WL 627794, ¶ 6 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Mar. 18, 2005);Davis-Blunt v. Myatt, 134 Ohio App.3d 213, 215, 730 N.E.2d 1003 (1st Dist. 1999); Winston v. Wolery , No. 96 -CA-2442, 1997 WL 28535, at *3 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Jan. 14, 1997);Hoover v. Elliott, No. CA-9160, 1985 WL 4927, at *2 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Dec. 30, 1985).See also Shump v. First Cont’l-Robinwood Assocs., 71 Ohio St.3d 414, 644 N.E.2d 291 (1994).

Therefore, when a landlord fails to maintain the premises, the tenant may have both a common law negligence claim and an R.C. 5321.04 n egligence per se claim against the landlord.
