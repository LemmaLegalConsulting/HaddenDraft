As a general rule, the measure of damages for damage to personal property is the difference between its market value immediately before and immediately after the
damage. Falter v. City of Toledo , 169 Ohio St. 238, 158 N.E.2d 893 (1959); Gibson
v. Shephard , 87 N.E.3d 846, 2017 -Ohio-1157 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., 2 017) (tenant claim against landlord); Warner v. Zuccola, No. 53070, 1987 WL 30379 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 24, 1987) (landlord claim against tenant); Cuzzort v. Rose , No. CA - 9791, 1986 WL 12632 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Nov. 6, 1986) (landlord claim agains t tenant); Cooper v. Feeney , 34 Ohio App.3d 282, 518 N.E.2d 46 (12th Dist. 1986) (tenant claim against landlord); Woodard v. Walker, No. 78AP-495, 1979 Ohio App.

LEXIS 12212 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Feb. 27, 1979) (tenant claim against landlord).

If the party who asserts the claim does not prove the difference between the value of the personal property pre -damage and post -damage, the party has failed to prove damages under the general rule.352 E.g., Warner, 1987 WL 30379, at *2;Cuzzort, 1986 WL 12632, at *2-3; Woodard, 1979 Ohio App. LEXIS, at *7.

When personal property is totally destroyed, the measure of damages is the reasonable market value of the property immediately before its destruction, less any salvage value. Falter v. City of Toledo, 169 Ohio St. 238, 158 N.E.2d 893 (1959);Oak Hill Inv. Co. v. Jablonski , 78 Ohio App.3d 643, 605 N.E.2d 998 (6th Dist. 1992) (landlord claim against tenant); Cooper v. Feeney, 34 Ohio App.3d 282, 518 N.E.2d 46 (12th Dist. 1986) (tenant claim against landlord).

352 A landlord may give opinion testimony on the value of the landlord’s personal property pre- damage and post -damage under the owner opinion rule. See Section XVIII.F.3, below.

Likewise, a tenant may give opinion testimony on the value of the tenant’s personal property pre-damage and post-damage under the owner opinion rule. See Section IX.A.11.b, above.

535 When personal property does not have a market value or its market value clearly does not reflect the injury suffered by the owner, the court may use a standard of value to the owner. Bishop v. East Ohio Gas Co., 143 Ohio St. 541, 56 N.E.2d 164 (1944);

Cooper v. Feeney, 34 Ohio App.3d 282, 518 N.E.2d 46 (12th Dist. 1986) (tenant claim against landlord); Craft v. Oney, No. 8766, 1984 WL 4060 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct.

17, 1984) (tenant claim against landlord). “This value [to the owner] is determined via consideration of a number of factors including value to the owner, original cost, replacement cost, salvage value, if any, and fair market value at the time of loss.” Cooper, 34 Ohio App.3d at 284.

Also, in some circumstances, the measure of dam ages for damage to personal property may be the cost of restoring the property to its condition before the damage
occurred. Adcock v. Rollins Protective Servs. Co., 1 Ohio App.3d 160, 440 N.E.2d 548 (1st Dist. 1981). E.g., Kenwood Courts Apts. v. Williams, No. K90-CVG-1043 (Ohio Mun., Portage Cty., Oct. 25, 1991) (tenant claim against landlord).
