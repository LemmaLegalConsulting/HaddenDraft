General Rule When the condition of the premises is a public nuisance, R.C. 3767.41 permits the tenant to bring a civil action against the owner for an injunction that orders the owner to abate the nuisance. R.C. 3767.41(B),(C).

“A building may be the subject of a nuisance-abatement action under R.C. 3767.41 if it is a building or structure that is used or intended to be used for residential purposes.

R.C. 3767.41(A)(1).” Northeast Shores Dev. Corp. v. Euclid Beach L.P. , No. 05 - CVH-03844, 2005 WL 1131729, ¶ 7 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous . Div., Mar. 25, 2005). The R.C. 3767.41(A)(1) phrase “used or intended to be used” means that the property is occupied by residents or is suitable for residential purposes based upon the property’s design, construction, and location. Id., 2005 WL 113172 9, ¶ 17. R.C.

3767.41 does not apply to any building that is occupied by its owner and contains three or fewer dwelling units. R.C. 3767.41(A)(1).

A public nuisance means:

453 a building that is a menace to the public health, welfare or safety; that is structurally unsafe, unsanitary, or not provided with adequate safe egress; that constitutes a fire hazard, is otherwise dangerous to human life, or is otherwise no longer fit and habitable; or that, in relation to its existing use, constitutes a hazard to t he public health, welfare, or safety by reason of inadequate maintenance, dilapidation, obsolescence, or abandonment.

R.C. 3767.41(A)(2)(a).

Under this definition, most residential rental premises that have significant code violations are a public nuisance.

When the condition of the premises is a public nuisance and the owner previously has been afforded a reasonable opportunity to abate the nuisance and has failed to do so, R.C. 3767.41 also permits the tenant to bring a civil action against the owner for the appointment of a receiver, who will be responsible for abating the nuisance. R.C.

3767.41(C).

Prior to appointing a receiver in these circumstances, the court must first determine whether any mortgagee, lienholder, or other interested party associated with the building is willing and able to undertake the work and to furnish the materials necessary to abate the nuisance. Id.

If a receiver is appointed, the receiver will be given control over the property to make repairs, collect rents, make loans agai nst the property for rehabilitation, and otherwise manage the property. E.g., Winters v. Valley Homes Mut. Hous. Corp., No.

A0601318 (Ohio C.P., Hamilton Cty., Feb. 10, 2006) (nuisance – threatened termination of water, gas, electric, and trash services due to management nonpayment;

receiver, for 90 days, to collect rent and charges, pay utility bills, and maintain the property).

In deciding whether to file an R.C. 3767.41 action for receivership, the tenant’s considerations include whether the amount of e quity in the property is adequate to support a rehabilitation loan (if a loan is needed) and whether the rent generated by the property is adequate to support the costs of operating the property, including a reasonable management fee.

When a tenant decidesto file an R.C. 3767.41 action for receivership, before filing, a helpful next step for the tenant is to identify a qualified housing management company (or individual) which (who) is willing to be appointed as the receiver.

Civil actions under R.C. 3767.41 may be commenced in a court of common pleas, a municipal court, a housing or an environmental division of a municipal court, or a county court. R.C. 1901.18(A)(12), 1901.181(A), 1907.031.

When an R.C. 3767.41 action can be filed in a housing division o r an environmental division of a municipal court, the housing division or environmental division has exclusive jurisdiction over the action. Cent. Residents Council v. Lucas Metro Hous. Auth., No. CI-200902483 (Ohio C.P., Lucas Cty., Apr. 27, 2009), aff’d on other grounds, No. L -09-1137, 2010 WL 497340 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Feb. 12, 2010). Under R.C. 1901.181(A), “the housing or environmental division of a municipal court ... has exclusive jurisdiction within the territory of the court in any civil action as described in division (B)(1) of section 3767.41 of the Revised Code that relates to a public nuisance.”
