Municipal courts, county courts, and courts of common pleas have concurrent original jurisdiction in eviction actions. Seventh Urban, Inc. v. Univ. Circle Prop.

Dev., Inc., 67 Ohio St.2d 19, 423 N.E.2d 1070 (1981). Nonetheless, eviction actions usually are filed in a municipal or county court.

In such cases, because municipal and county courts are courts of limited monetary jurisdiction,50 the filing of a counterclaim may requ ire the proceeding to be certified to the court of common pleas under Civil Rule 13(J). This certification is mandatory when a properly filed counterclaim requests monetary relief in excess of the court’s monetary jurisdiction. Widder & Widder v. Kutnick, 113 Ohio App.3d 616, 626, 681 N.E.2d 977 (8th Dist. 1996) (municipal court); O’Hara Realty v. Lloyd , 116 Ohio App.3d 439, 440, 688 N.E.2d 544 (7th Dist. 1996) (county court).
