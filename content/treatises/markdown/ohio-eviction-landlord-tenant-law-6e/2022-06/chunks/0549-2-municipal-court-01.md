## Source text

Municipal courts have limited subject -matter jurisdiction in civil actions, but it encompasses virtually all of the landlord -tenant claims that are discussed in Chapters I-XIX, above. The scop e of municipal court subject -matter jurisdiction is defined principally by R.C. 1901.18.

One court held that, u nder R.C. 1901.18(A)(2) ,(3), it had subject-matter jurisdiction over the tenant’s 42 U.S.C.§ 1983 monetary counterclaim against a public housing authority. Lorain Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Payne, No. 2021-CVG-02216 (Ohio Mun., Lorain, Feb. 8, 2022). But cf. White v. Chambers, Nos. 1-01-114,115,116, 2002 WL 1299781 (Ohio App., 3rdDist., May 29, 2002) (lack of subject-matter jurisdiction over plaintiff’s42 U.S.C. § 1983 claims alleged in a complaint).

Municipal courts do not have subject-matter jurisdiction in an eviction action if a dispute exists as to which party holds present record title. See Section I.L.3, above.
