# c. Vendee Claim for Payments in Excess of Fair Rental

## Source text

Value When a vendor declares the land installment contract void because of the vendee’s nonpayment, the vendee is generally entitled to recover any sums paid on the purchase price in excess of the fair rental value of the property, during the period of the vendee’s
possession. Brown v. Johnston, 95 Ohio App. 136, 108 N.E.2d 298 (2nd Dist. 1952).

But cf. Doty v. Potteiger, No. 2017-T-0029, 2017 WL 5998569 (Ohio App., 11th Dist.,
Dec. 4, 2017) (vendorin a land installment contract which had beenforfeited is entitled to keep the initial deposit that is in excess of the statutory damages allowed by R.C.

5313.10). For a discussion on fair rental value, see Section XXIII.E.3.b, below.
