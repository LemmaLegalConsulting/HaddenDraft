# B. Deficient Service of Process

## Table-of-contents context

## Source text

The service of process requirements in an eviction action are discussed in Section I.G, above. Under Civil Rule 3(A), the landlord has one year from the filing of the complaint to obtain proper service. Under Civil Rule 4(A), if service is not obtained within six months from the filing of the complaint, the court may dismiss the complaint without prejudice, unless good cause exists for the lack of service.

Therefore, in an eviction action, when the content or service of the summons is deficient, the appropriate response is a motion to quash service, if the response is filed before the expiration of the one -year period (or six -month period, if applicable) and before an eviction judgment has been entered. Cf. Thomas v. Corrigan , 135 Ohio App.3d 340, 733 N.E.2d 1213 (11th Dist. 1999) (improper to dismiss security deposit claim because the one -year period had not expired). But see Szabo v. Humphrey , 66 Ohio Misc.2d 55, 642 N.E.2d 1173 (Ohio Mun., Wadsworth, 1993) (granting motion to dismiss); Jackson View Ltd. v. Sturgill, No. 91-CVG-110 (Ohio Mun., Jackson Cty.,
Mar. 21, 1991) (same).

The requirement to obtain proper service of process is a jurisdictional requirement.

State ex rel. Ballard v. O’Donnell, 50 Ohio St.3d 182, 553 N.E.2d 650 (1990);Lincoln Tavern, Inc. v. Snader, 165 Ohio St. 61, 133 N.E.2d 606 (1956);O.B. Corp. v. Cordell, 47 Ohio App.3d 170, 547 N.E.2d 1201 (10th Dist. 1988).

Therefore, in an eviction action, absent proper service of process, the eviction judgment and the writ of restitution(if any) are void ab initio. Fourth & Plum Apts. v.
