# a. Pending Foreclosure Action, Effect on Tenancy

## Source text

During the pendency of a foreclosure action ( i.e., after commencem ent of the action and before confirmation of the foreclosure sale), generally the landlord -tenant relationship remains unchanged. The tenancy ( e.g., one-year lease, month -to-month
tenancy) continues. The landlord and tenant have the same rights and duties as before the filing of the foreclosure action.

The tenant still has a legal duty to pay the rent to the landlord. A tenant’s failure to pay the rent may permit the landlord to terminate the tenancy and file an eviction action.

Likewise, the landlord is still required to maintain the property and, if the rental agreement requires the landlord to pay for a utility service, the landlord is still required to make those utility payments. Generally, a foreclosure filing, alone, may not be a basis for rent depositing (see Chapter VIII, above). However, it may be a basis for rent depositingif the foreclosure filing and related facts support a claim for breach of the implied covenant of quiet enjoyment (see Section VII.C.9, above).

Three exceptions to these general rules, which (when applicable) modify the landlord-tenant relationship pendi ng the foreclosure action, are: (1) the court’s appointment of a receiver; (2) the court’s approval of a mortgagee-in-possession; and
(3) the mortgagee’s exercise of an assignment of rent provision. Generally, a receiver has whatever powers the court dele gates, a mortgagee -in-possession has the general duties of a landlord, and an assignee of rent does not have the general duties of a landlord.
