If the tenant remedies the R.C. 5321.05 violation before the termination date specified in the R.C. 5321.11 notice, the rental agre ement is not terminated pursuant to the notice and the tenant’s conduct that violated R.C. 5321.05 cannot be the basis for an eviction action. 19 R.C. 5321.11. R.C. 5321.11 does not expressly require a statement of the right to cure in the R.C.

5321.11 notice. One trial court has held that no such requirement exists and, thereupon, rejected the magistrate’s conclusion to the contrary. Akron Metro. Hous. Auth. v.

Mayson, No. 97 -CVG-1293 (Ohio Mun., Cuyahoga Falls, magistrate’s decision and judgment entry filed Sept. 8, 1997, order nunc pro tunc filed Sept. 29, 1997, and order filed Mar. 19, 1998).

However, the R.C. 5321.11 notice may not include language that suggests that the right to cure does not exist. Owner’s Mgmt. Co. v. Brent , No. 01 -CVG-1693 (Ohio Mun., Cuyahoga Falls, Aug. 1, 2001). For example, the R.C. 5321.11 notice may not include the language required for an R.C. 1923.04 notice to vacate and state categorically that, if the t enant fails to vacate as of the specified termination date, an eviction action will be commenced.20 Id.

When the R.C. 5321.05 violation specified in the R.C. 5321.11 notice is damage to the premises, the tenant may cure the violation by paying to the landlord the amount of the landlord’s cost to repair the damage.M.L. Gabriel Commons v. King, No. 13159, 1992 WL 288781 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 14, 1992); Akron Metro. Hous. Auth. v.

Mayson, No. 97-CVG-1293 (Ohio Mun., Cuyahoga Falls, May 5, 1998).

A landlord’s refusal to accept such payment is a violation of the landlord’s obligations under R.C. 5321.11. Akron Metro. Hous. Auth. v. When the R.C. 5321.05 violation specified in the R.C. 5321.11 notice is an ongoing activity of the tenant, the tenant may cure the vi olation by no longer engaging in the improper activity.

For example, the R.C. 5321.05 violation may be the tenant causing excessive noise in her dwelling unit that disturbs the neighbors’ peaceful enjoyment. E.g., Parker v.

Fisher, 17 Ohio App.3d 103, 477 N.E.2d 654 (9th Dist. 1984). In that case, the tenant may cure the R.C. 5321.11 violation by no longer causing such noise.21 Id.

For another example, the R.C. 5321.05 violation may be continuing to put trash bags on the yard.E.g., Columbiana Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Parker, No. 03-CO-46, 2004 WL 1533250 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., June 28, 2004). In that case, the tenant may cure the R.C. 5321.05 violation by ceasing to put trash bags on the yard. Id.
