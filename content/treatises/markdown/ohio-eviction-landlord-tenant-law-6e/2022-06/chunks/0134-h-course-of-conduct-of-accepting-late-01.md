Rent Payment A landlord’s repeated acceptance of late rent payments may establish a course of 139 conduct between the parties that waives the tenant’s obligation to pay rent by the due date specified in the rental agreement.111 Fairborn Apts. v. Herman , No. 90-CA-28, 1991 WL 10962 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Jan. 31, 1991); Crossroads Somerset Ltd. v.

Newland, 40 Ohio App.3d 20, 531 N.E.2d 327 (10th Dist. 1987); Finkbeiner v. Lutz, 44 Ohio App.2d 223, 337 N.E.2d 655 (1st Dist. 1975); Lauch v. Monning , 15 Ohio App.2d 112, 239 N.E.2d 675 (1st Dist. 1968); Bates & Springer, Inc. v. Nay, 91 Ohio Law Abs. 425, 187 N.E.2d 415 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., 1963); Milbourn v. Aska , 81 Ohio App. 79, 77 N.E.2d 619 (3rd Dist. 1946). But see Woda Mgmt. & Real Estate v.

Grant, No. 15 -16-43, 2017 WL 3382166 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Aug. 7, 2017) (held that the lease granted the landlord the right to select between accepting late rent payments with a late payment fee or terminating the lease, an d thus any pattern of accepting late rent payments and the fee is consistent with the lease, not a waiver of the lease).

When a waiver occurs, the due date for the tenant’s rent payments is established by the course of conduct between the parties. Id. If the tenant tenders rent in compliance with that course of conduct, the landlord is estopped from refusing the rent and from evicting the tenant for nonpayment or late payment of the rent.112 Id.

For example, if a rental agreement requires the tenant to pay rent by the fifth day of the month and, under an established course of conduct, the tenant has been permitted to pay rent by the tenth day of the month, the landlord may not refuse the tenant’s tender of the January rent on January 10 and may not evict the tenant for nonpayment or late payment of the January rent. However, if the same tenant tenders the January rent on January 12, the landlord may refuse the tenant’s tender of the January rent and may evict the tenant for nonpayment of the January ren t. See Byrneport Apts. II v.

Williams, No. L -19-1277, 2020 WL 3485726 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 26, 2020);

Southgate Woods Apts. v. Lanier, No. L-83-189, 1983 WL 7006 (Ohio App., 6th Dist.,
Nov. 25, 1983).

For this waiver, the scope of the course of conduct of accepting late rent payments may be tied to a notice period, instead of a particular date of the month. For example, in Schroeder Co. v. Coates, 172 Ohio App.3d 254, 874 N.E.2d 832 (6th Dist. 2007), the court found a course of conduct of rent paym ents up to the expiration of the ten - day notices of termination. The court held that the landlord properly rejected the tenant’s tender of rent because the tenant had tendered it after the expiration of the ten- day notice period.

There is no fixed standar d for determining whether a course of conduct of accepting late rent payments has been established. However, the case law provides some guidance for this determination.
