Civil Rule 4(B) is applicable to eviction actions because its application does not 25 The language required by R.C. 1923.06(B) is:

A complaint to evict you has been filed with this court. No person shall be evicted unless his right to possession has ended and no person shall be evicted in retaliation for the exercise of his lawful rights. If you are depositing rent with the clerk of this court you shall continue to deposit such rent until the time of the court hearing. The failure to continue to deposit such rent may result in your eviction. You may request a trial by jury. You have the right to seek legal
assistance. If you cannot afford a lawyer, you may contact your local legal aid or legal service
office. If none is available, you may contact your local bar association.

In an eviction action, since the time for the tenant “to appear and defend” may be the time of trial (see Section I.H, below), Civil Rule 4(B) requires the summons to contain a statement of the date and time of trial. See Costantino v. Lyons, No. 3774, 1987 WL 31096, at *4 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Dec. 18, 1987).
