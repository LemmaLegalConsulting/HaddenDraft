# 3. Summary of R.C. 4781.40 Rights and Obligations

## Source text

Before the installation of an owner’s manufactured or mobile home in a mh park, the park operator must offer the owner a written rental agreement for a term of one year or more. R.C. 4781.40(A)(1). The park operator must deliver this offer to the owner in person or by certified mail, return receipt requested. R.C. 4781.40(A)(2).

At the end of each successive rental agreement, the park operator must offer the owner another rental agreement “for a term that is mutually agreed upon, and that contains terms essentially the same asthe alternative month-to-month agreement.” Id.

The park operator must deliver subsequent rental offers by ordinary mail or personal
delivery. Id.

Before a resident executes a rental agreement and assumes occupancy in a mh park, the park operator must disclose in writing all fees, charges, and assessments, including rental fees. R.C. 4781.40(B). Those fees, charges, and assessments may include a fee for moving a manufactured or mobile home into or out of the mh park, but only for the actual costs and e xpenses that are incurred by the park operator in the move. R.C.

4781.40(H)(5).

If a park operator fails to disclose a fee, charge, or assessment: (1) the park operator may not collect the fee, charge, or assessment, and (2) the park operator may not evict the resident for nonpayment of the fee, charge, or assessment. R.C. 4781.40(B), 4781.40(H)(4).

A park operator may not increase a disclosed fee, charge, or assessment during the term of the resident’s rental agreement. R.C. 4781.40(B). Inaddition, a park operator may not increase a disclosed fee, charge, or assessment without providing the resident with at least 30 days advance written notice of the increase and its effective date. R.C.

4781.40(B).

Before a resident executes a rental agreement and assumes occupancy in a mh park, the park operator must disclose in writing the park rules and must provide a copy of the park rules to the resident. R.C. 4781.40(B),(C). The park operator may not change those rules without providing the resident with at least 30 days advance written notice of the change and its effective date. R.C. 4781.40(B).

The park operator may not adopt any park rule that is unreasonable, arbitrary, or
capricious. R.C. 4781.40(C). And the park operator may not adopt any rule (or include any provision in a rental agreement) that restricts a resident’s right to display the flag of the United States or certain other specified flags, provided the flag is displayed in accordance with certain specified requirements. R.C. 4781.40.

A park operator may not require an owner: (1) to purchase from the park operator any personal property, (2) to purchase a manufactured or mobile home from the park operator or any other specific person as a prerequisite to entering into a rental agreement, or (3) to use the services of the park operator or any other specific person for the performance of any service. R.C. 4781.40(D),(F),(G).

A park operator may not assess a fee for the installation of an owner’s electric or gas appliance, unless the installation is performed by the park operator at the owner’s
request. R.C. 4781.40(E).

R.C. 4781.40 establishes various rights and obligations regarding the sale of a manufactured or mobile home within a mh park. The park operator may not deny the owner the right to sell the home within the park, if the owner gives the park operator ten days advance notice of the owner’s intent to sell the home. R.C. 4781.40(H)(1).

The park operator may not require the owner to remove the home from the park solely because of the sale. R.C. 4781.40(H)(2). The park operator may not unreasonably refuse to enter into a rental agreement with the purchaser of the home. R.C.

4781.40(H)(3). The park operator may not assess the owner a fee because of the transfer of ownership. R.C. 4781.40(H)(5). A rental agreement may not require the owner to sell the home to any specific person or through any specific agent. R.C.

4781.40(J). A rental agreement may not require the owner to pay a fee to the park operator if the owner does not use the park operator or some other designated person as the owner’s agent in the sale of the home. R.C. 4781.40(K).
