# 3. Interest Award on Judgments

## Table-of-contents context

## Source text

The appellate districts are split on whether a residential lease may establish a contractual interest rate for judgments that is higher than the statutory interest rate under R.C. 1343.03. Compare Meadowbrook Dev. Corp. v. Roberts, No. 79747, 2001 WL 1654540 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 27, 2001) (rejects an 18% interest rate and applies the statutory rate) with Buchenroth v. Adkins , 7 N.E.3d 621, 2014 -Ohio-257 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., 2014) (upholds an 18% interest rate).

R.C. Chapter 5321 does not expressly address the permissible interest rate for judgments or whether a residential lease may establish a specific interest rate for
judgments. See Meadowbrook Dev. Corp. v. Roberts, No. 79747, 2001 WL 1654540, at *4 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 27, 2001).
