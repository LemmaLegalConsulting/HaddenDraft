# 5. Oral Modification, Written Rental Agreement

## Source text

The statute of frauds (see Section XXII.A.2, above), as a general rule, prohibits an oral modification of a written rental agreement. Miami Valley United Methodist Mission Soc’y v. White-Dawson, No. 17873, 2000 WL 234712 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist.,
Mar. 3, 2000); 200 W. Apts. v. Foreman, No. 66107, 1994 WL 505271 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Sept. 15, 1994).

However, there is an exception to this general rule “when the parties to the written agreement act upon the terms of the oral agreement.” 200 W. Apts. v. Foreman , No.

66107, 1994 WL 505271, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Sept. 15, 1994). “[W]hen an agreement [in writing] as orally modified has been acted upon, as in the instant case, the rights of the parties are to be determined by the modified agreement.”377 Id. Accord 3637 Green Road Co., Ltd. v. Specialized Component Sales Co., Inc., 69 N.E.3d 1083, 2016-Ohio-5342 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., 2016) (commercial lease).

377 In Foreman, the written lease contained a thirteen -month term and specified a $450 per month r ent. Concurrent with its execution, the tenant and landlord orally agreed that the tenant’s rental obligation would be only $225 per month because he would soon become the property manager and, in the interim, he would perform certain computer consultingwork. For the initial seven months, the tenant paid and the landlord accepted $225 per month rent. The landlord then notified the tenant that the property manager position was no longer available and the tenant must begin paying $450 per month rent. How ever, the landlord’s claim requested $450 per month rent for the entire thirteen-month term of the written lease. The court held that, in these circumstances, the oral modification of the rental obligation established the rights of the parties for the initial seven months of the lease.
