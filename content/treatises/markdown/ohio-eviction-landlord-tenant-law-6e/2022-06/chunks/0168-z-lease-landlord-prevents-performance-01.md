AA. Repayment Agreement, Contract of Adhesion A contract of adhesion is “a standardized form contract prepared by one party, and offered to the weaker party, usually a consumer, who has no realistic choice as to the contract terms. See Black’s Law Dictionary (8th Ed. 2004) 342.” Taylor Bldg. Corp.

of Am. v. Benfield, 117 Ohio St.3d 352, 363, 884 N.E.2d 12 (2008). Accord Worldwide Asset Purchasing, L.L.C. v. Easterling , 186 Ohio App.3d 478, 481 n.1, 928 N.E.2d 1148 (10th Dist. 2009);W.K. v. Farrell, 167 Ohio App.3d 14, 25, 853 N.E.2d 728 (2nd
Dist. 2006).

“[A] contract of adhesion is not in all ins tances unconscionable.” Taylor Bldg.
Corp. of Am. v. Benfield, 117 Ohio St.3d 352, 363, 884 N.E.2d 12 (2008).Accord Bohl
v. Hauke, 180 Ohio App.3d 526, 532-33, 906 N.E.2d 450 (4th Dist. 2009).

A contract of adhesion is unenforceable if the party challenging it can prove “that the agreement is both procedurally and substantively unconscionable.” Taylor Bldg.
Corp. of Am. v. Benfield , 117 Ohio St.3d 352, 359, 884 N.E.2d 12 (2008). Accord W.K. v. Farrell, 167 Ohio App.3d 14, 25, 853 N.E.2d 728 (2nd Dist. 2006).

“Substantive unconscionability exists when the contract terms are determined to be commercially unfair and unreasonable.” W.K. v. Farrell, 167 Ohio App.3d 14, 25, 853 N.E.2d 728 (2nd Dist. 2006).

“Procedural unconscionability, on the other hand,exists when it is determined that there was no voluntary meeting of the minds by the parties to the contract under circumstances particular to that contract. Id. at 25.

Therefore, a contract of adhesion may be unenforceable if the less advantaged party proves: “(1) that the contract terms are one -sided or unreasonably favorable to the other party, (2) that disparity between the parties’ bargaining power denies the less advantaged party meaningful choice in accepting the terms of the contract, and (3) that the less advantaged party cannot obtain the desired product or service except by acquiescing in the contract.” Id. at 25.

When an eviction action is based on the tenant’s breach of a repayment agreement, and the repayment agreement is a contract of adhesion and unconscionable, a defense to the eviction action may be that the repayment agreement is unenforceable. E.g., Lorain Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Jennings, No. 05-CVG-01669 (Ohio Mun., Lorain, Oct.

18, 2006).

In Jennings, the repayment agreement required the tenant to pay $145.15 per month, for 24 months, for fire damage to the dwelling unit. The tenant’s total income was $25 per month – a monthly gift from his brother to enable him to pay his $8.00 per month rent. There was no evidence that the tenant had started the fire or was otherwise responsible for it. The court held that the repayment agreement was unenforceable as a contract of adhesion.
