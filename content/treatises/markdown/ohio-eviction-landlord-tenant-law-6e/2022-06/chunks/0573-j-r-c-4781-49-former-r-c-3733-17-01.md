Eller v. Cont’l Inv. P’ship, 151 Ohio App.3d 729, 785 N.E.2d 802 (6th Dist. 2003) The park operator removed the re sident’s home from the park after the court had entered an eviction judgment against the resident, but before it had issued a writ of
restitution. The court of appeals reversed the summary judgment for the park operator, held that the park operator violat ed R.C. 3733.17, and remanded the case for a determination of damages.

Keesey v. Superior Mobile Homes, Inc., No. 2000-AP-080057, 2001 WL 293253 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Mar. 20, 2001) The court entered an eviction judgment against the resident and, under th e writ of restitution, the resident’s home was removed from the park. Thereafter, the court granted the resident’s Civil Rule 60(B) motion. The resident then filed an R.C. 3733.17 action against the park operator. “Because appellee [park operator] proceeded under a lawfully issued writ of restitution, the municipal court’s subsequent decision to vacate the judgment does not operate to retroactively transform a lawful act into a[n] unlawful one.” Therefore, on the R.C. 3733.17 claim, the court upheld thejudgment for the park operator.

Childers v. Russell, Nos. 01-4142,4145, 2002 WL 31950741 (Ohio C.P., Montgomery Cty., Feb. 7, 2002) The park operator terminated water service to two manufactured homes, after providing a 30-day notice of the park’s closing. The court held that the park operator violated R.C. 3733.17(A) and awarded damages to the residents.

Stein v. Fairview Cmty. Park, Co., No. 33061, 1981 WL 167766 (Ohio C.P., Cuyahoga Cty., Sept. 23, 1981) The park operator threatened to move a residen t’s home from the park. The court enjoined the park operator from moving or otherwise transporting the home and from seizing the resident’s personal possessions.

593 Callahan v. Croxford, No. 2006-CVF-00621, 2007 WL 4359907 (Ohio Mun., Athens Cty., July 31, 2007) The park operator removed the water meter (and thereby terminated the water service) for the resident’s manufactured home for two weeks. The court awarded the resident $100 compensatory damages and $500 punitive damages.

Shaw v. Mays Mobile Home Court , No. 87-CVE-233, 1987 WL 419803 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, Dec. 10, 1987) The court found that the park operator padlocked the resident’s home and seized the resident’s personal property in violation of R.C. 3733.17(A),(B) and 3733.10(A)(6).

The court found that the park operator’s actions were wilful, wanton, and malicious.
