# B. Conventional Defenses

## Source text

In an eviction action filed against a mh park tenant or a non -mh park tenant, the potential defenses include all of the defenses that are discussed in Chapter II, above.

Insofar as those defenses are based on R.C. Chapter 5321, they are potential defenses because these tenants are “tenants” under R.C. 5321.01(A).

In an eviction action filed against a mh park owner, the potential defenses include all of the defenses that are discussed in Chapter II, above, exce pt the defenses based on R.C. 5321.17(B). Insofar as these defenses are based on R.C. Chapter 5321, they are potential defenses because R.C. Chapter 4781 includes a corresponding provision to the relevant R.C. Chapter 5321 provision.

A chart of the corresponding provisions between R.C. Chapter 4781, former R.C.

Chapter 3733, and R.C. Chapter 5321 is set forth in Section XXI.B, below. Summaries of court decisions under R.C. Chapter 4781 (and former R.C. Chapter 3733) are set forth in Section XXI.C.4, below.

The defenses based on R.C. 5321.17(B) do not apply to a mh park owner because R.C. Chapter 4781 does not contain a provision similar to R.C. 5321.17(B). Schwartz
v. McAtee, 22 Ohio St.3d 14, 488 N.E.2d 479 (1986); May v. Sampsel , No. 06 -CA- 000034, 2007 WL 1651851 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 4, 2007);Cooper v. Curtis, No.

CA-8378, 1991 WL 147797 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., July 22, 1991).

As a result, a park operator may not terminate a month -to-month tenancy without
cause. Id.; Blue Water Manor v. Reyn olds, No. CVG-2100003 (Ohio Mun., Newton Falls, Mar. 11, 2021); Oldaker v. Warsaw Park Place, LLC, No. 2020-CI-0289 (Ohio C.P., Coshocton Cty., Feb. 26, 2021); Oak Park Estates LLC v. Burnley , No. 2015 - CVG-001007 (Ohio Mun., Wayne Cty., June 29, 2015); GS Holdings Brookside Ltd.
v. Rutherford, No. 13-CVG-1535 (Ohio Mun., Clark Cty., July 10, 2013); Daniels v.

Decker, No. 08-CVG-00042, 2008 WL 5537151 (Ohio County Ct., Harrison Cty., June 10, 2008); Stillberger v. Springman, No. 90-CVG-1644, 1990 WL 692200 (Ohio Mun., Findlay, Oct. 23, 1990); Superior Mobile Homes, Inc. v. Russell, No. 7-89-CVG-336, 1989 WL 572090 (Ohio Mun., New Philadelphia, Sept. 6, 1989).

For a discussion of the cause requirement for any termination of a mh park tenancy, see Section IV.C.1, below.
