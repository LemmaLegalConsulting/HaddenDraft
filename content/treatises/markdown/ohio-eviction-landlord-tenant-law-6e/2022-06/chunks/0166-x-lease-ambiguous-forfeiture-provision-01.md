# X. Lease, Ambiguous Forfeiture Provision

## Table-of-contents context

- II. Eviction Action, Common Defenses

## Source text

A lease is a contract and, therefore, is subject to the traditional rules governing contract interpretation. See Section VII.A, below.

When the language of a forfeiture provision in a lease is ambiguous, a court is required to construe it against the party who was responsible for d rafting it. Bevy’s Dry Cleaners & Shirt Laundry, Inc. v. Streble, 2 Ohio St.2d 250, 256, 208 N.E.2d 528 (1965); Sholiton Indus., Inc. v. Royal Arms, Ltd., No. 17480, 1999 WL 355898, at *6 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., June 4, 1999); McClorey v. Hamilton County Bd. of Elections, 130 Ohio App.3d 621, 625, 720 N.E.2d 954 (1st Dist. 1998); GMS Mgmt. Co. v.

Seminaro, No. 63007, 1993 WL 277073, at *3 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., July 22, 1993);

Cincinnati Metro. Hous. Auth. v. McCollum , 45 Ohio App.2d 197, 200, 341 N.E.2d 857 (1st Dist. 1975).

Likewise, when the punctuation of a forfeiture provision in a lease is ambiguous, a court is required to construe it against the party who was responsible for drafting it.

Farrell v. Deuble , 175 Ohio App.3d 646, 651, 888 N.E.2d 514 (9t h Dist. 2008);

Mandat v. Reinecker’s Bakery, No. 16241, 1993 WL 526653, at *3 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Dec. 15, 1993);Canady v. Cen. Benefits Mut. Ins. Co., 71 Ohio App.3d 363, 367, 594 N.E.2d 37 (10th Dist. 1991).

Therefore, with respect to most residentia l leases, any ambiguous forfeiture provision must be construed against the landlord, since the landlord typically is the party responsible for drafting it.

“Language [in a lease] is ambiguous if it is reasonably susceptible of two or more constructions.” McClorey v. Hamilton County Bd. of Elections, 130 Ohio App.3d 621, 625, 720 N.E.2d 954 (1st Dist. 1998). Accord Krantz v. Pahnke, No. 2021-CA-0043, 2022 WL 39486, ¶ 50 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Jan. 5, 2022).
