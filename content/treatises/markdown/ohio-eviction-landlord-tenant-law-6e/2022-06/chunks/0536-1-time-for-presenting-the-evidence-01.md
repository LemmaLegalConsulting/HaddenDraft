# 1. Time for Presenting the Evidence

## Source text

Attorney’s fees under R.C. Chapter 5321 are taxed as costs. See Section XIX.C, above.

“Because attorney fees [under R.C. Chapter 5321] are assessed as costs rather than as damages, a tenant is not required to offer evidence of the amount of those attorney fees at trial.” Gaitawe v. Mays, No. 25083, 2012 WL 4859073, ¶ 16 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 12, 2012). Accord Croxford v. Litchfield, No. 1491, 1992 WL 20818, at *1 (Ohio App., 4th Dis t., Jan. 27, 1992); Fant v. DiSabato , No. 87AP -265, 1987 WL 32190, at *3 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Dec. 29, 1987); Motz v. Liptak, No. CA-10310, 1987 WL 14269, at *3 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., July 9, 1987); Berlinger v. Suburban
Apt. Mgmt. Co. , 7 Ohio App.3d 12 2, 126, 454 N.E.2d 1367 (8th Dist. 1982). See generally Int’l Bd.of Elec. Workers, Local Union No. 8 v. Vaughn Indus., L.L.C., 116 Ohio St.3d 335, 879 N.E.2d 187, ¶¶ 14,15 (2007).

“An evidentiary hearing may be held to determine the reasonable amount of attorney fees to be awarded following a judgment finding that the tenant is entitled to attorney fees.” Gaitawe v. Mays, No. 25083, 2012 WL 4859073, ¶ 16 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 12, 2012).
