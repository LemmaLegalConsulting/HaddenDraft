# b. Fair Rental Value Claim

## Source text

The recovery for fair rental value plus deterioration or destruction is an exception to the otherwise exclusive remedy of filing an action under R.C. 5313.07 or R.C.

5313.08. Kennedy v. Kunze, No. 28978, 2019 WL 469767, ¶ 21 (Ohio App., 9th Dist.,
Feb. 6, 2019).

“[A] ‘fair renta l value claim’ is intended to compensate the vendor for any additional amount he could have earned during the period in which he was receiving payments under the contract .” Belknap v. Shears , No. 2012 -P-0007, 2012 WL 5995095, ¶ 29 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Dec. 3, 2012).

“Accordingly, it follows that the vendor must establish the fair rental value of the property during the period in which the payments were made. Generally, evidence only on the fair rental value of the property for a subsequent period will not meet this standard.” Id.

“The fair rental value is the amount a willing landlord rents the property for and what a reasonable tenant is willing to pay.” Voska v. Coffman , No. S -13-008, 2013 WL 6579576, ¶ 13 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Dec. 13, 2013). Accord Taylor v. Nickston Invs., No. 94 -APE02-259, 1995 WL 41638, at *2 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Jan. 26, 1995).

“The installment amount set forth in a land installment contract is evidence of its fair rental value.” Id. “Fair rental value may also includeunpaid taxes, insurance, and water charges.” Id.
