“‘It is well established thatpro se litigants are presumed to have knowledge of the law and legal procedures and that they are held to the same standard as litigants who are represented by counsel.’” State ex rel. Fuller v. Mengel, 100 Ohio St.3d 352, 354, 800 N.E.2d 25 (2003) (quotingSabouri v. Ohio Dep’t of Job & Family Serv., 145 Ohio App.3d 651, 654, 763 N.E.2d 1238 (2001)). Accord OH Seven LLC v. Lee, No. 2020- CA-00057, 2021 WL 286534, ¶ 11 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Jan. 28, 2021); Landings at Beckett Ridge v. Holmes, No. CA2020-04-050, 2020 WL 7690283, ¶ 32 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Dec. 28, 2020); Di Fiore v. Booker, No. 108946, 2020 WL 2992105, ¶ 15 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 4, 2020); Eckart v. Newman, No. WM-18-006, 2019 WL 3765412, ¶ 20 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Aug. 9, 2019); Holmes v. Cobblestone Grove ,
No. CA2016-04-075, 2017 WL 74986, ¶ 21 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Jan. 9, 2017);

Rasberry v. Taylor, No. 26510, 2013 WL 2327020, ¶ 6 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., May 29, 2013); Cook v. Criminger , No. 22313, 2005 WL 957023, ¶ 7 (Ohio App., 9th Dist.,
Apr. 27, 2005); Holman v. Keegan, 139 Ohio App.3d 911, 918, 746 N.E.2d 209 (6th
Dist. 2000); Meyers v. First Nat’l Bank of Cincinnati , 3 Ohio App.3d 209, 210, 444 N.E.2d 412 (1st Dist. 1981); Liggins v. Westminster Arms, Inc. , Nos. 98AP-377,378, 1998 WL 894613, at *3 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Dec. 22, 1998).

“A pro se litigant cannot expect or demand special treatment from the judge, who must be impartial.” Frew v. Frew, No. 2007-CA-17, 2008 WL 3856300 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Aug. 19, 2008). Accord Rasberry v. Taylor, No. 26510, 2013 WL 2327020, ¶ 6 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., May 29, 2013); Kilroy v. B.H. Lakeshore Co. , 111 Ohio App.3d 357, 363, 676 N.E.2d 171 (8th Dist. 1996).

“A pro se litigant ... must accept the results of his or her own errors or mistakes.” Holman v. Keegan , 139 Ohio App.3d 911, 918, 746 N.E.2d 209 (6th Dist. 2000).

Accord OH Seven LLC v. Lee , No. 2020 -CA-00057, 2021 WL 286534, ¶ 11 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Jan. 28, 2021); Landings at Beckett Ridge v. Holmes, No. CA2020-04- 050, 2020 WL 7690283, ¶ 32 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Dec. 28, 2020); Eckart v.

Newman, No. WM -18-006, 2019 WL 3765412, ¶ 20 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Aug. 9, 2019); Holmes v. Cobblestone Grove , No. CA2016 -04-075, 2017 WL 74986, ¶ 21 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Jan. 9, 2017);Cook v. Criminger, No. 22313, 2005 WL 957023, ¶ 7 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Apr. 27, 2005); Liggins v. Westminster Arms, Inc. , Nos.

98AP-377,378, 1998 WL 894613, at *3 (Ohio App., 10th Dist.,Dec. 22, 1998);Meyers
v. First Nat’l Bank of Cincinnati, 3 Ohio App.3d 209, 210, 444 N.E.2d 412 (1st Dist.

1981).

However, to “insure self-represented litigants the opportunity to have their matters fairly heard, a judge may make reasonable accommodation to a self-represented litigant consistent with the law.” Ohio Code of Judicial Conduct, Rule 2.2, Comment [4]. For example, the judge may provide “brief information about the proceeding and evidentiary and foundational requirements.”Id., Rule 2.6, Comment [1A]. E.g., Henry
v. Richardson , 193 Ohio App.3d 375, 382, 951 N.E.2d 1123 (12th Dist. 2011);

Cincinnati Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Morgan, 155 Ohio App.3d 189, 194, 800 N.E.2d 64 (1st Dist. 2003), rev’d on other grounds, 104 Ohio St.3d 445, 820 N.E.2d 315 (2004).

In an eviction action, the court is obligated under this law to treat unrepresented landlords the same as if they had retained counsel.Milano v. Jones, No. M8906-CVG- 20458 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., July 10, 1989). But see Wright v. Miller, No. 752, 1991 WL 37926 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Mar. 11, 1991).

The court may assist the unrepresented landlord in basic procedural matters, but not in the presentation of the case to the detriment of the tenant. Milano v. Jones, No.

M8906-CVG-20458 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., July 10, 1989). If an unrepresented landlord fails to prove the landlord’s case because of ignorance of the law, the case should be judged on the evidence that has been produced. Id. Absent a request by the landlord, the court shouldnot offer a continuance to the landlord to enable the landlord to prove the eviction claim at a subsequent hearing. Id.
