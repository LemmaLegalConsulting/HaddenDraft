# 1. Pet Deposits and Pet Fees

## Source text

A refundable pet deposit, given to secure pe rformance by the tenant under the lease, is a security deposit. Zeallear v. F & W Props. , No. 99AP -1215, 2000 WL 1015345 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., July 25, 2000); Pool v. Insignia Residential Group , 136 Ohio App.3d 266, 736 N.E.2d 507 (1st Dist. 1999).

Generally, a non -refundable, one-time pet fee is not a security deposit. Kopp v.

Associated Estates Realty Corp., No. 09AP-719, 2010 WL 1510196 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Apr. 15, 2010); Stauffer v. TGM Camelot, Inc., No. CA2005-12-508, 2006 WL 487 1974612 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., July 17, 2006);Ritter v. Fairway Park Props., L.L.C.,
No. 21509, 2004 WL 1104015 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., May 19, 2004); Zeallear v. F & W Props., No. 99AP-1215, 2000 WL 1015345 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., July 25, 2000).

Because the deposit is non-refundable, its purpose is not to secure performance under the lease. Id. Rather, “the fee’s purpose [is] for the contractual right to keep pets in the apartment.” Kopp, 2010 WL 1510196, ¶ 21. Accord Stauffer, 2006 WL 1974612, ¶ 27.

But three court decisions have held that, if the landlord’s purpose for imposing the non-refundable, one-time pet fee is to recoup the cost of property damage that pets cause, it is a security deposit. Leszczynski v. Brewer , No. 12523, 1991 WL 285435 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Dec. 27, 1991); Pool v. Insignia Residential Group, 136 Ohio App.3d 266, 736 N.E.2d 507 (1st Dist. 1999); Moore v. CM Props., No. 01-CV-4475, 2002 WL 34170615 (Ohio C.P., Montgomery Cty., Mar. 6, 2002).323 A non-refundable addition to the monthly rent payment, for the right to keep a pet in the rental premises, is not a security deposit. Pool v. Insignia Residential Group , 136 Ohio App.3d 266, 736 N.E.2d 507 (1st Dist. 1999).
