## Source text

Ohio appellate court decisions finding good cause to terminate an assisted housing tenancy, based on the criminal conduct of the tenant, a household member, or a guest, include those set forth below.
(1) Sandusky Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Jackson, No. S -19-046, 2020 WL 6377003 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Oct. 30, 2020) (Public Housing, tenant placed on community control for five years and, during that period, tested positive for marijuana on three occasions).
(2) Gorsuch Homes Inc. v. LeMaster , No. 10 -15-18, 2016 WL 3057971 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., May 31, 2016) (project-based subsidized housing, tenant assisted the trespass entry upon the premises by her husband).
(3) Cincinnati Metro. Hous. Auth. v . Patterson, No. C -130161, 2013 WL 6409302 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Dec. 6, 2013) (Public Housing, guest (tenant’s grandson), on two occasions, engaged in criminal conduct in the immediate vicinity of the premises – first, burglary of a neighbor’s unit; second, upon leaving tenant’s apartment, possession of marijuana in his pocket).
(4) Youngstown Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Scott , No. 99 -CA-238, 2001 WL 744496 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., June 26, 2001) (Public Housing, guest possessed drugs in the unit, without tenant’s knowledge).
(5) Lucas Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Carmony , No. L -00-1296, 2001 WL 672150 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 15, 2001) (Public Housing, guest possessed drugs in the unit, with tenant’s knowledge).
(6) Stark Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Wheaton , No. 1998-CA-00128, 1999 WL 34696 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Jan. 25, 1999) (Public Housing, guest (tenant’s son) engaged in drug activity near the unit).
