Actual Damages When a lease contains a liquidated damages provision for a breach of the lease, a court may not award both liquidated damages and actual damages. Mentor Lagoons,
Inc. v. Laity, No. 10-184, 1985 WL 9999 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., May 24, 1985). “If a valid stipulation is made in the contract for payment of liquid ated damages, the amount stipulated becomes the measure of damages. No further recovery may be had by the complaining party.” Id., 1985 WL 9999, at *1. Accord Carr v. Ed Stein Realtors, 10 Ohio App.3d 242, 243, 461 N.E.2d 930 (9th Dist. 1983) (“the forf eiture of the deposit … imposed in addition to the actual damage suffered is clearly an unlawful penalty”).
