In an R.C. Chapter 5321 claim for attorney’s fees, the moving party has the burden of proving the attorney services rendered, the reasonableness of the attorney’s hours, 555 the attorney’s hourly rate, and the attorney fee requ ested. Alcorso v. Correll , No.

110218, 2021 WL 4317396 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Sept. 23, 2021);Helfrich v. Mellon,
No. 06-CA-69, 2007 WL 1881537 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 27, 2007); Breault v.

Williamsburg Estates, No. L-86-116, 1986 WL 13169 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov. 21, 1986); Choi v. Katz, No. 44819, 1982 WL 2607 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 18, 1982);

Myers v. Loudy, No. 80-CA-8, 1980 Ohio App. LEXIS 11895 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist.,
Oct. 21, 1980). Cf. Enyart v. Columbus Metro. Area Cmty. Action Org. , 115 Ohio App.3d 348, 685 N.E.2d 550 (10th Dist. 1996) (R.C. 4123.90 claim).

The moving party must present “evidence going to the factors set forth in DR 2 - 106(B) [now Rule 1.5(a)].”Davis v. Reed, Nos. 68699,68700, 1996 WL 347814, at *7 (Ohio App., 8th Dis t., June 20, 1996). Most importantly, the moving party must present evidence of the number of attorney hours spent on the case, the type of services performed, and the fees customarily charged in the locality for similar services.Zamos
v. Dunn, No. 2000-P-0120, 2001 WL 1149844 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Sept. 28, 2001);

Myers v. Loudy, No. 80-CA-8, 1980 Ohio App. LEXIS 11895 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist.,
Oct. 21, 1980). See also Drake v. Menczer, 67 Ohio App.2d 122, 126, 425 N.E.2d 961 (8th Dist. 1980) (Krenzler, J., concurring).

A moving party’s failure to present evidence of the amount and nature of the attorney services provided and the reasonable value of that service is grounds for denying an attorney fee request. Myers v. Loudy , No. 80 -CA-8, 1980 Ohio App.

LEXIS 11895 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 21, 1980). See also Warner v. Zuccola, No.

53070, 1987 WL 30379 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 24, 1987).
