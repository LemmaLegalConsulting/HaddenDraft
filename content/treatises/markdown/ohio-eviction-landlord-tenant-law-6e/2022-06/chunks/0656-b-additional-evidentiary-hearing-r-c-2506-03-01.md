## Source text

The HCVP participant may supplement the evidentiary record via a hearing in the court of common pleas, if one or more of the criteria under R.C. 2506.03(A)(1) -(5)
apply. R.C. 2506.03. An R.C. 2506.03 hearing is mandatory, if the appellant shows that one or more of those criteria apply. City of Cleveland v. Posner, 193 Ohio App.3d 211, 951 N.E.2d 476, ¶¶ 37-39 (8th Dist. 2011).

While R.C. 2506.03(A)(1) expressly refers to an incomplete transcript, it also applies to cases in which the administrative agency fails to file any transcript.Woerner
v. Mentor Exempted Vill. Sch. Dist. Bd. of Educ., 84 Ohio App.3d 844, 619 N.E.2d 34 (11th Dist. 1993); Powell v. Meigs Local Sch. Dist., No. 502, 1993 WL 379085 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Sept. 8, 1993); Xetron Corp. v. Butler County Bd. of Zoning Appeals,
No. 87-12-163, 1988 WL 76814 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., July 25, 1988). In these cases, the court must hold an R.C. 2506.03 hearing in order to establish a record on which it may render a decision.407 Id.
