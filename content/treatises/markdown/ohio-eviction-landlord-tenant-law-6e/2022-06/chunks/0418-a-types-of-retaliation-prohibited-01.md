# A. Types of Retaliation Prohibited

## Table-of-contents context

## Source text

R.C. 5321.02(A) prohibits certain types of landlord retaliation against a tenant.

Subject to the exceptions established in R.C. 5321.03, it prohibits a landlord from increasing a tenant’s rent, decreasing the services that a tenant is entitled to recei ve under a rental agreement, or bringing or threatening to bring an eviction action against a tenant because the tenant engaged in one (or more) of three protected activities. The protected activities are:
(1) Making complaints to an appropriate governmental agency of a violation of a building, housing, health, or safety code that is applicable to the premises, where the violation materially affects health and safety;
(2) Making complaints to the landlord of any violation of R.C. 5321.04; or
(3) Joining with other te nants for the purpose of negotiating or dealing collectively with the landlord on any of the terms and conditions of a rental agreement.

R.C. 5321.02(A)(1)-(3).

The scope of this retaliation prohibition, and issues relative to proving such retaliation, are discussed in Section II.T, above.
