# C. Effect on Common Law

## Table-of-contents context

- V. Ohio Landlord-Tenant Act,
  - C. Effect on Common Law

## Source text

“The thrust of the new act [R.C. Chapter 5321] is to abrogate t he Ohio common law of caveat emptorand independent covenants and interpret residential leases in light of modern contract law.” Ricciardo v. Weber, No. CA-3452, 1989 WL 155719, at *5 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Dec. 22, 1989) (quoting Comments, Ohio Residential Landlord Tenant Act, 8 Akron L. Rev. 519, 520-21 (1975)).

“Ohio’s Landlords and Tenants Act imposes duties on landlords which were absent at common law.” Miller v. Ritchie, 45 Ohio St.3d 222, 224, 543 N.E.2d 1265 (1989).

Accord Shroades v. Rental Home s, Inc., 68 Ohio St.2d 20, 21 -22, 427 N.E.2d 774 (1981).

It also provides tenants with remedies that were absent at common law. Shroades, 68 Ohio St.2d at 21-25.

However, “neither R.C. Chapter 5321 nor Shroades eliminated a tenant’s right to seek common law remedies.” Winston v. Wolery, No. 96-CA-2442, 1997 WL 28535, at *2 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Jan. 14, 1997).

“[T]he new remedies given tenants in R.C. Chapter 5321 are intended to be preventive and supplemental to other remedial measures.” Shroades v. Rental Homes, 293 Inc., 68 Ohio St.2d 20, 25, 427 N.E.2d 774 (1981). Accord Ayres v. Simmons, No. 93- CA-827, 1993 WL 405489 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Sept. 30, 1993) (implied covenant of quiet enjoyment under common law); Meacham v. Miller , 79 Ohio App.3d 35, 606 N.E.2d 996 (4th Dist. 1992) (tort claims under common law); Cent. Mut. Ins. Co. v.

Faller, 71 Ohio Misc.2d 58, 654 N.E.2d 213 (Ohio C.P., Licking Cty., 1995) (contract claim for damages).

To find that the Act abrogates a longstanding rule of common law , a court must find an intent to do so “expressly declared by the legislature or necessarily implied in the language of the statute.” LaCourse v. Fleitz, 28 Ohio St.3d 209, 212, 503 N.E.2d 159 (1986). Applying this standard, court decisions have found th at the Act does not abrogate certain rules of common law under which a landlord is relieved of a duty.

E.g., LaCourse v. Fleitz, 28 Ohio St.3d 209, 212, 503 N.E.2d 159 (1986) (landlord is not obligated to keep common areas clear of natural accumulations o f ice and snow);

Plush v. Nat’l Church Residences, No. L -89-293, 1990 WL 106437 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., July 27, 1990) (landlord is not obligated to remove natural accumulations of leaves from common areas). See Section VII.C.4, below.
