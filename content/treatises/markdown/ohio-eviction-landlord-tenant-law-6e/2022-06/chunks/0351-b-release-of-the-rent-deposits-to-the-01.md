Landlord, R.C. 5321.09,.10 R.C. 5321.09 establishes the requirements and procedures for a landlord to secure the release of the R.C. 5321.07 rent deposits to the landlord.

The landlord may apply directly to the clerk of court for the release of the rent deposits, if the tenant has provided the clerk of court with a written notice stating that the condition specified in the R.C. 5321.07 notice has been remedied. R.C.

5321.09(A)(1). In these circumstances, the clerk of court is required to “forthwith release the rent deposits, less costs, to the landlord.” Id. For a discussion on the costs, see Section VIII.D, below.

Alternatively, the landlord may file an application with the court for the release of the rent deposits if one (or more) of the R.C. 5321.09(A)(2),(3) grounds for release is
applicable. Those grounds are:
(1) the tenant did not comply with the notice requirement under R.C.

5321.07(A);
(2) the tenant was not current in rent payments at the time that the tenant initiated the rent depositing;
(3) when the tenant initiated the rent depositing, the landlord was not in violation of R.C. 5321.04, the rental agreement, or the applicable codes;

or
(4) the condition specified in the R.C. 5321.07 notice has been remedied.294 The R.C. 5321.09(A)(2),(3) application is a complaint under the Ohio Rules of Civil Procedure. Anderson v. MRW II Prop., No. RE 541 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty.,
Jan. 9, 1990). See also Woodside Terrace Mobile Home Owners As s’n v. Woodside Terrace Co. Ltd. , 53 Ohio App.3d 20, 557 N.E.2d 804 (6th Dist. 1988) (construing R.C. 3733.122 (now R.C. 4781.43) – the manufactured home park provision for rent deposit release that is essentially identical to R.C. 5321.09); Stoots v. Huber Mobile Home Park, No. 1180, 1981 WL 2863 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., July 27, 1981) (same).

Stoyer v. Fogelman , No. 11AP -737, 2012 WL 10596 77 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 27, 2012).

The complaint must name the tenant as a party. R.C. 5321.09(B). And it must be served on the tenant in accordance with Civil Rules 4.1 and 4.6.See Woodside Terrace Mobile Home Owners Ass’n v. Woodside Terrace Co. Ltd. , 53 Ohio App.3d 20, 557 N.E.2d 804 (6th Dist. 1988); Stoots v. Huber Mobile Home Park, No. 1180, 1981 WL 2863 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., July 27, 1981).

In an R.C. 5321.09 action, the tenant may file an answer and counterclaims as in other civil actions. R.C. 5321.09(B). See Ferrato v. Maurer Realty, No. WD-86-28, 1986 WL 13815 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Dec. 5, 1986). Typically, the tenant’s counterclaims will include one or more claims that are based on the defective condition at the premises for which the tenant commenced the rent deposit procedure. For a discussion on some of those potential claims, see Chapter IX, below.

If an R.C. 5321.09 countercla im exceeds the municipal court’s monetary jurisdiction, an issue exists as to whether the municipal court has exclusive jurisdiction over the R.C. 5321.09 claim only, the R.C. 5321.09 claim and the counterclaim, or neither of them. In two such cases, without expressly addressing this issue, the courts certified the R.C. 5321.09 claim and the counterclaim to the court of common pleas.

Stoyer v. Fogelman, No. 11AP-737, 2012 WL 1059677 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar.

27, 2012); Bright Roads Assocs. v. Homoelle, No. 95-APE10-1361, 1996 WL 125551 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 19, 1996).

The trial on the landlord’s R.C. 5321.09 claim for full release of the rent deposits, and on the tenant’s counterclaims (if any), must be held within sixty days of the filing of the landlord’s R.C. 5321.09 complaint. R.C. 5321.09(B), as modified by Chernin
v. Welchans , 844 F.2d 322 (6th Cir. 1988) (striking down as unconstitutional the portion of R.C. 5321.09(B) that permits the court to continue the hearing date beyond the 60 -day period). See also Ferrato v. Maurer Realty , No. WD -86-28, 1986 WL 13815, at *2 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Dec. 5, 1986).

The court may not order a release of the rent deposits under R.C. 5321.09 unless it specifically finds, after conducting a trial, that at least one of the R.C.

5321.09(A)(2),(3) grounds for release is met. See Woodside Terrace Mobile Home Owners Ass’n v. Woodside Terrace Co. Ltd., 53 Ohio App.3d 20, 557 N.E.2d 804 (6th
Dist. 1988). This requirement is not met if the court merely finds the landlord’s “application for release of rent deposits well -taken, without stating any ground therefor.” Id. at 22.

In an R.C. 5321.09 action, costs may not be assessed against the tenant unless the court specifically finds that the tenant is liable for suc h costs under R.C. 5321.09(D) 389 (see Section VIII.E, below). See Woodside Terrace Mobile Home Owners Ass’n v.

Woodside Terrace Co. Ltd., 53 Ohio App.3d 20, 557 N.E.2d 804 (6th Dist. 1988).

During the pendency of an R.C. 5321.09 action (including any appea l), the landlord may file an application under R.C. 5321.10 for a partial release of the rent deposits, in order to meet certain operating expenses. Blum v. Pennington, No. 42422, 1981 WL 10409 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 5, 1981). Those expenses are “pa yment of the periodic interest on a mortgage on the premises, the periodic principal payments on a mortgage on the premises, the insurance premiums for the premises, real estate taxes on the premises, utility services, repairs, and other customary and usual costs of operating the premises as a rental unit.” R.C. 5321.10.

“The pendency of an R.C. 5321.09 action is a jurisdictional prerequisite for the filing of an R.C. 5321.10 proceeding.” Blum v. Pennington , No. 42422, 1981 WL 10409, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 5, 1981).

Prior to ruling on a landlord’s R.C. 5321.10 application for partial release, the court must provide the tenant with an opportunity to be heard regarding the application.

Blum v. Pennington, No. 42422, 1981 WL 10409 (Ohio App., 8thDist., Feb. 5, 1981).

In determining whether to grant a landlord’s R.C. 5321.10 application for partial release, the court must consider, among other things, “the costs which may be required to remedy the condition contained in the [R.C. 5321.07] notice ....” R.C. 5321.10(B).

Generally, a trial court’s order granting a landlord’s R.C. 5321.10 application for partial release is not a final appealable order. Pratt v. Dykema , Nos. 44624,44625, 44626, 1981 WL 4724 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 31, 1981). How ever, if the trial court repeatedly issues orders granting a partial release of the tenant’s accumulated rent deposits, the trial court’s order granting another partial release may be a final appealable order. Id.

R.C. 5321.10 is the only provision for a partial release of the rent deposits to the
landlord. Stewart v. Kalman, Nos. 43-57 (Ohio Mun., Athens, Aug. 12, 1981),aff’d in part, rev’d in part on other grounds and remanded, No. 1109, 1982 WL 3509 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Aug. 13, 1982). Neither R.C. 5321.07 nor R.C. 5321.09 authorizes a partial release of the rent deposits to the landlord. Id.
