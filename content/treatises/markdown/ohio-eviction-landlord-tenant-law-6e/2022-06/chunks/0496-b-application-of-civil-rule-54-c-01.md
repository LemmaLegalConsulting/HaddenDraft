When the complaint demands both a specified amount for past due rent and a specified amount per month for ongoing rent, the amount of the default judgment for the landlord may include the respective amounts specified for past du e rent and ongoing rent up to the date of restitution. Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Coley, No.

2001-CVG-19335 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., May 2, 2003, and Jan. 29, 2004). It may not include, for any month during the pendency of the action, a ren tal amount more than the specified ongoing rent. Id.

When the complaint demands only a specified amount for past due rent and late payment fees and the filing fee, the amount of the default judgment for the landlord may not include any rent that accrues d uring the pendency of the action. Masny v.

Vallo, No. 84983, 2005 WL 1048237 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 5, 2005).

A tenant may file a Civil Rule 60(B)(5) motion to enforce these Civil Rule 54(C)
limits. Masny v. Vallo , No. 84983, 2005 WL 1048237, ¶ 20 ( Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 5, 2005). A “trial court’s failure to comply with Civ.R. 54(C) constitutes a sufficient ground under Civ.R. 60(B)(5) justifying relief from judgment.” Id.
