## Source text

Service If a landlord is not required to pay for the water service under R.C. 5321.04(A)(6) or the PUCO rule, and if the rental agreement requires the tenant to pay for the water service and the tenant fails to do so, R.C. 5321.04(A)(1) may still require the landlord to pay for the water service. See Bess v. McBride , No. 96 -CVH-1586 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., May 6, 1997) (“Whether the tenant has paid for the water or not,” City of Cleveland ordinances, tog ether with R.C. 5321.04(A)(1), make the landlord “primarily liable for assuring that the water remains flowing”).

Also, if a landlord is not required to pay for the water service under R.C.

5321.04(A)(6) or the PUCO rule, and if two or more dwelling units receive the water service through a mastermeter, the landlord might require one of the tenants to contract for the water service and the other tenant(s) to reimburse that tenant for their share of the water bill. If a landlord imposes such requirements and one tenant fails to pay the tenant’s share of the water bill, the landlord may be liable for a breach of the implied covenant of quiet enjoyment. See Roger Colton & Janet Labella, Landlord Failure to Resolve Shared Meter Problem Breaches Tenant’s Right to Quiet Enjoyment , 29 Clearinghouse Rev. 536 (1995).
