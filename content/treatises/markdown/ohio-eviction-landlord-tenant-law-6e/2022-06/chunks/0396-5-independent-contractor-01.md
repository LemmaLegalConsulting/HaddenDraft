# 5. Independent Contractor

## Table-of-contents context

## Source text

When a landlord employs an independent contractor to make repairs in compliance with R.C. 5321.04(A), the landlord is liable to the tenant for any negligent acts of the independent contractor in the performance of those repairs. Strayer v. Lindeman, 68 Ohio St.2d 32, 427 N.E.2d 781 (1981). See also Wallenstein v. Marsol Towers , No.

58822, 1991 WL 378192 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug. 8, 1991);Simpson v. Delta Prop.

Mgmt., Inc., Nos. C -850562,850575, 1986 WL 7136 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., June 25, 1986).

“[N]otice [to the landlord] of the defect is not required to impute that liability to the landlord.” Kroeger v. East End Constr. Co. , No. 14080, 1994 WL 100698, at *3 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Mar. 2, 1994). Accord Wallace v. Golden Comb, Inc., 4 N.E.3d 445, 2013-Ohio-5320 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., 2013). But see Hodge v. Lackey, No. 93- T-4947, 1995 WL 378700 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., May 12, 1995).

The Strayer doctrine does not extend to an independent contractor’s repair of cable equipment if the cable company hired the independent contractor. Lee v. Babeli, No.

2000-CA-00371, 2001 WL 705558 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., May 29, 2001).
