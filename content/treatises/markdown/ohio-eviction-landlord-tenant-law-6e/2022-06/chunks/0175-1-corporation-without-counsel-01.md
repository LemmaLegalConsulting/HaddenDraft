# 1. Corporation Without Counsel

## Source text

A corporation may not maintain litigation or appear in court except by and through an attorney. Union Sav. Ass’n v. Home Owners Aid, Inc., 23 OhioSt.2d 60, 262 N.E.2d 558 (1970). See also Bd. of Educ. of Worthington City Sch. Dist. v. Bd. of Revision of Franklin County, 85 Ohio St.3d 156, 707 N.E.2d 499 (1999).

Therefore, a corporation may not file or maintain an eviction action without an
attorney. Sheridan Mobile Vill., Inc. v. Larsen, 78 Ohio App.3d 203, 604 N.E.2d 217 (4th Dist. 1992); Fone-X, Inc. v. Strohm, No. CA-3653, 1991 WL 261824 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Nov. 12, 1991); YWCA of Canton, Inc. v. Hager , No. CA-7351, 1988 WL 29658 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Mar. 7, 1988); Mentor Green Mobile Estates v. Shopak,
No. 15-CVG-761 (Ohio Mun., Mentor, Oct. 6, 2015);Crabtree Invs., Inc. v. Clevinger,
No. 99-CVG-289 (Ohio Mun., Hocking, Aug. 26, 1999);PGS Rentals, Inc. v. Hileman,
No. CVG91-0528 (Ohio Mu n., Portsmouth, June 24, 1991); Crystal Tower Apts. v.

Adkins, No. 83-CVG-223 (Ohio Mun., East Cleveland, Feb. 25, 1983); Philnat Corp.
v. McCune, No. R79-CVG-0725 (Ohio Mun., Portage Cty., June 28, 1979).

If a corporation files or maintains an evictio n action without an attorney, it is grounds to dismiss the action.160 Id. See Nat’l Church Publ’ns v. Hiser, No. CA-2388, 1986 WL 6988 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 12, 1986).

When a non -attorney files an eviction complaint on behalf of a corporation, the defect is not cured by either: (1) an attorney representing the corporation at the eviction hearing, Larsen, 78 Ohio App.3d at 205; Tr. of Kokosing Valley Trust v. Myers , No.

13-CVG-00428 (Ohio Mun., Mt. Vernon, Aug. 19, 2013) (trust); or (2) an amendment of the complaint to substitute an attorney’s signature for the non-attorney’s signature, Fone-X, Inc. v. Strohm, No. CA-3653, 1991 WL 261824 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Nov.

12, 1991).

The case law is split on whether the defect deprives the court of subject -matter jurisdiction or is an affirmative defense that may be waived.Compare Alliance Group,
Inc. v. Rosenfield , 115 Ohio App.3d 380, 685 N.E.2d 570 (1st Dist. 1996) (subject - matter jurisdiction), and Iberia Cabinet Mfg. Co. v. Walston , 121 Ohio Misc.2d 121, 782 N.E.2d 1259 (Ohio Mun., Marion, 2002) with Rental Mgmt. v. Au , No. 92-C-66, 1993 WL 307614 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., Aug. 10, 1993) (affirmative defense), and Two-32 Enters., Inc. v. Taylor , No. 91 -CA-2917, 1992 WL 274731 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Sept. 9, 1992).

If a counterclaim is served prior to the dismissal of a complaint under this defense, the counterclaim may survive the dismissal of the complaint. See Alliance Group, Inc.
v. Rosenfield, 115 Ohio App.3d 380, 685 N.E.2d 570 (1st Dist. 1996).
