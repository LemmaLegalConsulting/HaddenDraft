Landlord’s Failure to Make Repairs In the Housing Choice Voucher program (“HCVP”), the public housing authority (“PHA”) makes rent subsidy payments to a private landlord on behalf of a tenant pursuant to a housing assistance payments (“HAP”) contract. 24 C.F.R. § 982.451.

If a landlord fails to maintain the premises in compliance with the HQS, the PHA may: (1) abate the rent subsidy payments, while maintaining the HAP contract in full force and effect, and/or (2) terminate the HAP contract. Typically, a PHA abates the rent subsidy payments for a specified period of time ( e.g., thirty days) and, if the landlord fails to remedy the HQS violation during the abatement period, the PHA then terminates the HAP contract.

When a PHA abates the rent subsidy payments, the tenant’s payment of the tenant’s portion of the contract rent,224 during the period of the abatement, precludes an eviction for nonpayment of rent. 24 C.F.R. §§ 982.310(b), 982.451(b)(4)(iii).E.g., Weintraub v. Super. 151, 634 A.2d 1057 (1993). Cf. Denning v. Swanstrom , No. A -97- 761, 1998 WL 867250 (Neb. App. Nov. 3, 1998) (money claim).

When a PHA terminates the HAP contract, typically, the landlord will demand rent payments in an amount equal to the full contract rent, the tenant will be unable to make those rent payments, and the landlord will then file an eviction action for nonpayment of rent.225 In these circumstances, there are at least three potential eviction defenses.

The second potential defense is that the tenant should n ot be liable for, and thus should not be evicted for nonpayment of, the subsidy portion of the rent ( i.e., the amount of HCVP rent subsidy not paid due to the termination of the HAP contract).

The tenant should not be liable for the subsidy portion of the rent for two reasons.
