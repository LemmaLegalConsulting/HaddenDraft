“Appellate courts in this state are split on the meaning of the term ‘future rent,’ as used in this context.” Ebbing v. Mathis, No. CA2013-01-014, 2013 WL 4806940, ¶19 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Sept. 9, 2013). Accord Urban P’ship Bank v. Mosezit Acad., Inc., No. 100712, 2014 WL 4244346, ¶ 15 n.5 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug. 28, 2014).

Some courts have held that fu ture rent means a rent payment for any period of occupancy that is “after the date of the landlord’s acceptance of the payment” (“Carter standard”). Sheridan Manor Apts. v. Carter , No. 92 -CA-4, 1992 WL 388539, at *2 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Dec. 22, 1992). Accord Urban P’ship Bank v. Mosezit Acad., Inc., No. 100712, 2014 WL 4244346, ¶ 15 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug. 28, 2014);IDC Brunswick Crossroads, LLC v. Gack, Inc., No. 10CA-0088-M, 2012 WL 177997, ¶ 11 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Jan. 23, 2012); Sholiton Indus., Inc. v. Royal Arms, Ltd. , No.

17480, 1999 WL 355898, at *7 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., June 4, 1999).

Other courts have held that, “[i]n this context, ‘future’ [rent] payments includes payment for any period subsequent to the three-day [R.C. 1923.04(A)]eviction notice” (“Thomas standard”). Knights Ctr. Corp. v. Thomas, No. 49636, 1985 WL 9039, at *1 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 23, 1985). Accord Ondrick v. Courim, No. 98-CVG-00063 (Ohio County Ct., Belmont Cty., July 8, 1998). See also Colbert v. McLem ore, No.

81961, 2003 WL 21434169, ¶ 9 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 19, 2003).

The following three examples illustrate these standards. First, if a landlord serves a tenant with a notice to vacate on February 6 and the landlord accepts the tenant’s tender of January and February rent on February 10, the landlord has accepted future rent under either standard. Under theCarter standard, the landlord has accepted future rent for the period February 11 to 28. Under the Thomas standard, the landlord has accepted future rent for the period February 7 to 28.

Second, if a landlord serves a tenant with a notice to vacate on February 6 and the landlord accepts the tenant’s tender of January rent on February 10, the landlord has not accepted future rent under either standard. The January rent is neither rent for a period after February 10 ( Carter standard) nor rent for a period after February 6 (Thomas standard).

Third, if a landlord serves a tenant with a notice to vacate on February 6 and the landlord accepts the t enant’s tender of January and February rent on March 1, the landlord has accepted future rent under one of the two standards. Under the Thomas standard, the landlord has accepted future rent for the period February 7 to 28. Under the Carter standard, the landlord has not accepted future rent because the landlord has not accepted rent for any period after March 1.

Under either standard, the determination on whether the landlord has accepted future rent is generally a question of fact. Mitchell v. Walker , No. C -050546 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Dec. 6, 2006); Colbert v. McLemore, No. 81961, 2003 WL 21434169 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 19, 2003); Associated Estates Corp. v. Bartell , 24 Ohio App.3d 6, 492 N.E.2d 841 (8th Dist. 1985);Presidential Park Apts. v. Colston, 17 Ohio Op.3d 220 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., 1980).

When a tenant has been depositing rent with the court du ring the pendency of an eviction action, and the court dismisses the eviction action without prejudice and orders the release of the rent to the landlord without prejudice to the right of the landlord to pursue another eviction action, the landlord’s accep tance of those rent deposits does not constitute acceptance of future rent, relative to the notice to vacate. Wurzelbacher
v. Johnson, No. CA92-03-048, 1993 WL 19538 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Feb. 1, 1993).

When a tenancy is terminated during a period for which rent has been prepaid, the landlord’s failure to return the prepaid rent for the post-termination period may waive the notice to vacate. 1994-N1 Ohio Assocs., L.P. v. Planet Earth Entm’t, Inc. , No.

15338, 1995 WL 738416, at *5 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Dec. 15, 1995). In such cases, the relevant issue is whether the landlord’s action of failing to return the prepaid rent “was so inconsistent with the notice to vacate as to amount to a waiver of the notice.”
Id. The finding on this issue is a question of fact. Id.

In 1994-N1 Ohio Assocs., L.P. , the tenant prepaid rent in the amount of $50,000 for one year, the landlord terminated the tenancy approximately four months into that year, and the landlord did not tender repayment of the prepaid rent for the p ost- termination period. Id., 1995 WL 738416, at *1,5. The court also considered the time required for completion of the eviction process and the landlord’s related damage claims against the tenant. Id., 1995 WL 738416, at *5. The court held that, “[a]lthough it is a close call, in our view, the trial court could have reasonably concluded that Ohio Associates [landlord] did not take any action, including the retention of the Planet Earth’s [tenant’s] prepaid rent, which was so inconsistent with the noticeto vacate as to amount to a waiver of that notice.” Id.

127 The decision in Summit Mgmt. Servs. v. Gough, No. 19714, 2000 WL 1226605, at *2 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Aug. 30, 2000), erroneously holds that a notice to vacate always “must specify a [vacation] date after the expiration of the term for which rent is already paid.” The Gough decision is erroneous because it fails to distinguish between a failure to terminate the tenancy, which always precludes service of the notice to vacate, and a prepayment of ren t, which precludes service of the notice to vacate only when it precludes a termination of the tenancy.

The Gough decision’s reliance onHile v. Besecker, 82 Ohio App. 301, 79 N.E.2d 582 (2nd Dist. 1947), andSchmidt v. Hummell, 81 Ohio App. 167, 73 N.E.2d 806 (10th
Dist. 1947), is misplaced for this reason. BothHile and Schmidt concerned a payment of rent in a month -to-month tenancy that renewed the tenancy for another month. In those cases, the prepayment of rent invalidated the notice to vacate only because the prepayment of rent precluded the termination of the month-to-month tenancy. In other cases, especially those concerning a lease with a fixed term, a prepayment of rent does not preclude a termination of the tenancy. E.g., 1994-N1 Ohio Assocs., L.P. v. Planet Earth Entm’t, Inc., No. 15338, 1995 WL 738416 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Dec. 15, 1995).

The Gough decision’s reliance on Bristol Court v. Jones , No. 93 -CA-520, 1994 WL 534920 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Sept. 29, 1994), is misplaced because Bristol Court concerns acceptance of future rent after service of the notice to vacate, which waives the notice to vacate for the reasons discussed in this Section II.C.1.
