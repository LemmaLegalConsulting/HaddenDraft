## Source text

R.C. 5321.05(A)(6) requires a tenant to refrain personally and to forbid any other person who is on the premises with the tenant’s permission from intentionally or negligently damaging the premises.

“R.C. 5321.05(A)(6) does not require the tenant to preve nt damage to the leased premises, but rather personally to refrain from such activity and to forbid others from engaging in it.” DAK, PLL v. Borgerding, No. 02AP-1051, 2003 WL 21469136, ¶ 18 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., June 26, 2003).

To constitute a violat ion of R.C. 5321.05(A)(6), the property damage must be beyond normal wear and tear. Whitestone Co. v. Stittsworth, No. 06AP-371, 2007 WL 155299, ¶ 27 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Jan. 23, 2007).

For a discussion on normal wear and tear, see Section XVIII.B, below.

A tenant’s failure to report defective conditions that are causing damage to the rental unit may constitute negligent destruction of the property under R.C.

5321.05(A)(6). Bailey v. Fairchild , No. 10 -CA-10, 2010 WL 4867532 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Nov. 24, 2010) (damage resulting from water leaking from a shower where tiles had fallen off and water entering the basement through a sewer).

Damage to the premises by a child under seven years of age cannot, by itself, constitute an R.C. 5321.05(A)(6) violat ion. As a matter of law, these children are incapable of the requisite negligence or intent. See DeLuca v. Bowden, 42 Ohio St.2d 392, 329 N.E.2d 109 (1975). However, in such circumstances, the tenant’s negligent supervision of the child may be the basis for the R.C. 5321.05(A)(6) violation.
