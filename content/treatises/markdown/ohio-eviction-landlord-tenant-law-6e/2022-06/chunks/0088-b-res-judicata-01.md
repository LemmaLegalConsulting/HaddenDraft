The doctrine of res judicata (i.e., claim preclusion) does not apply to an eviction action, relative to any non -eviction claims between the parties. 65 R.C. 1923.03 65 For a discussion on permissive joinder of eviction and non -eviction claims, see Section I.E.2.f, above.

provides that “[j]udgments under this chapter are not a bar to a later action brought by either party.” Consequently, “a judgment in a forcible entry and detainer action does not bar a later action between the same parties arising out of the same subject matter.” Marous/Church, LLC v. Stanich , No. 2000 -L-188, 2001 WL 1561107, at *2 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Dec. 7, 2001). Accord N.T.I. Enters., Ltd. v. Dental Prods. & Servs.,
Inc. No. 2018 -T-0018, 2019 WL 117829 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Jan. 7, 201 9);

McAlpine v. Patrick , No. 86453, 2006 WL 562191 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 9, 2006); Great Lakes Mall, Inc. v. Deli Table, Inc. , No. 93 -L-154, 1994 WL 587559 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Sept. 16, 1994). See also Hamilton Brownfields Redevelopment, LLC v. Duro Tire & Wheel, 156 Ohio App.3d 525, 806 N.E.2d 1039 (12th Dist. 2004); Haney v. Roberts, 130 Ohio App.3d 293, 720 N.E.2d 101 (4th Dist.

1998); Reck v. Whalen, 114 Ohio App.3d 16, 682 N.E.2d 721 (2nd Dist. 1996); Chef Italiano Corp. v. Crucible Dev. Cor p., No. CV -2003-12-7277, 2004 WL 5491217 (Ohio C.P., Summit Cty., Oct. 28, 2004).

Courts are split on whether a final eviction judgment on the merits 66 precludes relitigation of the propriety of the eviction judgment. Compare Tillimon v. Rideout ,
No. L -00-1388, 2001 WL 1518111, at *2 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov. 30, 2001)
(precluded) with Machshonba v. Cleveland Metro. Hous. Auth., No. 96811, 2011 WL 6920728, ¶¶ 11-13 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 29, 2011) (not precluded).

But res judicata does not precludea second eviction action for new grounds, which arise after the first eviction action. Showe Mgmt. Corp. v. Hazelbaker, No. CA2006- 01-004, 2006 WL 3479856 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Dec. 4, 2006). The second eviction action may be filed even during a pending appeal in the first eviction action, where the appeal is from a judgment entered in favor of the landlord but stayed pending the
appeal. Id.

A related issue, which the courts have not yet addressed directly, is whether res judicata bars a second eviction action for grounds that existed before the filing of the first eviction action but were not raised in the first eviction action. While general res judicata law would bar a second eviction action for these grounds, R.C. 1923.03 may require a different result.
