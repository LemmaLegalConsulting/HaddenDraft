# d. Special Damages

## Source text

In R.C. 5321.04 contract claims, a tenant may recover any special damages that naturally and directly result from the landlord’s R.C. 5321.04(A) violation.

Vanderpool v. Waddell, No. 1822, 1987 WL 19853, at *3 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Nov.

12, 1987); Allen v. Lee , 43 Ohio App.3d 31, 33, 538 N.E.2d 1073 (8th Dist. 1987) (general contract principles apply). Cf. F. Enters., Inc. v. Kentucky Fried Chicken, 47 Ohio St.2d 154, 351 N.E. 2d 121 (1976) (commercial tenancy); Brown v. Spitzer Chevrolet Co., 181 Ohio App.3d 642, 910 N.E.2d 490 (5th Dist. 2009) (same).

Special damages include consequential damages if they result from the violation in the usual course of events or were otherwise foreseeable. Foard v. Severcool, 22 Ohio Law Abs. 650, 1 Ohio Op. 492 (Ohio C.P., Lucas Cty., 1933). See also Detling
v. Edelbrock, 671 S.W.2d 265 (Mo. 1984) (implied warranty of habitability);Mease v.

Fox, 200 N.W.2d 791 (Iowa 1972) (same). Cf. R & H Trucking, Inc. v. Occidental Fire & Cas. Co. , 2 Ohio App.3d 269, 441 N.E.2d 816 (10th Dist. 1981) (insurance contract); Markowitz & Co. v. Toledo Metro. Hous. Auth., 608 F.2d 699 (6th Cir. 1979) (breach of agreement to lease, applying Ohio law).

One example of these consequential damages is damage to the tenant’s personal property caused by the landlord’s R.C. 5321.04(A) violation. E.g., Marsh v. Lansing Gardens Apts., No. 07-BE-32, 2008 WL 2633467 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., July 2, 2008);

Stiffler v. Canterb ury Runn Apts., No. 19308, 2002 WL 31243541 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 4, 2002); Johnson v. Rahim , No. 2019 -CVG-008127 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Jan. 21, 2020); Leak v. Hale , No. 2006 -CVG-33367 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Jan. 10, 2008); Williams v. Wright , No. 2000 -CVG- 01040 (Ohio Mun., Marietta, Aug. 15, 2001); Kenwood Courts Apts. v. Williams, No.

K90-CVG-1043 (Ohio Mun., Portage Cty., Oct. 25, 1991); Glyco v. Schultz, 35 Ohio
Misc. 25, 289 N.E.2d 919 (Ohio Mun., Sylvania, 1972). See also Hall v. Myers , 49 Ohio Law Abs. 345, 77 N.E.2d 81 (Ohio Mun., Piqua, 1947) (breach of lease agreement); Miller v. Christian , 958 F.2d 1234 (3d Cir. 1992) (implied warranty of habitability); Darmetko v. Boston Hous. Auth., 378 Mass. 758, 393 N.E.2d 395 (1979) (same); Kipsborough Realty Corp. v. Goldbetter, 81 Misc.2d 1054, 367 N.Y.S.2d 916
(Civ. Ct. 1975) (same).

For a discussion on the measure of damages for damage to personal property, see Section XVIII.C.2, below.

A second of these consequentialdamages is moving expenses, when the landlord’s 419 R.C. 5321.04(A) violation has caused the tenant to move. E.g., Marsh v. Lansing Gardens Apts., No. 07-BE-32, 2008 WL 2633467 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., July 2, 2008);

Stiffler v. Canterbury Run n Apts., No. 19308 , 2002 WL 31243541 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 4, 2002); Allen v. Lee , 43 Ohio App.3d 31, 538 N.E.2d 1073 (8th Dist.

1987); Johnson v. Rahim, No. 2019-CVG-008127 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div.,
Jan. 21, 2020); Kenwood Courts Apts. v. Williams , No. K90-CVG-1043 (Ohio Mun., Portage Cty., Oct. 25, 1991).

A tenant may not recover special damages for moving expenses if the moving costs are not the result of the landlord’s R.C. 5321.04(A) violation. Harris v. Winston, No.

2004-CVG-28660 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Mar. 16, 2005).

A third example of these consequential damages is the tenant’s cost in transferring and reconnecting phone service and cable television service, when the landlord’s R.C.

5321.04(A) violation has caused the tenant to move. E.g., Marsh v. Lansing Gardens Apts., No. 07-BE-32, 2008 WL 2633467 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., July 2, 2008).

A fourth example of these consequential damages is excessive utility bills resulting from the defective condition at the premises. E.g., Householder v. Edgar, No. 06 - CVG-687 (Ohio County Ct., Tuscarawas, Mar. 6, 2007); Waterford Glen LLC v.

Dowell, No. 05-CVG-1055 (Ohio Mun., Crawford Cty., Apr. 7, 2006) (R.C. Chapter 3733 (now R.C. Chapter 4781) action). See also Fair v. Negley , 257 Pa. Super. 50, 390 A.2d 240 (1978).

A fifth example of these consequential damages is the tenant’s cost in remedying the R.C. 5321.04(A) violation. E.g., Householder v. Edgar, No. 06-CVG-687 (Ohio County Ct., Tuscarawas, Mar. 6, 2007) (furnace repair); Harris v. Winston, No. 2004- CVG-28660 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Mar. 16, 2005) (furnace repair);

McClellan v. Hagerty, No. 04-CV-008 (Ohio C.P., Meigs Cty., May 27, 2004) (heating fuel).

Special damages in an R.C. 5321.04 contract claim also include incidental damages caused by the landlord’s R.C. 5321.04(A) violation, such as the cost of temporary housing, restaurant meals, or water purchases. E.g., Benson v. Oaks Mobile Home Park, No. 1156, 1983 WL 6313 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Mar. 15, 1983) (R.C.

Chapter 3733 (now R.C. Chapter 4781) action, cost of living expenses at the another residence, as a result of sewer problem);Waterford Glen LLC v. Dowell, No. 05-CVG- 1055 (Ohio Mun., Crawford Cty., Apr. 7, 2006) (R.C. Chapter 3733 (now R.C. Chapter
4781) action, cost of restaurant meals and purchase of water); Woods v. Coleman, No.

01-CVG-444 (Ohio Mun., Jackson Cty., Aug. 6, 2001) (cost of purchasing water and using toilet facilities at other residences, as a result of no running water or working toilet); Beard v. Smith, No. 91-CVG-34340 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Apr.

24, 1991) (cost of alternative housing, as a result of no heat). See also Mease v. Fox, 200 N.W.2d 791, 797 (Iowa 1972) (implied warranty of habitability).

However, a court may deny incidental damages for the cost of temporary housing, if the court makes the tenant whole by relieving the tenant of any rental obligation to the landlord for the relevant period. Harris v. Winston, No. 2004-CVG-28660 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Mar. 16, 2005).

Special damages in an R.C. 5321.04 contract claim may not include damages for inconvenience and discomfort caused by the landlord’s R.C. 5321.04(A) violation.

The traditional view in Ohio is that a tenant may not recover contract damages for inconvenience and discomfort caused by the landlord’s breach of a covenant to repair.303 Cochran v. Widra, 35 Ohio Law Abs. 608, 41 N.E.2d 875 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., 1931). But cf. Deitsch v. Music Co., 6 Ohio Misc.2d 6, 453 N.E.2d 1302 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., 1983).

Likewise, special damages in an R.C. 5321.04 contract claim may not include damages for personal injury caused by the landlord’s R.C. 5321.04(A) violation. The traditional view in Ohio is that a tenant may not recover contract damages for personal injury caused by the landlord’s breach of a covenant to repair. See Cooper v. Roose, 151 Ohio St. 316, 85 N.E.2d 545 (1949). See also Auburn v. Amoco Oil Co., 106 Ill.

App.3d 60, 435 N.E.2d 780 (1982) (implied warranty of habitability). But see Boudreau v. Gen. Elec. Co., 2 Haw. App. 10, 625 P.2d 384 (1981).

However, it is insignificant whether special damages in an R.C. 5321.04 contract claim include damages for inconvenience and discomfort or damages for personal
injury. A tenant can recover these dama ges under an R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se
claim. See Section IX.B.4.a, below.
