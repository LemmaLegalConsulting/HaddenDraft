# 4. Admissibility of Estimates

## Table-of-contents context

- XVIII. Landlord Claim for Property
  - F. Practice and Procedure Issues

## Source text

An estimate of the cost of repairing the premises is not admissible as a business record under Evidence Rule 803(6) without the testimony of the person who actually prepared the estimate. Amjoy Co. v. Braggs, No. 64708, 1994 WL 43886 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 10, 1994); Mong v. Dotson, No. 1173CA, 1987 WL 8029 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., May 17, 1987).

As a result, a landlord’s written repair estimates from specified stores – Home Depot and Lowe’s– for replacing a carpet were not admissible because the “source of that information is wholly unidentified. In order to be credible, evidence must be elicited from a competent source.” Bailey v. Fairchild , No. 10 -CA-10, 2010 WL 4867532, ¶¶ 26,27 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Nov. 24, 2010).
