## Source text

When a landlord has knowledge of a tenant’s breach of the rental agreement, the landlord must decide whether to terminate the tenancy or to waive the breach and continue the tenancy. Petropoulos v. Clinical Pathology Facility, Inc., No. 87AP-685, 1988 WL 24397, at *4 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Feb. 18, 1988); Quinn v. Cardinal Foods, Inc., 20 Ohio App.3d 194, 196, 485 N.E.2d 741 (3rd Dist. 1984); Brokamp v.

Linneman, 20 Ohio App. 199, 202, 153 N.E. 130 (1st Dist. 1923).

A landlord waives the breach if, with knowledge of the breach, the landlord takes action that is inconsistent with a termination of the tenancy. Id.

A landlord may waive the right to terminate a tenancy irrespective of whether that right is premised on the tenant’s failure to pay rent or the tenant’s breach of another obligation under the rental agreement.

‘The forfeiture of a lease by breach of any other condition may be waived, in the same manner as a forfeiture for non-payment of rent, or a notice to quit; for if the landlord subsequently does any act, with knowledge of the breach, which can be considered as an acknowledgment of a tenancy, still subsisting, he will be held to have waived the forfeiture.’ Quinn v. Cardinal Foods, Inc. , 20 Ohio App.3d 194, 196, 485 N.E.2d 741 (3rd Dist.
1984) (quoting l Taylor on Landlord and Tenant § 287). Accord Millennia Hous.

Mgmt., Ltd. v. Williams , No. 101627, 2015 WL 1276464, ¶ 9 (Ohio App. , 8th Dist.,
Mar. 19, 2015); Petropoulos v. Clinical Pathology Facility, Inc., No. 87AP-685, 1988 WL 24397, at *4 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Feb. 18, 1988).

A waiver of the right to evict is an equitable defense. Northfield Park Assocs. v.

Northeast Ohio Har ness, 36 Ohio App.3d 14, 22, 521 N.E.2d 466 (8th Dist. 1987).

Therefore, in ruling on this defense, the trial court is “required to weigh and consider all equitable considerations before it.” Id.
