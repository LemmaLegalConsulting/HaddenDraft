# a. Nonpayment of Rent Breach

## Source text

A waiver of the right to terminate a tenancy for nonpayment of rent may occur as a result of the landlord’s acceptance of rent, where:
(1) the landlord accepts future rent payments, with knowledge of the rent arrearage, e.g., Navicky v. Copas , No. 9 7-CVG-00295 (Ohio Mun., Cambridge, July 3, 1997); Mt. Olive-Metro Hous., Inc. v. Craft, No. 93- G-2858 (Ohio Mun., Cuyahoga Falls, Feb. 28, 1994); Owner’s Mgmt.
Co. v. Osborne , No. 91 -CVG-774 (Ohio Mun., Barberton, Mar. 2, 1993);
(2) the landlord has a customar y practice of accepting late rent payments, with which the tenant has complied (see Section II.H, below); or
(3) the landlord accepts a partial payment of rent,105 e.g., Fairborn Apts. v.

Herman, No. 90 -CA-28, 1991 WL 10962 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Jan.

31, 1991); S. Heights Apts. v. Davis , No. 3737, 1985 WL 10685 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Apr. 17, 1985).
