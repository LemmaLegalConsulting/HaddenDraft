Attorney’s Fees Since attorney’s fees under R.C. Chapter 5321 are taxed as costs (see Section XIX.C, above), the time period for filing a motion for attorney’s fees should be the time period for filing a Civil Rule 54(D) motion to tax costs.

Civil Rule 54(D) does not provide a specific time period for filing a motion to tax
costs. Bookatz v. Kupps , 39 Ohio App.3d 36, 528 N.E.2d 961 (8th Dist. 1987).

Therefore, a motion to tax costs must be filed within a reasonable period of time. Id.

In Bookatz, the court held that a m otion to tax costs had been filed within the requisite reasonable time, even though it had been filed two months after the final judgment and one month after the time to perfect an appeal had expired.
