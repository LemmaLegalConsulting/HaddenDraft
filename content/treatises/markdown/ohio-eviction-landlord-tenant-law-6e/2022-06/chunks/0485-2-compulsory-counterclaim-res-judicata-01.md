# 2. Compulsory Counterclaim/Res Judicata

## Table-of-contents context

- XVII. Landlord Claim for Rent
  - C. Defense Issues

## Source text

When a tenant brings a security deposit claim, the landlord’s claim for unpaid rent due at that time is a compulsory counterclaim to the tenant’s claim. See Section XVI.H.5.a, above.

Likewise, when a tenant brings a claim under R.C. 5321.04 or 5321.15, the landlord’s claim for unpaid rent due at that time is a compulsory counterclaim to the tenant’s claim. E.g., Lujan v. Smith, No. L -83-197, 1983 WL 6990 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov. 11, 1983).

Therefore, if a tenant brings and prevails on one of these cl aims, and the landlord does not file a counterclaim for unpaid rent due at that time, under the doctrine of res judicata, the landlord may not thereafter bring an action against the tenant for the unpaid rent. Lujan v. Smith, No. L-83-197, 1983 WL 6990 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov.

11, 1983) (R.C. 5321.04 claim and R.C. 5321.15 claim); Thirion v. Tutoki , 94 Ohio Misc.2d 77, 703 N.E.2d 378 (Ohio Mun., Warren, 1998) (security deposit claim).
