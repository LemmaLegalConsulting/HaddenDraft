An R.C. Chapter 2506 appeal is perfected with the filing of a notice of ap peal.

R.C. 2505.04 (incorporated by R.C. 2506.01). See Jacobs v. Cuyahoga Metro. Hous.

Auth., No. 102248, 2015 WL 3647561, ¶ 15 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 11, 2015).

The notice of appeal must be filed with the court and the PHA, with one receiving the original of the notice and the other receiving a copy of the notice. Welsh Dev. Co.
v. Warren County Reg’l Planning Comm’n , 128 Ohio St.3d 471, 946 N.E.2d 215 (2011); Dudukovich v. Lorain Metro. Hous. Auth., 58 Ohio St.2d 202, 389 N.E.2d 1112 (1979). The original of the notice may be filed with either the court or the PHA. Id.

“R.C. 2505.04 is silent as to the permissible method for filing and serving the notice of appeal.” Jacobs v. Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. , No. 102248, 2015 WL 3647561, ¶ 17 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 11, 2015).

However, the Supreme Court has held that the “appellant may use any method reasonably certain to accomplish delivery to the agency within the required 30 days, which is filing that satisfies the jurisdictional requirement for an administrative appeal.” Welsh Dev. Co. v. Warren County Reg’l Planning Comm’n, 128 Ohio St.3d 471, 946 N.E.2d 215, ¶ 40 (2011).
