First, it is the only reasonable interpretation of the R.C. 5321.07(B) punctuation, which includes a semi-colon at the end of R.C. 5321.07(B)(1) and a period at the end of both R.C. 5321.07(B)(2) and 5321.07(B)(3). The legislature’s distinction between semi-colons and periods must be interpreted in a manner that does not render it nugatory and without meaning. See Schwartz v. McAtee , 22 Ohio St.3d 14, 21, 488 N.E.2d 479 (1986). The legislature’s distinction is meaningful only if the semi-colon between (B)(1) and (B)( 2) combines these remedies, and the period between (B)(2) and (B)(3) separates the (B)(3) remedy from the (B)(1) and (B)(2) remedies. If (B)(1), (B)(2), and (B)(3) are construed to be three separate and independent remedies from which the tenant may select only one, it would negate any distinction between the semi- colons and the periods.

Second, it is the interpretation that best advances the object sought to be obtained by R.C. 5321.07(B), and thus is the proper interpretation under R.C. 1.49(A) and 1.11.296 “The General Assembly enacted R.C. 5321.07 to provide tenants with leverage to redress breaches of [the landlord’s R.C. Chapter 5321] duties.”Miller v. Ritchie, 45 Ohio St.3d 222, 224, 543 N.E.2d 1265 (1989). Accord City of Lakewood v. Novak , 111 Ohio Misc.2d 1, 6, 746 N.E.2d 719 (Ohio Mun., Lakewood, 2000) (“The purpose of the rent deposit is to coerce a landlord to correct the code violations”). This purpose is best advanced by an interpretation of R.C. 5321.07(B) that permits a tenant to commence rent depositing under R.C. 5321.07(B)(1) and to file subsequently an R.C.

5321.07(B)(2) action.

Third, it is a reasonable interpretation of R.C. 5321.07(B) because, in requiring the tenant to select between the R.C. 5321.07(B)(1),(2) remedies and the R.C.

5321.07(B)(3) remedy, it recognizes that, if a tenant elects to terminate the rental agreement and vacate the premises under R.C. 5321.07(B)(3), the tenant lacks standing relative to the R.C. 5321.07(B)(1),(2) remedies ( i.e., future rent depositing, futur e landlord repairs of the premises, future rent reductions, and future rent deposit releases to make repairs).
