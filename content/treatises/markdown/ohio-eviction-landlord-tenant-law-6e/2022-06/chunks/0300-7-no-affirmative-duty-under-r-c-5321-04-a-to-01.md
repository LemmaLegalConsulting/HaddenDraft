## Source text

Inspect the Premises R.C. 5321.04(A) does not impose an affirmative duty on a landlord to inspect the premises to discover defective conditions. Person-Thomas v. Quilliams-Noble Apts., L.L.C., 45 N.E.3d 654, 2015 -Ohio-4277, ¶ 27 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., 2015);

Mandelbaum v. Smith , No. 101888, 2015 WL 1276573, ¶ 17 (Ohio App., 8th Dist.,
Mar. 19, 2015); Cook v. Crossings, LLC, No. 13-CA-010463, 2015 WL 1137860,¶ 12 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Mar. 16, 2015); Hollowell v. Aplis , No. 100275, 2014 WL 1327957, ¶ 11 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 20, 2014); Renzi v. Hillyer , No. 2012 -L- 331 041, 2012 WL 5995055, ¶ 20 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Dec. 3, 2012);Butler v. Wyndtree
Hous. Ltd. P’ship, No. CA2011-03-056, 2012 WL 37535, ¶ 42 (Ohio App., 12th Dist.,
Jan. 9, 2012); Avila v. Gerdenich Realty Co., No. L-07-1098, 2007 WL 4216972 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov. 30, 2007); Lilly v. Bradford Inv. Co., No. 06AP-1227, 2007 WL 1640850, ¶ 29 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., June 7, 2007);Boyd v. Hariani, No. 22500, 2005 WL 2087824, ¶ 31 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Aug. 31, 2005).

For a discussion on the related issue of the landlord’s constructive notice of defective conditions at the residential premises, see Section IX.B.2.b, below.
