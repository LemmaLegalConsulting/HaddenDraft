# G. Constitutionality of the Rent Deposit

## Source text

Procedure The rent deposit procedure established in R.C. 5321.07 -.10 comports with the requirements of due process. Chernin v. Welchans, 844 F.2d 322 (6th Cir. 1988). The Chernin decision struck down only a minor portion of R.C. 5321.09(B), namely: the provision that permitted a court, for good cause shown, to hold the trial on a landlord’s R.C. 5321.09 claim more than sixty days after the filing of the R.C. 5321.09 complaint.

The Chernin decision renders inconsequential the two municipal court decisions that, prior to Chernin, found that the rent deposit procedure violated due process.

Those decisions are Morris Inv. v. Kettman, No. 80-CV-10735 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., Mar. 20, 1981), and Rallie Co. v. Pastor , No. M76 -CVG-463763 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Apr. 11, 1977).
