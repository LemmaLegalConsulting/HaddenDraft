Leases The statute of conveyances generally requires all leases to be in writing, signed by the landlord, and acknowledged before a notary public (or other designated official).

R.C. 5301.01.373 Certain leases are exempt from these formal requirements, the most noteworthy being leases that are for a term of three years or less. R.C. 5301.08.

However, a perpetual lease is subject to the statute of conveyances, even if its initial term is less than three years. Cont’l Enters., Ltd. v. Hunt, No. 102200, 2015 WL 9460300 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 24, 2015); Zunshine v. Wallace F. Ackley Co. ,
No. 99AP-531, 2000 WL 329008 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 30, 2000); Friedrich v.

Matrka, No. 37178, 1978 Ohio App. LEXIS 9975 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 23, 1978); Frank v. Flynn, 120 Ohio App. 361, 197 N.E.2d 657 (8th Dist. 1964); Morales
v. Windsor R ealty & Mgmt., Inc. , No. 2000 -CVH-002037 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland,
Hous. Div., Nov. 21, 2000). See also Note, Residential Renewal Leases and the Ohio Statute of Conveyances: Invalidation and Subsequent Treatment, 27 Clev. St. L. Rev.

231 (1978).

A lease is a perpetual lease if its term automatically renews unless and until the tenant provides the landlord with a timely notice of nonrenewal.Id.; Verhovsek v. Mut.

Props., No. 51600, 1987 WL 7435 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 5, 1987); Hoffman v.

Foster & Kleise r, Inc., No. 44134, 1982 WL 5385 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 27, 1982); President & Trs. of Ohio Univ. v. Athens Livestock Sales, Inc. , 115 Ohio App.

21, 179 N.E.2d 382 (4th Dist. 1961).

373 The revision of R.C. 5301.01, effective February 1, 2002, eliminated the requirement for two witnesses. It also creates a presumption of validity for leases that were executed prior to February 1, 2001, without the signature of two witnesses.

595 A perpetual lease may permit the landlord as well to discontinue the automatic renewals via a timely notice of nonrenewal or a timely notice of a change in the rent (or a lease provision). Zunshine v. Wallace F. Ackley Co. , No. 99AP-531, 2000 WL 329008 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 30, 2000) (landlord notice of nonrenewal) ;

Friedrich v. Matrka, No. 37178, 1978 Ohio App. LEXIS 9975 (Ohio App., 8th Dist.,
Mar. 23, 1978) (landlord notice of rent/lease change); Frank v. Flynn, 120 Ohio App.

361, 197 N.E.2d 657 (8th Dist. 1964) (landlord notice of rent/lease change).

“‘[W]herea purported lessee takes possession under a defectively executed lease and pays rent, a tenancy will be implied and is subject to all of the terms of the purported lease except duration.’” Ruben v. S.M. & N. Corp., 83 Ohio App.3d 80, 83, 613 N.E.2d 1101 ( 8th Dist. 1993). Accord Zunshine v. Wallace F. Ackley Co. , No.

99AP-531, 2000 WL 329008 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 30, 2000); Schultz v.

Wurdlow, No. 94 -APG07-1033, 1995 WL 41648 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Jan. 31, 1995); Manifold v. Schuster, 67 Ohio App.3d 251, 586 N.E.2d 1142 (4th Dist. 1990).

“‘The duration of the term [of the implied tenancy] is determinable by the provisions for payment of rent, so a lease providing for annual rent creates a tenancy from year to year, whereas a lease providing for mon thly rent creates a tenancy from month to month.’” Ruben v. S.M. & N. Corp. , 83 Ohio App.3d 80, 83, 613 N.E.2d 1101 (8th Dist. 1993). Accord Zunshine v. Wallace F. Ackley Co. , No. 99AP -531, 2000 WL 329008, at *3-4 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 30, 2000).E.g., Delfino v. Paul Davies Chevrolet, Inc., 2 Ohio St.2d 282, 209 N.E.2d 194, syllabus no. 1 (1965) (“A defectively executed lease for a term of five years upon monthly rental creates a tenancy in the lessee from month to month”);9900 Timbers Dr. Inv. LLC v. Li, No. C- 190224, 2020 WL 1870257 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Apr. 15, 2020) (a defectively executed lease with a five -year term and monthly rent payments created a month -to- month tenancy); Tower Realty v. Zalenski , No. 07 -JE-24, 2008 WL 2583131, ¶ 16 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., June 23, 2008) (“a defective [three-year] lease that provides for monthly rent creates a tenancy from month-to-month”);R & S Props. v. Weiszner, No.

68637, 1995 WL 628342 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Oct. 26, 1995) (month -to-month tenancy created where a defectively executed lease had a term of five years, with a five year renewal option, and specified a rent amount to be paid monthly); Yelanosky v.

White, No. 67479, 1995 WL 32869, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 26, 1995) (“A month-to-month tenancy is created where a [defectively executed] lease provides for monthly payments”); Katzin v. Murad, No. 46553, 1984 WL 7150, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 5, 1984) (“When the lease is … defectively executed, a month-to-month tenancy is created , where there is a monthly rental payment”); Friedrich v. Matrka ,
No. 37178, 1978 Ohio App. LEXIS 9975 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 23, 1978) (when the invalid lease has an initial term of one year and an automatic renewal provision, and specifies a rent am ount to be paid monthly, the implied tenancy is month -to- month). But see Ruben v. S.M. & N. Corp., 83 Ohio App.3d 80, 83, 613 N.E.2d 1101 (8th Dist. 1993) (“‘year to year tenancy will be implied where the rent is reserved annually, although payable in monthly installments’”).
