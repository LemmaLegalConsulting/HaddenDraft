## Source text

Tenant Forcibly or by Demand A claim on the contract for wrongful eviction has three elements. First, the tenant was occupying the premises under a lease whose t erm had not expired. Second, the tenant was not in default of any lease term. Third, the landlord pursued a course of conduct that was intended to and did cause the tenant to vacate the premises.

Examples of wrongful evictions under this claim are summarized below.
(1) The landlord forcibly took possession of the premises. Ketcham v.

Miller, 104 Ohio St. 372, 136 N.E. 145 (1922).
(2) The landlord verbally ordered the tenant to vacate the premises and the tenant vacated. Butterfield v. Snively, 60 Ohio App. 14, 19 N.E.2d 284 (5th Dist. 1934); Wharton v. Pollock, 49 Ohio App. 443, 197 N.E. 379 (5th Dist. 1934).
(3) The landlord served the tenant with a written notice to leave the premises and the tenant vacated. Wehrley v. Sunchase Am., Ltd., No. CA99-11- 191, 2001 WL 88202 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Jan. 29, 2001); Roberts v.

GMS Mgmt. Co., No. 75419, 1999 WL 1068370 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., 479
Nov. 24, 1999);321 Weintraub v. Hurley, No. 93-APG10-1473, 1994 WL 171138 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., May 3, 1994);Armstrong Stationary Co.
v. FGM Props., Inc. , No. C -830914, 1984 WL 7016 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Oct. 24, 1984); Teicher Theaters, Inc. v. Thompson, No. 83-CA- 29, 1983 WL 2530 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 28, 1983).

As these examples demonstrate, an action on the contract for w rongful eviction may be based on a constructive eviction ( i.e., a tenant’s vacation of the premises in response to the landlord’s order to vacate). Armstrong Stationary Co. v. FGM Props., Inc., No. C-830914, 1984 WL 7016, at *2 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Oct. 24, 1984). The tenant does not forfeit this claim for money damages in electing in such circumstances to vacate the premises, rather than remain on the premises in anticipation of asserting defenses in an eviction action. Id.

In an action on the contract for wrongful eviction, the tenant may recover general damages in an amount equal to the market value of the unexpired lease term and special damages to the extent they are permitted under contract law. Butterfield v. Snively, 60 Ohio App. 14, 19 N.E.2d 284 (5th Dist. 1934).

If the tenant leases another rental unit at a higher monthly rent as a result of the wrongful eviction, the tenant may recover the amount of the rent increase for the period of the unexpired lease term. E.g., Wehrley v. Sunchase Am., Ltd., No. CA99-11-191, 2001 WL 88202 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Jan. 29, 2001). However, the tenant is required to make reasonable efforts to mitigate these damages. Id.

The tenant may not recover punitive damages. Ketcham v. Miller, 104 Ohio St.

372, 136 N.E. 145 (1922). See also R.C. 2315.21(A)(1).
