# 2. Registration/Approval of Overnight Guests

## Source text

Some landlo rds require registration and/or landlord approval for any overnight guests.

Court decisions have held such policies to be unconscionable and thus unenforceable under R.C. 5321.14. Hi-Land Terrace, Inc. v. Dinwiddie, No. 546, 1985 WL 6560 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Mar. 1, 1985); Heritage Hills, Ltd. v. Smith, No. 78- CVG-268 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, May 8, 1978).

For Public Housing, court decisions have struck down such policies on two additional grounds. One is that the policy violates the tenant’s right to i nvite guests under 24 C.F.R. § 966.4(d)(1). Lancor v. Lebanon Hous. Auth., 760 F.2d 361 (1st Cir.

1985); Wells v. Hous. Auth. of Wheeling , No. 90 -0131-W(S) (N.D. W. Va. Jan. 27, 1993). The other is that the policy violates the tenant’s First Amendment ri ghts of association and privacy. McKenna v. Peekskill Hous. Auth. , 647 F.2d 332 (2d Cir.

1981).
