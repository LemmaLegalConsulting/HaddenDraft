## Source text

HUD regulations specify the criminal activities that are grounds for terminating an assisted housing tenancy. With one exception, HUD regulations provide that any termination of an assisted housing tenancy based on these criminal activities is within the discretion of the landlord. The one exception is that, if the tenant or a household member is convicted for manufacture of methamphetamine on the premises of assisted housing, termination of the assisted housing tenancy is mandatory.

A description of each of the specified criminal activities is set forth below, along with a statement as to whether it applies to the conduct of the tenant, household members, or guests, 211 the assisted housing program(s) to which it applies, and the relevant HUD regulation(s).
(1) Conviction for manufacture of methamphetamine on the premises of assisted housing (tenant or household member).

Public Housing: 24 C.F.R. § 966.4(l)(5)(i)(A).
(2) Currently engaging in illegal use of a drug (tenant or household member).

Public Housing: 24 C.F.R. § 966.4(l)(5)(i)(B).
