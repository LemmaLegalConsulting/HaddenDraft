When the vendee’s default is nonpayment, a forfeiture of the vendee’s interest under the land installment contract “may be enforced only after the expiration of thirty days from the date of the default.” R.C. 5313.05. If the land installment contract provides a thirty-day grace period and does not specify when the R.C. 5313.05 thirty- day notice commences relative to the grace period, the R.C. 5313.05 thirty-day notice does not commence until the expiration of the thirty-day grace period. Before filing any eviction action against the vendee, the vendor must serve the vendee with a written notice that:
(1) Reasonably identifies the contract and describes the property covered by it;
(2) Specifies the terms and conditions of the contract with which the vendee has not complied;
(3) Notifies the vendee that the contract will stand forfeited unless the vendee performs the terms and conditions of the contract within ten days of the completed service of notice; and
(4) Notifies the vendee to leave the premises.

R.C. 5313.06.395 In these eviction actions, the R.C. 5313.06 notice supplants the R.C. 1923.04(A) notice (see Section I.C.1, above). R.C. 1923.04(B).

The permissible methods of service for an R.C. 5313.06 notice are: (1) certified mail, (2) registered mail, (3) personal service, (4) leaving it at the vendee’s usual place of abode, or (5) leaving it at the property that is the subject of the contract. R.C.

5313.06. E.g., Estate of Chasteen v. Cartee, No. 1993, 1992 WL 238918 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Sept. 25, 1992) (upheld service by registered mail).

For R.C. 5313.06 purposes, a vendee “performs the terms and conditions of the contract” so as to avoid forfeiture only if the vendee makes full payment of the entire contract price before the end of the ten-day period. Addair v. Mitchell, No. 03-CA-19, 2003 WL 22951899 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Dec. 15, 2003); Keene v. Schnetz, 13 Ohio App.3d 87, 468 N.E.2d 125 (9th Dist. 1983). A vendee’s oral offer to make full payment does not meet this requirement, if the vendee fails to tender the payment .

Ramsey v. Farris, No. 64179, 1993 WL 497059 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 2, 1993).

One appellate district has held that “R.C. 5313.06, like R.C. 1923.04, is jurisdictional in nature and a prerequisite to land contract forfeiture proceedings.” Austin v. Sullivan, No. 93-CA-43, 1994 WL 650228, at *2 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., Nov.

17, 1994). It thereupon held that a vendor’s “failure to comply with the provisions of R.C. 531 3.06, as it sets forth notice requirements, deprive[s] the trial court of jurisdiction over the subject matter of the land contract.” Id.

Two other appellate districts have stated that the jurisdictional issue is not settled, since only the Seventh District has ruled on it. Am. Servicing Corp. v. Wannemacher, 19 N.E.3d 566, 2014 -Ohio-3984, ¶ 44 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., 2014); State ex rel.

Invesco Mgmt. Co. LLC v. Geauga County Court of Common Pleas, No. 2012-G-3085, 2012 WL 4788965, ¶ 19 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Oct. 9, 2012). But neither court ruled on the issue. In Wannemacher, the court did not need to (and opted not to) decide the jurisdictional issue “because the trial court ultimately dismissed the complaint, which was the proper disposition of the c ase in light of the trial court’s conclusion that Amerian Servicing failed to satisfy R.C. 5313.06.”Wannemacher, 2014-Ohio-3984, ¶
44. In Invesco, the court did not decide the jurisdictional issue because the trial court had not yet determined (and woulddetermine on remand) whether the notice complied with R.C. 5313.06. Invesco, 2012 WL 4788965, ¶ 19 In three cases, where the R.C. 5313.06 notice instructed the vendee to vacate before the required ten days and the vendor commenced the eviction action after the required ten days, the trial courts refused to dismiss the eviction actions and the appellate courts found error but not prejudicial error. Goble v. Grosswiler, No. 2018-CA-0102, 2019 WL 5595795 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Oct. 29, 2019) (notice s pecified three days and eviction complaint filed 48 days after service of the notice); Estate of Chasteen v.

When a vendee cannot avoid forfeiture via th e rights to cure under the R.C.

5313.05 and 5313.06, the vendee still has a right to assert an equity defense. Because “forfeitures are highly disfavored by the law ..., the forfeiture clause of a land contract will be disregarded upon the vendee’s failure to perform on time, unless the failure is intentional or it results in a loss to the vendor which cannot be compensated by interest.” Krivins v. Smyers, No. 9935, 1981 WL 3945, at *2 (Ohio App., 9th Dist.,
Apr. 22, 1981). “Accordingly, courts have decl ined to enforce forfeitures of land contracts when the buyer later made an adequate tender of the amounts due.” Id. See also Blenheim Homes, Inc. v. Mathews, 119 Ohio App. 44, 196 N.E.2d 612 (10th Dist.

1963).
