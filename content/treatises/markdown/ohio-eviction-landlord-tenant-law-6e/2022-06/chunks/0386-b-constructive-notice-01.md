“In order to show that a landlord had constructive notice, plaintiff [tenant] has the burden of showing that the defect in questi on must have existed for such a length of time that the landlord, by exercising reasonable care, should have discovered it.” DeVore v. Franklin Manor Apts., No. 98AP-779, 1999 WL 236040, at *3 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Apr. 22, 1999). Accord Person-Thomas v. Quilliams-Noble Apts., L.L.C., 45 N.E.3d 654, 2015 -Ohio-4277, ¶ 21 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., 2015); Coeurvie v.

McGonigal, No. 27095, 2014 WL 4824449, ¶ 14 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Sept. 30, 2014);

Albert v. Showe Mgmt. Corp. , Nos. 99 -CA-00016,00007, 1999 WL 547883, at *2 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., July 22, 1999).

If the constructive notice is based on the landlord’s (or landlord’s agent’s) visit to the dwelling unit, the tenant has the burden of showing that the defective condition existed at the time of the visit and was discernible upon a reasonable inspection at that
time. See, e.g. , Johnston v. Filson , No. CA2014 -04-007, 2014 WL 5422214, ¶¶ 12,13,25,26 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Oct. 27, 2014) (no discernible evidence of the tree’s decay before the top portion of the tree’s trunk broke off in a windstorm);Swader
v. Paramount Prop. Mgmt., No. CA2011-05-084, 2012 WL 1107722, ¶¶ 30-32 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Apr. 2, 2012) (no evidence on when floorboard became defective);

Golden Leaf Baptist Church, No. C-860310, 1987 WL 10038, at *1 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Apr. 22, 1987) (no evidence that “a defect in the ceiling was discernible upon reasonable inspection at any time before this incident”).

“Where the landlord does not cause or know of the unsafe condition, the issue is whether ‘the landlord by the exercise of reasonable care could have disc overed the condition and the unreasonable risk involved therein and could have made the condition safe.’” Malagisi v. Marble Cliff Crossing Apts. , No. 19AP-475, 2020 WL 431 1320712, ¶ 16 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 19, 2020) (quotingMann v, Northgate Invs.

L.L.C., 138 Ohio St.3d, 175, 5 N.E.3d 594, ¶ 21 (2014)).

R.C. 5321.04(A) does not generally set forth an affirmative duty on the landlord to inspect the premises. See Section VII.C.7, above. But “[i]nspections can serve to satisfy the landlord’s duty” relative to reasonable care. Malagisi v. Marble Cliff Crossing Apts., No. 19AP-475, 2020 WL 1320712, ¶ 17 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar.

19, 2020). And, depending on the circumstances, these inspections “need not always be undertaken by professional inspectors.” Id.

Also, for alleged violations of the R.C. 5321.04(A)(4) duty to maintain elevators, where the landlord did not know of any mechanical issues before the incident, the tenant is required “to come forward with evidence showing why the elevator malfunctioned and that [the landlord] … should have known about the malfunction.

That showing require[s] expert evidence because ‘an elevator, like an automobile, is a complicated mechanical device.’” Mixon v. K&D Apt. Cmty. Owners and Managers,
No. 104338, 2017 WL 121770, ¶ 9 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 12, 2017) (quoting Sabolik v. HGG Chestnut Lake Ltd. P’ship , 180 Ohio App.3d 576, 906 N.E.2d 488 , ¶ 13 (8th Dist. 2009) ). And elevators may malfunction for non -mechanical reasons such as overcrowding. Id., ¶ 9.

Also, “general knowledge of the possibility of a defect does not rise to the level of either actual or constructive notice.”Burnworth v. Harper, 109 Ohio App.3d 401, 406, 672 N.E.2d 241 (4th Dist. 1996) (fumes from space heater not constructive notice of defect producing carbon monoxide). Accord Avila v. Gerdenich Realty Co., No. L-07- 1098, 2007 WL 4216972 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Nov. 30, 2007) (manufacturer recommendation for regular maintenance check not constructive notice of garage door defect); Parks v. Menyhart Plumbing & Heating Supply Co. , No. 75424, 1999 WL 1129591 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 9, 1999) (possibility that sandstone sidewalk could crack not constructive notice of latent defect in sidewalk); Craver v. Doogan,
No. 2003-CVG-00982, 2005 WL 4985282 (Ohio C.P., Clermont Cty., May 27, 2005) (water intrusion not constructive notice of mold).
