## Source text

“Before trying the cause in an action for forcible entry and detainer in the absence of the defendant, the court or referee [now magistrate] must first determine whether the summons was properly served.” Vistula Mgmt. Co. v. Stuckey, No. L-92-102, 1993 WL 315626, at *3 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Aug. 13, 1993).

In an eviction action for disturbing the neighbors, the testimony of neighbors may not be required to show that the tenant’s conduct disturbed the neighbors. E.g., Label & Co. v. Taylor, No. 86-C-26, 1987 WL 12887 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., June 17, 1987) (security guard’s testimony as to thenoise at the premises deemed sufficient).

67 For the definition of “assisted housing,” see note 163, below.

In an eviction action for repeated damage to the premises, the landlord may submit evidence of past incidents of damage to the premises. Stark Metro. Hous. Auth. v.

Kirksey, No. CA -9116, 1993 WL 135735 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Apr. 19, 1993). In such cases, the landlord has not waived those prior lease violations. Id.

In an eviction action for an unauthorized occupant, the determination as to whether a person is a guest or an occupant of the premises is a question of fact. New Boston Kiwanis Hous., Etc. v. Sparks, No. 1957, 1992 WL 79561 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Apr.

14, 1992).

When a local court rule requires a trial statement that identifies the witnesses who will be testifying and the documents that will be introduced, a landlord’s failure to file a trial statement is a potential ground for excluding the testimony of witnessesand the introduction of documents in the eviction action. See, e.g., Cuyahoga Metro. Hous.
Auth. v. Moon , No. 2006 -CVG-10893 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Nov. 21, 2006).
