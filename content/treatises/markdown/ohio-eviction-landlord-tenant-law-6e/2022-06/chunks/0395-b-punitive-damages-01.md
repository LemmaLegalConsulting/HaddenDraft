# b. Punitive Damages

## Source text

An R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se claim is a tort action within the purview of R.C.

2315.21. Sivit v. Vill. Green of Beachwood, L.P., 143 Ohio St.3d 168, 35 N.E.3d 508, ¶ 5 (2015).

Therefore, in an R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se claim, a tenant may recover punitive damages. Sivit, 2015-Ohio-1193, ¶¶ 5-7.

Under R.C. 2315.21, this right to recover puniti ve damages is subject to three
requirements. First, “[a]n award of punitive damages requires that actual malice be proven ….” Sivit, 2015-Ohio-1193, ¶ 7. Second, “a plaintiff must be awarded some measure of compensatory damages to receive punitive damag es.” Niskanen v. Giant Eagle, Inc., 122 Ohio St.3d 486, 912 N.E.2d 595, ¶ 12 (2009). Third, the punitive damage award may not exceed two times the amount of the compensatory damage
award. Sivit, 2015-Ohio-1193, ¶ 6.

One court awarded punitive damages for a landlord’s willful and malicious failure to restore water service to the premises for 77 days. Johnson v. Rahim , No. 2019 - CVG-008127 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Jan. 21, 2020) ($7,103 compensatory damages; $5,562 punitive damages).

Another court awarded punitive damages for a landlord’s wil lful and reckless failure to provide heat and hot water to the premises. Monaco v. Schultz, No. 1998- CV-100425, 2001 WL 1677087 (Ohio C.P., Tuscarawas Cty., Jan. 30, 2001) ($5,000 compensatory damages; $5,000 punitive damages).

If a tenant is awarded punitive damages in an R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se case, the tenant may also be entitled to an award of attorney’s fees under Ohio common law.
