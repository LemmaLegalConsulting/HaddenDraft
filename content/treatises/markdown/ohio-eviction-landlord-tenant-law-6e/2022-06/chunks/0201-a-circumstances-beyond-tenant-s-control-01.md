# a. Circumstances Beyond Tenant’s Control

## Source text

Nonpayment of rent is not good cause to terminate an assisted housing tenancy if:
(1) it is due to circumstances beyond the tenant’s control, (2) the tenant promptly informs the landlord of the circumstances, and (3) the tenant makes diligent efforts to pay the rent. E.g., Stark Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Ruffin, No. CA-8751, 1992 WL 147443 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 15, 1992), on remand, No. 91 -CVG-5463 (Ohio Mun., Canton, Oct. 2, 1992) (late welfare payment); Fairborn Apts. v. Herman, No. 90-CA- 28, 1991 WL10962 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Jan. 31, 1991) (purse stolen and non-receipt of child support payment); Cincinnati Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Green , 41 Ohio App.3d 365, 536 N.E.2d 1 (1st Dist. 1987) (money stolen); Northlake Hills Coop., Inc. v.

Barrett, No. 9699, 1987 WL 14238 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Dec. 8, 1986) (money stolen); Showe Mgmt. Corp. v. Bickmeyer , No. 06-CVG-00631 (Ohio Mun., Licking Cty., Apr. 19, 2006) (Bureau of Child Support failed to forward child support payments); Stark Metro. Hous. v. Short, No. 96-CVG-1298 (Ohio Mun., Canton, July 9, 1996) (husband abandoned household); Lima Towers v. Franklin , No. 96 -CVG- 00191 (Ohio Mun., Lima, Apr. 8, 1996) (purse stolen); New Prince Hall Hous. Auth.
v. Thornton , No. 91 -CVG-1754 (Ohio Mun., Clark Cty., Ju ly 9, 1991) (Worker’s Compensation benefits temporarily stopped); Dayton Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Lawson ,
No. 85 -CVG-9998 (Ohio Mun., Dayton, Nov. 20, 1985) (late welfare payment);

Dayton Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Cantrell , No. 85-CVG-7589 (Ohio Mun., Dayton, Oct.

10, 1985) (late welfare payment); Dayton Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Thompson , No. 80 - CVG-9597 (Ohio Mun., Dayton, Dec. 3, 1980) (initial welfare payment delay);Belvoir Cliffs Apts. v. Bembry , 56 Ohio Misc. 37, 383 N.E.2d 1170 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland,
1978) (in itial welfare payment delay). See also Cincinnati Metro. Hous. Auth. v.

Cockrell, No. C-070647 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Dec. 17, 2008) (recognizes the defense, but upholds the finding that the nonpayment was not due to circumstances beyond the tenant’s control); Stark Metro. Hous. Auth. v. McQueen , No. CA -8422, 1991 WL 136099 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., July 15, 1991) (remand for factual findings on the cause for termination).

One Ohio appellate court held that, in the appeal, a ruling on the “beyond tenant’s control” issue was premature because the trial court had failed “to resolve the underlying issue of whether a single missed payment of rent is sufficient to warrant eviction from federally assisted public housing.” CMHA v. Manns, No. 107156, 2019 WL 1755414, ¶ 15 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Apr. 18, 2019). The court remanded the case for further proceedings to resolve that issue. Id.

A deduction of child support payments is not a circumstance beyond the tenant’s
control. See Cincinnati Metro. Hous. Auth. v. E dwards, 174 Ohio App.3d 174, 881 N.E.2d 325 (1st Dist. 2007) (child support payments deducted from Social Security disability payments).
