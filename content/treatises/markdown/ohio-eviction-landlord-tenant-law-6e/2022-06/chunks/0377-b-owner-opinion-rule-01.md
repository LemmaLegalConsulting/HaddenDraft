A tenant, by virtue of the tenancy, is competent to give opinion testimony as to the rental value of the premises in their defective condition. Smith v. Padgett , 32 Ohio
St. 3d 344, 513 N.E.2d 737 (1987). See also Miller v. Ritchie, 45 Ohio St.3d 222, 543 N.E.2d 1265 (1989); Gammarino v. Smith, No. C-060636, 2007 WL 2284573 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Aug. 10, 2007) (applying Padgett); Boice v. Emshoff, No. 13 -98-23, 1998 WL 833686 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Dec. 3, 1998) (applyingPadgett); Weingarden
v. Eagle Ridge Condos ., 71 Ohio Misc.2d 7, 653 N.E.2d 759 (Ohio Mun., Toledo,
Hous. Div., 1995) (applying Padgett).

This tenant opinion testimony is admissible under the owner -opinion rule.

Padgett, 32 Ohio St.3d at 347-48.

The weight that the judge or jury will give this tenant opinion testimony is within the sound discretion of the trier of fact. Miller, 45 Ohio St.3d at 227 n.7; Padgett, 32 Ohio St.3d at 348. See also Pleasant v. Greenfield Station Apts., No. 15282, 1995 WL 614359 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 20, 1995) (applying Miller).

423 Three court de cisions illustrate this discretion, when their court findings are compared to the tenant opinion testimony on the monthly rental value of rental premises in their defective condition: Parker v. Hiles, No. 02-CVH-362 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, Jan. 6, 2003) (court finding – $75; tenant opinion testimony – $25);

Grayson v. Farmer, No. 2002-CVG-14619 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Feb.

12, 2002) (court finding – $250; tenant opinion testimony – $250 to $300); Tolliver v.

Warren, No. 99-CVG-13123 (Ohio Mun.,Cleveland, Hous. Div., Dec. 28, 1999) (court finding – $225; tenant opinion testimony – $175).

Under the owner opinion rule, a tenant may “testify as to the underlying factors which serve as the basis of his opinion in an effort to lend credibility to histestimony.” Weir v. Miller, No. 82-04-0044, 1983 WL 4336, at *2 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Apr. 13, 1983).

The owner opinion rule is subject to the hearsay rule. Id., 1983 WL 4336, at *2;

Hawkins v. Hawkins, 11 Ohio Misc.2d 18, 464 N.E.2d 199 (Ohio C.P.,Clermont Cty., 1984). Consequently, a tenant may not support the tenant’s opinion testimony by testifying as to rental amounts listed in advertisements for other premises.Hawkins, 11 Ohio Misc.2d at 19.

Under the owner opinion rule, a tenant is also competent to give opinion testimony as to the value of the tenant’s personal property, when the tenant’s special damages include damage to that property.McCain v. Brewer, No. 2014-CA-8, 2015 WL 302829 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Jan. 23, 2015);Smith v. Seitz, No. 97-CA-515, 1998 WL 393880 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., July 9, 1998); Bynum v. Huffman, No. 57730, 1990 WL 210225 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 20, 1990); Burfitt v. Stewart, Nos. 55530,55531, 1989 WL 113079 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Sept. 28, 1989). See generally Tokles & Son, Inc. v.

Midwestern Indem. Co. , 65 Ohio St.3d 621, 605 N.E.2d 936 (1992); Bishop v. East Ohio Gas Co., 143 Ohio St. 541, 56 N.E.2d 164 (1944).

Because the owner opinion rule is subject to the hearsay rule, a tenant may not simply identify a list that purports to establish the value of the personal property.Smith
v. Seitz, No. 97-CA-515, 1998 WL 393880 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., July 9, 1998).

Like rental v alue testimony, the owner opinion testimony is some evidence, but not conclusive evidence, of the value of the personal property.Bynum v. Huffman, No.

57730, 1990 WL 210225, at *3 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 20, 1990); Burfitt v.

Stewart, Nos. 55530,55531, 1989 WL 113079, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Sept. 28, 1989).
