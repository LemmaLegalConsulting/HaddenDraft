Housing The term “innocent tenant” is defined in the first paragraph of Section III.G.8.d, above.

Some courts have held that, in certain circumstances, landlords of project -based subsidized housing may not evict an innocent tenant. These holdings have relied on one of two grounds.

One ground is based on the subsidized housing lease, 216 ¶ 13(c), which provide s that the tenant shall not engage in or permit certain unlawful activity. In eviction actions based on ¶ 13(c) violations, courts have held that the innocent tenant did not engage in or permit the unlawful activity and, therefore, did not violate ¶ 13(c).217 E.g., Owners Mgmt.
