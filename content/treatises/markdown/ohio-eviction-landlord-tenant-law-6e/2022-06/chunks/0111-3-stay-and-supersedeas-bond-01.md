When a tenant appeals from an eviction judgment, if the tenant seeks to stay enforcement of the judgment pending the appeal, the tenant is required to post a supersedeas bond. Ohio R.Civ. P. 62(B); R.C. 2505.09, 2505.14. See also Miami Inv.
Corp. v. Baker, 109 Ohio App. 334, 165 N.E.2d 690 (2nd Dist. 1959) (land installment contract eviction). Cf. Winton Sav. & Loan Co. v. Eastfork Trace, Inc. , 119 Ohio Misc.2d 83, 774 N.E.2d 355 (Ohio C.P., Clermont Cty., 2001) (foreclosure).

Under Civil Rule 62(B), the posting of an adequate supersedeas bond is the only requirement for obtaining a stay of execution pending appeal. State ex rel. Ocasek v.

Riley, 54 Ohio St.2d 488, 490, 377 N.E.2d 792 (1978).

When a stay of execution is granted and the supersedeas bond is posted, the judge “shall issue his [stay] order to the sheriff, police officer, constable, or bailiff commanding him to delay all further proceedings upon the execution.” R.C. 1923.14.

Furthermore, where the premises have been restored to the landlord before the stay is granted and the supersedeas bond is posted, “the sheriff, police officer, constable, or bailiff shall forthwith place the defendant [tenant] in possession of them ....” Id. See Bowling Green Manor Ltd. P’ship v. Kirk, No. 94-WD-125 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Jan.

12, 1995); Crossings Dev. Ltd. P’ship v. H.O.T., Inc., 96 Ohio App.3d 475, 645 N.E.2d 159 (9th Dist. 1994).

The supersedeas bond must initially be sought from the trial court. Ohio R. App.
P. 7(A). See Mathews v. Cooper, No. 109974, 2021 WL 3560200,¶ 9 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug. 12, 2021); Bibb v. Home Sav. & Loan Co., 63 Ohio App.3d 751, 752, 580 N.E.2d 52 (6th Dist. 1989). The trial court is required to rule on a tenant’s timely request for a supersedeas bond before the writ of restitution has been executed.Martin
v. Mohler, No. 89-CA-1859, 1991 WL 28306, at *1 n.2 (Ohio App., 4th Dist.,Feb. 12, 1991).

If the trial court denies the tenant’s request for a supersedeas bond altogether, or fails to order the type of bond (i.e., amount or method of payment) that the tenant has requested, the tenant may then seek a stay and a supersedeas bond from the court of
appeals. Ohio R. App. P. 7(A). See Eagle View Apts. v. Pride, No. CT06-0050 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Aug. 10, 2006);Clayton Jaycee Hous. v. Salmon, No. 2006AP-060032 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 26, 2006). Because of the deference accorded a trial court’s exercise of discretionary authority, the court of appeals may be more likely to grant a tenant’s motion for a stay and supersedeas bond when the trial court has simply denied the tenant’s motion, than when the trial court has ordered a supersedeas bond but it is not the type of bond that the tenant sought.

The supersedeas bond must be sufficient to pay “all money costs and damages” that the appellee may be awarded in the appeal. R.C. 2505.14.
