In an eviction action in a municipal court, the trial may be heard by a magistrate, a judge, or a jury. The right to a trial by jury is discussed in Section I.J.1., above. If a municipal court has more than two judges, one or more magistrates must be appointed to he ar the eviction actions in which the right to a trial by jury is waived or not
demanded. Rules of Superintendence for the Courts of Ohio,Rule 19.1. If a municipal court has two or fewer judges, one or more magistrates may be appointed to hear the eviction actions in which the right to a trial by jury is waived or not demanded. Id.

Typically, eviction actions are tried to a judge (“bench trial”) in one of two
circumstances. The first is where the municipal court has two or fewer judges, the court decides not to appoint a magistrate, and the right to a trial by jury is waived or not demanded.
