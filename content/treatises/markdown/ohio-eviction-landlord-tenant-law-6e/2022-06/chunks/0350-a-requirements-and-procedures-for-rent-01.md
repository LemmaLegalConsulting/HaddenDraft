# A. Requirements and Procedures for Rent

## Source text

Depositing R.C. 5321.07 establishes the requirements and procedures for a tenant to deposit rent wit h the clerk of court of the municipal or county court. It also permits one category of landlords – those who lease three or fewer dwelling units – to become exempt from this rent deposit procedure. R.C. 5321.07(C). To perfect this exemption, the landlord must deliver to the tenant, at the time of the tenant’s initial occupancy, written notice of the fact that the landlord leases three or fewer dwelling units. Id.

The rent deposit procedure is comprised of five requirements.

First, one of three conditions must be met. The landlord must be in noncompliance with an obligation under the rental agreement or R.C. 5321.04 (see Section VII.C, above), other than R.C. 5321.04(A)(9). R.C. 5321.07(A). Or, based on the condition of the premises, the tenant must reasonably believe that the landlord is in noncompliance with one (or more) of those obligations.Id. Or a governmental agency must have found that the premises are in noncompliance with an applicable building, housing, health, or safety code and the noncompliance could materially affect the health and safety of an occupant. Id.

Second, the tenant must provide the landlord with a written notice that specifies the acts, omissions, or code violations that constitute the landlord’s noncompliance with R.C. 5321.04, the rental agreement, or the applicable code. 293 R.C. 5321.07(A).

The written notice is not required to state that, if the landlord fails to remedy the condition specified in the notice, the tenant may rent deposit. See R.C. 5321.07(A).

See also Swogger v. Subject to one exception, the landlord waives this notic e requirement if the landlord fails to provide the tenant, at the commencement of the tenancy, with written notice of the name and address of the landlord and the landlord’s agent (if any). R.C.

5321.18(C). E.g., Shoemaker v. Whitt , 129 Ohio App.3d 591, 718 N.E.2d 932 (2nd
Dist. 1998). The landlord’s noncompliance with R.C. 5321.18 does not waive this notice requirement if the tenant has actual knowledge of the landlord’s name and
address. Highland House Co. v. Gedos, No. 47064, 1984 WL 14194 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 16, 1984).

The landlord also may waive this notice requirement if the landlord suggests or consents to the rent deposit. Liggett v. Shitaker Props., No. 23425, 2010 WL 1410758 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Apr. 9, 2010). InLiggett, during a November 5 discussion with new management, the tenant expressed reluctance to pay November rent because of defective conditions. In response, the new management suggested that the tenant deposit the rent with the clerk of court. The court held that, in these circumstances, the landlord “consented to Liggett’s escrowing the November rent money with the court and has waived his right to object.” Id., 2010 WL 1410758, ¶ 44.

Third, the tenant must give the landlord a reasonable time up to thirty days to remedy the condition specified in the R.C. 5321.07 notice (or to respond otherwise, such as furnishing information to refute the alleged noncompliance). R.C. 5321.07(B).

The determination of what constitutes a reasonable amount of time is based on the “severity of the condition and the time necessary to remedy such condition ....” Id.

E.g., Chasteen v. Dix Road Prop. Mgmt. LLC , Nos. CA2020 -04-055,056, 2021 WL 669061, ¶ 40 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Feb. 22, 2021) (three weeks met the reasonable time requirement for landlord to remedy damage from sewage flood) ; Stiffler v.

Canterbury Runn Apts. No. 19308, 2002 WL 31243541, ¶ 3 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist.,
Oct. 4, 2002) (fourteen days met the reasonable time requirement for landlord to repair water leak in kitchen ceiling where “water was running down her cabinetsand onto her dishes, and mold was growing on her walls and carpet, which was becoming flooded.

In addition, the globes covering her light fixtures were filling with water”).

In any case, “R.C. 5321.07(B)(3) gives a landlord thirty day s, at the most , to remedy a condition that violates the obligations imposed by R.C. 5321.04 or that a tenant reasonably believes to violate those obligations.” CMB P’ship v. Baker, No.

18159, 2000 WL 706824, at *3 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., June 2, 2000) (italics original).

Accord Pedra Props., L.L.C. v. Justmann, No. 102909, 2015 WL 9438446,¶ 18 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 24, 2015).

Fourth, based on the landlord’s response to the R.C. 5321.07 notice, the tenant must reassess whether there are still valid grounds to rent deposit. The tenant is not authorized to rent deposit if, within a reasonable time after service of the notice, the landlord remedies the condition specified in the notice or, in refuting the tenant’s noncompliance claim, the landlord negates any reasonable basis for asserting the claim.

R.C. 5321.07(A),(B).

Fifth, the tenant must be current in rent payments due under the rental agreement.

R.C. 5321.07(B).

If these five requ irements for rent depositing have been satisfied, and if the landlord is not exempt from the rent deposit procedure, the tenant may then deposit rent, as the rent becomes due, with the clerk of court.

When a tenant tenders a rent deposit, the “clerk of co urt ... is under a clear legal duty to accept ... [the tenant’s] tendered rent monies under R.C. 5321.07 ....” State ex
rel. Taynor v. Hysell, 19 Ohio App.3d 120, 121, 483 N.E.2d 156, 158 (10th Dist. 1984).

It is a “ministerial duty” of the clerk. Id.

Before accepting a rent deposit, the clerk of court may require the tenant “to sign an affidavit swearing that the statutory preconditions to rent escrow have been met.” Chernin v. Welchans, 641 F. Supp. 1349, 1355 (N.D. Ohio 1986),aff’d, 844 F.2d 322, 324 (6th Cir. 1988). See, e.g., Crease v. Woods of Centerville, No. 14819, 1995 WL 632085, at *2 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 25, 1995).

However, the clerk of court may not require the tenant to pay a fee in advance of rent depositing. State ex rel. Gudzinas v. Constantino, 43 Ohio App.3d 52, 539 N.E.2d 173 (11th Dist. 1988). “[T]he statute clearly places the onus of paying the fee on the landlord after the procedure [of R.C. 5321.09] has been completed.” Id. at 54.

Upon receiving a tenant’s rent deposit,the clerk of court is required to provide the landlord with written notice of the rent deposit, to place the deposited rent in a separate rent escrow account, and to record the deposit in a separate docket. R.C. 5321.08.

However, if the landlord failed to comply with the landlord’s R.C. 5321.18 obligation (i.e., written notice to the tenant of the landlord’s name and address, at the commencement of the tenant’s occupancy), the landlord waives the right to receive the clerk of court’s R.C. 5321.08 notice of the rent deposit. R.C. 5321.18(C).
