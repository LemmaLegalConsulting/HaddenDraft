## Source text

“R.C. 5321.16 is a remedial statute intended to compensate the tenant for a wrongfully withheld deposit at no expense to the tenant.” Klein v. Moutz, 118 Ohio St.3d 256, 888 N.E.2d 404, ¶ 13 (2008). “[A]n important objective of the statute” is “to ensure that the tenant incurs no expense when seeking return of the deposit wrongfully withheld.” Id., ¶ 17. Accord Christe v. GMS Mgmt. Co. , 88 Ohio St.3d 376, 378, 726 N.E.2d 497 (2000).

If the landlord retains the tenant’s security deposit (in whole or in part) in violation of R.C. 5321.16(B) and the tenant has provided the landlord with a forwarding address in co mpliance with R.C. 5321.16(B), the tenant may bring an action against the landlord for: (1) the portion of the security deposit that has been wrongfully withheld,
(2) statutory damages in an amount equal to the portion of the security deposit that has been wrongfully withheld, and (3) reasonable attorney’s fees. R.C. 5321.16(C).

If the landlord retains the tenant’s security deposit (in whole or in part) in violation of R.C. 5321.16(B) and the tenant has not provided the landlord with a forwarding address in compliance with R.C. 5321.16(B), the tenant may bring an action against the landlord for the portion of the security deposit that has been wrongfully withheld, but not for statutory damages or reasonable attorney’s fees under R.C. 5321.16(C).
