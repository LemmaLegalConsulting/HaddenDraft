Provisions Every residential rental agreement incorporates by operation of law the rights and duties that are established in R.C. Chapter 5321.Shoemaker v. Whitt, 129 Ohio App.3d 591, 595, 718 N.E.2d 932 (2nd Dist. 1998); Delker v. Kerr, No. L-96-110, 1996 WL 532319, at *3 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Sept. 20, 1996); Rand v. Washington, No. 7822, 1983 WL 2448, at *1 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., May 26, 1983); Laster v. Bowman , 52 Ohio App.2d 379, 382, 370 N.E.2d 767 (8th Dist. 1977);Craver v. Doogan, No. 2003- CVC-00982, 2005 WL 4985282 (Ohio C.P., Clermont Cty., May 27, 2005).

Therefore, every residential rental agreement includes the:
(1) R.C. 5321.02,.03 prohibition against retaliation (see Section II.T, above, and Chapter XI, below);
(2) R.C. 53 21.04 landlord duty to maintain and provide services for the premises (see Section VII.C, below);
(3) R.C. 5321.05 tenant duty to maintain and properly use the premises (see Section VII.D, below);
(4) R.C. 5321.04 and 5321.05 rights and duties regarding landlord entry upon the premises (see Section VII.E and Chapter X, below);

(5) R.C. 5321.11 requirement for terminating a rental agreement for tenant conduct that violates R.C. 5321.05 (see Section I.D.2, above);
(6) R.C. 5321.15 prohibition against self-help eviction and distraint of rent (see Chapters XII and XIII, below);
(7) R.C. 5321.16 rights and duties regarding security deposits (see Chapter XVI, below);
(8) R.C. 5321.17 requirement for terminating a periodic tenancy (see Section I.D.3, above); and
(9) R.C. 5321.18 landlord duty to provide the tenant with written notice of the owner’s (and owner’s agent’s) name and address.

Residential rental agreements also may incorporate the contract remedies that R.C.

Chapter 5321 establishes. See, e.g., Kelly v. Chillicothe Metro. Hous . Auth., No. 10- 00430 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, Apr. 5, 2011). In Kelly, a Public Housing tenant asserted R.C. 5321.04, 5321.15, and common law tort claims against a public housing authority (“PHA”). The court held that the PHA’s immunity under R.C. Chapter 2744 (see Section XXII.I, below) did not bar the R.C. 5321.04 contract claim or the portion of the R.C. 5321.15 claim that is a contract claim. It then held that this contract claim included the tenant’s right under R.C. 5321.15 to recover reasonable attorney’s fees.
