Pojman v. Columbia -Brookpark Mgmt., LLC , No. 88666, 2007 WL 2269682 (O hio App., 8th Dist., Aug. 9, 2007) The court sets forth a two -part test – procedural ( e.g., bargaining power) and substantive (e.g., unfairness) – for determining whether a rental agreement provision is unconscionable in violation of R.C. 3733.16. For ren t increases, the relevant issue is whether the new rent charged (not the amount of the increase) is unconscionable.

The R.C. 3733.16 clause “a reasonable opportunity to present evidence” does not preclude a summary judgment ruling without a hearing.
Props. Ltd. v. Coffee, No. 93-CVG-8597 (Ohio Mun., Marion Cty., Nov. 16, 1993) The rental agreement imposed a fee for late payment of rent that permitted fees up to 61% of the basic rent charge. The late payment fee was unconscionable and thus violated R.C. 3733.16. The court limited the fee to a maximum of 25% of the $115 per month basic rent ($28.75 per month).
