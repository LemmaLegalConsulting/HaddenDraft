# d. Specificity of the Notice – Lead Paint

## Source text

The specificity required for the notice’s description of the defective condition has been a particularly important issue in lead-based paint cases.

435 In Winston Props. v. Sanders , 57 Ohio App.3d 28, 565 N.E.2d 1280 (1st Dist.

1989), the landlord did not know that the rental premises contained lead paint. The court held that the tenant’s notice to the landlord about peeling paint and cracked plaster was not notice of the existence of lead-based paint. On similar facts, other courts have followed the decision in Winston Props. E.g., Patterson v. Ahmed, No. L- 09-1222, 2010 WL 3449257 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Sept. 3, 2010); Lowery v. Ondrus,
No. L-08-1100, 2009 WL 50125 (Ohio App., 6 th Dist., Jan. 9, 2009); Murphy v. Leo Baur Realty, Inc., No. 63756, 1993 WL 425353 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Oct. 21, 1993);

Rice v. Reid, No. 3-91-34, 1992 WL 81424 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Apr. 23, 1992).

However, a landlord may have constructive notice of a lead-based paint hazard at the premises if the landlord has notice of peeling or chipping paint at the premises and the landlord is aware of the possibility of lead-based paint at the premises. See Webster
v. Shaw, 63 N.E.3d 677, 2016 WL 1484 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., 2016); Richardson v.

Boes, 179 Ohio App.3d 418, 902 N.E.2d 77 (6th Dist. 2008); Harris v. Tigner , 108 Ohio App.3d 152, 670 N.E.2d 304 (8th Dist. 1995); Walker v. Barnett Mgmt., Inc. ,
Nos. 84188,84210, 2004 WL 2847810 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 9, 2004); Bell v.

Goldsmith, No. 67893, 1995 WL 396352 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., July 6, 1995);

Straughter v. Stark Metro. Hous. Auth., No. CA-8696, 1992 WL 127098 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 1, 1992).

If a landlord is not on notice of peeling or chipping paint, the landlord may not be on notice of a lead-based paint hazard at the premises. See Knox v. Clark, No. 03-CA- 95, 2004 WL 1888283 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Aug. 23, 2004).
