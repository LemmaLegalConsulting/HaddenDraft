When a landlord claims nonpayment of rent or a charge as the basis for an eviction 116 Generally, a landlord will accept payment of the rent that accrues during an eviction action only after the conclusion of the action. Conway v. Nissley, No. 68536, 1995 WL 723298, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 7, 1995);Mazzarella v. McGinnes, No. 91-CVG-30291 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Dec. 30, 1991). A landlord’s earlier acceptance of such rent may constitute an acceptance of future rent that waives the R.C . 1923.04 notice to vacate. See Section II.C, above.

action, an accord and satisfaction may be a defense if two conditions are met: (1) the alleged nonpayment is an unliquidated debt or the tenant in good faith disputes the amount due; and (2) the tenant has tendered a payment to the landlord in an amount less than the amount that the landlord demanded, on the express condition that it shall be payment in full settlement of the landlord’s claim. Seeds, Grain & Hay Co. v.

Conger, 83 Ohio St. 169, 93 N.E. 892 (1910); Inger Interiors v. Peralta , 30 Ohio App.3d 94, 506 N.E.2d 1199 (8th Dist. 1986); First Nat ’l Bank & Trust Co. v.

Fireproof Warehouse & Storage , 8 Ohio App.3d 253, 456 N.E.2d 1336 (10th Dist.

1983).

The condition of full settlement may be stated either on the check,Inger Interiors, 30 Ohio App.3d at 95, or in a letter accompanying the payment, First Nat’l Bank & Trust Co., 8 Ohio App.3d at 255.117 In these circumstances, the landlord has two alternative courses of action. The landlord may reject or return the entire payment to thetenant or the landlord may accept and retain the payment upon the terms of the tenant’s condition. Conger, 83 Ohio St.

at 174-75; First Nat’l Bank & Trust Co., 8 Ohio App. 3d at 255. “He cannot accept the tender in such case and recover the balance which he claims ....” Conger, 83 Ohio
St. at 174-75.118 If the landlord accepts and retains the payment, the landlord takes the payment subject to the terms of the tenant’s condition, regardless of any protest that the landlord may make to the contrary. Inger Interiors, 30 Ohio App.3d at 95-96; First Nat’l Bank & Trust Co. , 8 Ohio App.3d at 255. For example, the landlord may not prevent an accord and satisfaction by crossing out the paid in full notation and writing partial payment on the check before cashing it. Inger Interiors, 30 Ohio App.3d at 95-96.
