# 2. Comply with the Building, Housing, Health, and

## Source text

Safety Codes, R.C. 5321.04(A)(1) R.C. 5321.04(A)(1) requires landlords “ to comply with the requirements of all applicable building, housing, health, and safety codes that materially affect health and safety.” E.g., McHugh v. Zataar, No. 14-CA-010591, 2015 WL 7273177 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Jan. 20, 2015) (city code, duty to in stall handrail on basement stairway);

Burdick v. Nevel, No. 98AP-697, 1999 WL 235435 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Apr. 20,
1999) (city code, duty to install and inspect smoke detectors); Primes v. Milbry, No.

18236, 1997 WL 760698 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Nov. 19, 1997) (building code, width and handrail requirements for stairways).

If the alleged defect does not violate an applicable building, housing, health, or safety code, it cannot constitute a violation of R.C. 5321.04(A)(1). McDaniels v.

Petrosky, No. 97-APE08-1027, 1998 WL 54363 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Feb. 5, 1998) (tree stump in backyard did not violate any applicable code);Taylor v. Alexander, No.

3550, 1986 WL 7800 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., July 11, 1986) (absence of handrail within interior of apartment did not violate any applicable code).

Also, if the alleged code violation does not materially affect health and safety, it cannot constitute a violation of R.C. 5321.04(A)(1).Pedra Props., L.L.C. v. Justmann,
No. 102909, 2015 WL 9438446 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 24, 2015) (code violation 315
– roof leak in laundry room located within the dwelling unit – did not constitute a violation of R.C. 5321.04(A)(1) because trial court found it did not materially affect health and safety and appellate reco rd did not include a transcript or an appropriate substitute); Wenzke v. Baird, No. L-13-1244, 2014 WL 3408590 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., July 11, 2014) (code violation– six inoperable windows– did not constitute a violation of R.C. 5321.04(A)(1) because the trial court failed to find the code violations materially affected health and safety).
