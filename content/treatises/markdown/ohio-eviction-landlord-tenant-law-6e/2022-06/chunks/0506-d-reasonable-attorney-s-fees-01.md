# D. Reasonable Attorney’s Fees

## Table-of-contents context

- XVIII. Landlord Claim for Property
  - D. Reasonable Attorney’s Fees

## Source text

In an R.C. 5321.05(C) claim, a landlord generally may recover reasonable attorney’s fees if the tenant violated an R.C. 5321.05 oblig ation. E.g., Hensel v.

Childress, 145 N.E.3d 1159, 2019-Ohio-3934 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., 2019). Snyder v.

Waldron, No. 12 -CA-9, 2013 WL 3988775 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., July 26, 2013);

Zimmerman v. Elmore , No. 47724, 1984 WL 5119 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 28, 1984); Katchmer v. Bishop, No. 90-CVG-34263 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div.,
Oct. 3, 1991).

But the case law is split on whether the tenant’s liability for attorney’s fees under R.C. 5321.05(C) is mandatory or lies within the discretion of the trial court. Compare Kinn v. Showe Mgmt. Corp. , No. 5 -01-46, 2002 WL 462859 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist.,
Mar. 27, 2002) (mandatory) with Fiffick v. Showe Mgmt., No. 2005-P-0011, 2005 WL 3610442 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Dec. 29, 2005) (discretionary).

For post-judgment work, the landlord’s entitlement to attorney’s fees under R.C.

5321.05(C) lies within the sound discretion of the trial court. Chapman v. Smith, No.

L-81-344, 1982 WL 6883 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Apr. 16, 1982).

Also, in two circumstances, the landlordcannot recover attorney’s fees under R.C.

5321.05(C), notwithstanding the tenant’s R.C. 5321.05 violation.

One is where: (1) an action includes the landlord’s R.C. 5321.05(C) claim and the tenant’s R.C. 5321.16(C) security deposit claim, (2) the amountof the R.C. 5321.05(C) judgment for the landlord is less than the portion of the security deposit that the landlord retained, and (3) the tenant is thus awarded an R.C. 5321.16(C) judgment.

Danello v. Holyko, No. 50339, 1986 WL 3471 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 20, 1986).

The second is where: (1) the amount of the landlord’s R.C. 5321.05(C) claim (and
judgment) is less than the tenant’s security deposit, and (2) the tenant forfeited the security deposit without dispute. Hines v. Riley , 129 Ohio App.3d 379, 717 N.E.2d 1133 (4th Dist. 1998); Snapp v. Rossiter, No. 14-CVF-00579 (Ohio Mun., Cambridge,
Aug. 27, 2015) (applying Hines).

The determination on the amount of the attorney’s fees to be awarded under R.C.

5321.05(C) lies within the sound discretion of th e trial court. Kinn v. Showe Mgmt.

Corp., No. 5-01-46, 2002 WL 462859 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Mar. 27, 2002).

When an action includes an R.C. 5321.05(C) claim along with other landlord claims (e.g., unpaid rent claim), the computation of the R.C. 5321.05 (C) attorney fee award is based solely on the time spent on the R.C. 5321.05(C) claim. Hines v. Riley, 129 Ohio App.3d 379, 717 N.E.2d 1133 (4th Dist. 1998); Svoboda v. Hayes, No. 90- CVG-35237 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Dec. 26, 1991);Schuab v. Roper, No.

90-CVG-26859 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Nov. 6, 1991).

For a discussion on issues relative to calculating and moving for R.C. Chapter 5321 attorney’s fees, see Chapter XIX, below.
