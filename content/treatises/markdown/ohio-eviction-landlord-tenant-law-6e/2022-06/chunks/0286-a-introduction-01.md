## Source text

R.C. 5321.04(A)(2) requires a landlord to “make all repairs and do whateve r is reasonably necessary to put and keep the premises in a fit and habitable condition.” This requirement is similar to the common law implied warranty of habitability,268 which other jurisdictions recognize and some Ohio courts recognized before the enactment of R.C. Chapter 5321 (see Section II.P, above).

However, R.C. 5321.04(A)(2) requires a landlord to maintain the premises in both a fit condition and a habitable condition. A defective condition in the premises may cause the premises to be less than fit, in violation of R.C. 5321.04(A)(2), even though it does not render the premises uninhabitable. Tellema v. Liette, No. 92-CA-14, 1993 WL 79270, at *2 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Mar. 11, 1993). Cf. Walling v. Holman, 858 F.2d 79, 83 (2d Cir. 1998) (New York law: rejects landlord’s contention that “the warranty [of habitability] is limited to conditions that endanger life, health, and safety, because the warranty extends to maintenance of the premises ‘in accord with the uses reasonably intended by the parties’”). But see V.R. v. Cincinnati -Hamilton County
Cmty. Action Agency, No. C-140230, 2014 WL 6090396, ¶ 12 (Ohio App., 1st Dist.,
Nov. 14, 2014).

268 See Pagoda Co. v. Smith, No. 37936, 1979 Ohio App. LEXIS 11691, at *12 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 8, 1979) (finding that the implied warranty of habitability in Glyco v. Schultz , 35 Ohio Misc. 25, 289 N.E.2d 919 (Ohio Mun., Sylvania, 1972), “sought to develop the same remedies through case law that the legislature later enacted through the statute”).
