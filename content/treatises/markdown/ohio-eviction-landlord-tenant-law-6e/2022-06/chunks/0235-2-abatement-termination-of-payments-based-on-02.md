## Source text

The third potential defense is equity. The landlord is not receiving the HCVP rent subsidy solely because of the landlord’s wrongful action– the landlord’s breach of the HQS obligations and thus the HAP contract and the lease. In these circumstances, it is not equitable to declare a forfeiture during the reasonable time that the tenant needs to transfer the HCVP subsidy to another rental unit, provided the tenant continues to pay the tenant’s HCVP portion of the rent.Cf. H-1 Mgmt. LLC v. Perryman, No. 2006- CVG-28200 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., July 10, 2007) (denial of landlord’s unjust enrichment claim for rent). The PHA determines this reasonable time when it sets the term of the voucher for the move. Generally, the term is between 60 and 120 days.
