# 9. Burden of Proof Issues, Assisted Housing

## Source text

In an eviction action for nonpayment of rent against an assisted housing tenant, 67 when the tenant’s defense is that the nonpayment of rent is due to circumstances beyond the tenant’s control (see Section III.G.1.a, below), the tenant has the burden of proving that the tenant did not pay the rent for reasons beyond the tenant’s control.

Stark Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Harris , No. CA-8687, 1992 WL 90131 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Apr. 20, 1992).

In any eviction acti on against an assisted housing tenant, the landlord has the burden of proving that it served the tenant with the applicable federal notice of termination (see Sections III.A-E, below). Nat’l Church Residences of Worthington v.

Timson, 78 Ohio App.3d 798, 605 N.E.2d 1346 (10th Dist. 1992).

However, if a tenant asserts as a defense the failure of a landlord to serve the applicable federal notice of termination, the tenant has the burden of proving the type of assisted housing in which the tenant resides. See Akron Metro. Hous. Auth. v.

Konich, No. 17177, 1995 WL 553250 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Sept. 20, 1995);Conifers
Ltd. P’ship v. McWilliams, No. 13721, 1993 WL 224755 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., June 23, 1993). But see Normar Corp. v. Richardson , No. 93 -CVG-9922 (Ohio Mun., Dayton, Mar. 3, 1994) (eviction claim dismissed because “Plaintiff has failed to present evidence showing which federal regulations in regard to Section 8 housing apply to the rental premises....Without such information the Court is prevented from making a crucial determination on whether Plaintiff or Defendant complied with the appropriate federal regulations”).
