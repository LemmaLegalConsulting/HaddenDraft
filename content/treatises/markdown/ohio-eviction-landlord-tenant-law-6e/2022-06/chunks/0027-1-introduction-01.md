In two circumstances, R.C. Chapter 5321 req uires a residential landlord to serve the tenant with a notice of termination, if the landlord seeks to terminate the tenancy.18 The first is where a landlord seeks to terminate a tenancy on the ground that the tenant has violated an R.C. 5321.05 duty (oth er than R.C. 5321.05(A)(9)) and the violation materially affects health and safety. R.C. 1923.02(A)(8), 5321.11.

The second is where a landlord seeks to terminate a month-to-month (or week-to-
week) tenancy and the tenant has not violated the rental agreement or any R.C. Chapter 5321 obligation. R.C. 5321.17(A),(B). See also Drake Townhouses, LLC v.

Woodberry, No. C -160632, 2017 WL 3168981 , ¶ 16 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., July 26, 2017); Eberly v. Irons, No. 2006AP-01-0004, 2007 WL 2351644, ¶ 34 (Ohio App.,5th Dist., Aug. 9, 2007). If the tenant has violated the rental agreement (e.g., failed to pay
rent) or an R.C. Chapter 5321 obligation, this notice of termination requirement is not
applicable. R.C. 5321.17(D). See also Drake Townhouses, LLC v. Woodberry, No. C- 160632, 2017 WL 3168981, ¶¶ 18-20 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., July 26, 2017); Denney v.

Carroll, No. 15 -14-10, 2015 WL 1966227 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., May 4, 2015);

Georgetown Park Apts. v. Woernley, 112 Ohio App.3d 428, 679 N.E.2d 16 (8th Dist.

1996); Gerber v. Bistor , No. 1795, 1982 WL 2806 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Oct. 27, 1982).

These notices of termination are a different type of notice than the R.C. 1923.04(A) notice to vacate, which is discussed in Section I.C, above. The notice of termination terminates or proposes to terminate the tenancy, whereas the R.C. 1923.04(A) notice to vacate is a prerequisite to the filing of an eviction action. See Gvozdanovic v.
