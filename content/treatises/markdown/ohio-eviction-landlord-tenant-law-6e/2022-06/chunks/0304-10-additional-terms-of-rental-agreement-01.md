# 10. Additional Terms of Rental Agreement

## Source text

In the express terms of a re ntal agreement, a landlord may agree to assume additional duties to maintain the premises, provided they are consistent with R.C.

Chapter 5321. See R.C. 5321.06. For example, R.C. 5321.13(F) permits a rental agreement provision under which thelandlord assumes responsibility for fulfilling one 275 In these circumstances, the landlord may also be in violation of the R.C. 5321.04(A)(2) obligation to “do whatever is reasonably necessary to put and keep the premises in a fit and habitable condition.” See Scarnati v. Owners of Georgetown of Columbus, No. 96-APE01-52, 1996 WL 339963 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., June 18, 1996) (applies R.C. 5321.04(A)(2) to complaint that neighboring tenant made excessive noise, but, based on landlord’s reasonable investigation, finds no excessive noise and thus no violation). See also Cohen v. Werner , 82 Misc.2d 295, 368 N.Y.S.2d 1005 (Civ. Ct. 1975) (breach of implied warranty of habitability).

or more of the tenant’s R.C. 5321.05 obligations, which are discussed in Section VII.D, below.
