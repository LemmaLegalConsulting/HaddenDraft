## Source text

A notice to vacate is not required to state the reason for the eviction. Snyder v.

Wylie, No. L -17-1019, 2017 WL 4334189 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Sept. 29, 2017);

Venetta v. Battle, No. 88AP-1044, 1990 WL 157344 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Oct. 18, 1990); N.W. Ohio Food Marts, Inc. v. Mugg , No. L -86-115, 1987 WL 7791 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Mar. 13, 1987); Gerber v. Bistor , No. 1795, 1982 WL 2806 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Oct. 27, 1982); McKoy v. Bell, No. 2021-CVG-008782 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Nov. 9, 2021); Hawkins v. Collier , No. 87 -CVG-884 (Ohio Mun., Portsmouth, Sept. 10, 1987). But see DePalmo v. George , No. 88-CVG-0572 (Ohio Mun., Licking Cty., Aug. 23, 1988) (“although there is authority to the contrary, this court finds that due process mandates that a landlord, at least in general terms which would put the tenant on notice of th e acts complained of, specify the grounds 17 for the eviction in the three-day notice”).

However, if the notice to vacate includes a statement of the reason for the eviction, it may preclude the landlord from asserting any other reasons in the eviction action .

Merriweather v. Wilcox, No. 2021-CVG-011765 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div.,
Jan. 26, 2022); McKoy v. Bell, No. 2021-CVG-008782 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous.

Div., Nov. 9, 2021); Venetta v. Staples, No. M88-CVG-12477 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., May 13, 1988). Cf. Milbourn v. Aska, 81 Ohio App. 79, 82, 77 N.E.2d 619 (3rd
Dist. 1946) (notice declaring forfeiture of lease).

Also, if the notice to vacate states more than one reason for the eviction and the complaint alleges some but not all of those reasons, the landlord may rely on only those reasons alleged in the complaint. M.L.R. Props., Inc. v. Baer, No. L-85-330, 1986 WL 6703 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 13, 1986).

For further discussion on eviction complaints, see Section I.E, below.
