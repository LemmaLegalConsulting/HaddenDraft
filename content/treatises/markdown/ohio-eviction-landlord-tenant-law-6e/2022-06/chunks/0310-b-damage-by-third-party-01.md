In Wills, the court held that the tenants did not violate the R.C. 5321.05(A)(6) obligation, relative to a guest’s conduct, notwithstanding the guest’s role in a fire that damaged the premises. The fire started in the kitchen from grease that became overheated, while the tenants’ daughter (age twenty) was cooking. The daughter was a permissive user but not a tenant of the premises. The tenants were not present in the kitchen at the time of the fire.

In Dorsey, on facts nearly identical to those in Wills, the court likewise held that the tenants did not violate the R.C. 5321.05(A)(6) obligation. TheDorsey tenants were asleep in the premises at the time of the fire.

In Killings, the tenant was shopping when a babysitter started a fire in the kitchen, while preparing food. The court, relying on Wills and Dorsey, held that the tenant could not be liable under R.C. 5321.04(A)(6) because the tenant was not in the rental unit at the time of the fire.

Whether the Wills/Dorsey rule, relative to a guest’s conduct, appliesto the conduct of a co-tenant may depend on whether the lease contains a joint and several liability
provision. See Wayne Mut. Ins. Co. v. Parks , No. 20945, 2002 WL 1800377 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Aug. 7, 2002); Kinn v. Showe Mgmt. Corp. , No. 5 -01-46, 2002 WL 462859 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Mar. 27, 2002); Young v. McCleese, No. 97-CA-2351, 1998 WL 399861 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., July 13, 1998); Cent. Mut. Ins. Co. v. Faller, 71 Ohio Misc.2d 58, 654 N.E.2d 213 (Ohio C.P., Licking Cty., 1995).

Young held that the Wills/Dorsey rule does not apply to a co-tenant. Kinn rejected the Young position and held that the Wills/Dorsey rule does apply to a co -tenant.

Wayne Mutual distinguished Kinn on the ground that the Kinn lease did not contain, and the Young lease did contain, a joint and several liability provision. Wayne Mutual held that a joint and several liability provision is consistent with R.C. 5321.05(A)(6) and that, if the lease contains a joint and several liabi lity provision, the Wills/Dorsey rule does not apply to a co-tenant. Faller held that the Wills/Dorsey rule applied to the co-tenant, but then held the non-negligent co-tenant liable under the lease, without any reference to a joint and several liability provision.

A tenant is not liable under R.C. 5321.05(A)(6) for the conduct of an unknown or non-guest third party. Wilhelm v. Ison, No. 92-CA-97, 1993 WL 76961 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Mar. 15, 1993) (when delivering a refrigerator for the landlord, store personnel damaged the floor of the premises); Jenkins v. Boyce, 94 Ohio Misc.2d 98, 703 N.E.2d 392 (Ohio Mun., Akron, 1998) (the criminal conduct of trespassers damaged the premises);Aetna Cas. & Surety Co. v. Neff, 30 F. Supp.2d 990 (S.D. Ohio
1998) (appl ying Ohio law) (the intentional acts of a third party, who was on the premises without the tenant’s permission, damaged the premises).
