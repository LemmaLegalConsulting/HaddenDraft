# a. Compulsory Counterclaims

## Source text

A landlord’s claim for unpaid rent, or for damage caused by the tenant’s violation of R.C. 5321.05 or the rent al agreement, is a compulsory counterclaim (as well as a
defense) to the tenant’s security deposit claim. Cochran v. Larson , No. 7311, 1982 WL 3656 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Jan. 8, 1982); Thirion v. Tutoki, 94 Ohio Misc.2d 77, 703 N.E.2d 378 (Ohio Mun., War ren, 1998). Cf. Pruitt v. Mauger Mgmt. Co. , No.

77AP-367, 1977 Ohio App. LEXIS 7605 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Sept. 29, 1977) (R.C.

5321.09 release of rent deposit action and R.C. 5321.16 security deposit action a rose out of the same transaction for Civil Rule 13(A) purposes).

Since Civil Rule 13(A) applies to proceedings in a municipal court, small claims division, these landlord claims are compulsory counterclaims even if the tenant files the security deposit claim in a small claims court. Thirion v. Tutoki, 94 Ohio Misc.2d 77, 703 N.E.2d 378 (Ohio Mun., Warren, 1998).

A landlord’s right to file these counterclaims is not waived by the landlord’s noncompliance with the R.C. 5321.16(B) written itemization requirement. See Section XVII.C.8, below.

A tenant’s claim for refund of a security deposit likewise may be a compulsory counterclaim to a landlord’s claim for unpaid rent. GMS Mgmt. Co. v. Seminaro, No.

63007, 1993 WL 277073 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., July 22, 1993).
