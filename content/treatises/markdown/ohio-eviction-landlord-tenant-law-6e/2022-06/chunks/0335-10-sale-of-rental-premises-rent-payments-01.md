# 10. Sale of Rental Premises, Rent Payments

## Source text

After a sale (including a foreclosure sale) of occupied rental premises, the tenant is obligated to pay the future rent to the new owner. See Commercial Bank & Sav. Co.
v. Woodville Sav. Bank Co., 126 Ohio St. 587, 186 N.E. 444 (1933);Heifner v. Heifner,
No. 1-88-32, 1989 WL 145162 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Nov. 28, 1989). Cf. Ucker v.

HER Realtors , No. 89AP -520, 1989 WL 110993 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Sept. 26, 1989).

The tenant is obligated to pay to the former landlord (and not to the new owner) any rent that has accrued and remained unpaid as of the time of the sale. See Commercial Bank & Sav. Co. v. Woodville Sav. Bank Co., 126 Ohio St. 587, 186 N.E.

444 (1933); Mintz v. Tannous, 74 Ohio App.3d 636, 600 N.E.2d 269 (8th Dist. 1991);

Williams v. Martin , 83 Ohio App. 130, 82 N.E.2d 547 (1st Dist. 1948); Varnau v.

Scanlon, 17 Ohio Law Abs. 246 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., 1934). Accord Ginsberg v.

Austin, 968 F.2d 1198 (Fed. Cir. 1992).

Therefore, the new owner is obligated to timely inform the tenant: (1) of the new owner’s purchase of the property, and (2) as to whom andwhere rent payments are to be made. In cases where the new owner failed to do so and then filed an eviction action for nonpayment of rent, courts have entered the eviction judgment in favor of the tenant on equity grounds. E.g., Zanetos v. Sparks, 13 Ohio App.3d 242, 244, 468 N.E.2d 938 (10th Dist. 1984) (among the equity factors was “plaintiff’s failure to timely notify Bolin [tenant] of the sale and advise him to whom payment should be made”); Clark
v. Mills, No. 20-CVG-01530 (Ohio Mun., Akron, June 10, 2020) (cites Zanetos; after the sale of the property, tenant “was uncertain who he should pay rent to and he wanted to meet the new owner before he made payment”).

Until the new owner provides the tenant with rent payment instructions, a reasonable approach for the tenant is to hold the rent money for later payment. This would be consistent with Ohio law: “In the absence of an agreement to the contrary, the general rule in Ohio is that payment of rent is to be made at the leased premises and that it is the landlord’s duty to go to the premise to collect rent from the tenant.” Davis v. Scullark, No. 57456, 1990 WL 145642, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Oct. 4, 1990).

If the new owner fails to provide a tenant with timely notice of the sale, it may be a direct cause of the tenant making a monthly rental payment to the former landlord, instead of the new owner. When this occurs, either: (1) the payment satisfies the tenant’s rental obligation for that month, or (2) the inaction of the new owner ( i.e., failure to notify the tenant) prevented the tenant from complying with the tenant’s rental obligation, and thus excuses the noncompliance. “Non -performance of a promise or condition in a lease is excused where performance is prevented by the other party to the lease.” Prof’l Invs. of Am., Inc. v. Ross, No. 44540, 1982 WL 2579, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 9, 1982). For additional discussion and case law on this issue, see Section II.Z, above.

Any claim of the new owner for prepaid rent is against the former landlord, not the
tenant. See Loveless v. Erie R.R., 2 Ohio App. 404 (11th Dist. 1914).

For a discussion on the respective liability of the former landlord and the new owner, when a landlord sells rental property and, as of the time of the sale, the landlord has failed to remedy a defective condition at the rental premises in violation of R.C.

5321.04(A), see Section IX.B.6, below.
