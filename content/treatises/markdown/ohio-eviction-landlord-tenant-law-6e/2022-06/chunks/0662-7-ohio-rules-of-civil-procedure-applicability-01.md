# 7. Ohio Rules of Civil Procedure, Applicability

## Source text

An R.C. Chapter 2506 appeal is a special statutory proceeding under Civ il Rule 1(C)(7). Quick v. Wolfe, No. 81AP-561, 1981 WL 3422 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Aug.

20, 1981). Therefore, “each rule must be considered independently to determine its
applicability. Id., 1981 WL 3422, at *1.

Court decisions on the applicability of a Civil Rule to R.C. Chapter 2506 proceedings include the following decisions.

Civil Rules 4 to 4.6: Not Applicable Jacobs v. Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. , No. 102248, 2015 WL 3647561 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 11, 2015).

Civil Rule 41(B)(1): Applicable Parker v. Lake Metro. Hous. Auth. , No. 2012 -L-054, 2012 WL 5995062 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Dec. 3, 2012).

Civil Rule 52: Applicable, with limitation per Jandecka Jandecka v. Petre , No. 46623, 1983 WL 2695 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Oct. 27,
1983) (applicable, if the court takes new evidence, or makes new findings from the administrative record, or accepts the administrative findings; not applicable, “if the court simply reviews the administrative record, and bases its decision on the legal conclusions”).

Civil Rule 59: Applicable Quick v. Wolfe , No. 81AP -561, 1981 WL 3422 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Aug. 20, 1981)
