182 A consolidation of the federal notice of termination and the R.C. 1923.04 notice to vacate raises a second issue – whether, in consolidating these notices, the assisted housing landlord is serving the R.C. 1923.04 notice to vacate before the termination of the tenancy and thus

As HUD noted in its introdu ctory comments to 24 C.F.R. § 966.4(l)(3)(iii): “Federal law does not affect the time when the State notice may be given, provided the State law notice procedure does not interfere with the Federally required notice of lease termination, or abridge the tenant’s Federal right to a grievance hearing.” 56 Fed. 215 However, one Ohio court hel d, relative to project-based subsidized housing, that consolidation of these notices is notpermitted if the language of the consolidated notice fails to fulfill the purpose of the federal regulations. Crossroads Somerset Ltd. v.

Newland, 40 Ohio App.3d 20, 24, 531 N.E.2d 327 (10th Dist. 1987). “[T]hat purpose envisions a meeting between landlord and tenant in which both parties attempt to resolve whatever proble m gave rise to the ten -day notice.” Id. Accord Gorsuch Homes, Inc. v. Wooten, 73 Ohio App.3d 426, 434, 597 N.E.2d 554 (2nd Dist. 1992).

In Newland, the court held a consolidated notice to be invalid because it included the statement: “If you refuse to le ave this unit within ten (10) days, Crossroads/Somerset may enforce this notice ONLY by bringing a judicial action.” Newland, 40 Ohio App. 3d at 24. The court held that this language was contrary to the intent of the federal regulations because it indica ted that the administrative meeting would be useless and that the tenant would be required to vacate despite the tenant’s opportunity to request a meeting with the landlord. Id.
