# 3. Recording of the Contract

## Table-of-contents context

- XXIII. Land Installment Contracts,

## Source text

R.C. 5313.02(C) requires the vendor to record the land installment contract, as provided in R.C. 5301.25, within twe nty days after the contract has been signed by both the vendor and the vendee. “[T]he purpose of R.C. 5313.02(C) is to protect subsequent bona fide purchasers of property.” Bradford v. B & P Wrecking Co., 171 Ohio App.3d 616, 872 N.E.2d 331, ¶ 32 (6th Dist. 2007). Accord McGinnis v. Fuller,
No. 3-04-29, 2005 WL 1205117, ¶ 20 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., May 23, 2005).

Therefore, a vendor’s failure to record the contract does not render it unenforceable as between the vendee and the vendor. Bradford v. B & P Wrecking Co., 171 Ohio App.3d 616, 872 N.E.2d 331, ¶ 32 (6th Dist. 2007); Burkett v. Strickland, No. 02-CV- 7400, (Ohio C.P., Montgomery Cty., Sept. 29, 2003). See also Merivale Invs. v.

Tuggle, No. L-08-1439, 2009 WL 4727760, ¶¶ 23 -31 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Dec. 11, 637 2009).

The failure to record a land installment contract also does not prevent a vendee from enforcing the vendee’s equitable interest against the vendor’s judgment creditors.

McGinnis v. Fuller, No. 3-04-29, 2005 WL 1205117 (Ohio App., 3r d Dist., May 23, 2005); State Fid. Fed. Sav. & Loan Ass’n v. Wehrby, 25 Ohio Misc. 221, 263 N.E.2d 801 (C.P., Montgomery Cty., 1970). “The law is clear that judgment creditors are not bona fide purchasers for value.” McGinnis, 2005 WL 1205117, ¶ 20.
