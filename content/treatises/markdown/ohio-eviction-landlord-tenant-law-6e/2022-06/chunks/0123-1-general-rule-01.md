“The only difference created by eviction based upon [another] breach instead of nonpayment of rent essentially is that [the landlord] has waived its three-day notice but may re -file its FED action after providing [the tenant] with another three -day notice….” N. Face Props., Inc. v. Lin , 2013 WL 2423802, ¶ 17.99 See also Cornerstone Cos. v. Zipkin , 60 Ohio Misc.2d 14, 16, 573 N.E.2d 228 (Ohio Mun., Akron, 1989). When the eviction action is based on nonpayment of rent, the landlord’s acceptance of future rent usually eliminates the tenant’s rent arrearage and thus the basis for re-filing the eviction action.

A lease provision cannot waive this waiver rule. IDC Brunswick Crossroads, LLC
v. Gack, Inc. , No. 10CA -0088-M, 2012 WL 177997 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Jan. 23, 2012).

When the landlord’s acceptance of future rent waives the notice to vacate, the trial court lacks subject -matter jurisdiction over the eviction action. See Section I.C.1.b, above.
