# 1. Omission or Misstatement of a Required Element

## Source text

If the notice of terminati on omits or misstates any of the elements that the applicable federal regulations require, the defect in the notice is a defense to the eviction action.173 The content of the notice must strictly comply with the requirements of the applicable federal regulations. See Section III.B, above.

Ohio state court decisions finding a notice invalid due to its omission or misstatement of a required element are set forth below, along with the relevant regulation (or lease or handbook provision) and the omission or misstatement.
(1) 24 C.F.R. § 247.4(a)(3) (project -based subsidized housing): the notice failed to advise the tenant that the landlord may seek to enforce the termination of tenancy only by bringing a judicial action, at which time the tenant may present a defense.
