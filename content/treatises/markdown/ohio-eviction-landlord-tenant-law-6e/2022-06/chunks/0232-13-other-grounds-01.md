# 13. Other Grounds

## Source text

Ohio court decisions that address other potential grounds for terminating an assisted housing tenancy are summarized below.

Poor housekeeping:

Cincinnati Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Cox , No. 01 -CV-24085 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., June 4, 2002) Tenant’s repeated failure to maintain the rental unit in compliance with the housing authority’s housekeeping standards was a material breach of the lease and constituted a threat to the health and safety of other tenants.

Court found sufficient cause to evict, and rejected an equity defense, because of tenant’s repeated failures to comply, tenant’s refusal to attend classes offered to improve her housekeeping, and the threat to health and safety.

Dayton Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Williams , No. 88 -CVG-1268 (Ohio Mun., Dayton, July 8, 1988) One instance of poor housekeeping was not grounds to evict the tenant because it was neither a serious nor repeated violation of the lease. Poor housekeeping that is not a threat to health or safety is a minor violation of the lease.

Madston Corp. v. Ahrens , No. 87 -CVG-815 (Ohio Mun., Port Clinton,
Feb. 5, 1988) The poor housekeeping of a mentally ill tenant, who had alocal counseling agency assisting him with housekeeping matters and where the poor housekeeping was not a hazard or danger to other tenants, was not good cause to evict the tenant.

Large Appliances Prohibited by Lease:

Taylor v. Marshall Plaza II , No. 88-CA-004372, 1989 WL 10309 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Feb. 8, 1989) The landlord’s rule that banned washers and dryers in the dwelling units was enforceable only if the rule was reasonable. The basis for the reasonableness requirement is the subsidized housing lease,221 ¶ 14(a).

Because there was a genuine issue as to whether the rule was reasonable, the granting of summary judgment was improper.
Mt. Moriah Dev. Corp. v. Smith, No. 83-CV-36104 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., Sept. 17, 1984) The tenant’s refusal to remove a compact freezer from her apartment was not a material breach of the rental agreement justifying termination of the
tenancy. Because the lease provision in issue prohibited all freezers, it was overly broad on its face and thus unenforceable. A rat ional rule regulating size or load limit would be enforceable.

221 For the definition of “subsidized housing lease,” see note 174, above.

259 Repair of Premises:

Wagar v. Hatnavy, No. 1433, 1985 WL 4734 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Dec.

26, 1985) Good cause to evict the tenant existed because the repairs of the premises that were necessaryto comply with orders of the county health department required vacation of the premises.

Colonial Am. Dev. v. Morey, No. M88-CVG-42042 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., Feb. 15, 1989) Because the number of repairs that the landlord made to the premises due to the tenant’s negligence was not unreasonable, and the tenant paid all of the charges associated with those repairs, the repairs and charges were not sufficient grounds to evict the tenant.

Fire:

Neal v. Manor, No. L-07-1055, 2008 WL 203648 (Ohio App., 6th Dist.,
Jan. 25, 2008) A fire at the tenant’s rental unit rendered it uninhabitable, but the landlord completed the rehab of the rental unit by the time of the preliminary injunction hearing. In these circumstances, the tenant was a valid leaseholder of the rental unit and entitled to reoccupy the rental unit because there had not been any termination of the tenancy by a court proceeding.

Canterbury Way Apts. v. Barnes, No. 87AP-1106, 1988 WL 44529 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., May 5, 1988) A tenant’s refusal to reimburse the landlord for repair costs arising from a fire at the premises, which was started by an electric motor on the tenant’s bed, was good cause to evict the tenant.

Martin v. Grandview Apts., No. 91-CV-1215 (Ohio C.P., Clermont Cty.,
Mar. 9, 1992) Under the subsidized housing lease,222 ¶ 24, when a fire damages a rental unit to the extent that it is unlivable, the tenant’s lease is not terminated and, upon completion of the r epair of the unit, the tenant has the right to recommence the tenant’s occupancy of the unit. To terminate a tenancy in these circumstances, the landlord must comply with 24 C.F.R. part 247 222 For the definition of “subsidized housing lease,” see note 174, above.

and the subsidized housing lease, ¶ 23.

Mayes v. Grandview Apts., No. 91-CV-000871 (Ohio C.P.,Clermont Cty.,
Sept. 25, 1991) (preliminary injunction) Where a fire damaged a rental unit to the extent that it was not habitable, the landlord was required to place the tenant on a waiting list for the next available rental unit. If the landlord seeks to evict the tenant, it may pursue its legal remedies under R.C. Chapter 1923.

Mandatory transfers:

Dayton Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Pitsinger, No. 21126 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist.,
Apr. 28, 2006) A Public Housing tenant and her family resided in a three -bedroom wheelchair-accessible unit, which accommodated her husband who was
paraplegic. After her husband died and her two daughters moved from the unit, the PHA notified the tenant that she was required to move to a one - bedroom unit, so that the wheelchair -accessible unit would be available for a family requiring it. The tenant’s refusal to move constituted good cause to terminate the tenancy.

Altercation:

Dayton Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Cantrell , No. 85 -CVG-7589 (Ohio Mun., Dayton, Oct. 10, 1985) The tenant’s involvement in an altercation not as the aggressor, but as the victim who attempted to defend herself, was not sufficient grounds to evict the tenant.
