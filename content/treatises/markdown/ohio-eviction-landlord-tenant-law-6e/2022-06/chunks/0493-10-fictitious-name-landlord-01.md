# 10. Fictitious Name (Landlord)

## Table-of-contents context

- XVII. Landlord Claim for Rent
  - C. Defense Issues

## Source text

If the name of the landlord in a lease is a fictitious name, and if the landlord failed to register the fictitious name with the Secretary of State under R.C. 1329.01, the landlord may not bring an action under the lease for unpaid rent. Ebner v. Caudill, 93 Ohio App.3d 785, 639 N.E.2d 1231 (10th Dist. 1994); Frate v. Al-Sol, Inc., 131 Ohio App.3d 283, 722 N.E.2d 185 (8th Dist. 1999).

The case law is split on whether, in these circumstances, the landlord may maintain the action in the fictitious name if the landlord has registered the fictitious name after the filing of the action but before the entry of the final judgment . See Section II.D, below.
