The basis for this rule is a general contract principle – the failure on the part of one party to perform in accordance with a lease will be excused when performance is prevented by the other party to the lease – which is discussed in Section II.Z, below.

“Timely tender of rent is a defense to an eviction for non -payment of rent.” Cuyahoga River Assocs. Ltd. P’ship v. MJK Corp., No. 68673, 1996 WL 17292, at *4 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 18, 1996).

For this defense, a timely tender of rent includes a tender of rent during any grace
period. E.g., Showe Mgmt. Corp. v. Moore, No. 08-CA-10, 2009 WL 1372986 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., May 14, 2009) (ten -day grace period); Columbus Metro. Hous. Auth.
v. Jackson, No. 81AP-802, 1982 WL 4010 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 4, 1982) (ten- day grace period).

A landlord may refuse a timely but partial payment of the rent and then evict for nonpayment of rent. Cuyahoga River Assocs. Ltd. P’ship v. MJK Corp., No. 68673, 1996 WL 17292, at *4-5 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 18, 1996). Cf. Julian Invs., Inc. v.

Dudley, No. 98-CA-85, 1999 WL 64227, at *7 (Ohio App., 2nd Di st., Feb. 12, 1999) (tender of partial payment of past due rent).

As a general rule , a landlord also may refuse a full but untimely payment of the rent and then evict for nonpayment of rent. See Pinewood Gardens Apts. v. Whiteside,
No. 25989, 2014 WL 2169720, ¶ 17 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., May 23, 2014); Simco
Mgmt. Corp. v. Snyder, No. 98-CA-210, 2000 WL 309396, at *5 (Ohio App., 7th Dist.,
Mar. 20, 2000). But, in some circumstance, a landlord’s refusal to accept a full but untimely payment of the rent may give rise to an eviction defense. Three of those circumstances are discussed in Section II.H, above (course of conduct), Section II.BB, below (equity), and Section III.E, below (right to cure, assisted housing).
