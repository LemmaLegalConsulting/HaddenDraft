Occupied Conveyance Rule When an FHA -insured mortgage on a single -family ( i.e., one to four unit) residential property is foreclosed, the mortgagee “conveys the property to HUD in exchange for an FHA mortgage insurance claim.” HUD Notice, 75 Fed. Reg. 66385, 66386 (Oct. 28, 2010). “HUD generally requires the mortgagee to convey the property unoccupied, but in certain circumstances, as described in HUD’s occupied conveyance regulations at 24 CFR 203.670-203.681, HUD will accept the property occupied.” Id.

When HUD requires an unoccupied conveyance, the mortgagee is still required to comply with PTFA (see Section XXII.C.3, above), for any bona fide rental agreement at the property. Id.; HUD Notice, 77 Fed. Reg. 15379 (Mar. 15, 2012).

In addition, pending HUD’s review of a tenant’s request for continued occupancy under 24 C.F.R. §§ 203.670-203.681, any eviction action against the tenant should be stayed on preemption and/or ripeness grounds. Bank United v. Vogel, No. 99-SP-2369
(Mass. Hous. Ct., Hampden Div., Nov. 5, 1999).

If the mortgagee fails to serve the tenant with the required notice of these continued occupancy rights ( see 24 C.F.R. § 203.675), it should be a defense to any eviction action against the tenant, at least until the ten ant is provided with the notice and an opportunity to exercise these continued occupancy rights. See BancPlus Mortgage
Corp. v. Smith , No. 57641, 1990 WL 15294 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 22, 1990) (under Appellate Rule 18(C), court finds that “appellant’s brief reasonably appears to sustain a reversal of the judgment upon the facts and issues presented”).
