R.C. 2506.01 authorizes an appeal to the court of common pleas from “every final order, adjudication, or decision of any officer, tribunal, authority, board, bureau, commission, department, or other division of any political subdivision of the state.” “In order for a decision of an administrative agency to be appealable pursuant to R.C. Chapter 2506, it m ust be a final resolution rendered in a quasi -judicial proceeding.”404 Lakota Local Sch. Dist. Bd. of Educ. v. Brickner , 108 Ohio App.3d 637, 644, 671 N.E.2d 578 (6th Dist. 1996). Accord Milhoan v. E. Local Sch. Dist. Bd.

of Educ. , 157 Ohio App.3d 716, 81 3 N.E.2d 692, ¶ 13 (4th Dist. 2004); Sebest v.

Campbell City Sch. Dist. Bd. of Educ., No. 00-CA-272, 2002 WL 1483917, ¶ 6 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., June 28, 2002); State ex rel. Barno v. Crestwood Bd. of Educ. , 134 Ohio App.3d 494, 500, 731 N.E.2d 701 (11th Dist. 1998).

However, “[w]here the administrative agency should have given notice, conducted a hearing, and afforded the parties an opportunity to be heard and to introduce evidence, the order is the result of an adjudication even if the agency fails to a fford such notice and hearing.” In re Howard, 73 Ohio App.3d 717, 719, 598 N.E.2d 165 (10th Dist. 1991). Accord Milhoan v. E. Local Sch. Dist. Bd. of Educ. , 157 Ohio App.3d 716, 813 N.E.2d 692, ¶ 13 (4th Dist. 2004);Sebest v. Campbell City Sch. Dist.
Bd. of Educ., No. 00-CA-272, 2002 WL 1483917, ¶¶ 6,7 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., June 28, 2002); State ex rel. Barno v. Crestwood Bd. of Educ., 134 Ohio App.3d 494, 500, 731 N.E.2d 701 (11th Dist. 1998); Lakota Local Sch. Dist. Bd. of Educ. v. Brickner , 108 Ohio App.3d 637, 644-45, 671 N.E.2d 578 (6th Dist. 1996).

Under Ohio law, a public housing authority (“PHA”) is a political subdivision of the State. Moore v. Lorain Metro. Hous. Auth., 121 Ohio St.3d 455, 457, 905 N.E.2d 606 (2009); Dudukovich v. Lorain Metro . Hous. Auth., 58 Ohio St. 2d 202, 203 n.2, 404 “The earmarks of a quasi -judicial proceeding include a requirement, imposed by law, of notice, a hearing and an opportunity to introduce evidence.” Lakota Local Sch. Dist. Bd. of
Educ. v. Brickner , 10 8 Ohio App.3d 637, 644, 671 N.E.2d 578 (6th Dist. 1996). Accord Milhoan v. E. Local Sch. Dist. Bd. of Educ. , 157 Ohio App.3d 716, 813 N.E.2d 692, ¶ 13 (4th
Dist. 2004); Sebest v. Campbell City Sch. Dist. Bd. of Educ. , No. 00 -CA-272, 2002 WL 1483917, ¶ 6 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., June 28, 2002).

Therefore, final PHA administrative hearing decisions may be appealed under R.C.

Chapter 2506 to the court of common pleas.Dudukovich v. Lorain Metro. Hous. Auth., 58 Ohio St.2d 202, 389 N.E.2d 1113 (1979).

Those decisions include final PHA administrative hearing decisions that terminate a family’s participation in the HCVP.405 Downie v. Lake Metro. Hous. Auth., No. 2014- L-060, 2015 WL 1000211, ¶ 18 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Mar. 9, 2015). For a summary of court decisions in R.C. Chapter 2506 appeals from PHA HCVP hearing decisions, see Section XXIV.C.8, below.
