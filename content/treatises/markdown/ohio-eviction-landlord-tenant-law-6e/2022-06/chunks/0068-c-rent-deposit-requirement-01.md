# c. Rent Deposit Requirement

## Source text

When the tenant’s filing of a jury demand requires the court to continue the trial date of the eviction action, as a condition precedent to the jury trial, the court may, in the exercise of its discretion, order the tenant to deposit with the court the rent for the current month and, thereafter, to deposit the rent as it becomes due. 45 E.g., State ex
rel. Dooley v. Riley, No. 14447, 1994 WL 53838 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Feb. 24, 1994);

44 A county court is required to waive for indigent parties any advance deposit require ment for filing a jury demand in an eviction action notwithstanding R.C. 1923.101, which provides that in such actions “a party demanding a jury shall first deposit money with the court sufficient to pay the jury fee.” Lable & Co. v. Francis, No. 89-J-39, 1991 WL 24982 (Ohio App., 7th Dist.,
Feb. 22, 1991).

45 These rent deposit orders are not final appealable orders. See McCarty v. Evans, No. 02-CA- 17, 2003 WL 1563771 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Mar. 19, 2003) (bond order not final appealable order, where tenant’s jury request on landlord’s money claim is conditioned on posting a bond in the amount of landlord’s rent claim).

Gem Mgmt. Co. v. Walker, No. 60139, 1991 WL 26635 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 28, 1991); Martin v. Rogers, 42 Ohio App.3d 110, 536 N.E.2d 665 (8th Dist. 1987). See also Cuyahoga River Assocs. Ltd. P’ship v. MJK Corp., No. 68673, 1996 WL 17292 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Jan. 18, 1996); State ex rel. GMS Mgmt. Co., Inc. v. Callahan, 65 Ohio App.3d 335, 583 N.E.2d 1339 (11th Dist. 1989).

The authority on which some courts rely for these rent deposit orders is R.C.

1923.08. Id. This reliance on R.C. 1923.08 seems misplaced. R.C. 1923.08 applies only when the tenant requests a continuance for a period of longer than eight days, and the court grants it. See Section I.K, below. Since a request for a trial by jury is not a request for a continuance, it seems to fall outside the purview of R.C. 1923.08.

These rent deposit orders are more appropriately issued under the court’s inherent
authority. “[A] court has inherent power to order the escrow of funds likely to be the subject of the final judgment.” Meyer v. Chieffo , Nos. 08AP -867,877, 2009 WL 1653561, ¶ 22 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., June 11, 2009). Therefore, a trial court has inherent authority to order a tenant to deposit with the court the tenant’s monthly rent while an eviction action is pending. Id., ¶ 23 (monthly land installment payments);PK
Mgmt. LLC v. Hayes, No. 2011-CVG-008188 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., July 7, 2011) (monthly rental payments). See also Cunningham v. Phoenix Mgmt., Inc. , 540 A.2d 1099 (D.C. App. 1988);Bell v. Tsintolas Realty Co., 430 F.2d 474 (D.C. Cir.

1970).

A requirement to pay rent as the rent becomes due during the pendency of the eviction action is a reasonable limitation on the tenant’s right to a trial by jury. See Kargman v. Dustin , 5 Mass. App. Ct. 101, 110, 359 N.E.2d 971 (1977); Bell v.

Tsintolas Realty Co., 430 F.2d 474, 482 (D.C. Cir. 1970). Therefore, it does not violate the tenant’s constitutional right to a trial by jury. See Section I.J.3.b, above.
