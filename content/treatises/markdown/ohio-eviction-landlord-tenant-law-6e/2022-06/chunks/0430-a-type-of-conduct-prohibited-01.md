# A. Type of Conduct Prohibited

## Source text

R.C. 5321.15(B) prohibits a landlord from seizing a tenant’s personal property for the purpose of recovering unpaid rent, unless the seizure is pursuant to a court order.

This prohibition applies even if the tenant’s right to possession has terminated. R .C.

5321.15(B).

If a tenant vacates the premises but leaves some (or all) of the tenant’s personal property at the premises, common law governs: (1) as to whether the tenant has abandoned the property,320 and (2) the landlord’s obligations regarding removalof the
property. See Edwards v. C.N. Inv. Co. , 27 Ohio Misc. 57, 272 N.E.2d 652 (Ohio Mun., Shaker Heights, 1971).

In these circumstances, if the tenant has not abandoned the personal property, the R.C. 5321.15(B) prohibition applies. E.g., Stark v. Weimer, No. H-98-031, 1999 WL 253451 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Apr. 30, 1999); Mounts v. Foerg, No. R90-CVG-0375 (Ohio Mun., Portage Cty., Dec. 5, 1990).

A lease provision that purports to establish a conclusive presumption of abandonment, relative to personal property on the premises upon the expiration of the lease, violates R.C. 5321.15(B) and is thus unenforceable. Rand v. Washington, No.

7822, 1983 WL 2448 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., May 26, 1983).
