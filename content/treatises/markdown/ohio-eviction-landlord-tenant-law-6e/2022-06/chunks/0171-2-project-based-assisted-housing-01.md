The equity defense is often asserted by tenants who reside in project-based assisted housing.154 A strong equitable consideration against a forfeiture of their tenancy is that, if a forfeiture is granted, the tenant loses a tenancy with a subsidized rent and with a lease term that the tenant may renew continually and the landlord must renew absent good cause to terminate the tenancy. Heritage Hills, Ltd. v. Nusser , No. 1183, 1986 WL 7882, at *3 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., July 3, 1986); Cincinnati Metro. Hous. Auth. v.

Harris, Nos. C-820540,820541, 1983 WL 8893, at *3 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., June 15, 1983). See also Urban Hollow Apts. v. Johnson , No. 93 -APG10-1495, 1994 WL 232274, at *3 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., May 26, 1994);Gorsuch Homes, Inc. v. Wooten, 73 Ohio App.3d 426, 436, 597 N.E.2d 554 (2nd Dist. 1992).

Under this defense, tenants who reside in project -based assisted housing have prevented a forfeiture of their tenancy in a variety of circumstances, including those set forth below.

Four appellate court decisions that upheld the denial (or denied) the equity defense of project-based subsidized housing tenants also provide some helpful guidance.

First, Portage Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Brumley , No. 2008 -P-0012, 2008 WL 4693200 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Oct. 24, 2008), upheld the trial court’s denial of the equity defense where, at meetings before the trial, the PHA had offered to permit the tenant to stay, if she removed the offending household member (her son) from her
lease. The tenant refused this offer consistently, and refused to cooperate with the PHA, until after the trial had commenced.

Second, Schroeder Co. v. Coates, 172 Ohio App.3d 254, 874 N.E.2d 832 (6th Dist.

2007), upheld the trial court’s denial of the equity defense where the tenant did not tender the rent payment until eight days after the notice o f termination period had expired and never offered an excuse for the nonpayment of rent.
