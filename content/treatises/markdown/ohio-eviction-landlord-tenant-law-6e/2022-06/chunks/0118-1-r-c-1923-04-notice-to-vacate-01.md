# 1. R.C. 1923.04 Notice to Vacate

## Source text

Proper service of an R.C. 1923.04 notice to vacate is an essential element of the landlord’s case in chief. Sternberg v. Washington , 113 Ohio App. 216, 221, 177 N.E.2d 525 (9th Dist. 1960).

If a landlord fails to serve a notice to vacate or serves a notice to vacate but its content or the service fails to comply with R.C. 1923.04,94 the trial court lacks subject- matter jurisdiction over the eviction action. See Section I.C.1.b, above. In such cases, if the court enters an eviction judgment for the landlord, the judgment is void ab initio.95 See Patton v. Diemer, 35 Ohio St.3d 68, 518 N.E.2d 941 (1988).
