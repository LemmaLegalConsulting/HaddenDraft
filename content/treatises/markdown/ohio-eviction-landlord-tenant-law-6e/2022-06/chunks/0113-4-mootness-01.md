# 4. Mootness

## Source text

In an eviction action, when the tenant appeals from the eviction judgment, a common issue is whether the tenant’s vacation of the premises during the pendency of the appeal makes the appeal moot.

The tenant’s vacation of the premises may be voluntary. E.g., Blosser v. Bowman,
No. 00AP-1140, 2001 WL 436088, at *1 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., May 1, 2001). Cf.

Showe Mgmt. Corp. v. Moore , No. 2007-CA-00102, 2009 WL 1372988 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., May 14, 2009) (tenant vacated pending landlord’s appeal).

Alternatively, the tenant’s vacation of the premises may be involuntary. An involuntary vacation usually is the result of one of four circumstances:
(1) the tenant’s failure to request a stay of execution,e.g., Art Paradise, Inc.
v. Washington, No. 29230, 2021 WL 6136347, ¶¶ 2,6 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Dec. 29, 2021);Tiefenbacher v. Shorter, No. L-20-1186, 2021 WL 3239856, ¶ 22 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., July 30, 2021); Kell v. Little, No.

2018-CA-00064, 2019 WL 192788, ¶ 8 (Ohio App., 5th Dist ., Jan. 14, 2019); Hussain v. Sheppard, No. 14AP-686, 2015 WL 915467, ¶¶ 3,6- 10 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Feb. 24, 2015); Millennia Hous. Mgmt., Ltd.
v. Withrow, No. 12-CA-2, 2013 WL 398944, ¶¶ 2,7,8 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., Jan. 28, 2013); Miami Valley Hous. v. Jackson, No. 25020, 2012 WL 5382834, ¶¶ 4-7 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Nov. 2, 2012);AVB Props., LLC v. Chesler, No. 05-CA-008702, 2006 WL 2390243, ¶¶ 14-16 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Aug. 21, 2006).
(2) the court’s denial of the tenant’s request for a stay of exe cution, e.g., Miller v. Johnson, No. 109453, 2021 WL 631242, ¶ 8 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 18, 2021); Lake Metro. Hous. Auth. v. McFadden, No. 2016- L-105, 2017 WL 1683178, ¶¶ 6-8 (Ohio App., 11th Dist.,May 1, 2017);

Gara v. Gara , No. 26671, 2015 WL 6410285, ¶ 17 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 23, 2015); Riolo v. Navin, No. 79809, 2002 WL 502408, at *1 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Apr. 4, 2002); Witkowski v. Arditi, 123 Ohio App.3d 26, 28-30, 702 N.E.2d 1231 (7th Dist. 1997).
(3) the tenant’s failure to post the supersedeas bond on which the stay of execution is contingent, e.g., OIG Homes, LLC v. Stricklen , No. L-21- 1029, 2021 WL 4935720, ¶¶ 4-6 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Oct. 22, 2021);

AKP Props., LLC v. Rutledge, No. 2018-CA-00058, 2018 WL 6822573, ¶ 17 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Dec. 21, 2018); Rithy Props., Inc. v.

Cheeseman, 63 N.E.3d 752, 2016-Ohio-1602, ¶¶ 9,10 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., 2016); Gromek v. Lepisto , No. 2015 -L-099, 2015 WL 5781653, ¶ 5 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Oct. 5, 2015); Valente v. Johnson, Nos. 06- CA-31,38, 2007 WL 1574769, ¶¶ 20,22 (Ohio App., 4th Dist., May 29, 2007); Long v. MacDonald, No. 3-02-10, 2002 WL 31007937, ¶ 9 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Sept. 9, 2002); Coshocton Metro. Hous. Auth. v.

Dockery, No. 99-CA-24, 2000 WL 968691, at *1 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., July 5, 2000); U.S. Sec’y of Hous. & Urban Dev. v. Chancellor , No.

73970, 1999 WL 126170, at *1-2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb. 25, 1999);

Reese Invs. v. Tucker , No. 89AP -1181, 1990 WL 26119, at *1 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 13, 1990).
(4) during a pending appeal, the landlord files a second eviction action against the same tenant, secures an eviction judgment, and enforces it, e.g., Park Lane Apts. v. Parks, No. L-20-1208, 2021 WL 4480879,¶¶ 5- 7 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Se pt. 30, 2021); Weikart v. Abbe, No. 02-CO- 32, 2003 WL 21377489 (Ohio App., 7th Dist., June 5, 2003); Reck v.

Whalen, 114 Ohio App.3d 16, 682 N.E.2d 721 (2nd Dist. 1996);see also Showe Mgmt. Corp. v. Hazelbaker , No. CA2006 -01-004, 2006 WL 3479856 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Dec. 4, 2006).

The general rule is that, if the tenant vacates the premises (voluntarily or involuntarily), the tenant’s appeal from the eviction judgment is moot. Farla LLC v.

Pretlow, No. 21-CA-011731, 2021 WL 6050713, ¶ 5 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Dec. 20, 2021); OIG Homes, LLC v. Bowman, No. 00AP- 1140, 2001 WL 436088, at *1 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., May 1, 2001); U.S. Sec’y of

Chapman, No. 14593, 1995 WL 127896, at *1 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Mar. 22, 1995) (appeal not moot because tenant “left the premises only in response to an official court ordered eviction notice”).

When a tenant’s appeal from an eviction judgment is moot under the general rule, the court of appeals generally will overr ule the assignment(s) of error, dismiss the appeal, and/or affirm the trial court’s decision. Id.

There are six circumstances in which, notwithstanding the tenant’s vacation of the premises, appellate courts have permitted the tenant to prosecute the appe al from the eviction judgment. They are discussed below. Four of them – the first, second, third, and fourth, below– are circumstances in which the appeal is not moot, notwithstanding the tenant’s vacation of the premises. Two of them – the fifth and sixth, below – are circumstances in which the appeal is moot because of the tenant’s vacation of the premises, but the courts make an exception to the general mootness rule for certain reasons.

The first circumstance is when the tenant’s vacation of the p remises was involuntary, the tenant has not permanently relocated to another residence, and the premises remain vacant. Agler Green Coop. v. Rivers, No. 87AP-915, 1987 WL 31282 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Dec. 23, 1987).
