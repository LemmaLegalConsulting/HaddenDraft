## Source text

Both R.C. 5321.01(C)(3) and R.C. 5321.01(C)(10) use the term “transient occupancy” in their respective exclusions from the definition of residential premises.

R.C. 5321.01(C)(3) provides that “residential premises” do not include “tourist homes, hotels, motels, recreational vehicle parks, recreational camps, combined park- camps, and other similar facilities where circumstances indicate a transient occupancy.” R.C. 5321.01(C)(10) provides that “residential premises” do not include “emergency shelters ... for persons whose circumstances indicate a transient occupancy, including homeless people, victims of domestic violence, and juvenile runaways.” However, R.C. Chapter 5321 does not define or establish criteria for determining the meaning of the term transient occupancy, for purposes of R.C. 5321.01(C)(3) or R.C. 5321.01(C)(10). This Section VI.F analyzes the relevant law (including existing case law) for making that determination.
