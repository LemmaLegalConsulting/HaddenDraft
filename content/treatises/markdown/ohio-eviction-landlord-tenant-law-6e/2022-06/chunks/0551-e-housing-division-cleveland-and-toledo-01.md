Municipal Courts Two municipal courts in Ohio have a housing division, Cleveland Municipal Court and Toledo Municipal Court. R.C. 1901.011.

A housing division has exclusive jurisdiction, within the territory of the municipal court, in civil actions commenced under R.C. Chapter 1923 or R.C. Chapter 5321. R.C.

1901.181(A). See also State ex rel. J.K. & E. Auto Wrecking v. Trumbo, 64 Ohio St.3d 73, 591 N.E.2d 1238 (1992); Edmunds v. Little , 103 Ohio App.3d 488, 659 N.E.2d 1291 (6th Dist. 1995); Higginbottom v. Rehoboth Temple Church, Etc. , No. 67909, 1995 WL 546935 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Sept. 14, 1995); Maduka v. Parries, 14 Ohio
App. 3d 191, 470 N.E.2d 464 (8th Dist. 1984).

A housing division also has exclusive jurisdiction, within the territory of the municipal court, in civil actions commenced under R.C. Chapter 4781 (former R.C.

Chapter 3733). Anderle v. Ideal Mobile Home Park, Inc., 101 Ohio App.3d 122, 655 N.E.2d 203 (8th Dist. 1995).

The R.C. 1901.17 monetary jurisdictional limit does not apply to a complaint, 364 See note 361, above.

counterclaim, or cross-claim filed in a housing division.365 R.C. 1901.17.

Whenever an action is properly brought in a housing division, the housing division has jurisdiction:

to determine, preserve, and enforce all rights involved in the action or proceeding, to hear and determine all legal and equitable remedies necessary or proper for a complete determination of the rights of the parties, including, but not limited to, the granting of temporary restraining orders and temporary and permanent injunctions, to render personal judgment irrespective of amount in favor of any party, and to render any judgments and make any findings and orders in the same manner and to the same extent that the court of common pleas can render a judgment or make a finding or order in a similar action or proceeding.

R.C. 1901.131.

“Thus, subject matter jurisdiction [under R.C. 1901.131] is a two-part test. First, the case must be ‘properly brought’ in the housing division. Second, the Housing Court’s jurisdiction cannot be greater than that of common pleas court.” Cuyahoga
Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Jenkins , No. 2005 -CVG-22841, slip op. at 1 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Feb. 7, 2006).

“Once the housing division has jurisdiction over a claim, its incidental jurisdiction [under R.C. 1901.131] over related claims is broadly defined.” Skerl v. Sheet Metal
Prods. Co., No. 43743, 1982 WL 2453, at *2 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., July 1, 1982).

The R.C. 1901.131 incidental jurisdiction permits a plaintiff to include related claims in the complaint. E.g., Cleveland Hous. Renewal Project v. Wells Fargo Bank, N.A., 188 Ohio App.3d 36, 934 N.E.2d 372 (8th Dist. 2010) (complaint for R.C.

3767.41 claims under R.C. 1901.181 jurisdiction, and for a related common law nuisance claim under R.C. 1901.131 jurisdiction).

The R.C. 1901.131 incidental jurisdiction also permits a defendan t to assert counterclaims related to the complaint. Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Jenkins, No.
