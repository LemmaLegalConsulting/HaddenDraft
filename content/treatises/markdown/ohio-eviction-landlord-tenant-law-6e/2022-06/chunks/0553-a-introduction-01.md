The Ohio landlord -tenant law for manufactured home parks (“mh parks”) is set forth in R.C. 4781.01, 4781.36 -.52 (former R.C. 3733.01, 3733.09 -.20). The legislature relocated the Ohio landlord -tenant law for mh parks, from R.C. Chapter 3733 to R.C. Chapter 4781, effective September 10, 2012. Am. Sub. H.B. No. 487, 129th General Assembly.

In 2017, the legislature dissolved the Manufactured Homes Commission and transferred the state regulation of ma nufactured home parks to the Department of Commerce, effective January 21, 2018. Am. Sub. H.B. No. 49, 132nd General
Assembly. The Director of Commerce promulgates rules, in Ohio Admin istrative Code Chapter 4781 -12, that supplement R.C. Chapter 4781. The Department of Commerce investigates complaints concerning violations of Ohio’s Manufactured Homes Law. Id.

The Ohio landlord-tenant law for mh parks is remedial legislation and, therefore, must be liberally construed in order to promote its object an d assist the parties in attaining justice. Schwartz v. McAtee, 22 Ohio St.3d 14, 488 N.E.2d 479 (1986).

The Ohio landlord -tenant law for mh parks establishes, in the mh park context, virtually all of the rights and obligations that are established in R.C. Chapter 5321 for residential landlords and tenants. Schwartz v. McAtee, 22 Ohio St.3d 14, 17 -18, 488 N.E.2d 479 (1986). “The two chapters are essentially identical but for the omission of a parallel statute in R.C. Chapter 3733 [now R.C.
