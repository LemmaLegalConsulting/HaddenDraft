## Source text

The PTFA makes eviction actions the exclusive remedy for evicting bona fide
tenants. The foreclosure court is without grounds to enter an eviction order against the tenant because the tenancy survives the foreclosure sale.

Noncompliance with the PTFA is an eviction action defense. 382 “The PTFA was intended to allow tenants who are victims of theforeclosure crisis a protection that can be used in the state courts to combat unlawful evictions.Nativi v. Deutsche Bank Nat’l Trust Co. , No. 09 -06090, 2010 WL 2179885, at *3 (N.D. Cal. May 26, 2010).

Moreover, in Ohio, a landlord’s noncompliance with a federal notice of termination requirement is a legal defense to an eviction action. See Sections III.A-E, above A trial court may raise, sua sponte, any noncompliance with the PTFA 90 -day notice requirement because it constitutes a jurisdictional is sue. Proper service of the R.C. 1923.04 notice to vacate is a jurisdictional requirement for commencement of an eviction action. See Section I.C.1.b, above. An R.C. 1923.04 notice to vacate may not be served before the expiration of the tenancy. See Section I.C.3.e, above. Therefore, if the new owner violates the PTFA 90-day notice requirement, the R.C. 1923.04 notice to vacate has been served prematurely (i.e., before the expiration of the tenancy) and, therefore, the court is without subject-matter jurisdiction to hear the eviction action.

If the eviction action is for nonpayment of rent, the PTFA 90 -day notice of termination requirement does not apply. Like the analogous R.C. 5321.17(B) notice, it is a notice for nonrenewal (or to enable the new owner to reside in the property), not for a tenant’s breach of the rental agreement.
