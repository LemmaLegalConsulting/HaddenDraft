# 6. Deposit for Option to Purchase

## Table-of-contents context

- XVI. Security Deposit
  - B. Distinction Between a Security Deposit
    - 6. Deposit for Option to Purchase

## Source text

When a purchase agreement between a landlord and tenant establishes the right of the tenant to purchase the premises on a future date at an agreed upon price, and requires the tenant to pay a deposit on the purchase agreement in order to preserve this option to purchase, the deposit is not a security deposit. Knoppe v. Applegate , No.

08CAG-08-0051, 2009 WL 1145073 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Apr. 27, 20 09) ($5,000 deposit); Delker v. Kerr, No. L-96-110, 1996 WL 532319 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Sept.

20, 1996) ($2,000 deposit);Stacy v. Zupp, No. CA-9566, 1994 WL 369899 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., June 20, 1994) ($1,500 deposit). Cf. Dye v. Ley , No. 1 -88-60, 1990 WL 11444 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Feb. 7, 1990) ($20,000 deposit – denied tenant’s unconscionability claim); Powell v. Castner, No. 11763, 1984 WL 7651 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Dec. 19, 1984) ($2,000 deposit – denied tenant’s penalty and un just enrichment claims).
