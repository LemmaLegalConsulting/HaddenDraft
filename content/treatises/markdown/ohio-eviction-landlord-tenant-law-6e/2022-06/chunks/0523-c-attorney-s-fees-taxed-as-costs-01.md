Christe v. GMS Mgmt. Co., 88 Ohio St.3d 376, 726 N.E.2d 497 (2000), only holds that attorney’s fees under R.C. 5321.16(C) are to be taxed as costs. The Supreme Court declined the opportunity in Christe to extend its ruling to the other provisions for attorney’s fees in R.C. Chapter 5321.

Nonetheless, the Supreme Court’s reasoning in Christe compels the conclusion that attorney’s fees under any section of R.C. Chapter 5321 are to be taxed as costs.

545 See Gaitawe v. Mays, No. 25083, 2012 WL 4859073, ¶ 15 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct.

12, 2012) (“We see no logical reason for attorney fees awarded in actions under R.C.

5321.15 to be handled differently than those awarded under R.C. 5321.16”);Staffilino Chevrolet, Inc. v. Balk , 158 Ohio App.3d 1, 813 N.E.2d 940, ¶ 14 (7th Dist. 2004) (“Although the court limited its analysis to that statutory section [R.C. 5321.16(C)], the rationale can reasonably be extended to all similar statutes”).

The reasoning in Christe is four-fold. First, “[u]nder common law, attorney fees are in the nature of costs.” 88 OhioSt. 3d at 378. Second, “we [Supreme Court] have repeatedly held that when a statute authorizes the awarding of attorney fees, it does so by allowing the fees to be taxed as costs rather than awarded as damages.” Id. Third, “the legislature could have expressly stated in R.C. 5321.16(C) that attorney fees are recoverable damages.” Id. Fourth, “in the absence of such express language, we are unwilling to depart from our long -standing practice of treating statutorily authorized attorney fees as costs.” Id.

Likewise, the legislature could have expressly stated in the other R.C. Chapter 5321 provisions for attorney’s fees that the fees are recoverable damages, but it failed to do so. Therefore, for these other provisions as well, the Christe reasoning dictates adherence to the longstanding practice of courts of treating statutorily authorized attorney’s fees as costs. Gaitawe v. Mays, No. 25083, 2012 WL 4859073, ¶ 15 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 12, 2012); Drake v. Menczer, 67 Ohio App.2d 122, 425 N.E.2d 961 (8th Dist. 1980). See also Yarber v.
