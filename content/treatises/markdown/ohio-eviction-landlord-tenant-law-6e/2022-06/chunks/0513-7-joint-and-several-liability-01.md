# 7. Joint and Several Liability

## Source text

A joint and several liability provision in a resi dential lease is enforceable. Vlcek
v. Brogee, No. 25499, 2013 WL 5436930, ¶ 56 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Sept. 27, 2013);

Wayne Mut. Ins. Co. v. Parks , No. 20945, 2002 WL 1800377, ¶¶ 15 -17 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Aug. 7, 2002). R.C. Chapter 5321 does not prohibit these provisions. Id.

When a lease contains a joint and several liability provision, a tenant is liable for any breach of the lease by any of the tenants. Id.

For a discussion on the effect of a joint and several liability provision, in the context of an alleged violation of R.C. 5321.05(A)(6) by a co -tenant or guest, see Section VII.D.3.b, above.
