# f. Agreement Between Tenants Cannot Waive Landlord’s

## Source text

Duty “No agreement can relieve a landlord of the mandatory duties imposed by R.C.

5321.16(B).” Timoneri v. Northsteppe Realty, 71 N.E.3d 630, 2016-Ohio-5901, ¶ 32 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., 2016). Consequently, an agreement between replacement tenants (i.e., one tenant is replacing another tenant under a lease) cannot relieve the landlord of the mandatory R.C. 5321.16(B) obligations relative to the replaced tenant.
Id. For example, if the new tenant fails to pay to the replaced tenant a specified security deposit amount in violation of the replacement agreement, the landlord is still obligated to return the replaced tenant’s security deposit to the replaced tenant in accordance with R.C. 5321.16(B). Id., ¶¶ 32-35.
