# V. Lease, Procedures for Termination

## Source text

“[A] landlord must strictly comply with the [termination procedure] terms of a lease before instituting forfeiture proceedings.” Holmes v. Convenient Food Mart, Inc., No. 98-L-272, 2000 WL 263736, at *2 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Mar. 3, 2000).

A lease may include procedures for a termination of the lease based on the tenant’s breach of the lease. E.g., Holmes v. Convenient Food Mart, Inc., No. 98-L-272, 2000 WL 263736 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Mar. 3, 200 0) (notice of default and opportunity to cure); Gallagher v. Borden, Inc., 84 Ohio App.3d 185, 616 N.E.2d 577 (10th Dist.
1992) (notice of default and opportunity to cure);Sandefur Co. v. Jones, 9 Ohio App.3d 85, 458 N.E.2d 390 (10th Dist. 1982) (notice of termination).

The landlord’s failure to comply strictly with those procedures is a defense to the eviction action. Id.

A lease may include a provision for its automatic renewal unless and until the landlord or tenant provides the other party with cert ain notice. E.g., Frank v. Flynn, 120 Ohio App. 361, 197 N.E.2d 657 (8th Dist. 1964).

Such provisions generally are enforceable. 144 Id. See also Note, Residential Renewal Leases and the Ohio Statute of Conveyances: Invalidation and Subsequent Treatment, 27 Clev. St. L. Rev. 231 (1978) (review of Ohio case law on automatic renewal provisions and on the relationship between such provisions and the statute of conveyances).145 A lease may include a tenant option to renew for a specified period of time. However, if a lease with a fixed term includes neither a notice of default provision nor a renewal provision, the procedural requirements for the landlord to terminate the tenancy and commence an eviction action are limited. During the term of the lease, with one exception,146 prior notice to the tenant is not required to effect a forfeiture of the lease. R.C. 1923.02(A)(9).147 At the expiration of the term of the lease, the lease automatically terminates, without any notice. Gladwell v. Holcomb, 60 Ohio St. 427, 54 N.E. 473 (1899). Upon the forfeiture or expiration of the lease, the only notice requirement for commencing the eviction action is the R.C. 1923.04 notice to vacate (see Section I.C.1.a, above).
