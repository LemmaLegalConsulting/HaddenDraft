# I. Landlord, Name and Address

## Table-of-contents context

## Source text

R.C. 5321.18 requires the owner of residential rental properties to provide the tenant with the name and address of the owner and the owner’s agent (if any). If the parties executed a written rental agreement , the information must be contained in the
agreement. If the parties did not execute a written rental agreement, “the landlord, at the commencement of the term of occupancy, shall deliver to tenant a written notice containing the information.” R.C. 5321.18(B).

A landlord’s noncompliance with this requirement may result in a waiver of the landlord’s right, under R.C. 5321.07(A), to receive the tenant’s pre-rent deposit notice of landlord violations (and thus the opportunity to cure). R.C. 5321.18(C). This waiver is discussed in Section VIII.A, below. The noncompliance also may result in a waiver of the landlord’s right, under R.C. 5321.08(A), to receive the clerk of court notice of the rent deposit. R.C. 5321.18(C).

The owners of residential rental properties that are located in counties that have a population of more than 200,000 are also required to file, with the county auditor of the county in which the rental property is located: (1) the owner’s name, address, and telephone number; (2) the street ad dress and permanent parcel number of the residential rental property; and (3) certain other information. R.C. 5323.02. For these purposes, residential rental property includes residential premises under R.C.

5321.01(C) and R.C. 4781.01(W) (former R.C. 3733.01(J)).

A landlord’s noncompliance with this requirement may subject the landlord to a special assessment, imposed by the county auditor, in the amount of not less than $50 or more than $150. R.C. 5323.99.
