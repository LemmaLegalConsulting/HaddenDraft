## Source text

An FED action “is one at law based upon contract.”Behrle v. Beam, 6 Ohio St.3d 41, 44, 451 N.E.2d 237 (1983). Consequently, a municipal court has jurisdiction under R.C. 1901.18(C) to hear an equity defense in an FED action. Id.; Terra Mgmt. Co. v.

Bishop, No. CA -9919, 1987 WL 5312, at *1 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Jan. 2, 1987);

Lauch v. Monning, 15 Ohio App.2d 112, 114, 239 N.E.2d 675 (1st Dist. 1968). If the FED action “is based upon a written lease, the municipal court has jurisdiction pursuant to R.C. 1901.18(C) to hear and determine all legal and equitable remedies necessary for a complete determination of the rights of the part ies to the contract.” Brown v.

Brown, No. 04 -CA-000018, 2005 WL 914490, ¶ 21 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Apr. 14, 2005). For additional discussion on the equity defense in FED actions, see Sections II.BB and XX.C.2, below.

2 Alternatively, an ejectment action under R.C. 5303.03 may be used to evict a member of a housing cooperative. Gvozdanovic v. Woodford Corp., 139 Ohio App.3d 11, 742 N.E.2d 1145 (1st Dist. 2000).
