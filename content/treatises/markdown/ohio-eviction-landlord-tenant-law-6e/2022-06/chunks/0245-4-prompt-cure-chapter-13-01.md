# 4. Prompt Cure, Chapter 13

## Table-of-contents context

- III. Eviction Action, Additional
  - J. Bankruptcy
    - 4. Prompt Cure, Chapter 13

## Source text

If a tenant files a chapter 13 bankruptcy, another issue that may arise is whether, under the tenant’s plan, the tenant will promptly pay the unpaid rent. The tenant may assume the unexpired lease only if the tenant provides adequate assurance that the tenant will “promptly cure” the unpaid rent. See 11 U.S.C. § 365(b)(1)(A). E.g., In re Whitsett, 163 B.R. 752 (Bankr. E.D. Pa. 1994) (slightly less than two years, $1,800 – prompt cure); In re Ross, 142 B.R. 1013 (S.D. Fla. 1992) (five months– prompt cure);

In re Liggins , 145 B.R. 227 (Bankr. E.D. Va. 1992) (48 to 60 months, $8,769 – not prompt cure); In re Yokley, 99 B.R. 394 (Bankr. M.D. Tenn. 1989) (approximatelytwo years, $1,076.50 – not prompt cure). See also In re DiCamillo, 206 B.R. 64, 66 (Bankr.

D.N.J. 1997) (“The courts have consistently held that a proposed cure over a period of two years or more was not ‘prompt’ for purposes of§ 365(b)(1)”).
