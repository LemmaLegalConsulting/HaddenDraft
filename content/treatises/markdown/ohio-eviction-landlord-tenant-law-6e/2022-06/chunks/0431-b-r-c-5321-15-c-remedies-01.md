# B. R.C. 5321.15(C) Remedies

## Source text

When a landlord seizes a tenant’s perso nal property in violation of R.C.

5321.15(B), under R.C. 5321.15(C), the tenant may bring an action against the landlord for “all damages” and reasonable attorney’s fees.

R.C. 5321.15(C) does not authorize an action by a third party, whose personal property has been unlawfully seized by the landlord, because the third party is not a
tenant. O’Neil v. Walburg, 70 Ohio App.2d 30, 433 N.E.2d 1286 (10th Dist. 1980).

For a discussion on “all damages” and reasonable attorney ’s fees under R.C.

5321.15(C), see Sections XII.B.1,2, above.

320 For a discussion on abandonment of property under common law, see Section XII.A., above.

Examples of court decisions awarding R.C. 5321.15(C) damages for R.C.

5321.15(B) violations include: Gordon v. Morris, No. 2000-CA-69, 2001 WL 85797 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Feb. 2, 2001) ($1,000 punitive damages); Stark v. Weimer, No.

H-98-031, 1999 WL 253451 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Apr. 30, 1999) ($18,000 compensatory damages reversed and remanded because evidence supported, at most, $13,877 compensatory damages); Milburn v. Henness, No. 03-CVH-208 (Ohio Mun., Chillicothe, Jan. 29, 2 004) ($100 nominal damages and $750 punitive damages);

Hornikel v. Watkins, No. 00-CVF-00401 (Ohio Mun., Bellefontaine, Nov. 29, 2000) ($10,000 compensatory damages).

In an R.C. 5321.15(B),(C) action, the tenant may request injunctive relief, in addition to monetary relief and reasonable attorney’s fees. For a discussion on injunctive relief in an R.C. 5321.15(C) action, see Section XII.B.3, above.
