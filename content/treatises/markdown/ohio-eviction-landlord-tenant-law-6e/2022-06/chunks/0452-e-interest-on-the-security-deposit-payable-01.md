to the Tenant A landlord is obligated to make interest payments to the tenant if the amount of the security deposit exceeds one month’s rent or fifty dollars, whichever is greater, and the deposit is held at least six months. R.C. 5321.16(A). See, e.g., Leak v. Hale, No.

2006-CVG-33367 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Jan. 10, 2008) ($63.60 interest, for 24 months).

In such cases, the landlord must pay annually to the tenant interest at the rate of five percent per year on the amount of the security deposit that exceeds one month’s rent (or fifty dollars). R.C. 5321.16(A). See also Hart v. Per van, No. 79915, 2002 WL 31528735 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Nov. 14, 2002); Williams v. Glaser III , No. C- 000539, 2001 WL 395165 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Apr. 20, 2001);Yancey v. Haehn, No.

99-G-2210, 2000 WL 263757 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Mar. 3, 2000);Mentor Lagoons,
Inc. v. Mayor , No. 10 -180, 1985 WL 7807 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Mar. 1, 1985);

Malcom v. Tate , No. 99 -CVH-21689 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., June 29, 2001).

If the amount of a security deposit exceeds fifty dollars but not one month’s rent, the tenant is not entitled to receive interest on the security deposit. Bonesteel v. Nash, 65 N.E.3d 326, 2016-Ohio-3114 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., 2016); Murry v. Heather Hill Apts., No. L-91-155, 1991 WL 280128 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Dec. 27, 1991).

For assisted housing tenants,327 the R.C. 5321.16(A) term “one month’s periodic rent” means the monthly contract rent, not the tenant’s portion of the contract rent.
