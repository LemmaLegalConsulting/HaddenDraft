# 4. Oral Condition Precedent, Written Rental

## Source text

Agreement Ohio courts have long recognized the exception to the parole evidence rule that permits oral evidence to prove a condition precedent to the existence of a contr act.

Beatley v. Knisley , 183 Ohio App.3d 356, 917 N.E.2d 280, ¶ 15 (10th Dist. 2009).

“Courts admit extrinsic evidence of a condition precedent because satisfaction of such a condition must occur before a contract comes into existence.” Id., ¶ 16.

The only exception to this rule is that “a party may not introduce extrinsic evidence of an oral condition precedent when the written contract addresses the subject matter of the condition and the contractual terms are inconsistent with the condition precedent.” Id., ¶ 19.
