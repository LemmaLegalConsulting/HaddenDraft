Under Civil Rule 18(A), a landlord may allege in a complaint both an eviction claim and additional, non -eviction claims that the landlord has against the tenant.

Cuyahoga Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Jackson, 67 Ohio St.2d 129, 132 n.6, 423 N.E.2d 177 (1981); Mathews v. Cooper , No. 109974, 2021 WL 3560200 , ¶ 76 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug. 12, 2021); Bard Props. LLC v. Dalton , No. 24521, 2009 WL 2033078, ¶¶ 12,13 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., July 15, 2009); Marvin v. Stemen, 68 Ohio App.2d 26, 29, 426 N.E.2d 205 (6th Dist. 1980). The additional claims often are monetary claims for nonpayment of rent and/or damage to the premises. Jackson, 67 Ohio St.2d at 132;

Mathews v. Cooper, No. 109974, 2021 WL 3560200, ¶ 76 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug.

12, 2021); Maduka v. Parries, 14 Ohio App.3d 191, 470 N.E.2d 464 (8th Dist. 1984).

41 This joinder of claims is permissive, not mandatory. Mathews v. Cooper , No.

109974, 2021 WL 3560200, ¶ 76 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug. 12, 20 21); Marvin v.

Stemen, 68 Ohio App.2d at 29; Stewart v. McWilliams , No. 34694 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Nov. 20, 1975). Consequently, a landlord may file a complaint alleging an FED claim only and secure an FED judgment, and thereafter file a complaint alleg ing a monetary claim against the tenant for nonpayment of rent and/or damage to the
premises. Mathews v. Cooper, No. 109974, 2021 WL 3560200, ¶ 76 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug. 12, 2021); Stewart v. McWilliams , No. 34694, 1975 WL 183103 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Nov. 20, 1975). See also Dennis v. Morgan, 89 Ohio St.3d 417, 419, 732 N.E.2d 391 (2000).

For eviction actions against a deceased resident of a manufactured home park, the legislature purports to prohibit joinder of claims for past due rent and other d amage claims under the rental agreement. R.C. 1923.081; Sub. H.B. No. 56, 127th General Assembly, Legislative Service Commission, Bill Analysis at 7 (“The act provides that a park operator may not seek a judgment for past due rent and other damages under a rental agreement against a deceased manufactured home park resident in an FE and D action but must proceed in a separate civil action for damages (R.C. 1923.081)”).

Notwithstanding this legislative intent, the unambiguous language of R.C.

1923.081 addresses consolidation of trials, and not joinder of claims.24 R.C. 1923.081 presumes that the claims have been consolidated. It permits a consolidation of the trial on these claims, unless good cause is shown. For this purpose, it specifies that good cause includes a defendant’s request to file an answer, to file a counterclaim, or to conduct discovery.

Construing R.C. 1923.081 as a joinder of claims provision renders its good cause exception illogical. A plaintiff decides whether to join claims when prepar ing the
complaint. At that stage, the plaintiff does not know whether the defendant will file an answer or counterclaim or conduct discovery and thus whether the relevant good cause exception exists.

24 When an eviction claim and a monetary claim are joined, the court may (and often does) bifurcate the trials on the respective claims under Civil Rule 42. See Section I.M. 1, below.
