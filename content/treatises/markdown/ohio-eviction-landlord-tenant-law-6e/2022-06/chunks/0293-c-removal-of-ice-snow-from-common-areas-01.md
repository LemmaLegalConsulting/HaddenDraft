# c. Removal of Ice/Snow from Common Areas

## Source text

R.C. 5321.04(A)(3) requires a landlord to keep the common areas of the premises clear of unnatural accumulations of ice and snow, if the accumulation results in a condition that is substantially more dangerous than would have resulted naturally.

Burress v. Associated Land Group, No. 2008-10-096, 2009 WL 1459699 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., May 26, 2009); Saunders v. Greenwood Colony, No. 14-2000-40, 2001 WL 196498 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Feb. 28, 2001); Rampersaud v. Madison Dev. Co., No.

97-CA-006768, 1998 WL 332956 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., June 24, 1998).

“[A]n unnatural accumulation is ‘man -made,’ and created by a person doing something that would cause ice and snow to accumulate in an unexpected place or way.” Au v. Waldman, No. 2010-CA-112, 2011 WL 1832719, ¶ 34 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., May 9, 2011). Accord Burress v. Associated Land Group , No. 2008-10-096, 2009 WL 1459699 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., May 26, 2009); Martin v. Edgewater Props., No. 92-CA-005307, 1992 WL 357321 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Nov. 25, 1992);

271 The LaCourse decision refers to the open and obvi ous doctrine in describing the longstanding rule of common law that a landlord has no duty to keep common areas clear of natural accumulations of ice and snow. It holds that R.C. 5321.04(A)(3) does not modify this rule of common law. The basis for its holding is a principle of statutory construction: in order to find legislative intent to abrogate a rule of common law, “[s]uch an intention must be expressly declared by the legislature or necessarily implied in the language of the statute.” 28 Ohio St.3d at 212.

Klein v. Ryan’s Family Steak House, No. 20683, 2002 WL 1022974 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., May 15, 2002). See also Morgan v. Mamone , No. 87612, 2006 WL 3804527 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 21, 2006).

R.C. 5321.04(A)(3) does not require a landlord to keep the common areas of the premises clear of natural accumulations of ice and snow. LaCourse v. Fleitz, 28 Ohio St.3d 209, 503 N.E.2d 159 (1986). See also Walker v. 24570 Lakeshore Prop. L.L.C., No.105017, 2017 WL 1055294 (Ohio App. , 8th Dist., May 11, 2017); Burress v.

Associated Land Group, No. 2008-10-096, 2009 WL 1459699 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., May 26, 2009); Barner v. Continent Apt. Homes , No. 04AP-774, 2005 WL 1022909 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., May 3, 2005); Worthan v. Bryan Hous. Ltd., No. WM-97-028, 1998 WL 230583 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., May 1, 1998);McCornell v. Bridges, 124 Ohio App.3d 610, 707 N.E.2d 8 (8th Dist. 1997);Moore v. Lupica Towers, No. 71551, 1997 WL 711295 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Nov. 13, 1997); Burns v. Barrington Group, Inc. ,
No. 2 -CA-92, 1992 WL 319152 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Nov. 2, 1992); Rivera v.

Associated Estates Corp., No. 53502, 1987 WL 13044 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 18, 1987).

However, where a landlord has superior knowledge of a particular danger caused by a natural accumulation of snow and ice, the landlord may have a duty to the tenant to remedy the danger. Barner v. Continent Apt. Homes , No. 04AP -774, 2005 WL 1022909 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., May 3, 2005); Garden Woods Apts. v. Gee , No.

13962, 1993 WL 385818 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Sept. 27, 1993);Martin v. Edgewater Props., No. 92-CA-005307, 1992 WL 357321 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Nov. 25, 1992).

See also Klein v. Ryan’s Family Steak House , No. 20683, 2002 WL 1022974 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., May 15, 2002).

A landlord may assume the obligation to keep the common areas of the premises clear of natural accumulations of ice and snow by an express agreement. LaCourse v.

Fleitz, 28 Ohio St.3d 209, 211, 503 N.E.2d 159 (1986); Barner v. Continent Apt.

Homes, No. 04AP-774, 2005 WL 1022909, ¶ 16 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., May 3, 2005);

Garden Woods Apts. v. Gee, No. 13962, 1993 WL 385818, at *3 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist.,
Sept. 27, 1993).

The traditional view has been that a landlord also may assume the obligation to keep the common areas of the premises clear of natural accumulations of iceand snow 325 by an implied agreement arising from the landlord’s course of conduct.272 Oswald v.

Jeraj, 146 Ohio St. 676, 67 N.E.2d 779 (1946); Espurvoa v. Calhoun Inv. Co. , No.

91AP-406, 1992 WL 15260 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Jan. 23, 1992); Rogers v. Ayjay Dev., Inc. , No. 2 -91-1, 1991 WL 240046 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Nov. 14, 1991);

Hammond v. Moon, 8 Ohio App.3d 66, 455 N.E.2d 1301 (10th Dist. 1982).

But several appellate districts have rejected or questioned the continued validity of this traditional view. See, e.g. , Brewton v. Monnette , No. L -13-1068, 2013 WL 5210514 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Sept. 13, 2013);Thatcher v. Lauffer Ravines, LLC, No.

11AP-851, 2013 WL 816428 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 5, 2013); Hill v. Monday Villas Prop. Owners Ass’n, No. 24714, 2012 WL 690316 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Mar.

2, 2012); Sanfilippo v. Vill. Green Mgmt. Co., No. CA2010-04-027, 2010 WL 3481981 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Sept. 7, 2010); Strazinsky v. Tri -County Props., Inc. , No.

71015, 1997 WL 253169 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., May 15, 1997); Brooks v. Lee , No.

CA95-05-091, 1995 WL 708227 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Dec. 4, 1995); Yanda v.
Consol. Mgmt. Inc. , No. 57268, 1990 WL 118703 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug. 16, 1990).

Also, if the language of a lease provision is substantially the same as the language of R.C. 5321.04(A)(3), the lease provision does not require the landlord to keep the common areas of the premises clear of natural accumulations of ice and snow. Lewis
v. Byram, No. L-92-286, 1993 WL 57293 (Ohio App., 6th Dist.,Mar. 5, 1993). But cf.

Rogers v. Ayjay Dev., Inc., No. 2-91-1, 1991 WL 240046 (Ohio App., 3rd Dist., Nov.

14, 1991) (landlord construed the lease provision as imposing the obligation and the court so enforced it, as an express agreement or, in the alterna tive, an implied agreement arising from the landlord’s course of conduct).
