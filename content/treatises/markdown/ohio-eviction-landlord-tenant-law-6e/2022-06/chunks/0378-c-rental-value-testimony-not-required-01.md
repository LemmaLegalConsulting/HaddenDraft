# c. Rental Value Testimony Not Required

## Table-of-contents context

## Source text

In an R.C. 5321.04 contract claim, a tenant may meet the tenant’s burden of proof for general damages without any testimony on the rental value of the premises in their defective condition. Miller v. Ritchie , 45 Ohio St.3d 222, 227, 543 N.E.2d 1265 (1989).

The tenant may meet the tenant’s burden of proof by simply proving the extent of the defective condition(s) at the premises. Id. Thereupon, “the finderof fact (judge or
jury) must determine the monetary amount by which the defects and the reduction in use have lessened the value of the leasehold.” Id. See also Gammarino v. Smith, No.

C-060636, 2007 WL 2284573 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Aug. 10, 2007) (applying Miller);

Heck v. Whitehurst Co., No. L-03-1134, 2004 WL 1857131 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Aug.

20, 2004) (applyingMiller); Pleasant v. Greenfield Station Apts., No. 15282, 1995 WL 614359 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Oct. 20, 1995) (applying Miller); Surrey Park Assocs.
v. Kuhner, No. 89-L-14-120, 1990 WL 222990 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., Dec. 28, 1990) (applying Miller).
