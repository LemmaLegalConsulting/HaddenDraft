Agreement Any liquidated damage provision in a rental agreement that conflicts with R.C.

5321.16(C) is unenforceable. R.C. 5321.13(A). E.g., Oldendick v. Crocker, 70 N.E.3d 1033, 2016-Ohio-5621 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., 2016); KGM Capital, LLC v . Jackson,
No. C-130438, 2014 WL 2566286 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., June 6, 2014); Albregt v.

Chen, 17 Ohio App.3d 79, 477 N.E.2d 1150 (6th Dist. 1983); Riding Club Apts. v.

Sargent, 2 Ohio App.3d 146, 440 N.E.2d 1368 (10th Dist. 1981);Ankney v. Dame, No.

L-76-307, 1977 Ohio App. LEXIS 10154 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Apr. 29, 1977). See also Chaney v. Breton Builder Co. , 130 Ohio App.3d 602, 720 N.E.2d 941 (6th Dist.

1998); Cook v. Heritage Glen Apts., No. CA96-03-055, 1996 WL 535238 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Sept. 23, 1996).

Likewise, any provision in a rental agreement that provides for an automatic forfeiture of the security deposit is a violation of R.C. 5321.16 and, therefore,
unenforceable. R.C. 5321.13(A). E.g., KGM Capital, LLC v. Jackson, No. C-130438, 2014 WL 2566286 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., June 6, 2014) (lease provided for an automatic forfeiture of the security deposit, plus the amount of one -month’s rent, if tenant vacated the premises before the expiration of the lease term); C & L Ventures, LLC v. Heistand, No. 00-CVG-01995 (Ohio Mun., Fairfield Cty., Nov. 14, 2001) (lease designated the security deposit a “non -refundable security deposit”); Littlejohn v.

Dehostos, No. 91 -CVG-14377 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Mar. 9, 1992) (lease provided for an automatic forfeiture of the security deposit if tenant vacated the premises within a specified time period).
