# 2. Landlord’s Instructions

## Source text

When a tenant retains (instead of tendering) a rent payment pursuant to the landlord’s instructions, the tenant is excu sed from the obligation to tender the rent subject to the terms of those instructions. E.g., Waterford Glen LLC v. Dowell , No.

05-CVG-1055 (Ohio Mun., Crawford Cty., Apr. 7, 2006).

In Dowell, the landlord instructed the tenant to hold onto the rent untilthe landlord resolved a water leak problem. Thereupon, the landlord commenced an eviction action against the tenant for nonpayment of the rent. The court held that the landlord’s instructions excused the tenant’s obligation to tender the rent and thus de nied the landlord’s claim for possession.
