# a. Compensatory Damages

## Source text

In an R.C. 5321.04 negligence per se claim, the types of compensatory damages that a tenant may recover include:
(1) personal injury damages, Anderson v. Ceccardi, 6 Ohio St.3d 110, 114- 15, 451 N.E.2d 780 (1983); Allen v. Lee , 43 Ohio App.3d 31, 34, 538 N.E.2d 1073 (8th Dist. 1987); e.g., Sanders v. McGee , No. 13 -CV- 003983 (Ohio C.P., Franklin Cty., Apr. 3, 2014).
(2) personal property damages , e.g., Ohio Specialized Invs., Ltd. v.

Campbell, No. 2016 -CA-00176, 2017 WL 1231727 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Mar. 31, 2017) ; Sanders v. McGee , No. 13 -CV-003983 (Ohio C.P., Franklin Cty., Apr. 3, 2014); see also Cooper v. Feeney, 34 Ohio App.3d 282, 282-83, 518 N.E.2d 46 (12th Dist. 1986); and 441
(3) inconvenience and discomfort damages , e.g., Johnson v. Rahim , No.

2019-CVG-008127 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Jan. 21, 2020) (tenant awarded $3,850 ($50 per day) for the inconvenience and discomfort caused by the termination of water service to the dwelling unit); Nobles v. Johns , No. 91 -CVG-28244 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland,
Hous. Div., Mar. 25, 1992) (tenant awarded $2,200 ($100 per day) for the inconvenience of residing in a shelter for twenty -two days after her premises had been flooded and rendered unlivable by a rain-storm, as a result of a defect in the roof).

In an R.C. 5321.04 ne gligence per se claim, a tenant is not permitted to recover emotional distress damages. Allen v. Lee, 43 Ohio App.3d 31, 34, 538 N.E.2d 1073 (8th Dist. 1987). However, if the emotional distress is serious and disabling, the tenant may recover emotional d istress damages in a claim for intentional, reckless, or negligent infliction of serious emotional distress. See Section IX.E, below.
