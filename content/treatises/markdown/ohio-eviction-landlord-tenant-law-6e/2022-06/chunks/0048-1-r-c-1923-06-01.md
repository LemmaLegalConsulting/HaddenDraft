## Source text

R.C. 1923.06(B) req uires the summons to contain, in a conspicuous manner, certain language, 25 if a landlord is seeking to recover residential premises in the eviction action.

Court decisions provide some guidance for determining whether a summons contains the R.C. 1923.06(B) language in a conspicuous manner. One court found the statutory language in a summons to be sufficiently conspicuous where it was “placed in the bottom left -hand corner of the page, was in small bold faced print, and was framed with red lines forming a r ectangle around the language.” Shewman v. Coons,
No. 89-CA-46, 1990 WL 34814, at *6 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Mar. 29, 1990). Because “the language was in bold print and was outlined in red,” the court found the conspicuous manner requirement to be met “des pite the fact that the [statutory] language was in smaller print than the rest of the print on the summons.” Id.

Several other court decisions have determined the analogous issue of whether an R.C. 1923.04 notice to vacate contains, in a conspicuous manne r, certain statutory language that is required for that notice. See Section I.C.2.c, above.

R.C. 1923.06(A) states that the summons “shall be ... in the form specified ... in this section.”26 But R.C. 1923.06 does not specify any form, other than the foregoing advisory language required by R.C. 1923.06(B).
