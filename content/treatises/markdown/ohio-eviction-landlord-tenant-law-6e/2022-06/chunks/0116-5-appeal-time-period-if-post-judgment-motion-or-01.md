# 5. Appeal Time Period, If Post-Judgment Motion or

## Source text

Objection Filed Under Rule 4(B)(2) of the Ohio Rules of Appellate Proced ure, if a party timely files a request for findings of fact and conclusions of law under Civil Rule 52, a motion for a new trial under Civil Rule 59, or objections to a magistrate’s decision under Civil Rule 53(D), “the time for filing a notice of appeal begins to run as to all parties when the order disposing of the motion is entered.” In eviction actions, this tolling rule should apply when: (1) a party files a motion for a new trial under Civil Rule 59 because Civil Rule 59 applies to eviction actions (see Section I.P.1, above); or (2) a party files an objection to the magistrate’s decision under Civil Rule 53(D) because Civil Rule 53(D) applies to eviction actions (see Section I.N.3, above).

However, in eviction actions, this tolling rule does not apply when a party files a request for findings of fact and conclusions of law because, even though such findings and conclusions may be issued within seven working days of the trial, Civil Rule 52 does not apply to eviction actions. See Section I.N.1, above.
