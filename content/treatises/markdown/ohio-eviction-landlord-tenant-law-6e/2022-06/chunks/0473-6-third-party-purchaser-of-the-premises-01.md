# 6. Third-Party Purchaser of the Premises

## Source text

When a third party purchases the premises during the tenancy, an issue may arise as to whether the original landlord or the new landlord is liable to the tenant for the security deposit.

Because a security deposit is a pledge (see Section XVI.A, above), the original landlord, as pledgee, always remains liable to the tenant for returning the security deposit in accordance with the terms of the rental agreement. Dovi Interests, Ltd. v.

Somerset Point Ltd. P’ship, No. 82788, 2004 WL 254211 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Feb.

12, 2004); Castlebrook, Ltd. v. Dayton Props. Ltd. P’ship, 78 Ohio App.3d 340, 604 N.E.2d 808 (2nd Dist. 1992); UNF Corp. v. Deffet Rentals, Inc., No. 81AP-337, 1981 WL 3661 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Dec. 10, 1981); Tuteur v. P. & F. Enters., Inc. , 21 Ohio App.2d 122, 255 N.E.2d 284 (8th Dist. 1970).

The new landlord is liable only if, in the purchase of the premises, the new landlord agreed to assume such liability or received a credit for the security deposit against the sale price. Castlebrook, Ltd., 78 Ohio App.3d at 348; UNF Corp., 1981 WL 3661, at *1,2; Tuteur, 21 Ohio App.2d at 133. E.g., Jordan v. McShan , No. 91 -CVG-20057 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Apr. 9, 1992) (purchase agreement provision providing for the transfer of the tenant’s security deposit made the new landlord liable for the deposit).

The new landlord’s agreement to assume such liability may be established by the lease between the tenant and the original landlord, if the lease contains a provision imposing such liability on any purchaser of the premises. Smith v. Applegate Prop.

Mgmt., No. 2012-CVF-2488 (Ohio Mun., Massillon, July 31, 2013). This conclusion is based, in part, on t he common law rule that, when a third -party purchases rental property, generally the third-party purchases the property subject to the existing leases.
Id. For a discussion on that common law, see Section XXII.B, below.
