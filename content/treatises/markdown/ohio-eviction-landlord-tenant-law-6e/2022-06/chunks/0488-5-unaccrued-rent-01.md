# 5. Unaccrued Rent

## Source text

“Generally, a landlord may not recover unaccrued rent because such recovery ignores the possibility that the landlord could recover rent from a new tenant.” Schulz
v. Grimes, No. 91-CA-3, 1992 WL 24883, at *7 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., Feb. 11, 1992).

Accord Gen. Dev. Corp. v. Wilbur-Rogers Atlanta Corp., 28 Ohio App.2d 35, 38, 273 N.E.2d 908 (1st Dist. 1971).

In addition, “termination [of the lease or tenancy] does not operate as a windfall for the landlord– the remaining rents under the agreement do not come due all at once.” Dennis v. Morgan, 89 Ohio St.3d 417, 419, 732 N.E.2d 391 (2000).

This rule does not deprive landlords of an adequate remedy to recover the rents that accrue after an eviction judgment (“future rent”). An eviction judgment in favor of the landlord does not bar the landlord from bringing a separate action against the tenant for the future rent, if and when it becomes due and owing. Dennis v. Morgan, 89 Ohio St.3d 417, 419, 732 N.E.2d 391 (2000); Gen. Dev. Corp. v. Wilbur -Rogers Atlanta Corp., 28 Ohio App.2d 35, 37, 273 N.E.2d 908 (1st Dist. 1971). For additional 523 discussion on issues related to a separate action for future rent, see Sections I.E.2.e and I.M.8, above.
