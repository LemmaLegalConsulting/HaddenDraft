# 5. Maintain Fixtures/Appliances in Good and Safe

## Source text

Working Order, R.C. 5321.04(A)(4) R.C. 5321.04(A)(4) requires a landlord to maintain in good and safe working order and condition all electrical, plumbing, sanitary, heating, ventilating, and air conditioning fixtures and appliances, and elevators, that the landlord has supplied or is required to supply. E.g., CMB P’ship v. Baker, No. 18159, 2000 WL 706824, at *3 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., June 2, 2000) (duty to repair a leaking faucet that resulted in a wet dining room carpet).

With respect to elevators, this “statutory ‘good and safe working order’ standard requires more than code compliance.” Cmty. Dev. Props. Cleveland, Inc. v. Griffin ,
No. 87954, 2007 WL 613956, ¶ 15 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Mar. 1,2007). The standard requires a landlord, in addition to achieving code compliance, “to ensure the proper functioning of the elevators and to prevent sudden stops upon the shifting of weight in the elevator, and excessive breakdowns/malfunctions.” Id., ¶¶ 4-6,15.

“R.C. 5321.04(A)(4) requires a landlord to keep air conditioning in good working order without regard to the season.” CMB P’ship v. Baker , No. 18159, 2000 WL 706824, at *3 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., June 2, 2000). “R.C. 5321.04(A)(4) requires an air conditioner to perform when needed, regardless of outside temperature.” Id. See also Howard v. Simon, 18 Ohio App.3d 14, 16, 480 N.E.2d 99 (8th Dist. 1984).

The R.C. 5321.04(A)(4) duty to maintain an air conditioner includes the duty to turn on the air conditioning system. “An air conditioner which is turned off results in the same uninhabitable conditions as one which is broken.”Howard v. Simon, 18 Ohio App.3d 14, 16, 480 N.E.2d 99 (8th Dist. 1984).
