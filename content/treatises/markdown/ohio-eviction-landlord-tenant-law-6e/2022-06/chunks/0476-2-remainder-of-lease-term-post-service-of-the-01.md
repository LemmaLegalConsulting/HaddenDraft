## Source text

Notice to Vacate “[T]he issuance of a three-day notice to vacate pursuant to R.C. 1923.04 does not terminate the obligation of the tenant to the landlord to pay rent for the remainder of the term or until a new tenant is secured in the exercise of reasonable diligence.” Dennis v. Morgan, 89 Ohio St.3d 417, 418, 732 N.E.2d 391 (2000).

“If the lessor has acted reasonably in attempting to secure a new tenant, the lessee is liable for the rent up to the point of the lessor’s finding a new tenant or the expiration of the lease, whichever is earlier.” Id. at 419. See also Pinnacle Mgmt. v. Bell , No.

CA2011-08-145, 2012 WL 1202678 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Apr. 9, 2012); Budler v.

Empire Mgmt. Group, No. 22376, 2005 WL 1026697 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., May 4, 2005).

The Dennis decision resolved a conflict among the lower courts on whether, in the absence of a continuing liability provision in the lease, the landlord’s service of a notice to vacate relieved the tenant of future rental obligations under the lease.

Notwithstanding Dennis, if a tenant vacates the premises in response to the landlord’s notice to vacate, and the landlord served the notice to vacate in violation of the tenant’s rights under the lease and applicable law, the tenant is not liable for any rent that accrues subse quent to the tenant’s vacation of the premises. Wehrley v.

Sunchase Am., Ltd., No. CA99-11-191, 2001 WL 88202 (Ohio App., 12th Dist., Jan.

29, 2001). In these circumstances, the tenant also has a claim against the landlord for wrongful eviction. See Section XIV.B, above.
