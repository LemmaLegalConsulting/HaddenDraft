“The Rules of Evidence apply in trials of forcible entry and detainer actions.”T&R Props., Inc. v. Wimberly , 158 N.E.3d 137, 2020 -Ohio-4279, ¶ 38 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., 2020). Therefore, in an eviction action, as in other cases, generally hearsay evidence is not admissible. Id.

A landlord’s affidavit of facts in support of an eviction claim is hearsay. Id., ¶¶ 38,39. Upon a timely objection, a court is required to deny the admission of the affidavit into evidence. Carl Edward Miller Trust v. Jones , No. 2018 -CVG-15385 (Ohio Mun., Franklin Cty., June 13, 2018). The case law has addressed some of the common examples of hearsay evidence in an eviction action.

The hearsay rule may preclude a manage r from testifying on the substance of complaints that the manager has received from other tenants.Associated Estates Corp.
v. Bartell, 24 Ohio App.3d 6, 492 N.E.2d 841 (8th Dist. 1985).

The hearsay rule may preclude a manager from testifying on the subst ance of complaints that the manager has received from the manager’s employees. Trail W.
Apts. v. Zigan , No. 05 -CVG-01267 (Ohio Mun., Licking Cty., June 23, 2005). Cf.

First Merit Venture v. Stringer, No. C-050238, slip op. at 3 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Dec.

14, 2005) (failure to prove drug activity where “manager conceded that she had no knowledge of drug activity at Stringer’s apartment other than what the police had told her”).

The hearsay rule may preclude a manager from introducing into evidence the written complaints that the manager has received from other tenants.Norwalk N. Apts.
v. Mackey , No. H -93-38, 1994 WL 262508 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 10, 1994);

Norwalk N. Apts. v. Holiday , No. H-93-37, 1994 WL 262509 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., June 10, 1994).

The hearsay rule may preclude a landlord from introducing a police report or a lab report on contraband, when the landlord fails to present a witness to authenticate the report, which is not self-authenticating. Detroit Terrace Ltd. v. Bender, No. 11-CVG- 19350 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Dec. 5, 2011) (police report); CMHA v.

Johnson, No. 2004-CVG-10962 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Aug. 11, 2004) (lab report).

The hearsay rule may preclude a police officer from testifying as to statements made to the officer by a non -tenant concerning the residency of the non -tenant and another non-tenant in the tenant’s unit. Geauga Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Biggs, No. 98- G-2207, 2000 WL 665567 (Ohio App., 11th Dist., May 19, 2000).

The hearsay rule may preclud e a police officer from testifying as to statements made to the officer by a confidential informant concerning unlawful property (guns) in a tenant’s apartment, where the testimony is sought as circumstantial evidence of the tenant’s knowledge of the exist ence of the property in the apartment. See Chicago
Hous. Auth. v. Rose, 203 Ill. App.3d 208, 217-18, 560 N.E.2d 1131 (1990). Cf. Camco,
Inc. v. Lowery , 362 Ill. App.3d 421, 434, 839 N.E.2d 655 (2005) (arrest and police reports inadmissible since they generally contain hearsay evidence).
