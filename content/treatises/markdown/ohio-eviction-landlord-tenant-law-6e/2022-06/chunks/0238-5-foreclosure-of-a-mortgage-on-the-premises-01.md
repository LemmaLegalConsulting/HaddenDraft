## Source text

During the Tenancy – Preemption Under Ohio common law, when a foreclosure action concerns tenant -occupied property and the tenant is a party to the foreclosure action, the foreclosure sale may terminate the tenancy. See Section XXII.C.2, below. If the tenant is not a party to the 267 foreclosure action, and the tenant seeks to affirm the tenancy, Ohio case law is split on whether the foreclosure sale may terminate the tenancy. Id.

The federal law on termination of a HCVP lease arguably preempts 229 this Ohio common law. Federal law requires good cause for any termination of a HCVP lease, during a lease term. 42 U.S.C. § 1437f(o)(7)(C); 24 C.F.R. § 982.310; HUD Tenancy Addendum, form HUD-52641-A (July 2019), ¶ 8.

The objective of this federal law is “to provide participating [HCVP] familieswith security of tenancy and reasonable assurance that, unless they breach a material term of their lease or the landlord has ‘good cause’ to terminate the tenancy, they can continue to reside in their homes.” Webster Bank v. Occhipinti, No. CV-970059147S, 1998 WL 846105, at *4 (Conn. Super. Nov. 20, 1998) (quoting EMC Mortgage Corp.
v. Smith, No. 95-04794, slip op. at 17 (Mass. Hous. Ct., Boston Div., Jan. 5, 1996)).

Accord German v. Fed. Home Loan Mortgage Corp. , 899 F. Supp. 1155 (S.D.N.Y.

1995).

Court decisions in other jurisdictions,230 addressing this precise preemption issue, have held that this federal law preempts any state foreclosure law that would terminate a HCVP lease, during a lease term, without good cause.231 E.g., German v. Fed. Home Loan Mortgage Corp., 899 F. Supp. 1155, 1164 (S.D.N.Y. 1995); Webster Bank v.

Occhipinti, No. CV -970059147S, 1998 WL 846105 (Conn. Super. Nov. 20, 1998);

Bristol Sav. Bank v. Savinelli, No. CV-950377478S, 1996 WL 166396 (Conn. Super.
Mar. 21, 1996); EMC Mortgage Corp. v. Smith , No. 95 -04794 (Mass. Housing Ct., Boston Div., Jan. 5, 1996); Fed. Home Loan Mortgage Corp. v. Surzhukov , No. 95 - SP-1487 (Mass. Hous. Ct., Hampden Div., Aug. 9, 1995). But see Atlantic Mortgage & Inv. Corp. v. Pervis, No. SPNH-970651076, 1997 WL 866672 (Conn. Super. Dec.

3, 1997).

Federal law does not require good cause for nonrenewal of a HCVP lease at the expiration of a lease term. See Section III.A., above. Therefore, federal law does not conflict with or preempt any state foreclosure law that terminates a HCVP lease, without good cause, at the expiration of a lease term.
