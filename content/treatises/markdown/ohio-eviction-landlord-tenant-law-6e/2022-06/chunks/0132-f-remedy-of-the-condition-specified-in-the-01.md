R.C. 5321.11 Notice of Termination When a lan dlord seeks to terminate a tenancy on the ground that the tenant has violated an R.C. 5321.05 duty (other than R.C. 5321.05(A)(9)) and the violation materially affects health and safety, the landlord is required to serve the tenant with an R.C. 5321.11 notice of termination. See Section I.D, above. The R.C. 5321.11 notice of termination must describe with specificity the acts and omissions of the tenant that constitute the R.C. 5321.05 violation. See Section I.D.2.c, above.

If the tenant remedies the R.C . 5321.05 violation prior to the termination date specified in the R.C. 5321.11 notice of termination, the rental agreement is not terminated under the notice and the tenant’s conduct that violated R.C. 5321.05 cannot be the basis for an eviction action. See Section I.D.2.e, above. For a discussion on the tenant’s right to remedy an R.C. 5321.05 violation by paying to the landlord the cost of repairing the damages, see Section I.D.2.e, above.

When a landlord’s eviction claim is based on an R.C. 5321.11 n otice of termination, an element of the claim is that the tenant failed to remedy timely the R.C.

5321.05 violation. See Meredith v. Nash, No. 93-CVG-2334 (Ohio Mun., Massillon,
Oct. 20, 1993); Stark Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Gwin , No. 91 -CVG-5767 (Ohio Mun., Canton, Nov. 15, 1991); Stark Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Moore, No. 89-CVG-9248 (Ohio Mun., Canton, Feb. 9, 1990). Cf. Vesta Cleveland LLC v. Swann, No. 2003-CVG-5468 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., May 20, 2003) (notice of termination under lease provision).

Consequently, the landlord is required to prove that, as of the termination date specified in the R.C. 5321.11 notice of termination, the tenant did not remedy the R.C.

5321.05 violation. Id.
