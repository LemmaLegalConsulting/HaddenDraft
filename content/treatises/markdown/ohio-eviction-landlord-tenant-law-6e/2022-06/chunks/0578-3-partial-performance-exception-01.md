## Source text

The equitable doctrine of partial performance applies to the statute of frauds and to the statute of conveyances. Delfino v. Paul Davies Chevrolet, Inc. , 2 Ohio St.2 d 282, 286, 209 N.E.2d 194 (1965); Cuvier Press Club v. Fourth & Race St. Assocs., Ltd., 1 Ohio App.3d 30, 35-36, 439 N.E.2d 443 (1st Dist. 1981); Schloss v. Sachs, 63 Ohio Misc.2d 457, 463, 631 N.E.2d 212 (Ohio Mun., Hamilton Cty., 1993).

Therefore, this doctrine may take an oral rental agreement out of the statute of frauds, and out of the statute of conveyances, and thereby render the agreement valid and enforceable.

The standard for determining whether this doctrine applies in a particular case is the same for both statutes. Delfino v. Paul Davies Chevrolet, Inc. , 2 Ohio St.2d 282, 286, 209 N.E.2d 194 (1965).

The first and second elements are taking possession of the premises and payment of rent, wher e the possession and rent payments are referable to the oral rental agreement and not to any other source. 375 Id. The third element is one of two alternative circumstances. Id.

The third element may be detrimental reliance by the party arguing partial performance, where the detrimental reliance makes it impossible or impractical to place the parties in status quo. 376 Id. See also Brannan v. Fowler , 100 Ohio App.3d 577, 584, 654 N.E.2d 434 (4th Dist. 1995).

Alternatively, the third element may be an unconscionable advantage obtained by the party raising the statute of frauds (or statute of conveyances). Brannan v. Fowler, 100 Ohio App.3d 577, 584, 654 N.E.2d 434 (4th Dist. 1995);Manifold v. Schuster, 67 Ohio App.3d 251, 254-55, 586 N.E.2d 1142 (4th Dist. 1990).

When the equitable doctrine of partial performance takes an oral rental agreement out of the statute of frauds (and the statute of conveyances), the permissible actions under the rental agreement include actions at law and actions in equity. Hughes v.

Oberholtzer, 162 Ohio St. 330, 123 N.E.2d 393, syllabus no. 3 (1954); Aircraft Specialty Co. v. Ctr. Woodland Realty Co. , 40 Ohio App. 125, 178 N.E.2d 213 (8th
Dist. 1931). But see Beggin v. Ft. Worth Mortgage Corp. , 93 Ohio App.3d 333, 638 N.E.2d 604 (3rd Dist. 1994) (misconstrues Hughes).
