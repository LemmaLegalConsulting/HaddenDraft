# 2. Wrongful Withholding

## Source text

In an R.C. 5321.16(C) claim, the determination on whether the withheld portion of the security deposit is wrongfully withheld is based solely on whether the landlord has a substantive right to retain it (i.e., whether the tenant owes the landlord for unpaid rent and/or damages in an amount equal to or greater than the withheld portion of the security deposit).

By virtue of the rulings in Smith v. Padgett, 32 Ohio St.3d 344, 513 N.E.2d 737 (1987), and Vardeman v. Llewellyn , 17 Ohio St.3d 24, 476 N.E.2d 1038 (1985), whether the landlord has complied with the R.C. 5321.16(B) written itemization requirement is irrelevant to the R.C. 5321.16(C) wrongful withholding determination.

In Padgett, the court held that, when a landlord has provided a tenant with a written itemization of the withheld portion of the security deposit and has provided the itemization timely and in good faith, it is still a wrongful withholding if the court ultimately finds that the landlord did not have a substantive right to retain it.333 In Vardeman, the court held that, when a landlord has a substantive right to retain the withheld portion of the security deposit, the landlord’s failure to provide thetenant with a written itemization does not cause the withholding to be a wrongful withholding.334 333 See also Warner v.
