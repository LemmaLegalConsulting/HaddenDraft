## Source text

When a foreclosure action concerns tenant-occupied property and the tenancy was created after the mortgage was executed, three issues often arise. The first is whether, 601 pending the foreclosure action ( i.e., after commencement of the action and before confirmation of the foreclosure sale), the landlord-tenant relationship continues and, if so, are the rights and du ties of the landlord or tenant modified in any respect. The second is whether the confirmation of the foreclosure sale terminates the tenancy. The third is whether, in the foreclosure action, the court may issue an order ( i.e., writ of
possession) for the forcible removal of the tenant from the premises.

Ohio common law on these issues is discussed in Section XXII.C.2, below. The Protecting Tenants at Foreclosure Act (“PTFA”)378 – a federal law that preempts Ohio common law on these issues – is discussed in Section XXII.C.3, below. Rules governing FHA-insured mortgages on these issues are discussed in Section XXII.C.4, below.
