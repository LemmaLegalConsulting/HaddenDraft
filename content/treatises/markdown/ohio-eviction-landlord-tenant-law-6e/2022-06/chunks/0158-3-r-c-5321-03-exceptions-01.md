## Source text

R.C. 5321.03 bars the R.C. 5321.02 retaliation defense if any of the following four circumstances exists.
(1) The tenant is in default in the payment of rent. R.C. 5321.03(A)(1).See Di Fiore v. Booker , No. 108946, 2020 WL 2992105 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., June 4, 2020); Mushi v. Dietz Prop. Group, No. 18-AP-774, 2019 WL 4450155 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Sept. 17, 2019);Timbercreek Vill.
Apts. v. Myles, No. 17422, 1999 WL 335307 (Ohio App., 2nd Dist., May 28, 1999); Witkowski v. Arditi, 123 Ohio App.3d 26, 702 N.E.2d 1231 (7th Dist. 1997);Weldon Square Apts. v. Lyons, No. 84AP-06, 1985 WL 9901 (Ohio App., 10th Dist., Mar. 19, 1985); Cuyahoga Metro. Hous.
Auth. v. Watkins, 23 Ohio App.3d 20, 491 N.E.2d 701 (8th Dist. 1984);

Gerber v. Bistor, No. 1795, 1982 WL 2806 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Oct.

27, 1982).
(2) The condition about which the tenant complained was caused by the tenant or the tenant’s guest. R.C. 5321.03(A)(2).
(3) The performance of the repairs necessary t o achieve code compliance would effectively deprive the tenant of the use of the premises. R.C.

5321.03(A)(3). See Wagar v. Hatnavy, No. 1433, 1985 WL 4734 (Ohio App., 9th Dist., Dec. 26, 1985).
(4) The tenant is holding over the tenant’s term. R.C. 5321.03(A)(4).

The R.C. 5321.03(A)(4) exception (“holdover exception”) applies when the alleged retaliatory act is the landlord’s refusal to renew a fixed-term lease. E.g., Krantz
v. Pahnke, No. 2021-CA-0043, 2022 WL 39486 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Jan. 5, 2022) (nonrenewal of a lease upon the expiration of its one-year term); Indian Hills Senior Cmty., Inc. v. Sanders, No. 78780, 2001 WL 958055 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Aug. 23,
2001) (nonrenewal of a lease upon the expiration of its two -year term); Kaleda v.

Whitehurst Co., No. L -00-1048, 2000 WL 1205420 (Ohio App., 6th Dist., Aug. 25,
2000) (nonrenewal of a lease upon the expiration of its one -year term). See also McCarthy v. Caplan , No. 79721, 2001 WL 1671422 (Ohio App., 8th Dist., Dec. 6,
2001) (dicta).

The holdover exception also applies to month-to-month tenancies when the alleged retaliatory act occurred after the tenant became a holdover tenant. E.g., Siegler v.

Batdorff, 63 Ohio App.2d 76, 83, 408 N.E.2d 1383 (8th Dist. 1979).
