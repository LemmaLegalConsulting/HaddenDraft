# b. On or Near the Premises

## Source text

Among the categories of criminal activity that are described in Section III.G.8.a, above, two establish a standard under which the criminal activity must occur on or near the premises. One is the standard for drug related criminal activity relative to a project- based subsidized housing tenancy or a HCVP tenancy. See Section III.G.8.a.(5),
above. The second is the standard for violent criminal activity relative to a HCVP
tenancy. See Section III.G.8.a.(6), above.

HUD regulations do not define the “near the premises” element of the standard.

However, HUD provides some guidance in its preamble:

HUD tracks the statutory standar d, and does not attempt to further define when a crime location is considered ‘near’ the assisted project or building. In general, this standard would cover drug crime in a street or other right of way that adjoins the project or building where a Section 8 unit is located. A landlord -tenant court can apply the statutory standard to the circumstances of a particular case.

60 Fed. Reg. 34660, 34673 (July 3, 1995) (HCVP rule).

For example, criminal activity “in front of the housing building” is near the
premises. Stark Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Wheaton, No. 1998-CA-00128, 1999 WL 34696 (Ohio App., 5th Dist., Jan. 25, 1999). But criminal activity more than one mile from the premises is not near the premises. Lake Metro. Hous. Auth. v. Journigan , No.

CVG-940539 (Ohio Mun., Painesville, June 21, 1994). Cf. Jones v. Cincinnati Metro.
Hous. Auth. , No. A -0709492 (Ohio C.P., Hamilton Cty., Apr. 15, 2008) (HCVP termination, 2/3 of mile did not meet the “immediate vicinity” test under 24 C.F.R.

§ 982.551(l)).212 The “on the premises” element requires a landlord to prove th at the criminal activity, and not simply the arrest of the guest for the criminal activity, occurred on the
premises. First Merit Venture v. Stringer , No. C-050238 (Ohio App., 1st Dist., Dec.

14, 2005); Warren Heights Apts. v. Bristol, No. 96-CVG-19 (Ohio Mun., Warren, Jan.

10, 1996). See also Detroit Terrace Ltd. v. Bender, No. 11-CVG-19350 (Ohio Mun., Cleveland, Hous. Div., Dec. 5, 2011) (a court journal entry proved that the court accepted the tenant’s guilty plea on drug-related criminal activity, but it did not prove that this criminal activity occurred on or near the premises).
