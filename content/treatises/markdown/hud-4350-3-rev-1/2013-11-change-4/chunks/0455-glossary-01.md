Common Household Pet A domesticated animal, such as a dog, cat, bird, rodent (including a rabbit), fish, or turtle, that is traditionally kept in the home for pleasure rather than for commercial purposes. Common household pets do not include reptiles (except turtles). If this definition conflicts with any applicable State or local law or regulation defining the pets that may be owned or kept in dwelling accommodations, the State or local law or regulations shall apply. This definition does not include animals that are used to assist persons with disabilities. [24 CFR 5.306] Contract Rent The rent HUD or the Contract Administrator has approved for each unit type covered under an assistance contract. The rent may be paid by the tenant, HUD, or both. Refer to the project’s rental schedule (form HUD-92458) or Rental Assistance contract for exact amounts.

Covered Person A tenant, any member of the tenant’s household, a guest, or another person under the tenant’s control. [24 CFR 5.100]
